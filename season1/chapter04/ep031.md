Another day comes to the desert. The sun rises over the red rocks. Ako-chan sits in her place again. She puts her hand on the warm sand. She does not hope for too much today. She just waits, calm and still. The small tortoise watches from its rock.

Then the tortoise stands up on the rock. It climbs down onto the sand. It starts to walk towards Ako-chan. This time it does not stop. It does not look around. It walks straight to her, slowly and steadily. Then it sits down right in front of her. It looks up into her face. That is all. But it is everything. "Hello," says Ako-chan softly. "I am Ako-chan." The tortoise is quiet for a moment. Then it says one word. "Gabu."

No one says a thing. Dango stays very still beside her. Dalu does not run or shout. He just watches with wide, bright eyes. Obia stands quietly behind them all. The desert is warm and silent around them. A soft wind moves over the sand. The moment feels big and quiet.

This is not like the day Dalu came. Dalu ran and shouted and jumped. Gabu came slowly, on his own feet. He took his time. He was sure. Dango rests gently against Ako-chan's leg. He looks at Gabu for a long time. Then his tail begins to wag slowly.

Gabu came on his own. He came when he was ready. No one pulled him here. No one called him over. He chose them, step by step. Now Gabu walks the long road with them. Ako-chan smiles down at him. "Welcome, Gabu," she says softly.

Late in the afternoon they reached the southern end of the Dalmatian coast.

The land changed here. Fewer trees. More bare rock. The hills were lower and more open. The sea looked different too — darker, heavier, like it was holding something down.

Ako-chan sat on a flat stone near the water. She put her hand on the rock. It was warm from the sun. But underneath the warmth, something else. Cold and steady. Coming from very far below.

"Obia," she said quietly. "The rock."

Obia scanned. "I am reading many small signals. The animals in this area are not moving. They are still. Quiet. Waiting."

At the water's edge, Daru put one foot in. He pulled it back fast. "The water is cold."

"Colder than it should be?" said Ako-chan.

"Yes. Much colder. For this time of year."

"It carries it," said Ako-chan. "The water carries the feeling from the south."

Dango stood on a high rock above them. He was looking south. His ears were forward. His whole body was very still. He was listening to something Ako-chan could not hear.

"Dango?" she said.

He looked down at her. His eyes were dark and serious. "Something is wrong," he said. "Far away. But it is not staying there."

"It is moving north?" said Daru.

"Slowly," said Dango. "But coming."

Obia said, "The data supports this. The signal is expanding outward. Moving north."

Ako-chan looked at the southern horizon. The sea went on and on. Somewhere out there — somewhere she could not see — something was moving toward them.

She took her hand off the rock. But the cold feeling stayed.

"Tomorrow. Early. We move on," she said.

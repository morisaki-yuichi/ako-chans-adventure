They sat in the morning sun on the flat rock. Dango lay close to Daru's shell, his nose near the ground. Daru sat in front of Ako-chan. His eyes were steady.

"You felt it," said Ako-chan. "The disturbance. From the south."

Daru was quiet for a moment. He was choosing the right words.

"It started in the ground," he said. "Not on top. Under. Like before a big wave — you feel it in the rock before the water comes. It was like that. Small at first. Then not small."

Ako-chan nodded. She understood.

"The lizards changed first," said Daru. "They stopped coming out in the mornings. Then the birds stopped landing here. Then the sea smell changed."

"Changed how?" said Ako-chan.

"Before, the sea smelled like wind and fish and clean water. Now something else is in it. From far away. From the south. I do not know what it is. But it should not be there."

Obia said, "When did it start?"

Daru looked at Obia carefully. "About eight days ago. Maybe nine."

Ako-chan and Obia looked at each other. Eight or nine days ago. That was when Obia arrived in this time.

"I did not run," said Daru. "The birds said: wait. Someone will come from the north. So I waited here."

"You waited alone?" said Ako-chan.

"Yes."

"Were you afraid?"

Daru thought about that. He was always honest with his answers. "A little," he said. "But not enough to leave. This is my home. I wanted to know what was wrong with it."

He looked up at Ako-chan. "So. Where are you going?"

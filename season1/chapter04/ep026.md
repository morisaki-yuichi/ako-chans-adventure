The sun is hot early in the morning. The red sand burns under their feet. Obia holds new things for them. "The ground is too hot," says Obia. "You need help out here." It gives Ako-chan a light cloth. It is thin and silver and cool. "It sends the heat away," says Obia. Ako-chan puts it on. The sun feels softer now.

Then Obia turns to Dango. It holds four small boots. It holds a little mask too. Dango takes one look at them. Then he says no. He backs away fast on the sand. He shakes his whole body hard. He paws at the boots. He rubs his nose on the ground. "No, no, no!" says Dango. Ako-chan tries not to laugh.

Ako-chan kneels down by him. "The hot sand burns your feet," she says. "Please, Dango. For me." Dango looks up at her face. He looks at the four little boots. Then he gives a big, long sigh. He lets her put them on. He lifts each paw up high. He walks in a slow, stiff way. Everyone laughs, even Daru.

Daru watches from inside his Pod. He is not hot at all in there. The Pod sends the hot air away. Inside it stays soft and cool. "It is nice in here!" says Daru. Dango gives him a flat look. Then he steps out onto the sand. The boots work. His feet do not burn now. Dango lifts his head a little higher. Maybe the boots are not so bad. He walks back to Ako-chan's side. He leans on her leg in the heat. The desert is very hot. But now they can walk.

The morning was clear and warm.

They walked south along the coast. Dango went ahead, the way he always did. But now he stopped more often. He looked back. He was checking where Daru was.

Daru did not walk in a straight line. He went left. He went right. He stopped at interesting rocks. He looked under dry leaves. He smelled old stone walls. Then he ran to catch up. Then he stopped again at something else.

"He is very busy," said Dango.

"He is curious," said Ako-chan. "It is good."

The coast was beautiful in the morning light. White limestone. Low trees with silver-green leaves. The sea on the left — deep blue, still. Small islands in the distance. Their shapes were soft and grey in the morning haze.

Ako-chan opened the red notebook and wrote as she walked.

*Dalmatian coast. Day nine. Daru is with us.*

Obia walked beside her. Its eyes moved slowly across the sea and then the hills and back. Always reading. Always recording.

"The signal from the south is steady," said Obia. "Not growing. Not fading."

"Is that good?" said Ako-chan.

"It means it is not growing yet."

A small lizard ran across the path. Daru stopped. He watched it go under a stone. Then he ran to catch up with Dango.

Dango looked at Daru sideways. Daru looked back. Neither said anything.

Then Dango walked a little slower.

And Daru walked beside him.

Two small animals on a big white road, moving south together.

Ako-chan watched them. Something in her chest felt warm.

The coast went south. They all kept walking.

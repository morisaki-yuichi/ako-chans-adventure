The road was narrower here. Old stone paths, worn smooth by feet and rain. The cliffs were taller. The sea was far below.

They walked in a line. Dango in front. Then Daru. Then Ako-chan. Then Obia.

Daru was quieter this morning. He still looked at things — rocks, dry bushes, old walls. But he did not run ahead. His steps were steady and even.

Ako-chan felt the change too. The animal voices were louder. Not in sound — in pressure. Like many small things pushing at once from far away.

A group of swifts flew fast overhead, going north. Then another group. All going north.

Daru watched them go. "They are all leaving."

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

"Where will they go?"

"North. Away from what they feel."

"But we are going toward it," said Daru.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

Daru was quiet. He watched the last swifts disappear over the hill. Then he said, "I have never been south of here. My whole life. Just this coast."

"Are you afraid?" said Ako-chan.

Daru thought about it. He was always honest. He did not answer quickly.

"A little," he said. "Not enough to stop."

Dango heard them. He turned around. He walked back to Daru. He stood beside him for a moment. He did not say anything. Then he walked forward again — but this time beside Daru, not ahead.

Daru looked at Dango. Dango looked straight ahead.

They walked together.

Behind them, Obia watched the southern horizon. Clear and empty. But the data was there. Day by day, very slowly, getting stronger.

They spent the night in Split. In the morning the old city was quiet. Stone walls. Narrow lanes. The smell of salt water everywhere.

Ako-chan stood at the edge of the harbor and looked south. The sea was bright. Very bright. But it felt heavy. Like it was carrying something it did not want.

Dango sat beside her. His ears were up. He was listening.

"The birds," said Ako-chan quietly. "I can feel them."

The gulls above the harbor moved in tight circles. One landed on a stone post near her feet. It looked at her. Its eyes were dark and unsteady. She felt it without trying. The same pressure as yesterday. But stronger now. Closer to the source.

"South," she said softly. "You feel it too."

The gull made one low sound. Then it flew away.

Obia stood beside her. "I have been reading the animal signals from the coast south of here. There is something close. About three kilometers. A small land animal. It has stayed in the same area for many days. It is not running away."

"Why not?" said Ako-chan.

"I do not know. But the signal is clear and steady."

Ako-chan looked at Dango. Dango looked back. His tail moved once.

They left the old city through a narrow stone gate. The road went south along the coast. White rock on one side. The sea on the other. The morning light was clean and sharp. Every stone. Every wave. Every bird.

Dango walked ahead. His big fluffy tail bobbed as he moved. Ako-chan followed. Obia came last, quiet and steady.

The coast went south. The thread was still there. Pulling.

She kept walking.

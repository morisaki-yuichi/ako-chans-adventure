The road left the harbor and the old stone walls behind. They walked on rocky ground now. White limestone under their feet. Dry grass. Low bushes with small silver leaves. The sea was below them — very blue, very still.

They climbed a low ridge. At the top, the coast spread out below. White rock going down into water. Small islands far out. Their shapes were grey and soft in the morning haze. No boats. Just sea and rock and sky.

Ako-chan stopped. She closed her eyes and listened.

The birds were gone. But something else was here. Close. Something quiet and small. It was not afraid. The feeling coming from it was more like — interest. Like something looking out from the rocks, waiting to see who was coming.

She opened her eyes. "There is something down there."

"Yes," said Obia. "The signal is very close now. About fifty meters. Below the ridge."

Dango was already moving. He had smelled it first. He picked his way carefully down the rocky slope. His short legs and wide paws were good on the uneven stone.

"Dango — slow," said Ako-chan.

He looked back. Then he slowed.

They went down together. The rock was warm in the sun. A lizard moved fast across a flat stone and disappeared. A small butterfly sat on a yellow flower and did not move.

Below the last ridge, the ground flattened. Old stone walls. A patch of dry scrub. A cluster of rocks near the wall.

Dango stopped. He put his nose to the ground. He raised it. He looked at the rocks. Then he looked back at Ako-chan.

Something was in those rocks. And it was looking at them.

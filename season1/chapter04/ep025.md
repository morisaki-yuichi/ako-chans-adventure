The desert opens wide in front of them. The sand is red. The rocks are red too. Tall red walls rise into the sky. This is Wadi Rum. The heat comes from the front. It touches Ako-chan's face. The air is dry and hard. "The temperature is very high," says Obia.

Ako-chan looks down at the ground. Something moves near the rocks. A scorpion crawls out into the sun. Then another one comes out. Small lizards come out too. They crawl from the dark cracks. They do not hide from the light. They sit still in the hot sun. All of them face the same way. They look to the south. "You should not be out now," says Ako-chan. A lizard turns its small head. "We cannot stay inside," it says. "Something pulls us from the south." Its small feet scratch on the hot stone. Then it goes still again.

The hot air shakes above the sand. It makes the rocks look soft. The light is white and very bright. It shines off the high stone walls. Dango pants hard beside her. His pink tongue hangs out long. Dalu peeks out from his Pod. "So hot! So red!" he says. But even Dalu goes quiet now. No bird flies in the empty sky. The scorpions do not move at all. The lizards do not blink.

Ako-chan stands very still. The desert is huge and quiet. The small creatures wait in the heat. They face the way she must go. The same dark fear is here too. She knows it from before. She feels it under the hot sand. She holds Dango close and looks ahead.

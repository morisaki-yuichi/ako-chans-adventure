A head came out from between two rocks.

It was small. Round. The shell was pale yellow-brown, each piece clearly marked in dark lines. The eyes were bright — very bright. They looked at Ako-chan. Then at Dango. Then at Obia. Then back at Ako-chan.

Then the little turtle ran toward them. Fast. Much faster than Ako-chan expected. It came straight at her. It stopped at her feet. It looked up.

"Hello!" it said. "I saw you coming. From up on the ridge. You took a long time."

Ako-chan blinked. "You saw us?"

"From up in the rocks. I can see the ridge from here. And I heard the birds. They said someone was coming from the north. With a dog and a silver thing."

She looked at Obia. Obia looked at the turtle. Steady. Calm.

Dango was already sniffing the turtle all over. The turtle stood very still and let him. It waited without moving. Then Dango sneezed. The turtle blinked once.

Dango's tail started to move. Fast.

"My name is Daru," said the turtle. "I live here. I know this whole coast. Every rock. Every path."

"I am Ako-chan," said Ako-chan. "And this is Dango."

Dango sneezed again. Daru looked at him, then back at Ako-chan.

"And the silver thing?" said Daru.

"That is Obia," said Ako-chan.

Daru turned to Obia. He looked at it for a long moment. His bright eyes did not move.

"What is it?" he said.

Ako-chan smiled. "That is a long story."

"Good," said Daru. "I like long stories."

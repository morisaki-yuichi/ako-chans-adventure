The day to leave the desert comes. The sun is soft in the early sky. Ako-chan walks out towards the road. Dango walks close beside her feet. Behind them rise the tall red rocks. Wadi Rum is quiet in the morning. It is time to go on now.

Daru and Gabu roll along together. Their two Pods move side by side. Daru runs hard inside his red Pod. It rolls fast and bright over the sand. Gabu walks at his steady, even pace. His blue Pod rolls smoothly behind. One is quick. One is calm. Red and blue go down the road. The two shells travel on as one.

The morning air is cool and light. The sand makes a soft sound under the Pods. A few birds call high in the sky. The desert lets them go now. Ako-chan feels the road open wide. So much is behind them now. So much is still ahead.

The red sand slips away behind them. The rocks grow small and far away. The air turns cooler as they go. Ako-chan looks at her two friends. Daru talks a lot in his Pod. Gabu listens and says nothing at all. They are so different, these two. But they roll on side by side.

Then Gabu stops for a moment. He turns and looks back at the desert. The red rocks stand quiet in the sun. This red desert was his home. He looks for just one moment. Then he turns to the road ahead. He does not look back again. His small face is calm now. Behind them, the red rocks grow small.

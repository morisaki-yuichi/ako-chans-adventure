They stopped at a low stone wall above the sea. The sun was behind the hills. The water below was orange and then pink and then deep blue, all at once. Very quiet.

Daru climbed the wall. He sat on top and looked out. His shell caught the last light.

Dango lay beside Ako-chan's feet. She sat on the wall. Obia stood behind them, still.

"Can you hear them?" said Daru. "The fish. The ones under the water."

"Sometimes," said Ako-chan. "When the water is very still. They are quiet. Not like birds. More like a feeling."

"What do they feel?"

"Something slow. Something cold. Like something came from very far away and is still moving through the water."

Daru watched the sea. He did not speak for a while.

"I heard the birds today," he said at last. "The ones going north. They said it is worse now. Much worse. South of here."

"I felt that too," said Ako-chan.

"How far south does it come from?"

Obia answered. "Very far. The signal comes from the south. Very far south. I do not know exactly where. Not yet."

Daru was quiet. He looked at the darkening water. "Very far south," he said slowly.

Then he turned to Ako-chan. "Are you afraid?"

Ako-chan thought about that. "Yes," she said. "Sometimes."

Daru looked at the sea again. The last color was fading. Almost dark now.

"Good," he said. "That means it is real."

They all sat and watched the sea go dark. The air was warm. But underneath the quiet, the thread was still there. Still pulling.

The ship runs west now. Japan is far behind them. The sea is calm and bright and blue. The warm days come back again. Five friends ride the long waves. Ako-chan stands by the rail and watches. The wind is soft on her face.

On the deck, two Pods sit close. Daru is in his red one. Gabu is in his blue. Daru talks and talks all day. "Do you remember the white rocks?" he says. "And the hot red sand? And the big old tortoise?" His words run fast, like always. Gabu listens, slow and calm. He says only a little. "I remember," he says. "I remember it all." Daru rolls a bit closer to him. They watch the sea side by side. Two shells, near and warm. They came so far side by side.

Ako-chan watches them and smiles. She does not tell them about the rocks ahead. Dango lies in the sun near her feet. He lifts one ear at Daru's bright voice. Obia stands quiet by the rail. The sea birds turn high and white. The waves shine gold in the light. The warm wind smells of salt and sun. Everything is calm and slow today.

The sun goes down low and red. The sky turns soft above the sea. Daru talks softly now, but still talks. Gabu listens, close beside him. Ako-chan does not break the quiet. She lets them have this last warm day. The sea holds them gently as they go. The two old friends are still together. For a little while more, still together. The warm waves carry the two friends west.

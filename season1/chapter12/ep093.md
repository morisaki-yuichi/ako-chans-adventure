The sea is far behind them now. The red desert opens wide and hot. Tall red rocks stand against the sky. This is Wadi Rum. Gabu knows it well. His blue Pod stops by a great rock. The hot wind moves the soft sand.

Gabu comes out of his Pod. He stands on the warm red sand. He looks up at the tall rock. Slowly, he walks to the stones. Step by step, he climbs the low rock. He is careful, like always. At the top he stops and turns. He looks down at all his friends. Daru. Dango. Obia. And Ako-chan. His dark eyes rest on each one. He does not say a word. He looks at Ako-chan last of all. One long, quiet look. Then he turns to the wide desert.

Daru rolls forward fast in his red Pod. "Gabu! Gabu!" he calls out loud. But Gabu does not stop now. Dango stands very still and watches. Ako-chan holds her breath in the heat. The air is hot and very dry. The sand shines gold and red around them. The tall rock burns under the high sun. Obia stands behind them, calm and still. No one moves at all.

Gabu steps down the far side of the rock. For a moment Ako-chan sees his shell. Then he is gone, over the rock. The desert is wide and quiet now. Ako-chan lifts her hand to wave. "Goodbye, Gabu," she says softly. He is home now, slow and sure. She watches the empty rock a long time. Now they are four. The tall red rock stands alone in the sun. The desert keeps him now, as it should.

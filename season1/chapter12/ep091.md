The ship comes to the port at last. Green mountains rise behind the town. The air is wet and warm and soft. Suke-san stands at the rail and looks. His ears lift high. His nose works fast. This is his home. Ako-chan knows it now.

Suke-san steps down onto the land. He walks a few steps and stops. He closes his eyes. He breathes the mountain air in slowly. The smell of pine, of earth, of home. For a moment he does not move. Then he turns back to the friends. Dango comes down and stands close. He presses his head to Suke-san's side. He does not bark. He makes no sound. But Ako-chan hears the meaning clear. You are good, the quiet touch says. Suke-san is still for a moment. "...You too," he says softly. The first warm words he gives.

The others watch from the deck above. Daru is quiet in his red Pod. Gabu watches, slow and calm. Obia stands back and does not move. Ako-chan kneels and holds Suke-san once. His red-brown fur is warm and dear. The wind brings the green smell down to them. "Goodbye, Suke-san," she says. Her eyes are wet now. Dango sits beside her, very still.

Suke-san turns to the green mountain. He walks up the path, sure and proud. He does not look back at all. The trees take him in, soft and dark. Then he is gone. The friends stand quiet on the deck. No one moves for a long time. The deck feels emptier without him. The mountain stands quiet and tall above them. Somewhere up there, Suke-san is home.

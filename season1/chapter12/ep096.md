The ship turns north now. The white rocks fade behind them. Evening falls softly on the sea. The deck is wide and empty now. No Pods roll across the boards. No turtle voice rings out. Only three friends are left here.

Ako-chan stands at the rail. Dango sits close by her feet. Obia stands a little away. No one says a word at all. The deck feels big and bare tonight. Daru's place is empty now. Gabu's place is empty too. Ako-chan looks at the quiet boards. She remembers the red Pod's hum. She remembers Gabu's slow blue light. She remembers Suke-san at her right. Now there is only the dark sea.

Dango lets out a long, low sigh. He puts his head on his paws. His ears are soft and a little sad. Dango misses his loud little friends. Ako-chan feels it in his sigh. She puts her hand on his back. Obia stands still and looks north. Its blue eyes are calm in the dark. The sea is dark and slow below them. The wind hums low in the sails. No one breaks the soft, deep quiet.

No one needs to say a thing. They are three again, like at the start. Ako-chan, Dango, and Obia. The first three, from the very start. They left the garden long ago. Just the three of them, that morning. So many friends came and went. Now they come back the same way. The garden waits far to the north. The stars are out above the mast now. The dark sea carries them through the night. And the quiet sits with them, soft and full.

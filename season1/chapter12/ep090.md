The ship sails north now. Day after day, the sea is grey and wide. The cold south is far behind them. The air grows a little warm again. Ako-chan stands on the deck. She listens to the sea and the wind.

Something changes as they go. The voices grow quiet now. Each day they are softer than before. The sea birds call, but not afraid. The fish move slowly, far below. Far down, a whale sings low and deep. Once the voices all pulled to the south. Once they were scared and wrong. Now they rest. No voice calls out in fear now. The sea is calm and slow. Ako-chan knows the reason. Obia stopped its sending. That night it said, "I will stop." And now the world grows still.

But one thing stays dark to her. She does not know where the data goes. She does not know why. Obia stands near the rail. Its blue eyes look out to sea. Ako-chan watches it for a while. It is quiet too. Dango lies on the warm deck. His long tail moves softly now. Suke-san sits at her right side. Daru and Gabu rest in their Pods. The six of them sail north together. The waves slap gently on the ship.

The voices are almost still now. The sea feels calm and safe again. Ako-chan holds this deep inside. Obia stopped. She knows that much. But the rest she cannot see. She breathes the warm air slowly. Each day feels more like home. The wind pushes them on to the north. So many days lie still ahead. And the ship sails home.

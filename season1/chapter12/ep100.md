It is night now. Ako-chan sits at her small desk. The lamp is on, soft and warm. The room is quiet and still. Dango lies asleep at her feet. Outside, the garden is dark and calm.

On the desk is the red notebook. The cover is worn and soft now. She opens it to the first page. There are the words from that night. "I have to go." Four small words. She reads them slowly, one more time. She remembers that first dark night. She was small and afraid back then. The road took her so far and back. The first page is the only full one. All that long road, and just four words.

Dango sleeps softly at her feet. She rests her hand on his warm fur. The lamp hums low in the quiet room. The night is long and quiet outside. Ako-chan looks out at the dark garden. Obia is out there in the night. It stands quiet by the old shed. Obia stays here now, with her. The garden sleeps in the soft dark. The thought is warm inside her chest.

She turns to a new clean page. It is white and empty and new. She picks up her pen again. She holds it above the page. For a long moment she does not move. But this time she does not write. Not yet. She just holds the pen and smiles. The page can wait for now. Her heart is calm and full tonight. Tomorrow is a new, quiet day. There is no rush at all now. She closes the notebook softly. And the lamp glows on in the quiet.

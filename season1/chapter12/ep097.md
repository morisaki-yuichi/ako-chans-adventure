The sea is calm and grey and wide. The ship sails north day after day. The air grows cool again up here. Ako-chan stands at the rail and thinks. Dango lies warm beside her feet. The sky is pale and soft above them.

She does not say a word out loud. But she thinks of the long, long road. She thinks of the white rocks. Daru ran there, fast and glad. She thinks of the hot red sand. Gabu came so slow and sure. She thinks of the green mountain. It took Suke-san safely home. She thinks of Pen-san on the ice. She thinks of the Colonel's brave nod. The whale's deep voice fills her mind. So many friends, so far away now.

Her heart is full and a little sad. But it is a good full, too. The grey waves roll long and slow. A cold wind moves across the deck. Ako-chan pulls her coat in close. Dango sleeps and dreams beside her. His paws twitch softly in his sleep. Obia stands near them at the rail. Ako-chan looks at Obia for a while. Its blue eyes are soft tonight. Softer than before, somehow.

Once those eyes were cold and far. Now they rest on the calm sea. Obia does not send anymore. It just stands and watches the waves. Maybe it thinks too, in its way. Ako-chan does not ask it now. The sea birds turn high and white. Their voices are soft and calm now. No fear is left in them at all. She lets the quiet stay between them. The cool sea air fills her chest. Home is somewhere up ahead.

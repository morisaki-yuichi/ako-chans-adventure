The garden is wet with morning dew. The sky is gray and soft and low. Ako-chan walks on the cool grass. This is her own garden again. Dango runs ahead and sniffs the leaves. The air smells of earth and home.

Near the hedge, something small moves. It is round and low by the roots. It is the hedgehog. Its little nose points up at her. Ako-chan kneels down on the grass. She listens with all her heart. The small voice comes to her, soft and clear. "The ground is quiet," it says. "I can sleep again." Its voice is calm and warm now. It is not afraid anymore. Ako-chan's heart feels light and glad.

Dango pushes his nose into the hedge. He sniffs deep in the cool leaves. His tail wags slowly behind him. Dew shines brightly on every leaf. The morning light grows warm and gold. The cold air feels fresh and clean. The birds begin to sing again. The garden wakes, soft and calm. A small wind moves through the trees. Everything feels alive and well here.

The hedgehog turns back to its roots. Then it is quiet under the leaves. Ako-chan stands up slowly on the grass. She turns and looks at Obia. Obia stands by the old shed. It looks back at her, calm and still. They do not say a word at all. But they both know it now. She thinks of all her friends far away. Each one is safely home now. Here in her own garden, all is calm. Obia is home with them now too. The long road is over. The ground is quiet. They are home.

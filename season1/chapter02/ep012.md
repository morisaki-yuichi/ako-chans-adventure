The train comes down from the mountains. The Alps are behind them now. They are in Italy. The land is flat and wet. White mist hangs over the fields. Water lies in long, flat squares. A slow river winds through them. The whole land is gray and still.

Then Ako-chan sees the birds. They stand in the shallow water. They are tall and thin and white. They have long necks and long legs. They are egrets. One bird lifts its head. It turns and looks at the train. Its eyes find Ako-chan. She hears it now.

"The water flows wrong now," says the egret. "The river feels strange. It comes from the south."

Obia sits up straight. Its pale eyes shine in the gray light. "The signal changes here," it says. Ako-chan looks at it. "What signal?" she asks. But Obia does not answer. It only looks out at the water. The mist moves slowly over the wide fields.

Dango wakes up beside her. He looks out at the white birds. He puts his nose to the cold glass. The wet land spreads out for miles. More egrets stand far away in the mist. They are still, like white stones in the water. The air outside looks cold and dim. No sun comes through.

Ako-chan looks at the near egret again. It steps slowly and quietly through the water. The mist does not lift. She thinks about the word "signal." She does not understand it. But the water is wrong here. The birds feel it. Dango leans warm against her side. The south pulls at the river, and at her.

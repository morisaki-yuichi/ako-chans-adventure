Italy went on and on.

She did not know it was so long. She did not know there were so many different greens. Dark green hills. Bright green fields. Yellow-green grass near the roads. Old stone walls between the fields. Long rows of vines on low hills.

Ako-chan watched and watched. She did not want to look away. She could watch it all day and all night.

The train slowed for a station. Bologna. The name was on a white sign. She said it quietly to herself. Bo-lo-gna. The sounds felt good in her mouth.

People got on. People got off. A child ran past on the platform. A dog on a lead. An old man with a bag of oranges. The station was loud for a moment. Then the train moved again. The station was gone.

Dango settled on the seat beside Ako-chan. He pushed against her and put his head on her leg. She kept her hand on his back while she looked out the window.

Two pigeons landed on a wire near the track. They sat for a moment. Then they flew north.

"The birds here," said Ako-chan. "Are they carrying messages too?"

"Yes," said Obia. "All birds carry things. They do not know they are doing it. They just fly. But the messages move with them."

Ako-chan looked at the place where the pigeons were. The wire moved in the wind.

She thought about the messages. Moving north through every bird. And she was moving south. Getting closer. Getting closer.

The train moved on. The green fields went on and on. The afternoon light turned slow and gold.

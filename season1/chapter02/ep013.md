It is night. The train runs on in the dark. Ako-chan is in a small sleeping room. Dango sleeps deeply beside her, curled up tight. Then something wakes her. She does not know what. The room is black and quiet. The train rocks slowly and softly. A thin line of light sits under the door.

Then she sees Obia. It stands by the window, all alone. It is very still. Its eyes shine pale blue in the dark. They are brighter than before. A low sound comes from it, soft like a hum. Obia faces the south. It does not move at all. Ako-chan watches from her bed. Something feels strange. She sits up slowly.

"Obia? What are you doing?" she asks softly.

But Obia does not turn. It does not answer her. The low hum stops. The pale eyes go dim again. The room is silent now. Only the wheels sound on the track. Dango's breath is slow and deep. He did not wake. Cold air comes through the window glass. Outside, dark fields rush past. There are no lights. There is just black.

Ako-chan stays very still. She does not make a sound. She just looks at Obia's quiet shape. The train moves on through the night. The cold creeps along the floor. She pulls her blanket up close. Her heart beats hard in her chest.

Obia sits back down. It is quiet again, like always. Maybe it was nothing. She thinks of the word Obia said by the water. Signal. But Ako-chan's chest feels tight. She lies back down on her bed. She watches Obia in the dark. She does not sleep again that night.

The train runs through France. Flat fields reach far away. They are green and very wide. Small farms pass by the window. A church stands far away. The sky is big and pale. Ako-chan watches the land go by. Dango sleeps now, warm at her side. Obia sits still across from them.

Then a large bird flies near. It is white with black wings. It has long red legs. It is a stork. It flies beside the train for a moment. It turns its head towards her. For a breath, their eyes meet. Ako-chan sits up. Now she can hear it.

"No wind comes from the south now," says the stork. "The warm wind is gone. Something holds it back."

Ako-chan looks out to the south. The fields are calm. The pale sky is still. Nothing looks wrong out there. But the stork feels it. So did the hedgehog. So did the fox and the owl. Far away in England, they all looked south. Now a bird in France feels the same thing.

Ako-chan keeps her eyes on the window. The fields stretch far and wide. A river shines for a moment, then goes. Cows stand near a small farm. The warm wind should move the grass. But the grass is too still. The air feels heavy and quiet. Dango sleeps on, soft and warm.

The stork turns away. It flies off over the fields. Its white wings grow small. Then it is gone. Ako-chan sits back in her seat. Dango's breath is slow and soft beside her. The land is quiet, but it is not right. Every country points the same way. She holds that thought close, and the train runs on.

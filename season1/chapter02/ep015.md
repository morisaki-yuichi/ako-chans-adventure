They leave the old town behind. The road climbs through dry brown hills. Olive trees stand in the warm wind. The grass is dry and gold. The high mountains are far behind them now. The road goes up a long, slow hill. Ako-chan walks at the front. Her heart beats a little fast.

Then they reach the top. Ako-chan stops and looks down. Below them lies the sea. It is the Adriatic Sea. It is wide and deep blue. The water shines under the sun. It stretches away to the south. Light dances on the small waves. She never saw water so blue and warm. She holds her breath for a moment.

Dango lifts his nose to the wind. He smells the air. He smells the sea. The smell is new and strong and full of salt. His nose works fast. His tail wags hard. He never smelled this warm sea before. He pulls toward it, happy and free. Ako-chan smiles down at him. She loves to see him like this.

The wind comes up from the water. It is warm and soft on her face. The air tastes of salt and sun. Far off, white gulls call. Small boats sit on the blue. The light is bright on Ako-chan's red glasses.

Somewhere across the water waits the coast. The Dalmatian coast. The south pulls harder here than before. Ako-chan does not know what waits there. But something does. She can feel it. Dango leans into the warm wind. Obia looks out at the open sea. The three of them stand on the hill. Together, they look toward the coast.

It is the next day. The night train stopped. They are off the train now. They walk through a small old town. The walls are made of warm stone. The sun is high and bright. After the cold mountains, the air is warm here. The street is quiet. The south feels very close.

Then Ako-chan stops. She looks at an old stone wall. Small shapes sit on it. They are geckos. Many small geckos, out in the sun. And there are bats too. Bats hang and crawl on the stone. But bats come out at night, not in the day. Something is wrong. All of them face the same way. They face south. They do not move. They are still, as if they hear something far off.

Dango sniffs slowly at the stone, low to the ground. He is not happy. The geckos do not run from him. They do not run at all. The bats keep their small eyes open. Ako-chan kneels down near the wall. She looks at one small gecko. Its tiny eyes point the same way, like all the rest. The sun is warm on her face. But a cold feeling runs down her back. The town is too quiet around them. No birds sing here.

Then she thinks of last night. Obia by the window. The low hum. The pale blue light. Obia faced the south too. Ako-chan looks at the geckos, then at the bats. She opens her mouth. But she does not speak. She is not sure. Not yet.

She looks at Obia. Obia looks ahead, calm and quiet. Dango leans close against her leg. Ako-chan says nothing. She holds the thought inside. Then they walk on, down the warm and silent street.

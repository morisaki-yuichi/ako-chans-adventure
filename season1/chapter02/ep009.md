The train goes fast now. Green fields run past the window. England is wide and grey-green. Sheep stand in a far field. Ako-chan watches the farms go by. Then the train slows down a little. Obia looks up from its seat.

"The Channel," it says. "We go under the sea now."

Ako-chan looks at Dango. Then the window goes black. The train is inside a long tunnel. They are deep under the sea. The light inside is the only light. Outside there is just dark. Dango jumps a little. His ears go straight up. He makes a small, low sound. He moves close to Ako-chan. She puts her hand on his back.

"It is okay," she says. "We are under the water now."

The tunnel goes on and on. The train hums all around them. The sound fills the long car. Ako-chan thinks of the sea above them. It is cold and deep and full of fish. But she cannot hear them here. They are too far away. The dark presses on the glass. Dango stays very still under her hand. Obia's eyes shine pale in the dark.

Then light comes back. The window is bright again. New fields run past the glass. The grass is a little different here. The small houses are different too. The roofs are red and low. The sun is out over the land.

"France," says Obia.

This is France. Their first new country. Dango looks out with big round eyes. He sniffs at the cold glass. The tunnel is behind them now. Ako-chan smiles at the bright fields. Her heart is light again. The train runs on, towards the south.

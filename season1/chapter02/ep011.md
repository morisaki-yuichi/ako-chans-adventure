The train to Venice left at 18:14. It was already dark outside when they left Paris.

France moved past the window. Cities. Small towns. Hills. The light left the sky. The land became dark shapes. She could see her own face in the glass. Red glasses. Tired eyes.

Dango was asleep at her feet. His legs moved a little. He was dreaming. She watched his paws and smiled.

Obia sat beside her. Its eyes were low. Dim blue. Not looking at her. Looking at something inside itself.

"Do you sleep?" Ako-chan asked.

"No," said Obia.

"Never?"

"No. I do not need to sleep. I will watch while you rest."

She looked at it for a moment. Small and silver. It did not sleep. It sat in the dark of the train. Watching and waiting. She did not know what she felt about that. Something warm. Something a little sad.

She put her head against the window. The glass was cool. The train rocked slowly. Outside, the dark went on and on. Stars came out. Bright and many.

She thought about the red notebook in her bag. She thought about writing. But her eyes were heavy.

She thought about the swallow. "The south wind is dead."

She thought about the owl. "Something started."

She thought about Obia. "Something is wrong in the south. It is very bad."

She heard all the voices. She wrote I have to go. And now she was going.

The train rocked. The stars moved past. Dango made a small sound in his sleep.

Ako-chan closed her eyes.

The dark went on south. And the train went with it.

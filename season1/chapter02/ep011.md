It is the next day. The train is new and fast. It runs to the south now. Ako-chan looks out the window. The land is not flat anymore. Green hills rise and fall. Then she sees something far ahead. It rises high. It is huge.

Mountains. They are the Alps. They stand high against the sky. Their tops are white with snow. Ako-chan never saw such big mountains. She puts her face to the glass. "Look, Dango," she says. "Look how high they are." The train climbs higher. The Alps come closer and closer. They fill the whole window now.

Then the window goes dark. The train is in a tunnel. Then light. Then dark again. Tunnel after tunnel. Rock and snow slide past the glass. Ako-chan's ears feel tight. She opens her mouth, and the feeling goes away. The air is colder here. Dango looks out too. White steam comes from his small nose. The tunnels come fast, one after another. Dango does not like them. He hides his nose when the light goes out. Ako-chan puts a warm hand on his back. "It is okay," she says softly.

The mountains are all around them now. They are quiet and old and huge. They hold up the grey sky. A river runs far below the train. It is white and fast. Ako-chan feels very small too. But she is not afraid.

She looks up at the high white peaks. The south is somewhere beyond them. Far past the snow and the rock. Dango settles against her leg and watches. Obia sits still and looks ahead. The train climbs on, into the cold.

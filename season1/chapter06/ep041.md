The next morning, the small dog comes down. It does not come to Ako-chan. It does not come to Dango. It walks to a low stone near Obia. Then it sits and watches Obia. Obia stands by the water and is still.

The small dog watches Obia for a long time. One hour goes by. Then two hours go by. Obia does not move much. The dog does not move at all. Its sharp eyes stay on Obia. It watches the silver body. It watches the pale blue eyes. It looks like it wants to know something. Ako-chan comes and sits down nearby. "What do you think?" she asks softly. The dog does not look at her. It keeps its eyes on Obia. A long time goes by. Then it speaks. "I do not know yet." Its voice is low and sharp.

Dango stands close to Ako-chan and watches. Dalu wants to ask many questions. But Gabu leans on him and keeps him still. The sun moves slowly across the quiet port. The water laps softly against the boats. The smell of salt and pine fills the air. A gull walks along the wet stones. It pecks once, then flies away. Still the small dog does not move. Obia stands in the light and waits.

"What is your name?" asks Ako-chan. The dog is quiet for a moment. "Suke-san," he says at last. Then he looks back at Obia again. He watches Obia still, low and tense. Ako-chan looks at Obia, then at Suke-san. This dog feels something about Obia. She feels it too, deep down. But like Suke-san, she does not know what it is.

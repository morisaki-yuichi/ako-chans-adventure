Morning.

The light came through the trees in long low lines. The air was cold and still. Mist sat between the trunks.

Ako-chan was the first to wake. She sat up and looked at the edge of the clearing.

It was there.

Not hiding now. Standing at the tree line, in plain sight. Young — round in the face, the way puppies are. But not soft. Reddish-brown fur, thick and clean. Ears straight up. Thick tail curled high over its back. The gaze was direct and did not move. It had decided to stand there. It had not changed its mind.

It looked at Ako-chan.

Dango woke. He saw it and went still. He did not bark. He did not move. He just watched.

The shape stood there for a long time. It looked at Ako-chan. Then at Dango. Then at Obia.

When it looked at Obia, something changed. Its ears went flat. Its body dropped very slightly. A low sound came from it — not quite a growl. A warning.

Then it was gone. One step back into the mist, and it was simply not there.

"That was a dog," said Dango. He sounded surprised.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

"A small one."

"Yes."

Gabu had been watching from the far edge of the camp. He said: "It will come back."

"How do you know?" said Daru.

"It did not leave in fear," said Gabu. "It left because it chose to."

Dango sat down. His tail was low. His ears were forward. He had a look on his face that Ako-chan did not know how to read.

"It is not afraid," she said.

"No," said Dango. "It is not."

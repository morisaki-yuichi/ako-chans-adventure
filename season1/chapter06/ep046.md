They made camp in a clearing in the forest.

The trees were tall here — straight trunks, high branches, the light coming down in thin strips. The ground was soft with old leaves.

All evening, Dango did not settle.

He paced around the edge of the clearing. He sniffed at the same spots again and again. He growled twice — low and short — then stopped. Each time he growled, his eyes went to the same place in the trees.

"There," he said. "Something is there."

Ako-chan looked. She could not see anything. The trees were dark and still.

But she could feel it. Something small. Something very still. Watching them. It had been there since they made camp. Maybe before.

This was not like Daru. Daru had run straight to them. This stayed back.

This was not like Gabu. Gabu had kept his distance but he had been in one place, still and patient. This thing moved. Slowly. Always around them, never getting closer.

Obia said: "It has been circling the camp. About forty meters out. All evening."

"What is it?" said Daru.

"Small. Four legs. Very light on its feet."

Dango sat down hard and stared into the trees. His ears were up. His tail was flat.

Ako-chan watched the dark spaces between the trunks. She could not see anything.

But then — just for a moment — two eyes. Small and sharp and very bright. Low to the ground.

Then gone.

Dango stood up again.

"It is still there," he said.

"Yes," said Ako-chan. "I saw it."

She did not look away from the trees.

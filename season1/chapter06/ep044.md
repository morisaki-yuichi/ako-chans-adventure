Another day starts at the port. The ship is almost a real ship now. Everyone works on the deck again. Obia fixes the last of the wood. Suke-san sits on his rock and watches. He watches like always.

Then something changes. Suke-san stands up on his rock. He looks down at the busy ship. He looks at Obia for a long time. Then, slowly, he walks down the rocks. He comes to the edge of the work. A rope lies on the stones near him. Suke-san takes the rope in his mouth. He carries it to the pile of wood. Then he drops it there and looks up. He waits to see what they do. No one says a word to him.

Ako-chan stops and holds her breath. Dango lifts his head and stares. Daru opens his mouth wide. He wants to shout. But Gabu leans on him and keeps him still. This is a quiet thing. They all feel it. They let it stay quiet. The sea wind moves his red-brown fur. He smells the cut wood and the salt. His ears stay up and sharp. He is still ready to run away. But he does not run. He stays right there with them. Suke-san picks up another rope. He goes back to work, close but apart.

Suke-san works with them now. But he is not all theirs yet. His eyes still go back to Obia. He helps. He stays close. He stands by Ako-chan's right side, a little behind. But he does not trust Obia. Not all the way. Not yet. Still, he is one more now. One more on the long road.

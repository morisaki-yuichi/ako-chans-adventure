Five days later, the flat land ended.

They stopped at the foot of the Pamirs. The road went up into grey rock and low cloud. The air here was already cool and thin.

Obia said: "Before we go up."

Gabu looked at Obia.

"The cold on that path will be too much," said Obia. "I made this last night."

It was a second pod. Clear walls. Warm orange light inside. The same quiet hum as Daru's.

Gabu looked at it for a long time. He said nothing. Then he walked in.

From inside, he pressed his face to the front wall and looked up at the grey rock above them.

"Welcome," said Daru.

"Quiet," said Gabu.

They went up. The path was narrow. The ground was loose and the turns were sharp. Dango moved close to Ako-chan. His thick coat held the warmth in, but the wind through the pass was cold. Ako-chan breathed out and a small white cloud came and went.

A bird called once from inside the rock face — one short clean sound, then gone.

She felt something in the mountains: high and still and very old. Not the push from the south. Something that had been here long before the disturbance began.

From inside his pod, Gabu said: "The path turns left. After the next rock."

Obia checked. "Correct."

"How did you know?" said Daru.

Gabu was quiet for a moment. "The shadow," he said. "The rock angle. Left is better."

They walked on. The wind moved through the pass. The two pods hummed. Dango stayed close to Ako-chan.

The mountains did not move. The cloud did.

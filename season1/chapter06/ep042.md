The work cannot wait now. Ako-chan looks out at the wide sea. Green hills stand behind the little port. Obia stands at the edge of the dock. Its pale blue eyes scan the far water. It is very still for a long time.

"The signal is very far south," says Obia. "We need a real ship." Ako-chan looks at the small fishing boats. They are too small for such big water. They walk along the old dock together. At the far end, something big and dark waits. It is an old ship. It sits low in the grey water. Its paint is all gone now. Its wood is grey and cracked. Rust runs down its metal sides. It is broken and quiet. But it is big. Big enough for the sea.

Dalu rolls fast to the old ship. "A ship! A ship! Look at it!" he says. He runs around it in his red Pod. Gabu comes slowly behind them all. He stops and looks up at the high deck. He says nothing at all. The air smells of salt and rusty metal. Far back on the rocks, Suke-san watches them. He does not come close. But he watches every move. Dango sniffs along the old grey hull. He does not like the smell of rust.

Ako-chan looks up at the broken ship. It does not look like a ship for the sea. It looks old and tired and sad. But Obia walks all around it slowly. Its eyes move over the wood and metal. "We can fix this," says Obia. Ako-chan feels something start. A big, hard thing. But a start.

It came back in the afternoon.

Not from the trees this time. From the path ahead. It walked into the clearing and stopped ten meters away. It looked at Ako-chan.

"Why are you here?" it said.

Its voice was short and direct. No extra words.

"We are going south," said Ako-chan. "Something is wrong there. We are going to find it."

The dog looked at her for a moment. Then at the others: Daru in the pod, Gabu very still at the edge of the camp, Dango standing just in front of Ako-chan.

Then it looked at Obia.

"Not that thing," it said.

Obia said: "I am Obia. I—"

"Not that thing," said the dog again. Flat. Clear.

Dango stepped forward. His chest went up a little. "Obia helped us," he said. "Obia travels with us."

The dog looked at Dango. For a moment the two dogs just looked at each other. Neither moved.

Then the dog turned back to Ako-chan. "You hear the animals," it said. It was not a question.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

"The south is wrong," the dog said. "I have felt it for many days."

"Yes. That is why we are going."

"Tell me more," it said. It sat down slowly. Its thick tail curled around its paws.

It did not look at Obia. It did not look at Dango.

It looked at Ako-chan.

Behind her, she heard Dango make a low sound in his throat.

"What is your name?" said Ako-chan.

"Suke-san," it said.

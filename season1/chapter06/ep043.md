The next days are full of work. The old ship sits by the dock. Everyone works to make it new. Obia knows what to do. It points and plans. It builds with care. Wood, rope, and metal lie all around. The port rings with busy sounds.

Obia works at the heart of it all. It fixes the broken wood with care. Ako-chan carries boards across the deck. Dalu runs here and there in his Pod. "What is this? What is this?" he asks. He gets under everyone's feet. Gabu works in his own quiet way. He pushes small things into a neat pile. Dango wants to help so much. He takes a rope in his mouth. He drops it, picks it up, and tries again. He gets in the way. But he tries so hard.

Far back on the rocks, Suke-san sits alone. He does not come down to the ship. He does not help with the work. But his sharp eyes never leave them. He watches Obia most of all. He sees Obia lift and fix and build. The smell of cut wood fills the warm air. The sound of work goes on all day. Sometimes Obia looks up at the high rocks. It does not call out to Suke-san. It just lets him watch from far away.

The days pass. The old ship changes slowly. The grey wood turns strong and new. Ako-chan looks up at it and smiles. Then she looks back at the rocks. Suke-san is still there, watching. He does not come down to them. But each day he sits a little nearer the edge. He cannot look away from Obia.

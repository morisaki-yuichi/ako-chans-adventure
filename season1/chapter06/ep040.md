The sun is high now over the port. The small red-brown dog is still there. It sits on the high rock and watches. It does not eat. It does not sleep. The others rest down by the boats. But Ako-chan cannot stop looking up.

Ako-chan stands up slowly. "Wait here," she says to Dango. She walks toward the rocks alone. Dango wants to come too. He stands up and whines softly. "Stay, Dango," she says. "Just this once." She walks up the path, step by step. The small dog watches her come. Its ears turn toward her. Its body goes tight. Ako-chan stops below the high rock. She does not climb up. She just looks up at the dog. "Hello," she says softly. "We are friends." The dog says nothing back to her.

The dog does not run away. But it does not come down. Its eyes stay sharp and careful. Far below, Dango watches with worried eyes. Obia waits by the boats and does not move. The wind moves the pine trees above her. A few brown needles fall on the rock. Down by the boats, Daru peeks from his Pod. Gabu watches with his calm, steady eyes. No one calls out. No one moves fast. The port is quiet all around them.

Ako-chan waits a long time below the rock. Then she walks back down to the others. The dog stays high up on its rock. The space between them is still the same. But something is different now. It did not run. It did not hide. It watched her come, and it stayed. That is something. That is a start.

The next day is grey. A low wind crosses the ice. Ako-chan stands outside the ship. Her friends are near her. But she watches Obia. Something about it is not the same today. It stands too still. It looks too far.

Obia stands apart from the others. It looks to the south again and again. It does not talk much now. When Ako-chan speaks, it answers slowly. Sometimes it does not answer at all. Its blue eyes seem far away. "Obia?" says Ako-chan. "Are you okay?" Obia turns to her. "I am fine," it says. But its voice is flat and low. Ako-chan does not feel fine. She feels it, cold and sure.

Suke-san feels it too. He stays close to Ako-chan. His eyes go to Obia, then to the south. Up on the ice, the Colonel watches. He does not move all day. Dango leans on Ako-chan's leg. Pen-san is quiet for once. Even Dalu does not run today. Gabu pulls deep into his shell. Suke-san's nose works hard at the cold air. The whole group feels the change. The air on the ice is heavy and still.

Night comes again to the white land. Obia stands alone by the ship. It looks south into the dark. Ako-chan watches it from the window. She remembers two strange nights. One on the train, back near the start. One on the ship at sea. Both times, Obia stood alone like this. Both times, it looked to the south. Now it does the same again. Ako-chan wants to go to it. But she does not know what to say. A cold worry fills her chest. And the long night is only starting.

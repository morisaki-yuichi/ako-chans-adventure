Before dawn, Obia woke everyone.

"Put these on," it said. "All of you. Before we go ashore."

On the deck there were things laid out in a row.

For Ako-chan: a suit. Very thin. Very light. Almost no weight at all. But when she put it on, the cold stopped. All of it. Like a wall.

"How does it do that?" she asked.

"It keeps the warmth in," said Obia. "It does not let the cold through."

For Dango and Suke-san: small boots for each paw, and a close-fitting vest. The boots went on one at a time. Dango looked at his feet very carefully. He took three steps, looked at his feet again, then walked in a small circle. He came back to Ako-chan and looked up. She laughed once, quietly. Suke-san put his on without a sound. He stood still, ears up, eyes front.

For Daru, Gabu, and Riro: their pods and capsule switched to a new setting. Obia pressed something on each one. The light inside changed — brighter, stronger, a deeper warmth than before.

Gabu: "Is it colder there?"

Obia: "Much colder. But you will be safe."

Daru: "How cold?"

Gabu: "Quiet."

The ice shelf was close now. Ako-chan could see it through the pale light — a flat white edge above the dark water. Long and solid and enormous. The air off it was a different kind of cold. Deeper. Older.

She looked at the group. Suits and boots and glowing pods and Riro's sealed capsule and Suke-san standing very straight with his new vest on.

"Ready?" she said.

Suke-san's ears went back and forward once. That was enough.

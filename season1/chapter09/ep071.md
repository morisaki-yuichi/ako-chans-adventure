The air changed on Day sixty-one.

Not like the cold before the storm. This was different. Clean and very still. Sharp in her nose, sharp in her throat. Ako-chan breathed in slowly and felt it all the way down.

The sea changed too. White shapes floated in the water. Small ones at first. Then larger. They moved in silence on the dark grey water. She did not know what they were.

"Icebergs," said Obia. "Small ones. There will be more ahead."

Riro was in his capsule. The door was shut and sealed. Daru and Gabu were in their pods — orange light, green light, steady and warm through the glass. Dango pressed close to Ako-chan's leg. His coat was thick, but she felt him shiver once. He pressed closer after that.

Suke-san stood at the front rail. He did not shiver. He looked at the white shapes, then at the sky, then ahead. His nose was up. He turned it slowly into the cold wind. His body was very still.

One of the shapes drifted near the ship. Taller than anything on the deck. Pale blue at the base, white above. It moved in silence and then it was behind them.

The whales went ahead, fast and steady. Their voices came up through the hull — low and deep. *Close now. Not far.*

Ako-chan put both hands on the cold rail. She held it and did not let go.

Something pulled at her. The same pull from England — from the garden, the hedgehog, the owl. But much stronger now. She felt it in her hands. In her chest. In the cold all around her.

They were getting close.

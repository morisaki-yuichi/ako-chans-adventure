Later that day, the sky is grey and still. Ako-chan stands on the ice. Pen-san is close at her feet. Then she sees a new shape. It comes from far off. It walks in a straight line. Step, step, step. It does not stop. It does not turn.

The new bird comes right up to them. It is another penguin. It is bigger than Pen-san, but it is still small. Its back is black. Its front is white. There is a white ring around each eye. Its beak is short and round. It stands very straight and stiff. Pen-san hops up to it. "You found me!" he says. He is happy to see it. "This is Ako-chan! She came!"

The penguin does not soften. It looks at everyone, one by one. "I am Colonel Adelie," he says. His voice is hard and sharp. He looks down at Pen-san. "I watch over the little one." Then his eyes stop on Obia, and they stay there. "That is Obia," says Ako-chan. The Colonel does not look away from it. "Tell me about Obia," he says. He stands straight and stiff. He is small, but he is not afraid. Suke-san steps up at Ako-chan's right. Two careful animals look at each other. Neither one looks away. Dango stands close too. No one speaks for a moment.

Ako-chan looks at Obia. Then at the Colonel. "Obia is my friend," she says. "Obia came all this way with us. Obia helps us." The Colonel does not answer that. He just watches Obia. His eyes are small and hard. Obia stands still and quiet. It does not move away. But the Colonel keeps watching it. Something new is on the ice now.

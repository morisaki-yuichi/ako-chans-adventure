They went ashore in the early light.

The ship stopped at the edge of the ice shelf. A wide flat surface, white and solid. Obia lowered a small ramp. Ako-chan went first. Her boot touched the ice and held. She took another step. Solid. Real.

She was standing in Antarctica. The ice was solid under her boots. Firm and cold and real.

The others came after her. Dango, then Suke-san. Obia, with the pods on a low carrier. Riro's capsule moved with it, detached now from the ship, rolling slowly on the ice.

Ako-chan turned back to look at the water.

The three whales were there. They had not left. They surfaced, all three at once. Their backs were dark and enormous against the grey water. Steam rose from their breaths into the cold air.

Ako-chan walked to the edge of the ice. She knelt down. She reached her hand out over the water toward the nearest whale. The whale held very still. Its eye looked at her — big and dark and calm.

She did not know how to say thank you. Not big enough. She stayed there, hand out, and the whale stayed too.

Then it went under. Slowly. The dark shape faded into the grey water. The second whale went. The third.

The water was empty.

Suke-san sat beside her. He did not speak. Dango pressed against her other side. The cold air came off the ice in long slow waves.

She stood up. She picked up the red notebook from her pocket and opened it.

*Day sixty-five. Antarctica. The whales went back. We go on.*

She closed it and turned to face the white.

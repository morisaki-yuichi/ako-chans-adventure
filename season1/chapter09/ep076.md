It was a penguin chick.

Very round. Very fluffy. Brown-grey all over, with a pale front. It came across the ice at full speed, rolling from side to side, wings out for balance, feet slapping the ice as fast as they could go.

It did not slow down. The ice made small sounds under its feet. Slap. Slap. Slap.

It ran straight to Ako-chan and pressed itself hard against her legs.

"You came!" it said. "You came, you came, you came! I knew you would come! I told them! I said, they will come, they will come from the north, and now you are here and you came!"

Ako-chan looked down. The chick looked up at her with very bright eyes. Its whole body was moving with excitement.

"Hello," said Ako-chan. "What is your name?"

"Pen-san!"

Dango came forward to look. Pen-san looked at Dango for one second, said "Hello, dog!" and turned back to Ako-chan.

Daru, from his pod, pressed his face to the glass. He was very interested.

Gabu said nothing.

Riro looked out from his capsule at the fluffy round shape against Ako-chan's legs. He checked his own fur twice.

Suke-san watched Pen-san from two steps back. He did not look at Ako-chan. He looked at Pen-san. A careful look. Not unfriendly. Just careful.

"How long have you been waiting?" asked Ako-chan.

"A long time," said Pen-san. "Many days. The voices told me. They said, wait here, and I waited." He looked up with enormous eyes. "Can you pick me up? Please?"

Ako-chan looked at Dango.

Dango looked at the round fluffy chick.

His ears went just slightly flat.

They made camp in a hollow in the ice.

The wind was low that evening. The sky turned a deep, cold blue.

Pen-san did not stop talking. By the time they stopped walking, everyone knew his name. They knew his favorite fish. They knew where he liked to sleep. They knew many other things. No one asked for any of it.

Then Pen-san looked up at Ako-chan.

"Now," he said. "Pick me up. Please. Please, please, please."

Ako-chan looked at him. He was very round. Very fluffy. Very sure of himself.

She reached down and lifted him.

He was heavier than he looked. He pressed himself to her chest with great force and settled there with a satisfied sound. His fluffy feathers were warm even in the cold air.

Dango watched from two meters away.

He did not make a sound. He just watched. His ears went flat, one then the other. His long fluffy tail dropped. It did not move.

He took one step toward Ako-chan, then stopped.

He sat down. He turned so that his back was to her. He looked out at the white ice to the south.

His ears were still pointing back toward her. She could see that.

Suke-san looked at Dango. Then at Pen-san. Then at Dango again. He said nothing. But he walked over and sat beside Dango, just a little to the right.

Neither of them spoke. They both looked south.

Pen-san looked at Dango's back and then up at Ako-chan. "Why is the dog sitting that way?"

"He is thinking," said Ako-chan.

"About what?"

Ako-chan held Pen-san a little tighter. "Things," she said.

The morning is bright and cold. Ako-chan walks on the ice again. Dango and Suke-san are close at her side. The far black birds are out there, like before. Then one of the shapes moves. It leaves the others. It comes toward them. It comes fast.

It is small and round and grey. It runs across the white ice. Its little feet slip and slide. It falls down flat. Then it gets up again. It runs and falls, runs and falls. But it does not stop. "Ako-chan!" it calls. "Ako-chan!" It knows her name. At last it stops right in front of her. It is a baby penguin, grey and soft. "Ako-chan! You came!" it says. "I knew it! You came!"

The baby penguin breathes fast and hard. Its grey down is thick and warm. The top of its head is black. Its cheeks are white. Its big black eyes shine up at her. Dango steps in front of Ako-chan. He looks at the small grey bird. Suke-san sniffs the cold air. He watches the little bird with care. Gabu looks out slowly from his blue Pod. Daru pops his head up from his Pod. "A bird! A new bird!" he says.

Ako-chan kneels down on the ice. "Hello," she says. "You know me?" The penguin nods its head fast. It hops from one foot to the other. It cannot stay still for one moment. Ako-chan laughs softly. "You are a little penguin," she says. "I have a name for you. Pen-san." The baby penguin cheeps. "Pen-san! Pen-san!" he says. He likes it. He came all this way to find her. And he came running.

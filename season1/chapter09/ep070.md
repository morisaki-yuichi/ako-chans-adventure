The night comes down on the ice. The sky turns deep and dark. The stars are cold and far. Inside the ship, the friends sleep warm. Dango lies close to Ako-chan. Pen-san went back to the birds for the night. But outside, on the dark ice, something moves.

Outside in the cold, Obia is awake. It does not sleep. It never sleeps. It walks here and there on the ice. Then it stops and looks south. It stands very still for a long time. Then it walks again. It cannot rest. It cannot be still. Up on a low rise of ice, the Colonel sits. His small eyes follow every move. He does not blink. He watches the silver shape in the dark.

Suke-san comes out of the ship too. He cannot sleep tonight. He stands near the Colonel on the ice. The two of them watch Obia together. "It never stops," says the Colonel. "I am watching it." Suke-san says nothing. But his ears point at Obia. His nose reads the cold air. He does not trust the silver thing. He never did. Two watchers in the dark, side by side. The cold wind moves between them. Neither one looks away.

At the window, Ako-chan wakes. She sees them all out there. Obia on the ice. The Colonel on his rise. Suke-san close by. All of them awake in the cold and dark. Something is not right with Obia. She feels it now. She felt this before, on the long road. But it is stronger now. She watches them for a while. Then she lies back down. But she does not sleep well.

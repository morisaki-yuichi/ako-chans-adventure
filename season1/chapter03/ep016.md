The ferry comes to the coast. Ako-chan steps off onto the rocks. The rocks are white and bright. The deep blue sea lies behind them. The sun is high and hot. This is the Dalmatian coast. Dango jumps down beside her. Obia walks slowly behind them. The air is warm and full of salt. White cliffs rise high above the water.

They go along the white rocks. The stone is warm under their feet. Then Ako-chan sees a small lizard. It sits on a flat rock. It does not run away. It looks straight at her. "A strange voice comes," says the lizard. "It comes from the south. Far away." "Can you hear it? Something is wrong." Ako-chan kneels down by the rock. "I hear it too," she says softly. The lizard's small sides move fast. "It is close now. Be careful." Then it slips into a crack.

The waves break softly on the rocks. The pale stone shines in the light. Dango sniffs the warm cracks. He smells the small lizard's trail. But he cannot hear its voice. Only Ako-chan can. He looks up at her face. The heat shimmers over the bright stone. A lone gull calls out over the sea. Sunlight flashes on the red of her glasses. Obia stands still and looks far away.

Ako-chan stands up slowly. The south pulls at her again. But something else is here too. Something close. Something near these rocks. She does not see it yet. But she feels it. The white rocks are very quiet. She listens hard. The waves come and go. Her heart beats a little fast.

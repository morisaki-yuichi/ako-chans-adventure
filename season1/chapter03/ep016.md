It was very late.

Ako-chan woke in the dark. The train moved slowly. Outside: nothing. No lights. No shapes. Just black.

She was about to close her eyes again. Then she saw it.

Obia was sitting very still at the end of the seat. More still than usual. Like something had stopped inside it. Its eyes were not blue-white. They were almost dark. Very little light.

Then — just for a moment — a small point of light came from Obia's chest. Not blue. Not white. Something else. She did not know the color. It lasted one second. Maybe two. Then it was gone.

Obia did not move. Its eyes came back to blue-white. Slow and soft.

"Obia?" she said. Very quiet.

Obia turned. "Yes?"

"What was that?"

A moment. Then: "I was checking something," said Obia.

"What were you checking?"

"Data," said Obia. "The data I have collected. From the animals. From the messages. I check it sometimes. To make sure it is correct."

She looked at it. Its face was the same as always. Steady. Quiet.

"The light," she said. "Where did it go?"

"It is part of how I work," said Obia. "Do not worry."

She waited. Obia said nothing more. It turned back to the dark window.

She lay down again. Dango moved in his sleep. He put his paw on her arm. She held it. His paw was warm.

She thought about the light. That one small point. Where did it go?

She did not know. But she did not forget it.

Outside, the dark moved past. The train went on.

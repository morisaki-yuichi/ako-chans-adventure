The train went into the mountains.

She did not see them at first. It was too dark. But she felt them. The air grew heavy. The train slowed going up the long slopes. Through the black windows she could see shapes — big, still, and dark.

Dango was asleep. His head was on her leg. She kept her hand on his side. Warm and slow.

The train stopped once. A small station. The name on the sign was hard to read in the dark. Men in uniforms came through the car. They looked at papers. They did not speak much. Then the train moved again.

"Was that the border?" she asked Obia.

"Yes," said Obia. "We are in a different country now."

She looked out at the dark. The same mountains. The same dark. But a different country.

She thought about home. The garden. The oak tree. The robin. It was very far away now. Dango shifted. He pressed closer to her.

Then she heard something.

Very faint. Just for a moment. Through the glass and the dark and the movement of the train — a voice. Or many voices. Deep and far away. Like they came from inside the mountains. She had never felt voices like this before.

She put her face close to the window.

"Can you hear that?" she said.

"I cannot hear what you hear," said Obia. "But something is here. The animals know we are passing."

She listened. The voices were very quiet. But they were there. Slow and careful. Not like the birds in Italy. Something older. Something patient.

She did not sleep for a long time after that.

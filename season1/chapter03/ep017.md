The white rocks are hot and still. Ako-chan does not move. The feeling is stronger now. Something is here, very close. Dango stands beside her leg. He leans close and warm. Obia waits a few steps back. The sea is calm and blue. The salt air hangs heavy and warm. No one makes a sound.

Then she hears it. A small sound. Tap. Tap. It is hard, like stone on stone. It comes from behind a low rock. Ako-chan turns her head slowly. The sound stops. Then it comes again. Tap. Tap. A round, hard sound. Something hides behind the rock. Something with a hard back. Ako-chan holds very still. She does not want to scare it. "It is okay," she says softly. "We are friends. Come out." But nothing comes. It stops once more.

The waves wash softly below them. The hot sun beats on the stone. Dango lifts his ears high. His nose moves fast in the air. He smells something new and small. But he does not step forward. He stays still, just like Ako-chan. The smell of warm stone fills the air. Obia's eyes shine pale and quiet. They all wait together by the rock. Nothing moves but the bright blue sea.

The rock is silent now. The thing in the crack does not move. Ako-chan kneels down on the warm stone. She looks at the dark crack. She cannot see inside. But she knows. Something small is in there. And it is watching her too. She breathes very slowly. Her heartbeat is soft and fast. She waits for it. She is sure now. The white rocks hold their quiet.

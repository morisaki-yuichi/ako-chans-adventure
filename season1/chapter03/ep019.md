The land kept changing.

Small white towns on hillsides. A church tower. Old stone walls on steep slopes. Goats on rough grass. A narrow bridge over a green river. Everything was different. Everything was new.

Ako-chan watched and watched. She did not want to look away.

The voices were louder now.

Not louder in sound. Louder in feeling. At home, the hedgehog's words came from deep inside her. Low and quiet. Here, the animals felt close. Right outside the window.

Birds flew near the train for a moment. Then they turned away. South.

"They are all going south," said Ako-chan.

"Yes," said Obia. "All morning."

Dango watched the birds go. He made a low sound in his chest. Not a bark. Something quieter. More careful.

On a stone wall beside the track, two sparrows sat very still. She felt them without trying.

"What do they feel?" asked Dango.

She listened. "Something is wrong in the south," she said. "They feel it. They do not know what it is. But they feel it. And it is not stopping."

Dango made the same quiet sound again.

The train moved down a long valley. Green fields on both sides. The mountains were behind them now. The air was warmer.

She pressed her hand to the glass.

At home, the fear was far. Low and slow. Here it was close. All around. She could feel it without trying now.

She wrote in the red notebook: The voices are louder. We are getting close.

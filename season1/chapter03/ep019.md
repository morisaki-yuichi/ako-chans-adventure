The morning sun is warm and bright. The four of them sit on the white rocks. Daru is awake already. He runs here and there. He looks at everything. Dango watches the little tortoise. He is not sure about him yet. His tail hangs low and still. Daru is new, and Dango knows it.

Then Daru runs right up to Dango. He stops close to the big dog's nose. The two look at each other. Nose to nose. Dango steps back. Daru steps back too. They both wait. Then Daru comes again, slowly. Dango lowers his head. He sniffs the small shell. Daru does not move now. He lets the dog smell him. "You are big," says Daru. "And soft! So soft!" Dango's ears come up a little. It is their first hello. Not friends yet. But it is okay.

Ako-chan kneels down by Daru. "You are so brave," she says. She pets his little head. Daru is very happy. He pushes his head up for more. He spins in a circle for her. Then Dango pushes in between them. He puts his body close to Ako-chan. He leans hard on her leg. He does not look at Daru now. His tail stays low. Ako-chan feels him there. She knows that feeling well.

Ako-chan puts her hand on Dango's head. "You are my Dango," she says softly. "Always." Dango leans into her hand. His tail moves, just a little. But he keeps one eye on Daru. The small tortoise runs off again. Dango watches him go. Something is new between them now. Dango stays close at her feet. That is enough for him now.

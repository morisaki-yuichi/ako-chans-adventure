Light came first.

Not all at once. The dark got less dark. Shapes came back — hills, trees, a road below. Then color. Grey, then brown, then green.

The sky turned from black to deep blue. Red came up from the east. Long and soft and bright.

Ako-chan pressed her face to the glass.

The land was different here. Different from England. Different from France. Different from Italy. The hills were rougher. The stone was lighter. The trees were a different shape. Everything felt older. She was far from home now. She knew it.

Dango woke. He stretched. His front paws went out, his back end went up. He yawned very wide. She could see all his teeth. She laughed. Just a small sound.

"Good morning," she said.

He wagged his tail. He climbed up and pushed his nose to the window. He breathed on the glass and made a small cloud.

Obia was at the end of the seat. It had been still all night. But its eyes were bright.

"Croatia," said Obia. "We are in Croatia."

She said it quietly. Croatia.

Below the train, a river ran between the hills. Silver in the morning light. A bird flew low over the water. A heron. Long and slow. It landed on the bank and stood very still.

She felt it through the glass. The heron was not at rest. It was watching. Waiting. Its stillness was like a question.

She opened the red notebook. She wrote: Croatia. Morning. Red sky.

She held the pen. There was more she wanted to write. But she did not have the words yet.

The wind comes over the dry brown hills. It smells of warm grass and salt. The sky is wide and blue above them. Ako-chan sits on the white rocks. Daru sits near her small feet. Then a red squirrel comes down the rocks. It stops and looks at Ako-chan. Its body is very still.

"You hear it too," says the squirrel. "Something is wrong. It comes from far away." Ako-chan nods slowly. "I hear it," she says. "I have to go to the south. I have to find it." Daru listens to her. He looks at the sea. He looks at the long way ahead. He is only a baby. He does not know the world. He is far from his home now. But his small eyes are bright. He looks up at Ako-chan. "I do not understand," he says. "But I will go with you."

The wind moves the dry grass. It is soft and warm on their faces. The red squirrel runs back up the rocks. Then Dango comes close. He sits down right beside Daru. He does not push now. He does not lean away. The big dog and the little tortoise sit together. They look out at the wide sea. The water is calm and bright below. Ako-chan watches them both. Something good is happening here.

Now they are four. Ako-chan, Dango, Obia, and Daru. Daru is small. The road is very long. He cannot see where it goes. But he is not afraid. He is with them now. Ako-chan smiles down at her new friend. Dango leans on her side. He is glad too. The south waits. They face it together.

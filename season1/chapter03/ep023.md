The morning is bright on the coast. The air smells of salt and stone. Today they leave these rocks. But first Obia stands very still. Its eyes glow pale and blue. It looks far out over the sea. It does not move at all. Ako-chan waits and watches it. Dango sits quietly by her feet.

Obia scans the sea for a long time. The pale light shines from its eyes. Daru cannot wait. He runs up to Obia. "What is it? What do you see?" Obia does not turn to him. "Wait, Daru," it says. "Be still." Daru runs off, then comes back again. He peeks at Obia once more. Obia stops him with a quiet word. At last the pale light goes soft. Obia turns to Ako-chan. "The signal is from somewhere deep," it says. "Far to the south."

It sits in the warm air, that word. Daru does not understand it. He runs off to look at the sea. He calls to a passing gull. The bird does not answer him. Dango lifts his head, then sleeps again. The blue waves move slowly below. The white rocks shine in the sun. The little tortoise is happy and curious. But Ako-chan stands very still now. One word stays in her mind.

Signal. She heard it before. She remembers the train at night. The pale blue light. The low, soft hum. The same word, in the dark. Obia faced the south that night. Its eyes were bright and strange. Her chest feels tight again. She wants to ask Obia why. But she does not say a thing. The sea gives her no answer.

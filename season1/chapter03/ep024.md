The ferry pulls away from the coast. The white rocks grow small behind them. The blue sea opens wide and calm. Ako-chan stands at the back rail. Dango stands close beside her. Dalu sits up on the warm deck. His Pod rests near him in the sun. The engine hums softly below them.

Dalu looks back at the shore. The white rocks are his home. He watches them for a long time. He does not run now. He does not talk. He is very still and quiet. This is new for Dalu. The coast grows small and far. Soon it is just a thin pale line. Dalu watches it go. "That is my home," he says softly. "It is far now." Ako-chan kneels down by him. She does not say much. She just stays close to him. He is brave for such a small one.

The wind comes warm off the water. It cools her face with salt. It carries the dry grass smell away. Gulls call high above the ferry. The sea is wide on every side. Ako-chan puts her hand near Dalu. Dango leans his head on her arm. The three of them watch the land. The deck rocks slowly beneath their feet. No one says a word.

Then the pale line is gone. There is only sea and sky now. Dalu turns and looks ahead. The south is out there, far away. A new land waits for them all. Dalu is small. But he is not alone. He has his friends now. Dalu sits up a little taller. His small eyes look out far. And the road goes on.

They changed trains in the early evening.

The station was big and busy. High roof. Long platforms. People going in every direction. The light inside was yellow and warm.

"The night train leaves at half past eight," said Obia. "Platform six."

She looked at the clock above the platform. They had fifty minutes.

She found a small shop nearby. A bread roll. Some water. An orange. She sat on a bench and ate. Dango sat at her feet. He did not bark. He just watched. So many people. So many smells. His nose moved the whole time.

Around them, voices in many languages. She could not understand most of them. But the sounds felt warm and full of life.

At last the night train came in. Very long. Dark green and silver. It moved slowly down the platform and stopped with a sound like a long breath.

"This train goes east," said Obia. "Through the mountains. Into Croatia."

Croatia. The next country. She said the word quietly to herself.

They found their seats. The car was quiet. An old man near the window. Two women with bags on their laps. A child asleep against a coat. No one looked at them twice.

The train gave a low sound. The platform began to slide past. Slowly at first. Then faster. The lights of the station went away one by one.

She watched them go. Streets. Lights. A river bridge. Then dark.

She looked at Obia. Its eyes were blue-white. Still and steady.

Somewhere ahead: the mountains. She could not see them yet. But she felt them. Big and quiet. Waiting.

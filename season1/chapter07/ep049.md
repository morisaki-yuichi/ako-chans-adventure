The tide pulls away fast now. The whale lies still in the low water. There is little time left. Ako-chan does not wait. She climbs down to the wet sand. "We help it now," she says. "All together." Her friends move close around the dark wet body.

Ako-chan gives them each a job. "Keep its skin wet," she tells Dango. She fills a bucket with seawater. She pours it slowly over the black back. Obia stands by the whale's side. "It is heavy," Obia says. "I am strong." It pushes hard against the whale. The big body shifts a little in the sand. Dango and Suke-san dig at the sand with their paws. They bark loudly and fast. Dalu and Gabu roll small stones away.

The sun climbs higher in the sky. The work is hard and hot. Ako-chan's arms hurt. Her glasses slip down her wet nose. But no one stops. They work on without a rest. Salt water splashes up at their faces. The whale's skin is dark and smooth. Ako-chan wets the wide back again and again. The whale waits in the sand. Its great eye watches them all. "Hold on," Ako-chan says to it. "The water comes back soon."

Then the sea begins to turn. The tide creeps back over the sand. Cool water rises around the whale. Ako-chan feels it at her feet. "Now!" she calls. "Push now! All together!" Obia pushes with all its power. The dogs bark loudly. The whale moves its great tail. Slowly, the huge body slides free. It slips out into the deeper water. Everyone stops and stands very still. They watch the whale float in the sea. It is free. It is safe. And no one says a word.

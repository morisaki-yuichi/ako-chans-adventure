The ship was waiting at the harbor.

It was not big. It was not like any ship Ako-chan had seen before. The sides were dark grey. The deck was wide and flat. There were no sharp edges. Everything was smooth.

"You made this," said Ako-chan.

"Yes," said Obia. "In Japan. While you were in the forest."

Dango walked around the ship slowly. His nose went left and right. He smelled metal and something else — something Ako-chan could not name. New. Like nothing before it.

Suke-san did not walk around. He stood at the edge of the dock and looked at the ship. Then he looked at Obia. He did not speak.

Daru said, from the pod: "Can we get on?"

They went on board. The deck was solid under Ako-chan's feet. The sea was grey-green below them. The harbor smelled of salt and fish and old wood.

Obia moved to the front of the ship. "The south is that way," it said. It pointed. Far ahead, the sea was open and very wide.

Ako-chan looked south. She felt something pull at her from that direction — the same pull she had felt since England. Still there. Still not stopping.

Gabu stepped to the rail beside her. He looked south too. He said nothing.

Dango sat down at her feet.

Suke-san came up on her other side. A little back. A little to the side. The same as always.

Obia: "Ready?"

Ako-chan put her hand on the rail. The metal was cold.

"Ready," she said.

The ship moved out of the harbor, into the open sea.

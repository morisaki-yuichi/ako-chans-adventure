The whales came close in the morning.

All three of them, just below the surface. So close that Ako-chan could see the rough white patches on its dark grey skin. Like small rocks, fixed to its body.

She stood at the front rail and listened.

The voices were clear now. Not words the way people use words. But clear all the same. She understood.

"They are ready," she said. "They say: now. Now we turn south."

"South," said Obia. It did not ask.

"South. With them. They know the current. They will go ahead of us."

Obia turned the ship.

Riro came and stood beside Ako-chan at the rail. He looked at the whales. His fur was full and dry now, standing away from his body in a thick round layer. He looked much bigger than when they pulled him out of the water.

"Big," said Riro.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

"Are they always like that?"

"I do not know. I think so."

Riro touched his own fur once — a quick check. Then he looked back at the whales.

Dango came up on Ako-chan's left. He sat down. He glanced at Riro. Then he looked at the sea.

Suke-san came up on her right — a little back, a little to the side.

All of them at the rail. Watching the whales turn south ahead of them.

Daru: "Seven now. Seven!"

Nobody counted out loud after that. But Ako-chan felt it — seven of them on the ship, on the sea, heading south. The whales ahead. The south below.

She took out the red notebook.

*Day fifty-one. North Pacific. Riro is with us. We go south now.*

She looked up. The whales moved ahead. The ship followed.

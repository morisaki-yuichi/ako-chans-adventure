The ship sails on the open sea now. The land of Japan is far behind. Water spreads wide on every side. It is blue and very deep. The sun is high. The sails are full. The warm wind pushes them to the south. Ako-chan stands at the front of the ship.

She looks up at the wide sky. Then she stops. Something is missing here. She listens hard. She waits for a sound. But nothing comes to her. There are no birds. No gulls cry above the waves. No small voices call from the air. The sky is big and blue and empty. At sea, birds often follow a ship. But this one holds nothing at all.

Ako-chan does not know what it means. Maybe the sea is just too wide. Maybe the land is too far for birds. Or maybe something is wrong out here. She cannot tell. The thought sits quiet in her chest.

Only the wind makes a sound up here. It hums in the white sails above. The ropes creak softly in the breeze. Suke-san stands close at her right side. He looks up high too. His ears turn. His nose lifts high. But he says nothing at all. Dango leans on her leg and stays still. Daru and Gabu rest quiet in their Pods.

The ship moves on across the water. The wide sky stays empty and pale. Ako-chan keeps her eyes turned up. She still waits for one small bird. None comes. She listens for one small voice. None comes. The sea is calm. The wind is warm. But the silent air feels strange to her. She holds the quiet close and says nothing.

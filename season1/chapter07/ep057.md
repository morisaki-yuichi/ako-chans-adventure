Riro could not reach his back.

He tried. He twisted left. He twisted right. His paws could not get to the spot between his shoulders. He sat down again and looked at Ako-chan.

"I cannot reach," he said.

Ako-chan sat beside him. "Can I help?"

Riro looked at her hands. "You have no fur. But yes. Please."

She began to work through his fur with her fingers — slow, careful strokes from neck to tail. The fur was thick and very soft, layer on layer. Riro's eyes half-closed. He made a small sound.

That was when Dango moved.

He had been lying six feet away. He stood up. He walked over. He sat down directly between Ako-chan and Riro — not touching either of them, but very close. Too close.

Ako-chan looked at him. "Dango."

Dango looked at the sea.

Riro opened one eye. He looked at Dango. He looked at Ako-chan. He said nothing.

Ako-chan moved around Dango and kept working on Riro's back.

Dango stood up again. He walked in a small circle. He sat back down — this time on Ako-chan's other side, right against her leg. His ears were flat. His long fluffy tail was very still.

Gabu watched from the pod. Daru leaned against the wall inside.

Daru said: "What is Dango doing?"

"Feelings," said Gabu.

Ako-chan finished Riro's back. Riro checked the spot himself, carefully. "Better," he said. "Much better."

He looked at Dango.

Dango looked away.

Ako-chan put her arm around Dango. He was very stiff. Then he leaned into her. Just a little.

"I know," she said softly. "You are still my Dango."

He made a small unhappy sound.

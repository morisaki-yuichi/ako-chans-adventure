The whale floats in the deeper water now. It is safe. The sea is calm and bright. Ako-chan stands on the wet sand. Her friends stand close beside her. They are all tired. No one speaks. They just watch the great dark shape. It rests on the soft waves. Its breath is slow and long.

Then the whale turns in the water. Its huge eye finds Ako-chan again. A voice comes to her, low and warm. "Thank you, child," says the whale. "You came to me. You did not turn away." Ako-chan holds her hands to her chest. "I heard you," she says softly. "We could not leave you here."

The whale lifts its great tail high. Water falls from it like rain. "We will not forget," the whale says. The words are deep and full and kind. Ako-chan does not know what they mean. But she feels them in her heart. The whale looks at her one last time. Then it turns toward the open sea.

The friends stand very still on the sand. No one wants to say goodbye. They watch the whale in the bright water. Ako-chan lifts one hand to wave. Then the whale begins to move away.

Slowly, the huge body sinks down. The dark back slides under the waves. A wide ring spreads across the water. Then the whale is gone. The deep takes it home. Dango barks once at the empty sea. Suke-san watches the waves, still and quiet. Daru and Gabu peek out from their Pods. The sea is calm again. Ako-chan keeps her eyes on the water. Something stays with her now. A warm and quiet thing. She does not have a word for it.

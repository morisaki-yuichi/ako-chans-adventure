Two days out, there was nothing around them but water.

No land. No ships. No birds. The sea went on and on in every direction. It was dark blue and very still.

Ako-chan sat on the front deck with the red notebook open on her knee. She did not write. She just looked at the water.

Dango stayed near her. He had been near her all day. The big open sea made him quiet. He did not bark. He did not run around the deck. He just lay close to her leg and watched the water.

Daru asked many questions at first: "How deep is it here? What lives down there? Can we stop and look?" Then the sea answered none of them and he grew quiet too.

Gabu was in his pod. He said: "The water under us. Something is moving in it. Very far down. I can feel it through the hull."

Ako-chan felt it too — a low, slow movement, like the ground breathing. Not the disturbance. Something older. Something that lived here.

Suke-san walked the deck. Slow loops, front to back and back to front. He stopped near Obia once. He looked at it. Obia looked back. Neither spoke. Suke-san walked on.

At the back of the ship, Obia watched south. Its eyes were steady and quiet. It did not sleep. It did not move much. Just watched.

The sun went down and turned the water orange. Then the stars came out — many more than Ako-chan could count.

She wrote in the red notebook: *Day forty-three. The Pacific. Very large.*

She closed it. The water moved below. Somewhere deep, something moved with it.

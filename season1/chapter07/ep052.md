The whales stayed all night.

In the morning they were still there — moving beside the ship, steady and slow. Ako-chan sat at the rail and listened.

Their voices came in waves. Long and low, like the sea itself was speaking. She could not catch words. Not yet. But she could feel the shape of it: something calling from the north. Something the whales were answering.

"What do they say?" said Daru.

"North," said Ako-chan. She did not look away from the water. "They are saying north."

"But south is the problem."

"I know." She was quiet for a moment. "They say: go north first. Follow us. There is a way."

Dango moved close to her side. He looked up at her face.

Obia came to the front of the ship. "The whales know a current. A very large ocean current. It moves north, then south, all the way down. If we follow it, it will take us to the south faster than going straight."

Gabu said: "They know the sea. We do not."

Suke-san had been listening from the back. He walked forward slowly. He looked at the whales. He looked at Ako-chan.

"You are sure?" he said.

"Yes."

He was quiet. Then: "Then go north."

Ako-chan looked at Obia. Obia turned the ship.

The front of the ship swung around. North. The whales adjusted with them — three enormous shapes moving easily, unhurried.

Daru pressed his face to the pod wall and watched them. "They are very big," he said.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

"Do they know we are here?"

She smiled. "Yes. They know."

The ship followed the whales north, into open water.

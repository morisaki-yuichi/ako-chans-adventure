The giant tortoise looks down at them. Its head moves slowly on its long neck. Dalu and Gabu stand very still below. Then Dalu takes one small step closer. Gabu comes beside him, slow and careful. The two baby tortoises stare up at it. The huge dark shell rises high above them.

"You have a shell too," the giant says. Its voice is deep and very slow. "The same shell. Like mine." Dalu looks at his own small back. Then he looks at the giant again. The same shape. The same hard shell. But one is small and new. The other is huge and grey and old.

Ako-chan watches them from the hot rock. Three tortoises, together on the stone. They are the same. But they are not the same. Time sits heavy on the great old shell. Cracks run deep across its back. Dalu's shell is smooth and young. Gabu looks from the giant to Dalu. He seems to understand something quiet.

Dango watches from a little way back. He does not bark at the great dark shape. Suke-san sits very still and keeps his eyes wide. The marine iguanas rest on the warm rocks. The hot sun beats down on them all. The giant breathes slowly, deep and long. It moves like a walking hill of stone.

"You are so big," Dalu says softly. His loud voice is small now. "So big. So very big." The giant does not speak again. It only looks at them with calm grey eyes. So many years rest behind them. Ako-chan feels something she cannot name. The same shell, far apart in time. Dalu and Gabu lean close together. And the giant watches over them both.

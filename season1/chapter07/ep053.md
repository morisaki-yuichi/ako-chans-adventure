The next day is hot and still. The giant tortoise sits on the warm rock. Daru and Gabu sit close by its feet. Ako-chan kneels down on the stone near them. The giant tortoise is quiet for a while. Then it begins to speak, slow and low. "Let me tell you an old thing," it says.

"This is not my own memory," the giant says. "The old ones gave it to me. They had it from the old ones before. It comes from far, far back in time." Daru leans in close. Gabu does not move at all. Ako-chan holds very still and listens.

"Long ago——a creature," the giant says. "On a far island. Much bigger than us." Its eyes look far away. "And the south was wrong then too." The words hang in the hot air. The giant says no more about it. It does not know what the story means. The giant looks far out to sea.

Daru opens his mouth to ask something. But no sound comes out. Gabu just looks down at the warm stone. The hot air hangs heavy all around them. Even the iguanas do not move now. Dango lies quiet at Ako-chan's feet. Suke-san keeps his sharp eyes on the giant.

Ako-chan turns the words over in her mind. A creature, big, on a far island. The south was wrong then too. She does not understand it at all. It is old and strange and far away. But the words stay with her, like a stone. She keeps them safe, and lets them rest. Daru and Gabu look up at the giant. The hot wind moves across the rock. And no one speaks for a while.

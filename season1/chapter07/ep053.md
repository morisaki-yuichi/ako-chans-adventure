Ako-chan heard it on the fifth day.

It was not a sound. It was more than that. It came from below the water and moved up through the hull and into her feet and then into her whole body. Low. Slow. Very big.

She stood very still.

Dango raised his head. He could not hear it — she could tell. But he felt something in her go quiet, and he went quiet too.

The voice came again. Long. Round. Like a sound but not a sound. Like a word said by something very, very large.

Ako-chan moved to the front rail. She looked at the water.

"Obia," she said.

Obia came up beside her.

"Something is here," said Ako-chan.

"I know. I have been watching it on the sensors. Since yesterday morning. But I could not name it."

"It is a whale. More than one."

Gabu spoke from inside the pod. "I feel it. In the hull. Very close now."

Suke-san stopped walking. He stood at the side of the ship and looked at the water. His ears were straight up.

Then — slowly, from the dark water below — something rose.

It was enormous. Grey-black and smooth. It came up just below the surface and moved beside the ship without touching it. Then another. And another.

Three humpback whales. Moving beside them. Slow and steady.

Ako-chan could feel their voices even without listening hard. They were not words yet. But they were there. Coming from the south — and yet moving north.

She gripped the rail. The whales moved beside the ship.

She listened.

Two days further north, the sea was very cold.

Mist sat low on the water. The air was sharp and clean. Ako-chan could see her breath. She kept her jacket closed and her hands in her pockets.

Gabu was back in the pod — the temperature capsule glowing warm orange. Daru was too. Both tortoises looked out through the clear walls at the grey sea.

The whales were still ahead. Closer today.

Obia stopped the ship.

"What is it?" said Ako-chan.

"Something in the water," said Obia. "Not a whale. Smaller. But not moving the way a fish moves."

Suke-san came to the rail at once. His nose went forward. He sniffed three times — fast, deep.

Dango came to the other side. He leaned over the rail.

"There," said Dango.

Ako-chan looked.

About thirty meters off the side of the ship — something small. Dark brown. Floating on its back.

She felt it before she could name it: tired. Cold. Very cold. And frightened. But also — trying. Still trying to stay afloat.

"It is an otter," she said. "A small one."

"It is not moving well," said Obia.

Ako-chan's heart moved. "Can we reach it?"

"Yes. But we must be careful. Fast movement will frighten it."

Ako-chan looked at the water. The small shape rose and fell with the swell. Its front paws were pressed to its chest. Its eyes were open and very dark.

It saw the ship. It did not dive.

"It knows we are here," said Ako-chan.

She turned to Obia. "Get closer. Slowly."

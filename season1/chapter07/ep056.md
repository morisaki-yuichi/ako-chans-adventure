Riro slept for a long time.

Ako-chan made a warm place for him on the deck — a folded blanket in a low, sheltered spot. He curled up tight and did not move. His small chest rose and fell.

Dango watched him from two meters away. He lay flat on the deck with his chin on his paws. He did not sleep.

When Riro woke, he sat up and looked around — at the ship, at the sea, at the group. Then he looked down at himself.

His fur was wrong. It was flat and tangled and damp in places. He touched it with one paw. Touched it again. His face changed.

"My fur," he said. "It is wrong."

"You were in the cold water a long time," said Ako-chan.

"It is wrong." He touched it again. "I need to fix it. Now."

He began to groom. Both front paws working fast. Stomach. Chest. The sides. He licked his paws and smoothed the fur down and then checked it and started again. Very careful. Very serious.

Daru watched him with great interest. "What is he doing?"

"Grooming," said Gabu.

"But he has been doing it for a very long time."

"Yes."

Suke-san looked at Riro for a moment. Then he looked away. His expression said: I understand nothing about this.

The grooming went on.

Riro's fur was already looking better — fuller, drier, lifting away from his body. Sea otter fur was very thick. That was what kept them warm. Ako-chan knew this. Riro knew it more than anyone.

At last he stopped. He checked himself carefully, head to tail.

"Better," he said. Then: "A little better. I need to do it again."

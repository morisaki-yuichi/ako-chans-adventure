Many days pass on the open sea. Then, at last, land comes into view. Black rocks rise out of the blue water. The island is dry and bare and hot. "Galapagos," Obia says. The ship stops near the rocky shore. One by one, they step onto the land. The black rock is hot under their feet. The heat comes up through their shoes. The air shakes above the dark stones.

Ako-chan looks around the strange new place. Dark lizards lie still on the warm stones. They are marine iguanas. They do not run away. They just sit in the bright sun. Some swim in the waves near the rocks. Daru rolls out of his Pod to see. "What are they? What are they?" he says. Gabu comes out too and watches them. The warm wind smells of salt and stone.

Then a long shadow falls across the rocks. Ako-chan turns around to look. Something very big sits up ahead. It is round. It has a hard shell. It moves so slowly in the heat. It is a tortoise. But it is huge. It is bigger than Dango. It is bigger than them all. Its long neck reaches up to the sky.

Daru goes quiet for once. He leans his small shell against Gabu. The two baby tortoises look up high. The giant shape rises above them like a rock. Its old eyes are deep and calm. Its great shell shines in the sun. Daru and Gabu are so small beside it. Ako-chan holds her breath and stares. Dango stays close at her side. Suke-san does not make a sound. Even the iguanas seem to wait. No one moves on the hot black rock. Something old and slow waits there for them.

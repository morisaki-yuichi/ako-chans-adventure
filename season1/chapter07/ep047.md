The night is deep over the sea. The ship rocks slowly on dark water. Ako-chan cannot sleep at all tonight. She comes up to the deck for air. The wind is cold. The stars are out. Then she stops still. She is not alone up here.

Obia stands at the front of the ship. It is all alone in the dark. Its eyes shine pale blue and bright. They are brighter than before. A low hum comes from it, soft and deep. Obia faces the south. It does not move at all. Ako-chan knows this shape. She saw it once before, on the train. Her heart beats fast. She steps closer this time.

"Obia? What are you doing?" she asks softly.

Obia does not turn. It does not answer her. She comes nearer and asks again. "Please. What is it? Tell me." But still no answer comes. The low hum stops at last. The pale eyes go dim again. Obia is quiet again, like always.

Then Ako-chan hears a low sound behind her. It is Suke-san. He stands in the dark, very still. His sharp eyes are fixed on Obia. His ears stand straight up. A soft growl sits in his throat. He saw it too. He saw all of it. He does not trust the silver thing. Ako-chan can read that on his face.

The sea is black and wide around them. The cold air bites at her hands. Ako-chan looks from Obia to Suke-san. No one says a word out here. The ship moves on through the night. She thinks of the train, far behind now. Two times now. The same strange thing. Something is wrong. But she does not say it. Not yet.

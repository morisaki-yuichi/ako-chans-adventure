Morning comes grey over the sea. The ship moves near a low green island. The water turns from deep blue to pale. It grows shallow under the ship now. Then Ako-chan sees something dark ahead. A huge black shape lies in the shallows. It does not move with the waves.

She runs to the side of the ship. She looks down over the rail. It is a whale. It is very big and very still. Its black back rises above the water. The tide pulls slowly away from it. The whale is stuck in the low water. Soon it cannot reach the deep again. Ako-chan's heart beats hard. She listens with all her heart. Then a voice comes to her, deep and slow.

"Lost," says the whale. "The deep is wrong." Its voice is low and full of pain. "Cold rises from the south. I came the wrong way." The big eye turns and finds Ako-chan. "Help me. The water pulls away."

Ako-chan holds the rail tight. The others run to the side too. Dango barks down at the great dark shape. Suke-san stands stiff and watches the shallows. Daru and Gabu peek from their Pods. The sun is pale and weak. The wet sand shines. Still no birds call above.

Then a cold thought comes to Ako-chan. Last night Obia stood and hummed. Now a whale lies stuck below. It happened once before too. The small things in the daylight. A strange night, then a strange day. Two times now. She opens her mouth to speak. But no words come out. She cannot be sure of it. And there is no time. The whale needs help now.

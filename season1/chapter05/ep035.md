More days pass on the road east. The desert is long behind them now. The land here is green and wet. Low hills roll under a grey sky. Ako-chan stops to rest by a field. The air feels heavy and still. Something makes her look up.

High above her, black birds fly. They are crows. There are many of them. They turn slowly, round and round. But they make no sound at all. Crows are loud birds. They call and caw all day. These crows do not. Ako-chan listens hard for a voice. She waits and holds her breath. But nothing comes to her. The crows fly on in silence. Their dark wings beat without a sound.

Dango stands close to her feet. His ears go flat against his head. A low growl comes from his throat. He does not like the quiet birds. Dalu peeks out from his red Pod. "Why are they so quiet?" he asks. Ako-chan has no answer for him. Gabu looks up at the sky and stays still.

Nothing else makes a sound out here. No frogs call from the wet grass. No small birds sing in the trees. The wind does not move the leaves. Even Dalu stays low in his Pod. The only thing that moves is above. The dark birds keep their slow circle.

The grey light fades into evening. Still the black birds turn above. They do not stop at all. No voice falls down from the sky. Ako-chan feels the wrong thing again. It is the same wrong as before. It comes from the south, she knows. She holds Dango close and walks on.

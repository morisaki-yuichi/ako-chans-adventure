They moved through the desert toward the signal. The red walls were tall on both sides. The sand was pale orange. Their feet made small sounds.

Obia said: "Fifty meters."

Ako-chan looked at the rocks. Nothing moved. Nothing made a sound.

But she felt it. The way she felt birds before they came close. Something very still and very careful. Just watching.

Then she saw it.

On a low ledge of rock, just above the sand. A small dark shape, rounded. And then — gone. One moment it was there. The next — just stone.

She did not move.

Evening came. The light in the valley turned orange, then red, then was gone. They made camp below the rock wall.

Ako-chan opened her eyes before dawn.

The desert was very cold now. Different from the heat of the day. She lay still and looked up at the rock wall above. Stars showed at the top of the canyon — bright and many, different from England's sky.

She turned her head.

The shape was back. On the same ledge. Eight meters away. Very still. Looking at her.

She did not know how long it had been there.

She did not move. She breathed slowly. The shape did not move either.

The sky turned dark blue. Then grey. The rocks began to show their red color.

It was a tortoise. Small. Shell dark like the stone. Eyes steady and open. Still looking.

Then Dango made a small sound in his sleep. He turned over. His big tail swept the sand.

The shape was gone.

"Dango," said Ako-chan softly.

He opened one eye.

"Something was on that rock," she said. "I think it was there all night."

Dango sat up. He looked at the empty ledge. He sniffed.

"Still close," he said. "Not running."

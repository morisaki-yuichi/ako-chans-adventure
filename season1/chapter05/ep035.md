The desert was cold before dawn.

Ako-chan woke and sat up. The air was sharp and clean. Stars above — bright and many and different from England. She could see the red rocks as dark shapes against the lighter sky.

She looked at them without moving.

Then she saw it.

On a ledge about thirty meters away. A small shape, very still. Looking toward her. She could not see its color in the dark. But she felt it: calm and careful and patient. Just looking.

She did not move.

She breathed slowly.

The shape did not move either.

For a long time they stayed like that. Ako-chan sitting. The small shape on the rock. The stars above and the desert all around them.

Then the sky turned dark blue. Then grey. The rocks began to show their red color again.

The shape was still there. She could see it better now. A tortoise. Small. Shell dark like the rock. Eyes open and steady. Looking straight at her.

She did not speak. She did not try to move closer.

Then Dango made a small sound in his sleep. He turned over. His big fluffy tail swept the sand.

The shape was gone. She did not see it go. One moment it was there. The next — just stone.

Dango opened one eye. "What is it?" he said.

"Something was there," said Ako-chan. "On the rock. All night, I think."

Dango sat up. He looked at the empty ledge. He sniffed the air slowly.

"It is still close," he said. "I can smell it."

Ako-chan looked at the red rocks in the early light.

"It is not running away," she said.

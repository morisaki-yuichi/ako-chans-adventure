"We should not go to it," said Ako-chan. "Let it come to us."

They found a flat patch of sand in the shadow of the rock wall and sat. Daru watched from inside the pod.

They waited.

An hour passed. Maybe more. Dango sat without moving. He seemed to understand.

Ako-chan kept her eyes soft. Not looking too hard at any one place.

Then: movement.

From behind a tall orange rock, about ten meters away. A tortoise. The same small dark shape. Three careful steps. Stop. Three steps. Stop.

Getting closer.

Shell pale yellow with dark markings. Eyes dark and very steady. Each step placed down with care.

Five meters. Stop.

It looked at Ako-chan. Then at Dango. Then at Obia. Then at Daru in the pod. It took its time with each.

"Hello," said Ako-chan. Very soft.

The tortoise did not answer.

It looked at her again for a long moment. Then at Obia. Longer this time.

Then it turned and walked slowly back toward the rocks. One careful step at a time.

At the last rock before the shadows, it stopped. It looked back once.

Ako-chan did not move.

The tortoise went between the rocks and was gone.

Dango's tail moved once.

"It is not afraid," he said.

"No," said Ako-chan. "It is thinking."

She looked at the place where it had gone. The red rocks. The sand. The silence.

It was coming to them on its own terms. She could wait.

"We should not go to it," said Obia. "Let it come to us."

They found a flat rock in the shade of the tall red walls. They sat. Obia set up the cooling field. Daru watched from the pod.

They waited.

An hour passed. Maybe more.

Dango sat without moving. He seemed to understand. His ears were forward but he was very still.

Ako-chan watched the rocks. She kept her eyes soft. Not looking too hard at any one place.

Then: movement.

Slow and careful. From behind a large orange rock, about thirty meters away. A tortoise. The same dark rounded shape she had seen in the night. Walking one careful step at a time. Three steps. Stop. Three steps. Stop.

Closer.

She could see it better now. Small body. Shell patterned brown and black, like the rock around it. Eyes very dark and very steady.

Fifteen meters. Stop.

It looked at Ako-chan. Then at Dango. Then at Obia. Then at Daru in the pod. It took its time with each one.

"Hello," said Ako-chan. Very soft.

The tortoise did not answer.

It looked at her again for a long moment. Then it turned and walked back toward the rocks. Not fast. Not afraid. Just turning away, one careful step at a time.

But it stopped once. It looked back.

Ako-chan did not move.

The tortoise turned again and disappeared between two red stones.

Dango's tail moved slowly once.

"It is not afraid," he said.

"No," said Ako-chan. "It is thinking."

The desert was quiet and still. The shadows moved very slowly across the sand.

She thought: tomorrow.

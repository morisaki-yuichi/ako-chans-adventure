The road comes down to the sea. A grey sea waits under a grey sky. Ako-chan smells the salt in the air. A small ferry sits by the dock. They stop to rest on the shore. The wet gravel shines under her feet. Then she sees them on the sand.

Fish lie all along the shore. They are silver and small and still. Many of them are washed up here. Some still move a little bit. Ako-chan kneels down on the wet stones. She listens. A small voice comes to her. It is weak and full of fear. "Cold," says the voice. "The water is cold." "Wrong way," says another one. "We came the wrong way." "Where is the warm sea?" says a third. The voices are weak and lost. They do not know where they are.

The smell of the sea is strong. Salt, cold water, and wet stone. Dango steps back from the fish. He does not sniff them this time. Dalu looks out from his red Pod. "What is wrong with them?" he asks. Gabu watches the grey water and says nothing. The waves push more fish up the sand. The gravel clicks beneath the tide. Obia looks down at the silver bodies. It does not speak.

Ako-chan wants to help them all. But there are too many on the shore. They came so far from their warm home. They swam up to the far north. She cannot put them all back. She puts one small fish back herself. It slips down into the dark waves. The salt wind pulls at her hair. She holds Dango close and stands up.

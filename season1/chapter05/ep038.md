In the afternoon, Gabu came and sat near them in the shade. He spoke slowly. Ako-chan did not hurry him.

"The sand at night," he said. "It should be cold. The desert cools very fast after dark. It has always been this way."

"And now?" said Ako-chan.

"Now the sand stays warm. All night. Like something is pushing heat up from very far below."

Daru started to speak. Gabu waited without looking at him.

"It is like the ground in Croatia," said Daru. "From underneath. Something wrong."

"Yes," said Gabu. "Like that." He looked at the sand in front of him. "The wind too. The south wind used to change. Fast some days, slow others. Now it does not change at all. Same direction. Same strength. Every day."

"From the south," said Ako-chan.

"Very far south." A pause. "The lizards left first. About ten days ago. Before them, the snakes. Before them, the sand beetles."

"You counted the days," said Obia.

Gabu looked at Obia. "Yes," he said. "I counted."

Even Daru was quiet now.

"Why did you stay?" said Ako-chan.

"To know the full pattern. If I leave too soon, I miss something. A pattern tells you more than a feeling alone."

She looked at him. He was not like Daru. Daru had felt something and come running. Gabu had felt the same thing and stayed to watch. To count. To wait.

"Will you come with us?" she said.

He looked south. Then back at her.

"I need to think," he said.

He stood. He walked back to the rocks. Slow. Steady. No hurry. No looking back.

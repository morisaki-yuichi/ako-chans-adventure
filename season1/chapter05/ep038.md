In the afternoon, Gabu sat near them in the shade and spoke slowly.

"The sand at night," he said. "It should be cold. The desert cools very fast after dark. It has always been that way. Since I was small."

"And now?" said Ako-chan.

"Now the sand stays warm. All night. Like something is pushing heat up from very far below."

Daru started to say something. Gabu waited without looking at him.

"It is like what I felt in Croatia," said Daru. "The ground felt wrong. From underneath."

"Yes," said Gabu. "Like that." He looked at the sand in front of him. "The wind too. The south wind used to change. Fast some days. Slow other days. Now it does not change. It comes from the same place every day. The same direction. The same speed."

"From the south," said Ako-chan.

"From very far south." He paused. "The lizards left first. About fifteen days ago. Before them, the snakes. Before them, the sand beetles."

"You counted the days," said Obia.

Gabu looked at Obia. "Yes," he said. "I counted."

Daru was very still now.

"Why did you stay?" said Ako-chan.

Gabu was quiet for a moment. "Because I wanted to know the full pattern. If I leave too soon, I miss it. The pattern tells you more than the feeling."

Ako-chan looked at him. He was not like Daru. He did not run to the thing. He watched first. He counted. He waited until he understood.

"Will you come with us?" she said.

Gabu looked south. Then back at her.

"I need to think," he said.

He stood and walked slowly back toward the rocks. No hurry. No looking back.

The ferry comes into a small port. This is Japan, at the end of the road. Green mountains stand close behind the town. They are steep and dark and very high. The air is warm and full of water. Ako-chan smells salt, fish, and wet wood. Boats sit quiet along the dock.

They step off the ferry onto the dock. The wood feels firm under their feet. Ako-chan looks all around the port. Small fishing boats rock on the grey water. Ropes and nets lie on the stones. The long road over land is done now. Dango runs ahead to smell it all. He sniffs the ropes and the nets. Daru and Gabu look from their Pods. "We are here!" says Daru. Gabu just looks at the tall green hills.

The town is small and old. Wood houses stand close to the water. Old men sit and mend their nets. They look up at the strange group. A dog, a girl, a silver thing. And two round Pods on the stones. No one says a word to them.

The wet air sits warm upon the town. Behind the roofs, the dark mountains rise. A soft rain starts to fall outside. It makes small rings on the still bay. A few birds call out nearby. The smell of the sea is deep here. Obia looks far out across the bay.

The long road is behind them now. But a wide sea waits in front. How can they cross such big water? Ako-chan does not know yet. Then Dango stops and looks up. High on the rocks, something is there. He stares up at it for a moment. But he does not bark.

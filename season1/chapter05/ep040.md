The air was cool and blue before dawn.

Gabu came from the rocks at first light. He walked to them and stopped.

"Ready," he said.

They moved east, out of the red valley and into the open desert. Five now: Ako-chan, Dango, Obia, Daru in the pod, Gabu walking free at the front.

Gabu moved slowly. He stopped where two paths met and looked left, then right, then straight ahead.

"Left," he said.

Obia checked. "Correct. The left path is better."

"How did you know?" said Daru.

Gabu looked at him. "The ground is harder on the left. Better to walk on in the heat."

"But how did you know before the heat came?" said Daru.

"I live here," said Gabu.

"Oh," said Daru. He thought about this. "Right."

They walked on. The sand was pale orange. The sky turned from blue to light. The sun came up red and large over the edge of the desert.

A small desert bird crossed high above — south to north. Ako-chan felt it pass: the same push from the south, the same wrong note. But she was less afraid of it now.

She took out the red notebook.

*Day twenty. Wadi Rum. Gabu is with us.*

She put the notebook away. The desert went east and south. Gabu led the way. Daru watched everything through the clear wall of the pod. Dango walked beside Ako-chan.

Obia said: "The signal from the south is still there. Not smaller."

"No," said Ako-chan. "But we are bigger now."

She walked on into the morning.

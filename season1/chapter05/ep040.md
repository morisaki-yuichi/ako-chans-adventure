The air was cool and blue before dawn.

Gabu came from the rocks at first light. He walked to them and stopped beside Dango.

"Ready," he said.

They walked east, out of the deep red valley and into the open desert. Five of them now: Ako-chan, Dango, Obia, Daru in the pod, Gabu walking free.

Gabu walked at the front. Slow and careful. He stopped where two paths met and looked left. Then right. Then straight ahead.

"Left," he said.

Obia checked. "He is right. Left is the better path."

"How did you know?" said Daru.

Gabu looked at him. "The ground on the left is harder. Better to walk on in the heat."

Daru said "Oh." He was quiet for a moment. Then: "But how did you know before the heat?"

"I live here," said Gabu.

"Oh," said Daru again. He thought about this. "Right."

They walked on. The sand was pale orange. The sky turned from blue to light. The sun came up over the edge of the desert floor — red and large and very bright.

Ako-chan took out the red notebook.

*Day twenty. Wadi Rum. Gabu is with us.*

She looked ahead. The desert went on to the east and south. Dango walked beside her. Gabu moved ahead, picking the path. Daru watched everything through the clear wall of the pod.

Obia said, "The signal from the south is still there. Not smaller."

"No," said Ako-chan. "But we are bigger now."

She put the notebook away. The morning light was warm on her face. The cool field moved with her, steady and quiet.

She walked on into the day.

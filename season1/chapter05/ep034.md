They moved into the desert toward the signal.

The sand was smooth between the red rock walls. Pale orange in the afternoon light. Their feet made small sounds with each step. No wind. No birds. Very quiet.

Obia walked ahead and stopped every now and then. "Closer," it said. "About two hundred meters."

Dango walked beside Ako-chan. His ears were up. He was listening.

Daru watched from inside the pod. He turned his head left, then right. His eyes were busy.

The rocks grew taller around them. Strange shapes. Some looked like towers. Some like very still animals. The afternoon light turned gold. The shadows were long.

Ako-chan stopped.

Something was watching.

She did not hear it. She did not see it. But she felt it. The way she sometimes felt birds before they came close. Careful attention. Quiet and steady. Not afraid. Just — looking.

"It is here," said Obia. "Fifty meters."

Ako-chan looked at the rocks around her. Nothing moved.

Dango turned his head slowly to the right. He was looking at a low ledge of rock near the sand. Just stone. Just shadow.

Then Ako-chan saw it.

A shape. Small and dark against the red rock. Still. Rounded. And then — gone. It pulled back into the rock and was not there anymore.

She did not move. She did not speak.

Dango did not bark. He looked at the empty space on the ledge.

"Did you see?" said Ako-chan.

"Yes," said Dango.

Obia said: "The signal has not moved far. It is still very close. It is watching us."

The desert was quiet. The shadows kept growing. Nothing came out.

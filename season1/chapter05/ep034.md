The desert is far behind them now. The land rolls on towards the east. The land is still dry at first. Red sand turns to pale brown stone. Then small green plants start to grow. Ako-chan watches the world change slowly. Each day looks new and different.

They travel on, day after day. The brown ground turns soft and green. Grass grows tall on every side. Then come the farms, wide and flat. People work far off in the fields. Big mountains stand blue in the distance. Dalu watches it all from his Pod. "And then?" he asks Obia. "East," says Obia. Dalu waits for more words. But none come. "And then?" he asks again. "East," says Obia again.

Dalu runs hard inside his red Pod. It rolls fast and bright down the road. Gabu keeps his calm, steady pace. His blue Pod hums in an even line. One is quick. One is slow. Dango trots along between the two. He looks tired, but his tail is up.

The smells change too along the way. Hot dry sand becomes wet earth. Ako-chan breathes the cool fresh air. New birds sing in the tall trees. The road climbs up and then down again. They pass small towns and big towns. People look at the two round Pods. They point and smile as they roll by.

The sun goes down behind them now. Their shadows grow long on the ground. The shadows point the way ahead. Ako-chan does not know this land. But the way is always the same. She holds that small word close. It is the only word she needs. East.

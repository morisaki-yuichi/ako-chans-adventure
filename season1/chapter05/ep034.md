Obia opened a panel on its side — lower down, different from the cooling field.

From inside it took out a round pod. Not much bigger than a melon, but it opened wide, like a flower. The sides were clear. Inside, soft warm light moved. A gentle circulation of air.

Obia set it on the sand in front of Daru.

Daru looked at it. He walked up slowly and put one foot through the opening.

The air inside touched him. He went still.

"It is the right temperature," said Obia. "The right light. For a tortoise from Croatia."

Daru put in his other foot. Then his whole body. He turned around once and settled.

The door closed softly.

Ako-chan crouched down and looked at him through the clear side. Daru looked back. His eyes were more open now. He blinked.

"Good?" she said.

"Warm," said Daru. His voice was small through the wall. "Good."

She put her hand on the outside of the pod. Smooth. Slightly warm.

Inside, Daru turned his head. Left. Right. He was watching the desert through the clear sides. The tall red walls. The pale sand. Everything was there, but quiet, seen through the clear shell.

"I can see everything," he said.

Obia was already looking south. Its eyes were very still.

"Obia?" said Ako-chan.

"A signal," it said. "Small. Very still. In the same place for many days. Not leaving."

Ako-chan looked at the red rock walls around her.

Daru turned his head south too, through the clear wall.

"Let us find it," he said.

Obia walked. Ako-chan followed. The pod moved with them, steady above the pale sand.

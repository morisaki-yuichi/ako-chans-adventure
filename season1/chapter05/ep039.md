All day, Gabu was in the rocks.

Ako-chan could see him sometimes — a small dark shape on a ledge, watching. She did not go to him.

Daru moved inside the pod. "He is still there," he said.

"Yes."

"He is just sitting."

"Yes."

"It is taking a very long time."

"That is Gabu," said Ako-chan.

Dango lay in the shadow of the rock wall with his nose pointed toward the rocks. He said nothing.

The day passed. The shadows moved across the red walls. Three desert birds flew over — south to north, fast. Ako-chan felt them as they passed: the same push from the south. Not smaller.

Near sunset, the light turned orange and the shadows grew very long.

A small dark shape came from the rocks. Slow. Steady.

Gabu stopped in front of Ako-chan.

"I have been here alone," he said. "Since the lizards left. Watching. Counting. Waiting for the pattern to be clear."

Ako-chan listened.

"The pattern is moving north. Slowly. But it will not stop on its own."

"No," she said.

"I can stay and keep watching. But watching is not enough now." A pause. "I will come with you."

He said it once. Quietly. Without looking away.

"We leave tomorrow," said Ako-chan. "Early."

Gabu looked at the pod. Daru was very still inside it.

"Hello again," said Daru.

"Hello," said Gabu.

He turned and walked back to the rocks. To sleep one more night in his home.

Dango came and sat beside Ako-chan. They watched the small dark shape disappear between the stones.

The last orange light was on the red walls.

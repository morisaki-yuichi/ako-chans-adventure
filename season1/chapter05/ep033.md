They came around a long curve in the road — and the rocks were there.

Ako-chan stopped.

Red. Not orange. Not brown. Red. Walls of stone going straight up from the desert floor. Hundreds of meters tall. She had to tilt her head back to see the tops. Between them: pale sand, smooth and still as water.

No sound. Nothing moved.

"It is very big," she said.

Then the heat reached her.

It did not come from one place. It came from above and below and from the red walls on both sides, all at the same time. Like stepping inside something solid. Ako-chan could not take a full breath. Her glasses went hot on her face.

Dango pressed hard against her leg. He was panting fast.

Obia placed a small flat square on the sand. About the size of a book. A quiet hum started. Then, slowly, the air around them changed. Cool. Still. Clean.

Ako-chan stepped into it.

She breathed in.

The air was not cold. Not windy. Just right. A stillness that was exactly right.

Dango stepped in beside her. His panting stopped. His ears came up. His big fluffy tail gave one slow wag.

"The field will move with you," said Obia. "Stay close."

Ako-chan looked at the tall red walls, the pale sand, the wide silence.

Then she looked at Daru. He had stopped moving. His legs were pulled in. His eyes were almost closed.

"Daru," she said quietly.

"Hot," said Daru.

Obia turned toward him. "Yes," it said. "I know. Come here."

Ako-chan came around a bend in the road — and stopped.

The rocks were red. Not orange. Not brown. Red. They rose straight up from the desert floor — tall, flat walls of stone. Some were hundreds of meters high. The desert between them was pale sand, smooth as still water.

She stood and looked.

"It is very big," she said.

"Yes," said Obia.

Then the heat reached her. It came from the sky. It came from the sand. It came from the red rock walls around them. Her glasses went hot on her face. Dango pressed close to her leg and panted hard.

Obia placed a small flat square on the sand. A quiet hum. Then cool air moved out from it — not cold, just right. Still and clean.

Ako-chan stepped into the cool field. She breathed out slowly.

Dango stepped in beside her. His ears came up. His tail moved once.

"Thank you," said Ako-chan.

Obia turned to Daru. Daru's legs were very slow. His eyes were almost closed.

"Daru," said Obia. "I need you to go inside this."

It opened a small round pod — clear sides, soft warm light inside, a gentle movement of air.

Daru looked at it. He walked up and put one foot in the opening.

"It is the right temperature," said Obia. "For you."

Daru went inside. The door closed softly. He moved once. Then he settled. He looked out through the clear wall at Ako-chan.

She put her hand on the outside of the pod. "Good?"

"Warm," said Daru. "Good."

Obia was already looking south. "There is a signal. Small. Very still. It has stayed in the same place for many days."

Ako-chan looked at the red walls around them.

"Someone is waiting," she said.

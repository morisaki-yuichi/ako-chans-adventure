Four days of travel since Croatia.

The mountains were behind them. The land was flat now and very wide. No trees. No harbor. No stone walls. Just pale ground and the long road and the wide blue sky.

Daru stopped beside a low grey bush. He put his head close and looked carefully. "There is almost nothing green here," he said.

"A little," said Ako-chan. She pointed to a small cluster of pale yellow flowers near the road. "Not much. But some."

Daru looked at the flowers for a moment. He nodded once. Then he walked on.

The air was warm. Not yet hot. But getting there. Ako-chan felt it on the back of her neck and on her arms.

The disturbance was here too. In the dry ground. In the empty sky. The same push from the south. Quiet in some ways. Big in others.

"We are near Jordan," said Obia. "One more day."

Dango walked beside Ako-chan. His ears turned slowly. He was checking the quiet.

Ako-chan took out the red notebook.

*Day fifteen. One more day to the desert.*

She closed it. She thought about Daru on his last rocky point in Croatia. Standing at the edge. Looking at everything he knew.

*I am ready.* And then walking away without looking back.

Here there was no edge to stand at. Just flat land going on and on. The road turned south and east. The sky was very blue above and very empty.

Dango touched her hand with his nose.

She walked on.

One more day.

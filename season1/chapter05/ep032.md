Two days after the ferry, the road was straight and very pale.

No trees. No harbor. No stone walls. Just flat brown ground and the long road and the sky. Nothing green in any direction.

Ako-chan was hot. The sun came from directly above. The air did not move. Her glasses were warm on her face. She could feel the heat through the soles of her shoes.

Daru had slowed down. A few steps. Stop. A few steps. His eyes were low.

Obia was quiet this morning. It walked ahead. Then it stopped and opened a small panel on its chest. It looked at something inside. It closed the panel and walked on.

"What are you doing?" said Ako-chan.

"Preparing," said Obia.

"For what?"

"For the desert. You will see."

Dango walked close beside Ako-chan. His tongue was out. He was working hard. But he did not complain.

Ako-chan took out the red notebook.

*Day fifteen. The road goes south. Very hot.*

She closed it. The sun moved slowly. The heat did not change.

Then Obia stopped. It was looking at the horizon to the south.

"What is it?" said Ako-chan.

"Look," said Obia.

She looked.

Far to the south: shapes. Red. Tall. Just the tops of something huge, showing above the flat land. Like the edge of another world.

"Wadi Rum," said Obia. "We are close now."

Daru raised his head. He looked at the red shapes for a long time.

"Red," he said.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

He looked a little longer. Then he walked on. A little faster than before.

It came back in the morning.

Ako-chan was writing in the red notebook when she heard feet on sand. Small, careful sounds. She looked up.

The tortoise was three steps away. It stopped.

It looked at her. The same steady, dark eyes.

Ako-chan closed the notebook. She waited.

"Why are you here?" said the tortoise.

Its voice was quiet. Not small — just quiet. Each word came one at a time.

"We are going south," said Ako-chan. "Something is wrong there. We are going to find out what."

The tortoise was still for a moment.

"I know something is wrong," it said. "I have been watching it."

Dango looked up but did not move.

"What do you see?" said Ako-chan.

"Many things. I will tell you later." It looked at her. "What is your name?"

"Ako-chan."

"My name is Gabu." He looked at Obia. "That thing. What is it?"

"That is a long story," said Ako-chan.

"I have time," said Gabu.

She told him. About Obia. About the animals in England — the hedgehog, the robin, the owl. About the signals moving north. About Daru in Croatia. Gabu listened and did not ask questions until she stopped.

"You are going south," he said.

"Yes."

"To find the source."

"Yes."

Gabu was quiet for a long moment. He looked at the pod where Daru was watching with wide eyes.

"Hello," said Daru.

"Hello," said Gabu. He was quiet again after that.

He looked south at the red rock walls and the bright sky between them.

"I have been tracking this," he said slowly. "For many days now."

"What have you found?" said Ako-chan.

Gabu looked at the sand. "Come back this afternoon," he said. "I will show you."

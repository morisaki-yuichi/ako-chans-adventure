It came back in the morning.

Ako-chan was writing in the red notebook when she heard feet on sand. Small and careful. She looked up.

The tortoise was three steps away. It stopped.

The same dark, steady eyes.

Ako-chan closed the notebook and waited.

"Why are you here?" said the tortoise.

The voice was quiet. Each word came one at a time.

"We are going south," said Ako-chan. "Something is wrong there. We want to find it."

A pause. "I know something is wrong. I have been watching it."

"What do you see?" said Ako-chan.

"Many things." It looked at her. "What is your name?"

"Ako-chan. What is yours?"

"Gabu." He looked at Obia. "That thing. What is it?"

"That is a long story," said Ako-chan.

"I have time."

She told him. About Obia. About the hedgehog in England, and the owl, and the robin. About the signals moving north. About Daru in Croatia. Gabu listened without moving. He did not speak until she stopped.

"You are going south," he said. "To find the source."

"Yes."

He was quiet. Then he looked at the pod. Daru was watching him with wide eyes.

"Hello," said Daru.

"Hello," said Gabu. Then quiet again.

He looked south, at the tall red walls and the bright sky above them.

"I have been watching this for many days," he said. "Come back this afternoon. I will show you what I found."

He turned. He walked back to the rocks at the same slow pace.

Ako-chan looked at Dango. Dango looked back. His tail moved once.

"He will come," said Dango.

"Yes," she said. "In his own time."

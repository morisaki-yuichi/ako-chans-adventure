Two days after Croatia, the sea was gone.

The road climbed into the mountains. The ground was grey and brown. No green coast. No white rocks. Just dry stone and thin scrub and the sound of their feet.

Daru stopped every few minutes. He looked at the stones. He studied a dry plant with grey leaves. He put his head very close to a small hole in the rock.

"What is in there?" he said.

"I do not know," said Obia.

"Something," said Daru. He looked a little longer. Then he walked on.

The morning was already warm. Warmer than Croatia. The air felt dry. It touched the inside of Ako-chan's nose in a new way.

Dango walked slowly. His big fluffy tail was not bouncing. He was saving his energy.

They walked in a line: Daru, Dango, Ako-chan, Obia last.

"How much further?" said Ako-chan.

"Many days," said Obia. "South, then east, then south again into Jordan."

Ako-chan looked at the road ahead. It went between tall grey peaks. The sky was pale blue and wide. A large dark bird turned slow circles high above. She did not know its name. She felt it: old and patient. Very wide.

But underneath: the same push. The same pressure from the south. It was not smaller here.

Daru came back from a flat stone he had been studying. "The road goes south," he said.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

"Good." He turned and walked ahead.

She followed. The mountain road went on. The peaks were very still on both sides. No wind. Just grey rock and pale sky and the four of them moving south.

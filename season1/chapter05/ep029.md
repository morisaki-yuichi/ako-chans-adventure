The sea was very blue.

Not the grey sea of England. Not the cold dark water near Croatia. This was a different blue — warm and clear and very deep.

The ferry moved east. It was big and white. The engine was a low, steady sound below their feet.

Daru sat near the front rail. He kept his head up. He watched the water below.

"The sea is different here," he said.

"We have moved south," said Ako-chan. "The water is warmer."

Daru put one foot on the metal rail and looked down. Far below, the water slid past the side of the boat. He stayed like that for a long time.

Dango sat beside Ako-chan. His big tail was very still. He did not like the boat the way he liked firm ground. But he was calm.

The coast of Greece was behind them now. Pale cliffs. White buildings. A blue sky. All getting smaller. Ahead: just sea and sky.

Ako-chan looked east. She felt something there. Very faint. The same push she had felt in England — the same wrong note. Quieter here, from out on the water. But still there.

"The signal?" she said.

"Present," said Obia. "Still from the south. Far south."

A fish broke the surface near the boat. One quick jump. Then gone.

Daru watched the spot for a moment. "The fish here feel different too," he said.

"Different how?" said Ako-chan.

He thought. "Older," he said. "Like they have been here a very long time."

Ako-chan looked at the water. The sea moved past, wide and blue and very still under the bright sky.

Yes, she thought. That was the word.

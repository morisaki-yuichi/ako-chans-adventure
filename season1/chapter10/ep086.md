Ako-chan looked at Obia. Then she looked at the screen.

"How long do we have?" she said.

"An hour," said Obia. "Maybe two. I do not know exactly."

Suke-san walked to the table. He put his front paws on the edge and looked at the screen. He looked for a long time. Then he stepped down.

The Colonel stood very straight. "This data. You carried it from England to here." He was not asking. He was saying it to himself.

Obia said nothing.

Pen-san looked up at Ako-chan. "What does it mean?" he said, very quietly.

"It means we go east," said Ako-chan.

Nobody spoke. The ship groaned. Ice pressed hard against the right side.

Ako-chan looked at the Colonel. He was looking at Obia. His face was very still. Not angry. Not afraid. Something she could not read.

Then he turned to her. "Is this what you want to do?"

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

The Colonel nodded once. He walked to the engine room door. "Obia. Come."

Obia followed without a sound.

Dango pressed against Ako-chan's leg. She put her hand on his back.

The engines started. Low at first. Then louder. The ship moved — just a little. Then a little more.

Ice pushed back. Ice scraped the hull. A long, slow sound.

Then the sound changed. The scraping grew softer. Something was opening.

"East," Obia said from the engine room. "Hold east."

The ship went east.

Outside the porthole, the white shapes moved — and then there was dark water. Open water.

Ako-chan let out a long breath.

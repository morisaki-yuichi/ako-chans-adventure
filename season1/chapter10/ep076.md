The wind is loud outside the ship. The storm is almost here now. The others get ready by the door. But Obia is very quiet. It looks at Dango for a moment. Then it calls him over, softly and low. "Dango," it says. "Come here."

Dango walks to Obia, slow and unsure. Obia looks around first. No one else is near them. Then it takes off its red collar. It holds the collar out to Dango. "Hold this," it says. Dango looks at the red collar. He does not understand at all. "I do not know why," says Obia. "But it is important. Hold it for me." Dango takes the collar in his mouth. His eyes are full of questions.

Dango holds the collar tight in his teeth. It is warm from Obia's body. He looks up at Obia's blue eyes. He wants to ask so many things. But he is only a dog. He cannot use words. The wind shakes the ship hard now. Snow hits the side like sand. Outside, the sky is almost black now. The day looks like night to him. The cold creeps in through the walls. Dango feels the floor shake under him. Obia turns and looks to the south.

Obia puts one paw on Dango's head. Just for a small moment. Then it takes the paw away again. "Good," it says, softly and low. Dango holds the red collar close. He does not let it go. He looks toward the others by the door. Then he looks back at Obia again. Something is wrong. He feels it deep inside. But he does not know what to do.

The next morning, the blizzard was gone.

The sky was clear — pale blue and very thin. The ice was flat and white and still. The ship sat in open water and did not move.

Ako-chan went out early. She stood at the front of the ship.

She listened.

The disturbance was still there.

She thought: maybe the blizzard was the end. Maybe something was over. But it was not over. The pull from the south was still there — the same pull she felt in England, in Croatia, in the desert, on the sea. Here. Under the ice. At the end of everything.

Obia came and stood beside her.

"It is still there," Ako-chan said.

"Yes," said Obia.

"We got through the blizzard. We got out of the ice. But the source—"

"The source is still there," said Obia. "Yes."

Ako-chan looked south. Nothing but ice and sky. "Can we stop it?"

Obia was quiet for a moment. Then: "I do not know."

Ako-chan let out a long breath. The cold took it and turned it white.

"Then what do we do?" she said.

"I am still looking," said Obia. "At the data. I am still looking."

One by one, the others came out. Dango. Suke-san. Pen-san, very quiet. Daru's face pressed to his pod window. Gabu watching.

Nobody spoke.

The ice was flat. The sky was pale. The pull from the south was still there.

They stopped the danger. But the root of it — far below the ice — was still connected to something.

Still there.

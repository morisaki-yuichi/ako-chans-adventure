That evening, Suke-san walked to Obia.

Obia was at the ship's side, by the sensors. The blizzard was gone. The ice was still. Just the cold and the dark and the quiet sound of water.

Suke-san stopped two steps away. He looked at Obia for a long time. Obia's eyes were blue-white and steady. It did not move away. It did not come forward. It waited.

Suke-san sat down.

"You have no bad feeling in you," Suke-san said.

Obia did not speak.

"I watched. Since England." Suke-san's voice was quiet. "I could not tell before. You are not like a dog. Not like anything I know. So I could not tell."

He looked at the ice. Then back at Obia.

"But today. The way you worked. No hiding. No stopping. Just helping. The same as always."

A long moment of silence.

"No bad feeling. None. I am sure now."

Obia said nothing. But its eyes stayed on Suke-san.

Suke-san stood. He walked back to his place near Ako-chan.

From across the ship, Colonel Adelie watched. He saw Suke-san walk to Obia. He saw Suke-san sit. He saw Suke-san speak. He saw Suke-san walk back.

The Colonel looked at Obia for a long time.

Obia stood very still. Blue-white eyes. Steady. The sensors were quiet. The ice outside was quiet.

The Colonel did not speak. He did not move.

But something in the way he held himself was different now.

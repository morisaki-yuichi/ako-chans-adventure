The sound started late that night.

At first it was low, like something very far away. Then it grew. A long, slow groan — from the hull, from under the ship.

Obia went to the engines. The ship moved a little. Then stopped.

"What is it?" said Ako-chan.

"Ice," said Obia. "Moving against the hull. From the south and east."

Ako-chan went to the porthole and pressed her face to the glass. Outside, through the blizzard, white shapes moved slow and enormous, pushing in from both sides.

The ship groaned again. Long and deep.

Dango lay flat on the floor, chin on the ground, ears down. Suke-san stood in the middle of the room, legs wide, not moving, his nose pointing at the wall where the sound came from.

Pen-san held onto Ako-chan's arm and did not speak.

Colonel Adelie stood in the corridor, very straight. He watched the porthole. Then Obia. Then the porthole. Then Obia.

Obia went to the navigation table and pulled up the ice readings. The numbers changed as it watched. The ice was moving fast. Too fast.

"We are surrounded on two sides," Obia said. "North and west are still open. But not for long."

Another groan. Deeper. The floor shook once.

From his pod, Daru said: "Is the ship all right?"

Nobody answered.

Obia looked at the numbers. They kept changing. Ako-chan watched them too. She could not read what they meant. But she could see they were not stopping.

Outside, the ice pressed and pressed and did not stop.

The Colonel walks away across the ice. But the group still stands close. Obia is quiet by the ship. The sky is low and grey. Ako-chan's heart beats fast and hard. She holds her hands together tight. She looks at all her friends. Then she takes a slow breath. She cannot stay quiet now. There is something she must say.

"I have to tell you," she says. "Last night was not the first time." Everyone turns and looks at her. "I saw this before. Two times." She points far behind them all. "Once on the train. Near the start." Then she looks out at the sea. "Once on the ship. At night." Her voice is low and slow. "Obia stood alone. It looked south." "The same light. The same hum." She stops for a moment. "This is the third time."

No one speaks for a moment. Then Suke-san looks up at her. "I saw it too," he says. "On the ship. In the dark." Dango leans his full weight on her leg. Daru and Gabu stay still in their shells. Pen-san moves close to Ako-chan. The wind is cold and thin. Snow moves across the white ice. Up on the ice, the Colonel stands. He watches them from far away. He says nothing. But he hears every word.

Ako-chan looks at Obia again. "What does it mean?" she asks softly. Obia does not answer her. Its blue eyes are far away. She does not know what it means. She does not know why. But three times is too many. The cold sits deep in her chest. Something is wrong here. And now they all know it.

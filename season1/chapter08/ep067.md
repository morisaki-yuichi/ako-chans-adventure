Ako-chan went to the front of the deck alone.

Everyone else was inside. Riro was fixing his fur. Dango had gone to sleep at last. Daru and Gabu were still in their pods. Suke-san had gone below without a word.

It was quiet. The sea was flat and grey. The sky was grey. Everything the same color.

She sat down on the deck. She pulled her knees up to her chest.

She took off her red glasses.

She held them in her hands. Small and red. Still a little wet from the storm. She had never taken them off outside before. Not like this.

Without them, the world was soft at the edges. The sea was grey and soft. The sky too. But the whales — she could still see them ahead. Three dark shapes. Even without her glasses, they were clear.

She did not know when she started crying.

Big tears. Slow at first. Then fast. She pressed her hand to her mouth. She did not make a sound.

She had been so afraid.

She told Suke-san she was afraid. Yes. But she said it quickly and looked away. The real fear — she kept it inside. All of it. When the biggest wave came. When the ship went up and did not seem like it would stop. She did not let any of it out. Not once. Not during the storm.

Now it came.

She heard Dango's paws on the deck.

He came to her. He sat in front of her. He pushed his head against her chest.

She put both arms around him and held on.

"I was so scared," she said. Very small. Very quiet.

Dango did not move. He just stayed.

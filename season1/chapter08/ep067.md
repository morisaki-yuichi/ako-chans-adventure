The storm did not stop.

All night, it kept going. Rain. Wind. Waves, one after another.

Below deck, Dango lay flat on the floor. His ears were down. His body was very still. He did not bark. He just waited.

Riro was in his capsule — sealed in, outside the hull, below the waterline. Gabu checked the sensor once. "The capsule is holding," said Gabu. "He is all right."

From the pods, Gabu and Daru watched through the clear walls.

Daru: "Is it over?"

Gabu: "No."

Daru: "When will it be over?"

Gabu: "I do not know."

A long silence.

Daru: "Is Ako-chan all right?"

Gabu: "She is on deck."

Daru: "In this?"

Gabu: "Yes."

Another long silence. Then, very quietly: "Then she is all right," said Daru. He said it like he was telling himself.

On deck, Ako-chan was still standing. Her coat was soaked through. The sky was dark — but a little less dark than before. From black to very dark grey. Not light yet. But something.

Suke-san was still beside her. He had been there all night. He did not speak. He did not need to.

The whale voices were still there too. Steady. Calm. Not afraid. *Hold. This way. Hold.*

Obia came close. "The pressure is changing," it said. "I think it is ending."

"How long?" Ako-chan's voice was rough and low.

"I do not know exactly. Soon, I think."

She looked at the sky. Dark grey. Just a little lighter.

Her hands were stiff on the rail. Her legs hurt. Her coat was heavy with water.

She kept going.

One more wave. One more direction. One more voice from below.

She kept going.

The whales hold the ship safe. But the storm is not over. The sea is still wild and dark. The ship needs a way out. Ako-chan stands at the front. Wind and rain hit her face. She holds the rail with cold hands. She listens. She listens hard through the storm. The wind is loud in her ears. The whales' voices are under it. She must catch them. She must hear the way.

Then a voice comes. It is the close whale. Its voice is low and clear. It cuts through the wind. "This way," says the whale. "Follow us. Go where we go." It shows her the way out. Ako-chan hears it well. She knows the way now. She turns to her friends. Her voice is calm and strong.

"Obia: route. Dango: stay down." Obia turns the ship the right way. Dango drops low on the deck. "Suke-san: watch the south." Suke-san points his nose into the wind. "Daru, Gabu: stay in your Pods." The two tortoises hold still and safe. Everyone does their part. No one panics. No one cries out. They trust her voice. Suke-san's eyes burn into the dark. The Pods glow red and blue below. Ako-chan's voice is cold and clear. It cuts the storm like the whale's.

The whales move through the dark sea. The ship follows close behind them. The waves grow smaller now. The wind starts to fall. Slowly, the storm lets them go. The whales' great backs lead the way. Ako-chan keeps her eyes on the whales. Her hands shake on the rail. But her voice did not shake. Not once.

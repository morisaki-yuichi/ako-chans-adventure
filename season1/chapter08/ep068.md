The next morning was still.

The sea was calm and flat. The sky was pale blue — the first blue they had seen in days. The whales moved ahead, a little slower than before. As if they too were resting.

Everyone came up on deck one by one. Riro first. He checked his fur section by section, very carefully. He did not rush. Then the glow of Gabu's pod appeared at the window, then Daru's. Suke-san came last — quiet and steady, the same as always.

Dango did not leave Ako-chan's side all morning. When she sat, he sat. When she moved, he moved.

Nobody talked about the storm.

But something had changed. Ako-chan could feel it. When Riro looked at her, it was different from before — quieter, closer. When Suke-san walked past, he went just a little nearer than usual. Daru talked less. Gabu watched more.

Obia came beside her. "The distance," it said.

"What about it?"

"The storm pushed us south very fast. We covered a lot of ground."

"How much?"

"Several days more than I expected. We are much closer to the south now."

She looked at the whales. Still there, still steady. She listened. Their voices were calm — but something in them felt warmer now. Brighter. *Not far now. Hold on. Almost there.*

She took out the red notebook.

*Day fifty-nine. The storm has passed. We are all here. We are closer now.*

She closed the notebook. She looked up. The sun came through the grey. Pale and cold, but real. She turned her face toward it.

For the first time in days, she felt warm.

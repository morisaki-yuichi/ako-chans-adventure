The rain stopped.

Not slowly. One moment loud and heavy. The next, gone.

The wind fell. The waves got smaller, then smaller again. The ship stopped rocking and began to move level and steady.

Ako-chan looked up. The sky was grey — but soft grey now, not the black of last night. Far in the east, very faint, there was a thin line of pale light.

Dawn.

"The storm has passed," said Obia.

Ako-chan did not answer right away. She just stood there. Her hands were still on the rail. Her coat was heavy with water. The deck was wet all around her.

Suke-san walked to the side rail and looked at the sea. He walked back.

"It is over," he said.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

Below, she heard Dango on the stairs. He came up and stepped out onto the deck. He looked at the sky. He looked at Ako-chan. He came to her and sat down against her leg, his head pressed to her knee.

She put her hand on his head.

Riro came up next. He stood still and looked at his fur. He pressed it down in five or six places. He smoothed it. "I need to fix all of this," he said. But his voice was quiet and tired. Not the usual urgency.

From the pods, Daru and Gabu watched through the clear walls. Both still. Both safe.

The whales were still there — three dark shapes ahead, moving south. Steady. Always south.

Ako-chan looked at the thin pale light in the east. Her hands were stiff. Her legs were very tired. Her coat was cold and wet.

She was exhausted.

But they were all here.

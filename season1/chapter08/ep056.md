Many days pass. The ship sails on, far to the south. Then, one morning, the sky changes. It is not grey now. It is dark and low. The clouds hang heavy over the sea. The wind grows. It is fast and hard and loud. It pushes the ship. The waves rise higher than before. The ship climbs each one and drops.

Obia stands on the deck. Its blue eyes blink. "The air is wrong," it says. "The pressure drops. The wind comes fast." Ako-chan holds the rail tight. "What is it, Obia?" she asks. "A storm," says Obia. "A big one. It comes from the south." The sky turns from grey to black. Far off, the sea and the sky meet in dark. Rain starts to fall, cold and hard.

Then Ako-chan hears something. It comes from far away. It is low and deep. It is a whale's voice. Then another. Then more. Many whales call at once. The voices come together over the waves. They are deep and long. Ako-chan turns her head south. "They are calling," she says softly. Dango presses to her leg. Suke-san growls low at the dark sky. The voices fill the whole grey sky. They are sad and far. Ako-chan cannot see the whales. But she feels them out there. They call to the storm. Or they call to her.

Obia looks at the black clouds. "We cannot turn back," it says. "The storm is too close." Ako-chan looks at the rising waves. Her heart beats fast. The wind pulls at her hair. The whales call again, far in the dark. Something big is coming for them. And the sea knows it too.

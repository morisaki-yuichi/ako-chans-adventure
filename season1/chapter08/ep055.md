The ship sails south. Day after day, the sea changes. It is not blue now. It is grey. The water is grey and cold. Ako-chan stands at the side of the ship. She looks down at the waves. They are dark and slow and heavy. The wind is colder than before. It is not the right time for cold. But the cold is here.

She puts her hand on the handrail. The metal is very cold. It bites her skin. She pulls her hand back. Then she looks to the front of the ship. Suke-san stands there. He is very still. His nose is up in the wind. He reads the south air. He smells the cold. His ears point forward. Something is out there. Ako-chan watches him. She does not call to him.

Dango sits at her feet. He leans on her leg. He does not bark. Obia stands near her, quiet. Its blue eyes look south too. Below them, Daru and Gabu rest in their Pods. The red light and the blue light glow softly. The ship rocks slowly on the grey sea. The air smells of salt and ice. Ako-chan pulls her coat close. Her warm breath makes small clouds. The sea has no birds now. No fish jump in the waves. The water is empty for many miles.

Then Suke-san turns his head. He looks back at Ako-chan. Just once. His eyes are sharp and clear. He does not bark. He does not move. Then he looks south again. Ako-chan feels her heart go cold. The grey sea runs far and wide. Something waits down there. She knows it now. She looks at Suke-san once more. He feels the danger first.

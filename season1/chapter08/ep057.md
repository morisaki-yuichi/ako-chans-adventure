Then, all at once, the wind stops. The sea goes flat and still. The dark clouds hang low and wait. There is no sound now. No wind. No waves. The whales are quiet too. The air is heavy and cold. It is too quiet. Ako-chan stands on the deck and listens. Her ears find nothing at all. The world holds its breath. No bird flies over the still sea.

Obia comes to her side. Its blue eyes look at the sky. "It is close now," it says. Ako-chan looks at the still water. "How long?" she asks. Obia is quiet for a moment. "Two hours," it says. "Maybe less." The words hang in the cold air. Ako-chan holds her hands close to her chest. This calm is wrong. She knows this kind of wrong. It is the calm before the worst.

Dango stays at her feet. He does not move from her. He pushes hard into her leg. His body is warm. He looks up at her face. He does not bark. He does not play. He just stays close. Ako-chan kneels down beside him. She puts her arms around him. "I am here," she says softly. "Stay close to me."

The ship sits still on the flat sea. Everyone waits. Suke-san stands at her right, low and tense. Dalu and Gabu hide deep in their Pods. Obia stands at the rail and watches. No one speaks a word. The sea is a flat grey glass. The grey sky grows darker now. Two hours. Maybe less. Ako-chan looks to the south and waits. She holds Dango close. She does not look away. The storm is coming for them.

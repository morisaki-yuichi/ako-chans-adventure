The night is dark. The garden is black and still. Ako-chan stands at the back door. The door is open. Cold air comes in. Dango stands beside her. They look out at the dark garden. No birds sing. No insects call. The night is too quiet.

Then something moves by the hedge. It is low and quick. A red shape comes out of the dark. It is a fox. It stops on the wet grass. It looks at Ako-chan.

"Do you feel it?" says the fox. "It comes from the south. The night is not right."

Then a voice comes from the old oak. Ako-chan looks up. An owl sits on a high branch. Its round eyes are wide.

"South," says the owl. "All of us look south now."

Ako-chan holds her coat close. The cold air touches her face. It feels wrong, just like the hedgehog said. First the hedgehog. Now the fox and the owl. They all feel it. They all look south.

The garden is very still. No wind moves the trees. By the old shed, Obia stands in the dark. It does not move. Its pale eyes shine far away. Dango leans on her leg. He looks at the shed too. He does not bark.

The fox turns and goes. It runs into the dark. The owl opens its wings and flies. It is gone without a sound. Then the garden is still again. Ako-chan looks at the black sky. The south is far away and dark. Something is there. She can feel it. It pulls at her, soft and cold. Dango stays close. They do not go inside yet.

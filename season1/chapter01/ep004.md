Night came. The sky was dark and full of stars. Ako-chan could not sleep. She sat at her window. Below, the garden was still. Only the oak tree moved a little in the wind.

Dango lay on the floor beside her. He did not sleep either. He watched the garden.

Then something moved by the fence. A fox stepped out of the shadow. It walked slowly into the middle of the garden. It stopped. Its nose moved from side to side. Its tail was low.

The fox looked up at the window. Its eyes were bright in the dark.

"The smell is different now," the fox said. "It changed a few days ago. The night air is not the same. Something is in it. Before, it was not there. Now it is always there. It comes from far away. From the south."

"Do you know what it is?" Ako-chan asked softly.

"No," the fox said. "But it is not good."

Then she heard a sound. Slow and soft. Up in the oak tree. A barn owl landed on the highest branch. It sat very still. Its white face caught the light from the stars.

"Something started," the owl said. "In the south. I do not know what. But it started. And it is not stopping."

The fox turned. It walked back to the fence and was gone.

The owl looked at Ako-chan for a long moment. Then it opened its wings and flew. The garden was empty.

Dango stood up. He walked to the window. He put his front paws on the sill. He looked out. He did not bark. He did not move. He just watched the dark garden.

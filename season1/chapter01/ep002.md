The sky is gray now. The sun is low behind the trees. The garden is slowly waking. Ako-chan walks on the wet grass. Dango runs ahead of her. He is busy this morning. He sniffs every leaf and root. The cold air smells of earth.

Near the hedge, something moves. It is small and round. It is a hedgehog. It sits low by the roots. Its little nose points up at her.

"Cold," says the hedgehog. "The ground feels wrong."

Ako-chan kneels down on the grass. She can hear the small voice. She can always hear them. The animals. Their voices come to her, soft and clear.

"I cannot sleep," says the hedgehog. "It comes from far away. From the ground. From the south."

Dango pushes his nose into the hedge. He sniffs all around. His ears turn left and right. He can smell the hedgehog there, under the leaves. But he cannot hear the small voice. Only Ako-chan can. Dango looks up at her. He does not understand. He wants to know what she hears. He just smells the cold leaves and waits. His fluffy tail wags fast.

Ako-chan looks toward the south. The sky is pale and far. No birds sing yet over the garden. The hedgehog turns back to its roots. "Be careful," it says. Then it is quiet again.

Ako-chan stands up slowly. She holds her coat close. Dango comes to her side at last. She looks at the gray sky. The garden feels too quiet now. Something is wrong. She can feel it. The strange feeling comes from the south. And it stays with her.

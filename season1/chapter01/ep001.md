The garden was quiet. It was early morning. The sun was just above the old oak tree. The grass was wet and cold. A thin mist sat low near the fence.

Ako-chan came out of the back door. She had her red glasses on. She pulled her coat a little closer. The air was cool. Dango came out behind her. His big fluffy tail moved slowly from side to side. He put his nose to the grass.

She liked mornings like this. The garden felt alive. Not in a loud way. In a quiet way. She could hear the robin up in the oak tree. She could hear the wind in the leaves. She could hear the earth wake up.

Dango stopped. He lifted his head. He looked toward the far corner of the garden. He did not bark. He just looked.

Ako-chan looked too.

The corner was in shadow. The mist was thicker there. Something was in the shadow. Something small. Something very still.

And then it looked at her.

Two eyes. Bright in the shadow. Quiet and steady. They looked right at her red glasses.

Ako-chan did not move. She did not speak. Her heart beat a little faster. But she was not afraid. It was a strange feeling. Not new. More like something she always knew.

Dango made a soft sound. Not a bark. Just a small sound from his throat.

The mist moved. The shadow changed. The eyes were gone.

Ako-chan stood there for a long moment. The robin sang again up in the oak tree. The morning went on.

But she did not forget those eyes.

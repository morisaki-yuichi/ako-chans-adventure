The garden is dark. It is before the sun. The air is cold and still. Dew sits heavy on the grass. A thin mist rests by the fence. Ako-chan stands at the back door. She has her red glasses on. Dango sits close beside her. He is a little corgi puppy. His legs are short and his ears are big. His long fluffy tail is still. The morning is quiet, like always.

Then something feels different. The wind stops. The small sounds go away. Ako-chan does not know why. Her eyes move slowly to the far corner. The corner is dark with mist. Something is there. Something small. Something very still.

She looks harder. Then she sees them. Two eyes. Pale and blue. They shine in the shadow. They look right at her red glasses. Ako-chan holds her breath. She wants to see more. She leans a little closer. But in one blink, the eyes are gone.

The corner is just grass now. Just grass and mist. The old oak stands quiet. The hedge does not move. The birds do not sing yet. Ako-chan breathes out slowly. Her breath is white in the cold. She can smell the wet earth. A drop falls from a branch. It taps the grass, then stops. Soft and grey and still.

The garden is the same. But Ako-chan is not the same. She does not move. She just looks at the corner. She waits for the eyes to come back. They do not come back. The corner stays dark and empty. She does not know what she saw. Something small turns in her chest. Dango leans on her leg. He is quiet too. The sun is still not up.

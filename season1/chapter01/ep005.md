It is night now. Ako-chan sits at her small desk. A lamp is on. It is the only light. The room is warm and quiet. Outside, the garden is dark. Dango lies at her feet. He is quiet now. The house is still.

But Ako-chan is not still inside. The voices come back in her head. "The ground feels wrong," said the hedgehog. "It comes from the south," said the fox. "All of us look south now," said the owl. The voices come again and again. No one else can hear them. Only Ako-chan. She closes her eyes. Then she remembers Obia by the shed. She remembers Obia's words. "You will go. I will come with you."

On the desk is a new notebook. The cover is red. It is new and clean. No words are inside yet. The lamp shines on the red cover. Ako-chan looks at it, still and unsure. Dango lifts his head. He watches her. He does not understand. Then he rests his head down again. The room is very quiet. Only the lamp hums softly. Ako-chan picks up her pen. Her hand is small. The pen feels heavy in her hand.

She opens the red notebook. The first page is white and clean. She holds the pen above it. For a moment she does not move. Then she writes. Four small words. "I have to go." She looks at them for a long time. She reads them again, slowly. The words are small. But they are real now. Outside, the south is dark and far. She closes the notebook softly. But she knows. She has to go.

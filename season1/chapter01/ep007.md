It is before the sun. The garden is dark and gray. Dew sits heavy on the grass. A thin mist rests by the fence. The air is cold and still. It is just like the first morning. Ako-chan stands at the back door. She has her red glasses on. Her bag is on her back.

Obia comes from the shed. It walks to her slowly. It stops in front of her. Dango stands close at her side. For the first time, the three of them are together. Ako-chan looks at Obia. Obia looks at the garden gate.

"It is time," says Obia.

Ako-chan takes a deep breath. Her breath is white in the cold. She holds the strap of her bag. Then she takes the first step.

They walk across the wet grass. Dango walks beside her. Obia walks in front. The garden is quiet. No birds sing yet. The old oak stands dark and still. A drop falls from a branch. It taps the grass. Their steps are soft on the grass. Her breath floats white in front of her. The cold touches her face. The gate is open ahead of them. The path goes on to the south.

Ako-chan stops at the gate. She looks back one time. The garden is gray and quiet. It is her home. She loves this quiet place. But the south calls to her now. Then she looks ahead. The road is long. The way is far. But she is not afraid. She holds her bag close. She steps through the gate. Dango walks on. Obia walks on. Ako-chan follows them. They go together, toward the south.

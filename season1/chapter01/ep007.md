She woke before the alarm. The room was dark. She sat still for a moment. Then she got up. She dressed quietly. She put on her coat. She put on her red glasses. She picked up her bag. It felt light.

She went down the stairs. She opened the back door. The cold air came in. Outside, the sky was still dark. But in the east, a thin line of light was growing.

Dango was at the door. His tail moved slowly. His eyes were calm. She clipped his lead. He pressed his side against her leg.

Obia was at the far end of the garden. It was still and quiet in the dark. Ako-chan walked toward it. Its eyes turned on. Blue-white. Soft.

"Ready?" Obia said.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

The three of them walked to the gate. Ako-chan opened it. The road outside was empty. She could hear the town. A car far away. A door somewhere. A soft radio. The morning was starting.

She stopped. She looked back at the house. The dark windows. The old oak tree. The fence. The rosebush. All the same as always. It was a very ordinary house. A very ordinary garden.

The robin was up in the oak tree. Not singing. Just watching.

Ako-chan lifted her hand. "Goodbye," she said. Very soft.

The robin made one small sound. Then it was quiet.

She turned. The road went south. The wind touched her face. It was cold and quiet. Dango walked at her side. Obia walked behind them. Their feet made soft sounds on the road.

The sun was not up yet. But the sky was growing lighter. And the three of them walked on.

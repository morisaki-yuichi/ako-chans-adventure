It is the next morning. The garden is bright and cool. The dew is still on the grass. Ako-chan comes out with Dango. She looks towards the south again. She remembers the strange feeling.

Then she sees it. By the old shed, something stands. It is small. It is silver. It looks a little like a dog. But it is not a dog. Its body shines in the morning light. It wears a red collar.

Dango stops. He steps in front of Ako-chan. His tail is high. He does not bark. He just watches.

The silver thing looks at Ako-chan. Its eyes are pale and blue. Something about it feels strange. But she does not know why.

"You will go," it says. "I will come with you."

Ako-chan steps back. "Who are you?" she says.

"I am Obia," it says.

Ako-chan holds Dango close. Obia does not move. It does not come near. It does not go away. It just stands by the shed and waits.

"No," says Ako-chan. "I cannot go. I do not know you."

Obia says nothing. Its blue eyes stay on her. The garden is very quiet. A bird sings once, then stops. Dango keeps close in front of her. He watches the silver thing.

The morning light shines on Obia's silver body. It does not move at all. Not its head. Not its small feet. The air feels different near the shed. It is cooler there. Dango stays where he is. He is still too.

Ako-chan turns and goes back inside. But at the door, she stops. She looks back one time. Obia is still there. Silver and quiet by the shed.

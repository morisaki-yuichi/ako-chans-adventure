The morning was cold. Ako-chan sat at the window. She did not go to the garden. But she heard the robin outside on the fence. She went out. Dango came with her. The garden was cold and gray.

The robin looked at her. "Strange news," it said. "It comes from the south. The birds there do not fly the same way. They do not sound the same." Then a swallow flew in. It stopped on the oak tree. "The south wind," it said. "Before, it came every morning. Now it is dead." Dango sat very still. He did not bark.

Then something moved by the old shed. A shape stepped out into the garden. It looked like a small dog. But it was not a dog. It was smooth and silver. Its body was small. Its eyes were bright lights. They looked at Ako-chan. "Hello," it said. "My name is Obia."

Ako-chan stood still. Dango looked at Obia. He made a small sound. He did not run. "I listened to the animals," Obia said. "Something is wrong in the south. It is very bad." It was quiet for a moment. "I want to go south," it said. "I cannot go alone. I need help." Ako-chan looked at the swallow. She looked at the robin. She looked at Dango. "No," she said quietly. "I cannot go. I do not know you."

Obia was quiet. Its eyes did not change. Dango looked at Ako-chan. Then he looked at Obia. He put his nose to the ground. He did not bark. He just sat there in the cold. The swallow was gone. The sky was gray and still.

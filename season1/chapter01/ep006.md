It is morning. The day is bright and cool. Ako-chan comes out into the garden. She looks at the old shed. Obia is still there. It stands in the same place. It did not move all night. Its silver body shines in the sun.

Ako-chan walks across the wet grass. Dango walks close beside her. She stops in front of Obia. Her heart beats fast. But she is sure now.

"I am coming with you," she says. "I am going to the south."

Obia's blue eyes look at her. It is quiet for a moment.

"I thought you might come," says Obia.

Ako-chan takes a breath. "We leave tomorrow," she says.

Obia does not move. But its eyes shine a little.

The morning is bright now. The birds sing in the trees. A soft wind moves the green leaves. Ako-chan feels something new. Her fear grows small. Her heart grows sure.

Ako-chan goes back inside. She finds her bag. She packs warm clothes for the cold. She packs gloves and a warm hat. She packs the red notebook too. Then she stops. Dango watches her from the floor. His ears are up. So she packs his favorite toy. Dango wags his tail fast. He knows now. He is coming too. He runs around her feet.

Ako-chan closes the bag. It is ready by the door. She looks out the window. Obia is still by the shed. The garden is bright and green. But her mind is far away. She thinks of the south. Tomorrow they go. Her bag is full now. She is ready. Dango sits beside the bag. He does not leave it.

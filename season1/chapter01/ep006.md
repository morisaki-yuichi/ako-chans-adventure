After school, she went straight to her room. She put her bag on the bed. She opened it. It was not big. But she thought carefully about each thing.

A warm jumper. A small torch. The red notebook. Her pencil case. A few snacks. Her water bottle. That was all. She could not bring more. And she did not need more.

She went downstairs and out to the garden. Obia was near the old shed. It was waiting. Dango was already there. He sat beside Obia. Not close. But not far.

"You came back," Ako-chan said.

"I waited," Obia said. "I thought you might come."

She sat down on the garden step. "Tell me what you know," she said.

Obia turned its head. "The animals send messages. They all come from the south. They go north. East. West. All the messages say the same thing. Something is wrong in the south. It was wrong from the start. Now it is getting worse. Very fast."

"Can we stop it?" she asked.

"I do not know," Obia said. "But we have to go and see."

She looked at Obia for a long time. Its eyes were steady and calm. Then she looked at Dango. He looked back at her. His ears were forward. He was ready.

She stood up. "We leave tomorrow," she said. "Early. Before the light."

"I will be here," Obia said.

She went back inside. She sat by the window. She looked at the garden. The old oak tree. The rosebush. The cold fence. It was an ordinary garden. She picked up the red notebook. She held her pen. But she did not write.

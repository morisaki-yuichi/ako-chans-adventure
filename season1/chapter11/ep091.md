The North Pacific. Day eighty.

The water was dark and cold. Not the cold of Antarctica — a different cold. It moved and breathed.

Riro stood at the front of the ship and did not move.

"This is the water," he said quietly. "This is it."

Ako-chan looked out. Dark blue, far and wide. "How do you know?"

"I know this water. I know the way it moves." He touched his fur — forehead, chest, both sides. "This is right."

Obia checked its sensors. "The current matches. This is the right place."

One by one the others came to the rail. Dango. Suke-san. Daru pressed his face to the pod window. Gabu watched from inside his pod.

Riro turned to Ako-chan.

"My fur is good now," he said.

She smiled. "Yes. It is very good."

"Will you do it one more time? Please."

She knelt. She ran her fingers over his back — slow and careful. Riro's eyes went half-closed.

Dango sat right beside Ako-chan. He watched. He made no sound.

When she stopped, Riro stood straight. He looked at each of them. Dango. Suke-san. The pods. Obia. Then back to Ako-chan.

"Good water," he said. "Good food. Good fur." He paused. "Good friends."

He went to the rail. He climbed up. He looked back once.

Then he dropped into the sea.

The water closed over him.

For a moment, nothing.

Then a small dark shape came up ten meters out. Riro looked back. His fur was flat and wet. His eyes were bright.

Then he turned. He went under.

He did not come back up.

Dango put his chin on the rail. He looked at the place where Riro went in. He stayed like that for a long time.

Japan. Day eighty-three.

The hills were dark green. The air smelled of pine and wet earth.

Ako-chan knew this smell. She first smelled it when they came to Japan, weeks and weeks ago. Suke-san's country.

Suke-san stood at the front of the ship. His nose was up. His ears were forward. He did not move.

Dango came and stood beside him. The two dogs looked at the green hills together.

Neither spoke.

Then Suke-san turned to Dango.

"You are not bad," Suke-san said. "For a soft dog."

Dango's tail moved once. "You are not bad. For a hard one."

Suke-san made a sound that was almost not a sound. He looked away.

He went to Ako-chan and sat down in front of her. She knelt. His eyes were steady on her face.

"You heard the animals," he said. "All the way. You did not stop."

"No," she said. "I did not stop."

"The south is still there. The root of it."

"I know."

"You will keep listening."

"Yes," she said. "I will."

He held her eyes for a long moment. Then he stood. He walked to Obia and stopped.

"Watch over her," he said. "That is all."

"Yes," said Obia.

Suke-san went down the ramp. He stepped onto the dock. He walked toward the green trees.

He did not look back.

Dango watched from the rail. He watched until Suke-san went into the trees and was gone.

Then Dango sat down. He looked at the place where Suke-san had been.

Ako-chan put her hand on Dango's back. She kept it there.

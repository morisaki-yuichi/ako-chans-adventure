The Dalmatian coast. Day ninety-five.

White rocks. Clear water. The smell of the sea.

They were still two hundred meters from shore when the sound came from Daru's pod.

"Open it," he said from inside. "Open it now. I can smell it."

Obia opened the pod. The warm salt air came in.

Daru stepped out, all four feet on the deck, and stood very still. One second. Two. His head moved left, then right. His nose lifted to the air.

"This is Croatia," he said. "This is my coast."

He sounded very sure. Like there was no other coast in the world.

Obia brought the ship in close to a rocky inlet. Clear pale water below. White limestone above. The kind of stone Daru had spent his whole life walking on.

Daru ran to the rail. He looked down at the rocks. He looked up at the sky.

"I know that rock," he said. "That one there. I know it."

He looked at Gabu's pod. The orange light was on inside.

"Gabu. Do you see? White rock. Very bright."

A moment. Then from inside the pod, quietly: "I see."

Dango came to the rail and stood beside Daru. He looked at the rock too. He said nothing. His tail moved once.

Daru turned and ran to Ako-chan's feet. He ran back to the rail. He ran to her feet again. He could not stop moving.

She watched him run. She laughed. She could not help it.

He stopped and looked up at her.

She knelt. She held out her hands.

She did not say anything. There was nothing to say. He was home. And he knew it before any of them had even seen the shore.

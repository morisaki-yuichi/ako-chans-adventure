Five days after Wadi Rum.

Just three of them now.

Ako-chan. Dango. Obia.

The ship was very quiet. No pods. No capsule. No green light. No orange light. Just the grey water and the wide sky and the three of them.

Dango stayed at Ako-chan's side all day. He did not pace. He just sat.

In the evening, Ako-chan sat at the front of the ship. The water was grey-blue. The sun was going down behind them.

Obia came and stood beside her.

"How long?" Ako-chan said.

"Four days. Maybe five."

Ako-chan nodded. She looked at the water.

"Obia," she said.

"Yes."

"The pull from the south. Is it smaller now?"

Obia was quiet for a moment. "No," it said. "Not smaller. But different."

"Different how?"

"I do not know yet. I am still looking."

Ako-chan looked at the sun going down. Red and orange behind them. West. England was west.

"Riro is in the water," she said. "Suke-san is in the forest. Daru is on his coast. Gabu is in the desert."

She stopped.

"They are all where they should be," Obia said.

"Yes." Ako-chan was quiet for a moment. "Are we?"

Dango pushed his head against her knee. She put her hand on him.

The sun went under. The sky turned dark blue. The first small star came out.

Obia's eyes were blue-white in the dark.

"We will be," it said. "Soon."

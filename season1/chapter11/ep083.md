The day is over now. Outside, the dark comes down. The snow turns gray, then blue, then black. Inside the ship, one small lamp glows. It is warm there. Everyone sits close together. Obia sits among them. It is quiet. It does not speak yet. Ako-chan waits beside it.

Then Obia looks up at Ako-chan. It picks its words slowly. "I watch you," it says. "All of you." Its voice is low and soft. "On the train. On the ship. Here." It stops. "I send data. I do not know where it goes." Ako-chan holds her breath. She thinks of the nights. The blue light. The low hum. The dark window, far away. Each strange night comes back to her. She asked it then. It never spoke. Now she understands. "I think my sending caused this," says Obia. It looks at them all. "The storm. The lost voices. All the wrong things."

The room is very still. Dango leans on Ako-chan's leg. Suke-san sits close at her right. The Colonel watches Obia, quiet now. Pen-san does not make a sound. Daru and Gabu stay inside their Pods. Even Daru does not speak. The lamp light moves on the silver face. Outside, the wind is soft and low.

Ako-chan looks at Obia for a moment. She is not angry. Her heart is full and sad. "You came back," she says softly. "That is what matters now." Obia looks down at the floor. Its blue eyes are dim and low. It does not say a thing. But it does not look away. Ako-chan reaches out. She touches the cold silver hand. Obia is here. That is what matters tonight.

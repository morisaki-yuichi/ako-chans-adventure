Evening. The house was quiet.

Ako-chan sat at the table by the window. The light was on. Outside: the oak tree, the dark shapes of the garden.

Dango was asleep at her feet.

Obia sat in the corner of the room. Its eyes were blue-white and very soft in the dark.

Ako-chan took out the red notebook.

She opened it to the first page. *I have to go.* Four words.

She turned the pages slowly. Italy. Morning. Mountains and red roofs. The voices in Croatia. Wadi Rum. The desert. Japan. The whales. The ice. The storm. The aurora. The blizzard. Suke-san walking to Obia in the dark. And then, the last line:

*Day seventy-two. Antarctica. The blizzard is gone. We are safe. But the pull from the south is still here. The root of it is still under the ice.*

She sat with that for a long time.

Then she took the pen.

She wrote:

*Day one hundred and seven. England. We are home. Riro is in the sea. Suke-san is in the forest. Daru is on his coast. Gabu is in the desert. Pen-san and the Colonel are on the ice.*

She stopped. She looked at what she wrote.

Then she wrote more:

*The root of it is still there. Under the ice. We have not reached it yet. But we went all the way. And we all came back.*

She looked at Obia in the corner.

Obia looked back. Blue-white. Steady.

She wrote one more line.

*We are not finished.*

She closed the notebook.

Outside, the robin made one small sound in the oak tree.

Then quiet.

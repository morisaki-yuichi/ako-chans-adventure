The morning was white and very still.

Ako-chan stood at the bottom of the ramp. The sky was pale. The ice went out very far in every direction.

Pen-san pressed against her legs and did not let go.

"You cannot go," he said. "Not yet."

"We have a long way to go back," Ako-chan said. "We have to start."

"But the storm is over. And the ice is flat. And you are here."

Ako-chan knelt down. She put both arms around him. He was very round and very warm.

"We will come back," she said. "One day."

"Promise?"

"Yes. I promise."

She held him a little longer. Then she stood.

Dango sat beside her. He did not look at Pen-san. But his ears were back.

Colonel Adelie stood nearby and said nothing. He watched the pods go up — Daru's green light, Gabu's orange. Riro's capsule went up last. Suke-san walked up the ramp and did not look back.

Then the Colonel walked to Obia. He stopped two steps away.

"You carried the data from England to here," he said. "Every reading. Every day."

Obia's eyes were still. "Yes."

"You did not hide it. You just worked." A pause. "That is enough."

He turned and walked back.

Ako-chan went up the ramp. Dango stayed close. The engine started, low and steady.

She stood at the rail and looked back.

Pen-san had both small wings raised. Colonel Adelie stood beside him, straight and still. The two shapes were already small against the white.

The ship moved north.

The ice fell away. The two shapes grew smaller. Then the white was just white, and there was only sea.

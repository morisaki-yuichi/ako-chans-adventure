The next morning is bright and cold. The sun is low on the white ice. Ako-chan steps out of the ship. The air is clear and very still. She stands quiet and listens hard. Far off, the sea birds call.

Something is different this morning. The voices are still there, all around. But they are softer now. Not so afraid. Not so wrong. Yesterday they all pulled to the south. Today they feel more calm. A seal calls out near the ice. Its voice is slow and easy. The sea birds turn high in the sky. They do not cry so loud now. The sharp, scared sound is small. It is only a little change. But Ako-chan hears it well. The animals are not so lost today.

Pen-san runs on the ice and plays. He chases his own feet and falls. Dalu rolls fast in his red Pod. Gabu moves slowly behind him. Dango sniffs the cold air. His ears are soft, not tight. Suke-san stands at her right and lifts his nose. A small bird lands near the ship. It does not look afraid of them. Even the wind feels less wild today. The white land is quiet and wide.

Ako-chan does not say a word. She holds the feeling deep inside. She thinks of Obia. Last night it said, "I will stop." And now the world feels a little soft. She looks at Obia by the ship. Obia looks back at her. Maybe it feels the change too. The voices are soft now. And the cold bright morning is calm. She keeps this small hope inside her heart.

Three days north of Antarctica.

The ice was gone. The water was dark and moving. The sky was grey and wide.

The ship was quiet. It was a different kind of quiet than before. Pen-san was not there. No one said this. No one talked about it. But the ship felt it.

Dango stayed close to Ako-chan all morning. He did not walk the deck. He sat near her, his side against her leg.

Riro came out of his capsule and went to the front rail. He watched the water for a long time. He touched his fur once. Then he put his paws back on the rail.

Ako-chan stood beside him.

"The water looks different," Riro said.

"We are going north," Ako-chan said. "It is warmer here."

"Yes." He looked out at the grey. "Where you found me — it was north of here. Far north."

"Is that where you want to go back?"

Riro was quiet for a moment. "I do not know if it is my home," he said. "But the food is good there. And the water is right."

Suke-san passed them on his loop of the deck. His eyes moved to Riro for one moment. Then he looked forward and walked on.

From his pod, Gabu said: "The water is getting warmer. I can feel it through the walls."

Nobody answered.

The pods glowed — green and orange — in the grey light.

The ship went north. The grey water opened ahead.

Ako-chan thought about Pen-san on the ice. His wings raised. Getting smaller.

She took out the red notebook. She opened it. She looked at the last words inside.

Then she closed it again.

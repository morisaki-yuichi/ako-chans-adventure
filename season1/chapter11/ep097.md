Then Gabu turned to Ako-chan.

She came to stand beside him. Not too close. She had learned that with Gabu. He always needed a little space.

He looked at the sand for a moment.

"The lizards will come back," he said. "When the south is quiet. They will come back."

"Yes," she said. "I think they will."

"I will count the days." He pressed one foot into the sand again, slowly. "Until they come back. I will count."

"You will know the full pattern," she said.

Gabu looked at her. "Yes."

He held her eyes for a long time. She did not look away. He did not either.

Then he turned toward the ship.

Obia was at the top of the ramp. Still. Steady.

Gabu looked at Obia for a long time. Slow and careful. Like he needed to see every part of it again.

Then he gave one slow nod.

Obia's eyes were steady. It did not speak.

Gabu turned away.

He walked toward the nearest red wall. Slow. Each step the same. No hurry. No looking back.

He walked in the shadow of the wall. He went around the rock.

He was gone.

Ako-chan stood in the hot sand. Red walls on every side. Blue sky above. The pull from the south was still there — small and far away.

Dango came down the ramp and stood beside her. He looked at the place where Gabu had gone.

She put her hand on Dango's back. He pressed against her leg.

Neither of them moved for a long time.

She let out a long breath.

Above them, the red walls were very still. The sky was very blue.

She thought: Daru is on his coast. Gabu is in his desert.

That was right. That was exactly right.

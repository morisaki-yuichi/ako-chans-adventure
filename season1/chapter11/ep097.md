Wadi Rum. Day ninety-eight.

Red walls. Pale sand. The air dry and very still.

Ako-chan had not been warm like this in a long time.

When the ramp went down, Obia opened Gabu's pod. Gabu stepped out.

He walked to the edge of the ramp and stopped. He looked at the red rock. He looked at the pale sand. He looked at the sky above the walls — very blue, very deep.

He did not speak.

"The sensors say this is the right place," Obia said.

"I know," said Gabu. "I know this place."

He walked down the ramp. He walked to the sand and stopped. He pressed one foot down. Then the other.

"Warm," he said. "The right kind of warm."

He turned to Ako-chan. She had come down with him.

"The lizards will come back," he said. "When the south is quiet. They will come back."

"Yes," Ako-chan said. "I think they will."

"I will count the days." He looked at the sand. "Until they come back. I will count."

"You will know the full pattern," Ako-chan said.

Gabu looked at her. "Yes."

He held her eyes for a long moment. Then he turned to Obia.

He did not speak. He looked at Obia for a long time. Then he gave one slow nod.

Obia's eyes were steady.

Gabu turned. He walked toward the nearest red wall. Slow. No hurry. No looking back.

He went around the rock.

He was gone.

Ako-chan stood in the hot sand. Red walls. Blue sky. The pull from the south was still there — small and far away.

She let out a long breath.

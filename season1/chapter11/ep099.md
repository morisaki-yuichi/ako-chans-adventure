Day one hundred and seven. Early morning.

They came into the harbor in grey light. Grey water. Grey sky. Cold and familiar.

Dango knew first.

He was up before Ako-chan. His nose was on the rail. His tail moved — not a big wag, a small one, very certain.

When Ako-chan came on deck, she knew too.

The smell. Cold sea. Earth. Grass. Something green.

England.

They came off the ship. A road. Then another road. Ako-chan knew this one.

Obia walked beside her. Dango went ahead, then came back, then went ahead again.

The town was quiet. Early morning. A few birds.

Then Ako-chan saw it.

The oak tree. The fence. The rosebush. The gate.

She stopped.

It was all the same. The garden wall. The back path. The same grey sky above the same oak tree.

The robin was in the tree. Not singing. Just sitting.

Just like the morning they left.

Ako-chan opened the gate. She walked in.

Dango ran past her, nose to the ground. He checked the hedge. He checked the rosebush. He came back to her feet.

Something moved near the hedge. Small. Very slow.

The hedgehog.

Ako-chan knelt down.

"You came back," said the hedgehog.

"Yes," said Ako-chan. "I came back."

The hedgehog was quiet for a moment. "The ground still feels wrong," it said. "But less. Not as loud."

"I know," said Ako-chan.

The hedgehog looked at her. Then it went back under the hedge.

Obia came in last. It stood in the garden and looked around. Its eyes were quiet.

Ako-chan looked at the oak tree. The robin looked at her.

She was home.

Obia was still there beside her.

That was something she had not thought about before she left. Now it was just true.

Wadi Rum. Day ninety-eight.

Obia took a different way south.

Red walls. Pale sand. The air dry and very still. No wind. No sound. Just the color of the rock.

Ako-chan had not been warm like this in a long time.

When the ramp went down, Obia opened Gabu's pod. Gabu stepped out.

He walked to the edge of the ramp and stopped.

He did not look at anyone. He looked at the red rock. He looked at the pale sand. He looked at the deep blue sky above the walls.

He was very still.

"The sensors say this is the right place," said Obia.

Gabu did not answer right away. He studied the rock above the ramp. He studied the line where the wall met the sand.

"I know," he said at last. "I know this place."

He walked down the ramp slowly. Each step careful. Not afraid — just paying attention.

He reached the sand and stopped. He pressed his front right foot down. Then the left. Then he pressed all four feet into the warm sand.

"Warm," he said. "The right kind of warm."

Ako-chan stepped down after him. She stood a little back, not too close.

Dango was still on the ramp behind her. He looked at the sand, then at the sky, then at Gabu. He did not move.

Gabu stood in the middle of the sand. Around him: red walls going up on every side. Deep shadows under the rock. Bright light above.

He breathed in.

She watched him breathe the desert in. He was not in a hurry. He never was.

The pull from the south was still there. Small now. Far away. But still there.

The Dalmatian coast. Day ninety-five.

White rocks. Clear water. The smell of the sea.

Daru heard it first — from inside his pod, still sealed.

"Open it," he said. "Open it now. I can smell it."

Obia opened the pod. The warm air came in. Daru stepped out, all four feet on the deck, and stopped.

He smelled the air. He turned left. He turned right.

"This is Croatia," he said. "This is my coast."

Obia brought the ship close to a rocky inlet. Clear pale water. White stone above.

Daru ran to the rail. He looked down at the rock. He looked up at the sky.

"I know that rock," he said. "That one there. I know it."

He looked at Gabu's pod. "Gabu. Do you see? White rock. Very bright."

From inside the pod, Gabu said: "I see."

Daru turned to Ako-chan. He ran to her feet and ran back again. He ran to the rail and looked down.

Ako-chan laughed. She knelt and held out her hands. Daru ran into them. He pressed his whole small body against her palms. She held him for a moment. He was warm and very alive.

"You are back," she said.

"Yes," said Daru. "I am back."

He looked at Dango. He looked at Gabu's pod one more time.

Then he turned to the rail and went over.

He landed on the rock below and ran left. He ran right. He found a crack in the stone and went in. He came back out.

He went up to a higher rock. He turned and looked at the ship.

He was still for one moment. His head high.

Then he turned. And he was gone.

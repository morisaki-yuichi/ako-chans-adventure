## Episode-by-Episode Notes

**Minor issue — word count:** 276 words. This is within the lower boundary (270) but close to it. No action required.

### Ep36 — "North" (story_day: 35)

## Overall Issues

### Story Development

**Arc summary (Ep31–60):**
- Ch16 (Ep31–39): Reunion arc — Japan (Suke-san) and North Pacific (Riro). Well-paced at roughly 2–3 episodes per reunion. The group gels naturally.
- Ch17 (Ep40–50): Antarctica approach and Pen-san/General Adelie reunion. Day 39 uses 4 episodes (41–43 = approach and landing, 44 = first step), which is appropriate pacing for the climactic arrival. The reunion with the penguins in Ep48–49 is emotionally effective.
- Ch18 (Ep51–60): Discovery of the time machine and the mission reveal. 10 episodes to cover: approach to the machine (51–54), Obia touching and unlocking (55–56), memory revelation (57), mission explanation (58), decision (59), and entering (60). This is careful, deliberate pacing. Each episode has a clear single beat.

**Day 40 covers Ep44–60 (17 episodes).** This is a very long single "day" in the story world. The narrative justification is that Ep44–60 is essentially all happening within one continuous sequence: they land in the morning (Ep44), walk to the cave (Ep45–47), meet Pen-san and General Adelie (Ep48–50), walk deeper to the machine (Ep51–54), and Obia unlocks it over a period of perhaps an hour or two (Ep55–59), then they enter (Ep60). This is a single "day" in story time covering a few hours of intense activity. The framing is consistent and justified — this is the climactic sequence of Part 3.

**However:** the episode text never makes explicit that all of Ep44–60 happens within a few hours. A reader who checked story_day values between chapters would see Day 40 spanning 17 episodes, which could be confusing. None of the episodes reference the time of day after Ep44's "By morning" opener, except that Ep44 takes place in the morning. There is no way for a reader to confirm they understand all of Ep44–60 as a single continuous sequence without external reference.

**Recommendation:** Consider adding a brief time anchor in one of the later Day 40 episodes (perhaps Ep47 or Ep50) noting something like "The cave was cold — not yet midday, she thought" or similar, to confirm the continuity.

**Story flow strength:** The overall flow from Ep31 to Ep60 is well-constructed. The Suke-san reunion (Ep33–34) is efficient and emotionally satisfying. The Riro reunion (Ep37) is particularly strong. The Pen-san reunion (Ep48) is the emotional peak of the section. The machine discovery (Ep52–60) is deliberately paced and appropriate. No significant story flow problems.

### Text Naturalness

**Padding:** No significant padding detected. Word counts are generally tight. Ep37 ("The Water") at 344 words is the only over-limit episode and the excess is modest.

**Abrupt cuts / missing transitions:**
- The gap between Ep47 (everyone stopped, nobody moving) and Ep48 (opening line: "They went deeper") is the clearest transition issue. The reader must infer that the group resumed walking — there is no signal word. A single phrase at the start of Ep48 like "After a long moment, they moved on" would bridge this cleanly.

**Prose quality is consistently high across the range.** The short-sentence rhythm is maintained throughout. The character voices are distinct and true to their guide-defined patterns. No passages feel like obvious word-count padding.

**One slightly awkward construction:**
- Ep53, line 26–27: "Something this shape did not belong here." The phrase "Something this shape" is grammatically unusual. It reads as a fragment rearrangement of "Something of this shape did not belong here." Consider: "A shape like this did not belong here." or "This shape did not belong here."

**Repetition of "You came back":** This is used as the reunion line for Suke-san (Ep33), Riro (Ep37), and Pen-san (Ep48). It is also used by General Adelie in Ep49. Four characters using the same reunion opener is intentional thematically (the promise structure of the series), but it may feel repetitive to a reader experiencing the episodes sequentially. This is a design-level decision rather than an error, but it bears noting.

Paris was big and fast and loud. The train came in under a great roof. She heard French words all around her. She did not know French. But the sounds were warm. Round and fast. She listened as she walked.

Gare du Nord was full of people. All going somewhere. All in a hurry. Bags and coats and voices. The boards showed many trains. Many cities. Many times.

Ako-chan stopped. She looked at the board. Venice. Train at 18:14. Track eight. She checked her watch. She had two hours.

She found a food stand near the wall. She bought a sandwich and a small bottle of water. The bread was soft and warm. She sat on a bench and ate. Dango sat at her feet. She gave him a small piece of bread. He ate it fast and looked up for more.

Obia stood beside the bench. Its eyes moved around the station. Watching. Listening. It did a lot of that.

"Are the voices stronger here?" Ako-chan asked.

"A little," said Obia. "The animal messages pass through here. Birds from the south. Their sounds carry through."

Ako-chan listened. She heard only trains and people and the station. But maybe somewhere outside, a bird was carrying a message south. Maybe many birds were.

She finished her sandwich. She put the bottle in her bag. She stood up.

Track eight. The train to Venice. It was long and dark red. She never saw a train that color before. A different train for a different world.

She walked to the platform. Dango at her side. Obia behind her.

One step closer to the south.

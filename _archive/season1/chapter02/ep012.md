She woke when the light changed.

The window was grey at first. Then pink. Then pale cold white. She sat up. She looked out.

Mountains.

They were everywhere. Big and white. Snow on every top. The peaks went up into a pale sky. She never saw mountains like this. She pressed her face to the glass.

Dango woke up too. He sat up fast. He looked at the mountains. He made a low sound. Not a bark. Just a sound of surprise.

"The Alps," said Obia. "We are crossing into Italy."

Italy. The word felt new in her mind. Bright and clean.

The train went into a tunnel. Dark again. Short this time. Then out. And the mountains were different now. Not white. Grey and brown and green. Lower. The snow stayed only on the very tops. Below the snow, green came down the sides.

And below the green, red roofs. Small towns. White walls. A church tower in the morning light.

"This is Italy?" said Ako-chan.

"Yes," said Obia.

She looked at the red roofs. They were a different red from home. Warmer. More orange. She liked them.

Dango had his nose on the glass again. He was watching a bird move across the sky. A large bird. Dark and slow. It turned. It went south.

Ako-chan watched the bird until it was gone.

She took out her red notebook. She opened it. She wrote one line: Italy. Morning. Mountains and red roofs.

She closed it and looked out the window.

The train ran south. The mountains stayed with it for a long time. She did not have words for them.

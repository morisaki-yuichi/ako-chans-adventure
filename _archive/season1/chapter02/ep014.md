She almost missed it.

She was writing in her notebook. Just a few words. She was not looking out the window. Then Dango made a sound. Low and excited. She looked up.

Blue.

So much blue. Between the buildings, between the trees, between everything — water. Flat and bright and still. The light was on it. It broke into a thousand small pieces.

The Adriatic Sea.

She pressed to the window. She pushed her face close to the glass. The train was running along the coast now. The water was right there. Close. She could almost touch it.

Obia looked at the water. It was quiet for a long time. Then it said: "We are close to Croatia."

Croatia. That was the next part of the journey. Over the water. Into the hills.

But first, this. Just this. The sea in the afternoon light.

Dango put his front paws on the window ledge. His tail moved very fast. He never saw the sea before. She could tell. He was very still. But his eyes were very bright.

A bird flew over the water. Then another. They were white. She could not hear them through the glass. But she felt them.

Something was wrong.

At home, the hedgehog, the fox, the owl — their fear was quiet. Low and slow. But these birds felt different. Louder. More urgent. Like something was pushing them.

She pressed her hand to the glass.

We are getting closer, she thought. The south is getting closer. She was sure of it.

The train moved on. The sea fell behind. But the feeling did not go away.

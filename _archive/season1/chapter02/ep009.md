England was outside the window. Green fields. Small roads. Old stone walls. The train moved very fast. Everything went past before she could look at it properly.

Dango pressed his nose against the glass. He left a small wet mark. He watched the fields.

Then the light changed.

A hill appeared ahead. Dark and round. A great opening in it. Round like a mouth. The train ran straight toward it. Ako-chan held her breath.

Then they were in it.

Dark. Complete dark. The window was black. The sound changed. Deeper. Closer. The walls were very near. She could feel it. The tunnel pressed tight around the train. The sound filled everything. She held very still.

Dango pulled his nose back from the glass. He looked at Ako-chan. She reached down and put her hand on his back. He was warm. She kept her hand there.

"The sea is above us now," said Obia. Its voice was quiet.

Ako-chan looked up at the ceiling of the train. It looked like any ceiling. White and plain. But above it was rock. And above the rock was the sea.

All that water, she thought. All those fish. All that cold dark.

She thought about the animals back home. The hedgehog. The robin. The fox. Were they still there? Still listening to the sound from the south?

Then, without warning, the dark ended.

White light came in. Green fields. A flat open sky. The world was the same. But different. A different kind of green. A different kind of light.

Dango barked once. Short and happy. His tail moved fast.

Ako-chan let out a long breath.

France.

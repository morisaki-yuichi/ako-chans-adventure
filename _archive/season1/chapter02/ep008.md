They reached London by mid-morning. The streets were very loud. The tube was louder. But now they were at St Pancras. It was the loudest place of all.

The station had a great iron roof. It curved high over everything. Light came down through the glass. Voices and footsteps filled the air. Long trains waited on the platforms below.

Ako-chan looked around. She never saw a place so big. She pulled her bag higher and walked in. Dango was at her side. He kept close. He looked at everything. His big tail did not stop moving. Obia walked behind them. Small and silver. No one stopped them. No one looked twice.

She found the big board. Letters. Times. Names of cities. She looked for one word. Paris. There it was. Platform fourteen. Twenty minutes.

The Eurostar was long and white. It ran from one end of the platform to the other. Ako-chan walked beside it. She felt very small next to it.

"This train goes under the sea," said Obia.

Ako-chan stopped. "Under?"

"Under the sea. A tunnel. Very deep. The water is above your head."

She looked at the train. It looked like any train. White and clean and plain. She tried to picture water above her head. Dark water. She could not quite do it.

She got on. She found a window seat. Dango sat at her feet and looked up at her. His tail moved once. Then he was still.

The doors closed. The station moved away. England filled the window. Grey rooftops. Green hills. It all went past, fast and quiet.

Ako-chan put her hand on the glass.

South, she thought. We are going south.

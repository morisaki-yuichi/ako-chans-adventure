The biggest wave came at night.

Ako-chan saw it first. A dark wall of water from the east. Taller than the ship. Much taller. Big enough to push the ship over.

"Obia," she said.

"I see it," said Obia. "I cannot turn in time."

There was no direction to run. No time. The wave was too close and too fast.

Then the whales moved.

All three of them. They were far ahead one moment. Then they came around in a wide arc — fast, much faster than Ako-chan ever saw them move. They put themselves between the ship and the wave.

The wave hit.

It hit them.

Three massive bodies in the water. The wave broke against them and around them. Some water still came — cold and very hard, across the deck. The ship shook. But not the full wave. Not the wall of it.

The ship steadied. The deck was wet. Ako-chan held on.

Then it was past.

The whales came up on the other side. Still there. Still whole. One came very close to the ship — very close. Ako-chan could see the white patches on its dark skin. Rough, like small rocks fixed to its body. The same patches she had seen back in the north.

It looked at her.

She did not move for a long time.

She put her hand out over the rail. Not far enough to touch. But out. Toward it.

The whale was still for a moment. Then it went slowly back under the water and moved ahead.

"Thank you," said Ako-chan.

Very small. Very quiet.

The storm was not over. But the whales were still there.

Two days later, the sea changed color.

It was no longer dark blue. It was darker — heavy, almost black at the edges. The sky stayed the same pale grey. But the sea felt different. Heavier. Slower.

Ako-chan noticed it first from the rail. The whales were still ahead. But they were moving slower too.

"Obia," she said. "Something is happening."

Obia checked the sensors. "The water temperature is dropping fast. The current here is different from two days ago. Changing direction."

"Changing toward what?"

"Against us. It is pushing back."

From his pod, Gabu spoke quietly. "Something large is under us. I can feel it through the hull. Moving slowly. Very far down."

Ako-chan put her hand on the rail. She felt nothing through the metal. But she felt it another way — something slow and heavy and very old. Moving below them in the dark water.

The whales felt it too. That was why they were slowing down.

Suke-san walked the deck in his usual loops. East side, back. West side, back. He did it every morning.

Then he stopped.

He turned to face south. His nose went up. He turned very slowly, a few degrees into the wind, and held still.

He stayed like that for a long time.

From his pod, Daru: "What is Suke-san doing?"

Gabu: "Smelling."

"Smelling what?"

Gabu paused. "Something in the air," he said. "Not here yet. But coming."

Ako-chan watched Suke-san. He did not move. His ears were forward. His tail did not move.

The deck was quiet. Everyone else was inside.

The sea stayed dark. The whales stayed slow.

Something was coming.

Suke-san did not move from the front of the ship.

All morning, he stood there. Facing south. Ears forward. Body still. He had not done his loops around the deck. He just stood and looked.

Dango came up beside him. He stood there too, looking south. A low sound started in his throat — not a bark. Just a sound that meant something.

"What do you feel?" Ako-chan asked.

"Nothing yet," said Dango. "But it is there. In the south. It is coming."

Riro came on deck and looked. He looked at Suke-san. He looked at Dango. He looked at the sky. Then he went back inside without a word. Ako-chan did not call after him.

Ako-chan looked at the horizon.

At the south edge of the sky, there was a line. Dark — not the grey she knew. Something darker. Something that was not there yesterday morning.

"Obia."

Obia was already at the sensors. "I see it," it said. "Pressure dropping. Wind building from the south. It is coming fast."

"How long?"

"Hours. Maybe less."

Ako-chan felt it then — not the pull from the south she knew. Something else. Colder. Sharper.

She stood at the rail and looked at that dark line. It was not moving fast. But it was growing — slowly bigger at the bottom, like something from below the world.

"Daru," she called. "Gabu. Make sure your pods are locked."

"Already done," said Gabu.

"Riro," she called inside. "When it comes — stay in your capsule."

"Yes," Riro called back.

Dango sat down against her leg and pressed close.

The line in the south grew darker.

Suke-san still did not move.

It came before dawn.

The wind hit first — flat and very hard. Then the rain. It did not fall straight down. It went sideways, in sheets. The deck was wet in seconds.

Then the waves.

The ship went up. Came back down. Went up again. Below, Dango lay flat on the floor and did not move. Riro was sealed inside his capsule on the hull — below the waves. It held fast to the ship.

Obia had locked Daru and Gabu in their pods the night before. They were safe. But on deck, the water came over the sides. Cold. Heavy.

Ako-chan held the rail with both hands.

"Go inside," Obia said over the noise.

"No," she said. "I need to hear the whales. I have to stay here."

"It is not safe."

"I know." She held tighter. "I have to be here."

Her coat was already soaked. Her face was wet. But she held on.

A wave hit the port side. The ship tilted hard. Ako-chan's feet slipped. She held on. The ship came back. Water ran across the deck and off the other side.

Then Suke-san came out.

He was already wet. He walked straight to Ako-chan and sat down against her leg — not a little to the side, not a little back. Right against her.

"You do not have to stay," she said.

"I know," said Suke-san.

He did not go back inside.

The storm grew louder. The waves got bigger. The sky was completely black. But below all of it — below the noise, below the rain and the cold — she could still hear the whales. Low and steady. Very far down.

*We are here. Hold on.*

She held on.

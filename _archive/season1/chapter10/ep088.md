Evening on the ship. Very quiet.

One by one the others went to rest. Pen-san first, pressed against Dango's side. Then Riro, fur smooth and careful. Then Daru, talking softly to himself until the words got very small. Gabu last, very still in his pod, eyes half-closed.

Colonel Adelie sat by the door. His eyes were open.

He was watching Obia. But not the way he watched before. Something was different. Ako-chan could not say what. She could only see it.

Suke-san lay near Ako-chan, eyes half-open.

Obia sat by the sensor panel and did not move.

Ako-chan took out the red notebook.

She wrote for a long time. She wrote about the blizzard. The ice pressing the hull. The gap to the east. Obia's data — all those readings, all those days, from England to here. Suke-san walking to Obia in the dark. What Suke-san said.

Then she stopped.

She thought about Obia's words that morning. *The source is still there.* They stopped the danger to the ship. But they did not stop the thing that made the danger.

She wrote:

*Day seventy-two. Antarctica. The blizzard is gone. We are safe. But the pull from the south is still here. The root of it is still under the ice.*

She closed the notebook.

Obia's eyes were blue-white and steady in the dark.

The Colonel looked at Obia. Then at the notebook in Ako-chan's hands. Then out at the ice.

Something was still there. Under the ice. Connected to something far away.

They did not know what it was. But it was not over.

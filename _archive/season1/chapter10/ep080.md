Later that morning, Obia went back into the ship.

Ako-chan followed. She stood in the doorway and watched.

Obia was at the navigation table. It pulled up the sensor readings from the whole journey. England. Croatia. Jordan. Japan. The Pacific. All of it, in numbers and lines on the small screen.

Obia looked at the screen for a long time. Then it moved things and looked again.

Ako-chan came closer. "What are you doing?"

"Looking at the pattern," said Obia. "From the first day. Every reading. Every day of the journey."

"What does it say?"

Obia was quiet for a moment. "The source is here. Under the ice. Very deep." Its light touched the screen. "The readings today are the biggest I have seen. Bigger than at sea. Bigger than anything before."

Ako-chan felt a cold that was not the air. "What does that mean?"

"Something is building," said Obia. "At the source. Very fast."

Then Colonel Adelie appeared at the door. He looked at the screen. Then at Obia. Then at the screen again.

Obia did not look up.

The Colonel walked in. He stood behind Obia and studied the numbers. His face did not change.

"You collected all this," he said. "From England."

"Yes," said Obia.

The Colonel said nothing more. He looked at the numbers one more time. Then he walked out.

Obia turned back to the screen.

Ako-chan watched the numbers. They were not the same as an hour ago. They kept moving.

Outside, the air was very still.

Too still.

The morning after the aurora, everything was different.

The sky was the same pale grey. The ice was the same flat white. But something in the camp had changed. Something was watching something else.

Colonel Adelie stood at the far edge of camp. He did not look at the sky or the ice. He looked at Obia.

Obia moved through the morning as it always did. It checked the pods. It adjusted Riro's capsule. It read the sensors on the ship's side. Each movement was slow and careful. But under the Colonel's eyes, every movement looked different.

Pen-san noticed. He stood close to Ako-chan and said nothing. That was very unusual for Pen-san.

Suke-san sat to the far side of camp. He was not watching Obia. He was watching the Colonel watch Obia.

Dango pressed against Ako-chan's leg and stayed. Daru's face appeared at his pod window. Gabu watched from behind his glass, very still.

The pull from the south was stronger this morning. Much stronger. Ako-chan felt it low in her chest — not fear, but something deeper than fear. Something was changing far below the ice. Far below, at the end of everything. She looked at the Colonel. She looked at Obia. She did not speak.

Obia stopped at the sensor panel on the ship's side. It looked at the numbers for a long time.

"Something is changing," Obia said. Not to anyone. Just the words, in the cold air.

The Colonel took one step forward.

Obia did not turn. It looked at the panel and said nothing more.

Ako-chan watched them both. Obia, very still. The Colonel, very still. The ice all around them, very still.

The pull from the south was there. And it was growing.

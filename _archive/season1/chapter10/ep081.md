The next morning, the south wind came. Very early. Before the light.

One moment the air was still and cold and clear. Then Suke-san's ears went flat. He made one low sound in his throat. Then the wind hit.

Hard and low, from the south. The temperature dropped. Snow came — not falling snow. Sideways. White and sharp and fast.

"Back to the ship!" Obia called. "Now!"

They were two hundred meters from the ship. Ako-chan grabbed Pen-san under one arm. Dango was already running. Suke-san pressed close at her right side.

The pods. She turned. Daru's pod was moving on its wheels. Gabu's pod too. Slow, but moving. Riro's capsule was sealed and rolling. Obia went to Gabu's pod and pushed from behind.

The white came down around them. Ako-chan could not see the ship. She could see three meters, then two, then one.

"Obia!" she called.

"Straight ahead!" Obia's voice came through the white. "Fifteen meters. Keep going."

Dango pressed against her left leg. Suke-san pressed against her right. They moved together through the white.

The ship's hull appeared out of the blizzard — dark and solid. Ako-chan put her hand on the metal. It was very cold.

They got up the ramp. Pods in. Capsule in. Everyone in.

Obia came last. The ramp came up. The door sealed.

Outside, the blizzard hit the hull. A sound she had not heard before. Long and low, like the whole world breathing hard. Not the storm at sea. Something that came from the ground, not from the sky.

Pen-san was very still in her arms.

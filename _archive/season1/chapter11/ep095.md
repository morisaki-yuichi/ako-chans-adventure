Daru ran into her hands.

He pressed his whole small body against her palms. She held him carefully. He was warm and very still now.

"You are back," she said.

"Yes," said Daru. "I am back."

He sat in her hands for a moment. Not moving.

Then he looked at Dango. Dango was standing right behind her, close. His big paws on the deck. His long coat moving in the sea wind.

They looked at each other. Daru and Dango.

Dango made a low sound in his throat. Not a bark. Something quieter. Something for just the two of them.

Daru looked at Gabu's pod one more time. The orange light inside, steady and warm.

Then he looked back at Ako-chan.

She lowered her hands toward the rail.

Daru turned. He climbed to the top of the rail and stopped for one moment.

Then he dropped.

He landed on the white rock below. His feet found the stone right away. He knew it.

He ran left. He ran right. He found a thin crack in the limestone and pushed inside. She lost him for a moment in the shadow.

He came back out.

He looked up the cliff face. He started to climb. Quick and sure, each step certain.

He reached a higher rock and turned to face the ship.

He was still for a moment. His head high.

She held her breath.

Then he turned. And he was gone.

She stayed at the rail for a long time after that.

Five days after Japan.

The ship was quiet. Just five of them now.

Daru's pod glowed green. Gabu's glowed orange. Small warm lights in the grey.

Dango walked the deck in slow loops. He was not thinking about it. He just walked.

Ako-chan watched him. She said nothing.

In the afternoon, Daru's face came to his pod window.

"How far to Croatia?" he said.

"Many days still," said Obia.

Daru pressed closer to the glass. "How many days?"

"I do not know exactly. The sea is slow here."

Daru made a small sound. He pulled back from the glass.

From the other pod, Gabu spoke. "Daru."

"What."

"You will see it when you see it."

A pause. Then Daru: "I know that."

But his face stayed at the glass.

Ako-chan sat at the front of the ship. The sea was grey and wide. The sun was low ahead.

She listened.

The pull from the south was still there. Far away. Not smaller. But quieter, somehow. Like something very loud had moved far away and was still moving.

She thought: Riro is in the water. Suke-san is in the trees. Pen-san is on the ice.

She looked at Dango's slow loops. She looked at the two pod lights glowing in the cabin window.

Still here. Some of them.

Dango finished his loop. He came to her and sat. His side against her leg. She put her hand on him.

The sky went dark at the edges. East first, then all around.

Five of them. Moving west.

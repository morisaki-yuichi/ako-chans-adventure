Before sunrise the sky was pale grey.

Daru stood on the last rocky point above the sea. The sea was dark below. The white rocks were very white in the grey light. He looked at them for a long time.

He had lived here his whole life. Every rock. Every stone wall. Every small pool in the stone where the sea came in. He knew them all.

Ako-chan came and stood beside him. She did not say anything.

Then Daru said, "I am ready."

He stepped back from the edge. He walked to the road. He did not look back.

They moved together — Dango, Daru, Ako-chan, Obia. The road went south and then inland, away from the sea. The coast disappeared behind a hill. The sea was gone.

Ako-chan wrote in the red notebook.

*Day eleven. We leave Croatia.*

The morning grew slowly brighter. A thin orange line appeared in the east. The birds were quiet. No swifts going north. Just still air and the sound of their feet on the stone road.

Daru walked beside Dango now. Not behind. Beside.

Obia stopped on a low rise ahead and turned to Ako-chan. "The next part of the journey will take many days. The road goes south through mountains. Then east. Then south again into Jordan."

"Jordan," said Daru. He said the word slowly, like he was holding something new.

"Wadi Rum," said Obia. "The desert."

Daru looked at Ako-chan. "Have you been there?"

"No," she said.

"Neither have I." A small pause. "Good."

He turned south. They all walked on into the pale morning light.

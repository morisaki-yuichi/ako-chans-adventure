"South," said Ako-chan. "We are going south. To find what is wrong."

Daru did not answer right away. He turned and looked south. The sea was wide and calm. The coast went on and then curved away. He looked at it for a long moment.

Then he turned back. "I want to come."

Ako-chan looked at Obia. Obia said, "His knowledge of this coast is useful. And he has been tracking the disturbance from a fixed point for many days."

"I am not asking about useful," said Daru. "I want to come because I want to come."

Ako-chan looked at him. There was something very clear about the way he said things. He did not ask. He said exactly what he meant.

"It is a very long journey," she said. "We do not know how long. We do not know where it ends."

"I know," said Daru.

"Some parts will be cold. Very cold."

"I do not like cold," said Daru. "But I can be cold."

"You will leave this place. This coast."

Daru looked at the rocks around him. The dry grass. The small pools in the stone where the sea came in. He took a long look. Then he looked at Ako-chan.

"The birds stopped coming," he said. "The ground does not feel right. The sea smells wrong. If I stay here, it only gets worse. If I go with you, maybe we can help stop it."

He looked at her. He waited.

Ako-chan was quiet for a moment.

Then she said, "All right, Daru. Come with us."

Daru turned south. He took one step. Then another.

"Then let us go," he said.

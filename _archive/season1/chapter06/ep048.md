Suke-san came back at dawn.

He walked into the clearing without sound. He stopped in front of Ako-chan and looked up at her.

"I will come," he said.

"It is a long way," said Ako-chan. "Cold at the end. Very cold. And we do not know what we will find there."

"I know."

"Are you sure?"

He sat down. "I am not coming for it." He looked at Obia, then away. "I am coming because the south is wrong. And that matters more."

Obia did not speak.

Dango came up beside Ako-chan. He looked at Suke-san. Suke-san looked back at him.

This time Dango sat down first. Suke-san sat too. They were not close. But they were both on the same side.

"Six now," said Daru. Then louder, from inside the pod: "Six!"

"Six," said Ako-chan.

Gabu came from the edge of the clearing. He looked at Suke-san the way he looked at everything — careful, slow, no hurry. Then he said: "Welcome."

Suke-san looked at him. "You are the desert tortoise."

"Yes."

"The one who counted the days."

Gabu blinked. "Yes."

Suke-san nodded once.

They moved out of the forest in the morning. Gabu at the front. Daru in the pod. Dango on Ako-chan's left. Suke-san on her right — a little back, a little to the side.

Obia walked at the back.

Ako-chan took out the red notebook.

*Day forty. Japan. Suke-san is with us.*

She closed it. The trees moved in the morning wind. The path went south, toward the sea.

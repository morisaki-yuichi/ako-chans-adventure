Light came first.

Not all at once. The dark got less dark. Shapes came back — hills, trees, a road below. Then color. Grey, then brown, then green.

The sky turned red in the east.

Dango woke. He stretched. His front paws went out, his back end went up. He yawned very wide. She laughed. Just a small sound.

"Good morning," she said.

He wagged his tail. He climbed up and pushed his nose to the window. He breathed on the glass and made a small cloud.

"Croatia," said Obia. "We are in Croatia."

Below the train, a river ran between the hills. A heron stood very still on the bank. It was not at rest. It was watching. Waiting.

She opened the red notebook. She wrote: Croatia. Morning. Red sky.

She held the pen. There was more she wanted to write. But she did not have the words yet.

All morning, birds flew near the train. Then they turned away. North. Every time.

"They are all going north," said Ako-chan.

"Yes," said Obia. "All morning."

On a stone wall beside the track, two sparrows sat very still.

"What do they feel?" asked Dango.

She listened. "Something is wrong in the south," she said. "They feel it. They do not know what it is. But they feel it. And it is not stopping."

Dango made a low sound in his chest.

She pressed her hand to the glass. Here, the voices were not louder in sound. Louder in feeling. Close. All around.

She wrote again: The voices are louder. We are getting close.

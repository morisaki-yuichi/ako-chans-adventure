She did not sleep well. In the night, she heard the owl again. She heard the fox. She heard the hedgehog's words: "It should not be there." She heard the swallow: "The south wind is dead." She heard the robin: "Strange news from the south."

All the voices. All at once. In the dark.

When morning came, she lay still in her bed. Dango was warm beside her. His side moved up and down. She put her hand on his fur. She looked at the ceiling. The light was gray.

She thought about Obia. "Something is wrong in the south. It is very bad." She did not know if she trusted it. But the animals did not lie. The hedgehog could not sleep. The fox said the smell was wrong. The owl said something started and it was not stopping.

She sat up.

She went to her desk. She opened the drawer. Inside was a small red notebook. She got it long ago at a market in town. She never wrote in it. She did not know why she kept it. But she always kept it. She picked up her pen.

She wrote slowly. One word. Then the next.

*I have to go.*

She looked at the words. Then she closed the notebook.

Dango looked up from the bed. His brown eyes were warm and serious at the same time. He made a small sound. Not a question. More like he already knew.

"Dango," she said. "We are going."

He got off the bed. He sat at her feet. His tail moved once. Then he was still.

The room was quiet. The morning light was pale. But Ako-chan felt it. Something was different now. She was sure.

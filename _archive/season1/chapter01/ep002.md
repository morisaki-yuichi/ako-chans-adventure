That afternoon, Ako-chan came home from school. She changed her shoes. She went to the garden.

The garden was warm and quiet. The mist from the morning was gone. The robin was up in the oak tree. "Good afternoon," it said. "Good afternoon," Ako-chan said. The robin flew away.

She walked to the old rosebush near the fence. A small hedgehog was there. He was under the leaves. She sat down on the grass.

He was her oldest friend. She knew him for a long time. Since before she started school. He was always there when she came home.

But today he looked tired.

"Hello," she said softly.

He looked up slowly. His eyes were not bright. "Hello," he said.

"Are you all right?" she asked.

"I cannot sleep," he said. "Not for many nights now."

She watched him. He moved a little. He looked at the ground.

"The ground feels wrong," he said. "Under my feet. All the time."

"Wrong how?" she asked.

He was quiet for a moment. "It is like a sound. But not a sound. It comes from far away. It should not be there."

Dango came and sat beside Ako-chan. He was quiet. He looked at the hedgehog. He did not try to play.

"Something is wrong," the hedgehog said. "I am sure of it."

Ako-chan put her hand near him. He moved a little closer. She stayed very still.

The hedgehog looked small. Smaller than she remembered. His nose moved. He smelled the air. Something was not right, and he knew it.

She thought about the eyes in the garden. The eyes from that morning. She thought about Dango. He did not bark. He just looked.

The robin sang again. But it did not sound happy. It sounded different. And Ako-chan listened.

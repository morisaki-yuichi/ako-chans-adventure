Before dawn, Ako-chan woke.

The desert was cold and dark. Stars above the red walls.

Four things were on the sand beside her. A pale, folded cloth. Four small dark shapes. A light object she did not recognise. And a little apart: a round pod, already glowing softly, Daru inside and asleep.

Obia stood nearby. Its eyes were blue-white in the dark.

"For the heat," it said. "Try the cloth first."

Ako-chan picked it up. It was very light — almost nothing in her hands. She wrapped it around her shoulders. She could still feel the cold. But something about it was different. Like it was waiting.

"It turns the heat away," said Obia. "Keep it on."

Dango was awake now, sniffing at the two small shapes. He looked up.

"Boots," said Obia. "The sand will burn your paws."

Ako-chan put them on him one at a time. He tried a step. Then another. He looked down at his feet with a very serious expression.

Obia held up the last object. It was small and light — made to fit over a dog's nose. Open at the sides to let air through.

Dango stepped back.

"It cools the air before you breathe it in," said Obia. "Your breathing pushes the hot air out the other way."

He did not look certain. But he stayed still while Ako-chan put it on.

He panted once. His ears came up.

He panted again — longer this time. His tail began to move.

"Good?" said Ako-chan.

He looked at her. His tail moved faster.

The first grey light was coming over the tops of the red walls.

Obia turned south. "There is a signal," it said. "Small. Very still. Many days in the same place."

Ako-chan looked at the red walls around them.

Something was waiting in there.

"Then let us go," said Dango.

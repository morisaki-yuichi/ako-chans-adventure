That night, Ako-chan woke.

She did not know why. The camp was quiet. Cold. The sky above was dark and full of stars. There were more stars than she ever saw at home. They were brighter. So bright they did not look real.

Obia was very still.

Then a light appeared. Small. From Obia's chest. The same place as before — on the night train many weeks ago. That was when they crossed into Croatia. The same strange color she still could not name. Not blue. Not white. Not any color she had a word for.

This time it lasted longer. Much longer.

Ako-chan did not move. She watched. Obia's eyes stayed almost dark. Only the chest light moved — a slow, quiet pulse.

Then she heard a sound. Very small. Very careful.

She turned.

Colonel Adelie stood three steps away in the dark. He was completely still. His eyes were on Obia. They did not move. He did not blink.

The light in Obia's chest went out. Obia's eyes came back to their blue-white glow.

The Colonel was quiet for a long moment. Then he looked at Ako-chan.

"What is that?" he said. Quietly. Very quietly.

"I asked once," said Ako-chan. "Obia said it was checking data."

The Colonel said nothing. He looked back at Obia. Obia did not move.

He turned and walked back to his place in the camp. He did not lie down. He sat upright, facing Obia, in the dark.

Ako-chan lay back and looked at the stars.

She did not know what Obia was doing. She did not know what the Colonel thought.

But she knew the Colonel would not stop watching.

A second penguin appeared behind Pen-san.

A little taller. Very upright. Dark back, white front. An Adélie penguin, old and very careful. He walked across the ice at a steady pace — not slow, not fast. Each step exactly placed.

He was small. Just a little taller than Pen-san, and not by much. But something about him made everyone stand straight.

He stopped in front of Ako-chan. He looked at her once. He gave one short nod.

Then he looked at Dango. Then at Suke-san. Then at Daru's pod. Gabu's pod. Riro's capsule. Each one, in order, for exactly the right amount of time.

He reached Obia.

He stopped.

He looked at Obia for a long time. Not with fear. Not with anger. Just a very careful look. His eyes did not move.

Pen-san said, "This is Colonel Adelie! He is my teacher and my guard! He knows everything about everything!"

"Stand straight, Pen-san," said the Colonel. He did not look away from Obia.

Pen-san stood straight.

Suke-san made one quiet sound in his throat. Not a bark. Just a sound. He looked at Ako-chan, then at the Colonel, then at Obia.

Ako-chan said, "This is Obia. It travels with us. It has helped us on the whole journey."

The Colonel said nothing. He looked at Obia for one more moment. Then he turned back to Ako-chan.

"You heard the voices," he said. "The ones from the south."

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

He nodded again. One short nod. "I know who you are. You are expected." He turned and looked south across the flat white ice. "We will talk tonight. There is much to say."

He began to walk.

"Come," he said. "Follow me."

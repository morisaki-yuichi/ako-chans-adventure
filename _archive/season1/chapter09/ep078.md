The light came later that night.

Not Obia's light. The sky's light.

It moved across the dark in long, slow waves. Green at the edges. White at the center. Then green again. It did not hurry. It filled half the sky and moved like a living thing.

Ako-chan watched it from the ice.

One by one the others came out. Dango. Suke-san. Daru's face was against the glass of his pod. Riro was in his capsule. The door was open a little. Even Gabu moved close to the window of his pod. Pen-san walked out, going from side to side, stopped, looked up, and went very quiet. He opened his mouth once to speak. Then he looked at the sky and closed it again.

Nobody spoke for a long time.

The pull from the south was the strongest it had ever been. Not the disturbance now — not fear, not urgency. Something older. Like the ice itself was breathing. Like the ground under the ice knew they were here.

Ako-chan sat on the ice with her knees up and her back straight. Dango lay against her left side. Pen-san sat on her right, not asking for anything, just watching the sky. The light moved above them all.

The Colonel sat apart from the group. He did not watch the sky. He watched the group. His eyes moved slowly from one face to the next.

Then they stopped. They rested on Obia.

Obia looked up at the light like everyone else. Still. Silver. Eyes bright.

Ako-chan took out the red notebook. She opened it. She wrote:

*Day sixty-eight. Antarctica. The light fills the whole sky. We are all here. Nine.*

She closed it. The light kept moving.

Something would begin tomorrow.

The ice was enormous. Flat and white in every direction. No trees. No rocks. No hills. Just white, and the sky above it — grey and wide and very quiet. Even the wind was quiet here. It did not push. It moved slowly past and was gone.

They walked south. Slowly. There was no road. No path. Only the ice.

Ako-chan listened.

The voices here were different from any she knew. In England, the animals spoke with small and close sounds — hedgehog, robin, fox. In the desert, with patience. In the Pacific, with the big slow pulse of the whales. But here the voices were something else. Old. Very old. It did not come from animals nearby. It came from far under the ice. It rose up through her feet.

She stopped walking.

"What do you hear?" asked Dango.

She tried to find the words. "Something very old," she said. "Very far down. Like it has been there for a long time."

Daru, from his pod: "Is it the thing we came to find?"

"I do not know," said Ako-chan. "It is not an animal. It is something else."

Obia turned south. Its eyes were bright and steady. "The signal is here," it said. "I can feel it. Stronger than anywhere."

Gabu said, "Something else is here too. Not the signal. Something alive."

Suke-san's ears went flat. He turned east, then west. Then he stopped. His body went very still.

"There," said Suke-san. Quietly. "To the south."

They all looked.

Nothing. Just white ice and the grey sky. But Suke-san did not move. And Dango stood up straight beside him.

Something was coming.

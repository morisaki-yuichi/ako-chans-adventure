They walked deeper into the forest.

The fog did not lift. Their steps on the soft ground made almost no sound. The trees grew closer together, the spaces between them darker. Everything was grey and still.

Then it came.

From somewhere past the trees, past the fog, past the mountain maybe — a sound. Long. Rising. Clear even through the grey.

A howl.

Suke-san stopped.

Not slowly. Not carefully. He just stopped, completely, and every part of him listened. Ears straight up. Eyes fixed on the grey ahead. Tail low and still.

Ako-chan stopped too.

She felt it then — not only with her ears, but with something else. Something older. A voice with no words in it, only weight. The weight of winters. Of long open runs through snow. Of forests that went on further than any map.

Something that had always been here. Something that knew it.

Dango had stopped beside Suke-san. The two dogs stood side by side, facing the fog, not making any sound at all.

The howl faded slowly. The forest closed around it. The grey came back.

Suke-san did not move for a long time.

Then he said one word.

"There."

That was all.

Ako-chan stood still and let the feeling settle through her. The howl was gone. But it was still in the air — in the cold, in the fog between the trees.

She looked at Suke-san. Something had opened in his eyes — something old, something she had never seen there before.

The pods followed quietly behind them as they moved toward the sound.

It came out of the fog slowly.

Not the way Daru came — fast and straight. Not the way Gabu came — careful, step by step. This was different. The wolf moved the way the fog moved. Already there before you looked.

It stopped ten steps away.

Small by wolf measure. A grey coat, white at the chest. A narrow face. Eyes like still water — dark, calm, and very deep.

It looked at Suke-san first.

Suke-san did not move. He was not afraid — Ako-chan could feel that clearly. There was no fear in him at all. Only something with no name she knew. Something very old — much further back than either of them.

The wolf looked at Suke-san. Suke-san looked at the wolf.

Neither moved.

Ako-chan stood very still beside Dango. The fog moved slowly between them and the wolf.

The wolf's eyes moved across the others — Obia, Pen-san, General Adelie, the pods and capsule. Then they found Ako-chan.

She met them.

The wolf looked at her the way Gabu sometimes looked at things — not to check them, but to know them fully. Not quickly. All the way.

She held still and let the wolf see her.

After a long moment, the wolf looked back at Suke-san.

Suke-san sat down.

The wolf sat down too.

They stayed that way in the grey light, in the fog between the old trees. Not enemies. Not friends. Something older than those words. Something the mountain had always known.

Ako-chan did not breathe too loudly.

Ako-chan moved to the nearest bird.

The Great Auk — that was what Obia had called them. The original penguin. They had lived here for thousands of years, in these cold waters, on these grey rocks. And then, not long after this moment, they would be gone.

She knelt on the rock in front of it.

Its eyes were small and dark and very clear.

She opened herself to its voice and waited.

The bird looked at her. It gave what it had — not words, but a feeling of water and cold and the long time of being in the world. The dark sea. The fish just below the surface. The islands in summer, full of others. The sound of waves coming in and going back.

She held it. She held all of it.

Then she nodded to Obia.

Obia went still for a moment. Then: "Done."

Pen-san had not moved from where he stood. The bird he had spoken to was still looking at him. General Adelie was still at Pen-san's side, very quiet.

Ako-chan watched them for a moment. She did not call them away too soon.

The wind came off the water, cold and sharp.

"It is time," she said quietly.

Pen-san looked at her. Something heavy moved through his face. He turned back to the bird — one last look — and then turned away.

They walked back to the machine.

The seam closed. The floor moved.

This time the shake came harder. Two seconds. Three. The walls made a low sound she had not heard before — something deep in them straining.

Then it passed.

Nobody spoke.

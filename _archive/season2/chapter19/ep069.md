Riro saw them first.

He made a short, quiet sound and raised one paw toward the water.

There — two of them, moving through the sea toward the ice shelf. Moving slowly. Everything about them was slow.

Large. Very large. Round-backed, smooth, grey-brown like old stone. They moved like something with all the time in the world.

Steller's sea cows.

They came to the edge of the ice and stopped.

Riro was very still beside Ako-chan. She could feel something from him — not fear, nothing close to fear. Something with no name she had a word for.

The nearest one raised its head above the surface. It looked at the group on the ice with small, dark, warm eyes.

Ako-chan felt the voice before she tried to hear it.

It came like something wide and slow — the way a wave comes on a long flat beach, taking its time, filling every space before it goes back. The cold sea. The things below the surface. The others, moving together in the deep water. The simple fact of being in the world.

She stood very still and let it fill her.

When she finally nodded to Obia, her eyes were wet. She did not know exactly when that had happened.

"Done," said Obia.

Riro sat down on the ice. He pressed his front paws together. He looked at the sea cow and did not look away.

The sea cow looked back at him.

For a long moment, neither moved.

Around them, the Bering Sea was very quiet.

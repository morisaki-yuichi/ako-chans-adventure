The others gathered without being asked.

Pen-san sat near General Adelie. Suke-san was on Ako-chan's right. Riro had come out of his capsule. Daru and Gabu were close together, Ruka between them. Dango pressed against Ako-chan's left side.

They had all woken and come together. As if they already knew something was coming.

Obia stood in the middle of them.

"I have to tell you something," it said.

Nobody moved.

"The four voices are safe. That part is done. But the machine — the connection it made between this time and the future — it is still open. Both ends are still open. Things still pass through it. That is why the world does not feel fully right."

Pen-san looked at Ako-chan. She did not look back.

"To close it," Obia continued, "someone has to go to the future end. And close it from inside."

A silence.

"I have all four voices," Obia said. "And I know the way. The machine will take me there. To the future. To the time the machine was made."

Another pause. Shorter.

"I have to go," said Obia. "I have to take the data. And I have to close the connection."

And then: "I must go alone."

Nobody spoke.

Dango made a very low sound. Not a growl. Something older and quieter than that. He made it once, and then the sound was gone, and the machine was quiet again.

Suke-san looked at Obia for a long time.

Then he looked away.

Riro reached back and began to groom.

Nobody said a word.

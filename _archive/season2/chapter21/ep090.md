Morning came slowly.

There was no light from outside — only the cave, the blue glow, the stillness. But something changed in the machine's small lights. They grew a little different. Obia's eyes changed too. And one by one, without being called, the others woke.

They came together.

Not inside the machine. Outside it — in the open space between the cave walls and the machine's great hull. The blue light of the cave fell on all of them. Cold and clear.

Obia stood at the machine's entrance.

Dango sat on Ako-chan's left. Suke-san was on her right, a little back, a little to the side — his place, the place he had always chosen. Riro stood near his capsule, fur smooth and full. Daru and Gabu beside each other, Ruka between them. Pen-san and General Adelie nearby, still and quiet.

All of them there.

All of them looking at Obia.

The machine behind Obia was large and dark and very quiet. Deep under the ice, the connection was still running — thin and open, the thread she had felt two mornings ago.

Not yet closed.

Ako-chan looked at Obia.

Obia looked at her.

Nobody spoke.

The blue light moved on the walls. Cold air held them. She thought of the red notebook in her coat. She thought of all the voices she had heard — the wolf, the Auks, the sea cows, the warm sound from the oldest ground she had ever stood on.

She breathed in. She breathed out.

This was the morning before it happened.

They all knew it.

She did not know what came after. She had never been at the edge of a morning like this before.

But she was here.

And so were they.

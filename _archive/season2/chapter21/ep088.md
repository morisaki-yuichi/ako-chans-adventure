The machine was quiet.

Everyone else was asleep. Dango against her side. Suke-san near the door. Daru and Gabu together, Ruka between them. Riro with his head resting on his capsule. Pen-san and General Adelie close to each other.

Ruka alone was not fully asleep. She sat still in the dim yellow glow, watching.

Ako-chan got up carefully.

Obia was at the side panel. Its eyes were softer than usual — dim blue-white, not the sharp working light.

Ako-chan came and stood beside it.

"Are you afraid?" she asked.

Obia was quiet for a moment.

"I am not sure what I am," it said.

She thought about that.

"I am afraid," she said. "For you."

Obia turned and looked at her. Its eyes were steady.

"Will you keep listening?" it said. "After this."

"Yes," she said. "I will."

"The animals," said Obia. "The ones that are still here. Keep listening to them. Always."

"I will," she said again.

"And write it down," said Obia. "In the red notebook. All of it. Every voice."

She put her hand on the notebook in her coat pocket. She always kept it close.

"I will write all of it," she said. "Every voice I hear. I will not forget any of them."

Obia looked at her for a long time.

"I know," it said at last. "I know you will not."

The machine was very quiet. The lights moved slowly on the walls. Outside, the cave was dark and cold and had been there for a very long time.

Dango shifted in his sleep.

Ako-chan sat down on the floor beside Obia's feet. She stayed there until the machine grew light again.

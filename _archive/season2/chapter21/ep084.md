Obia had been at the side panel since before she woke.

Now Obia turned around.

"I have been looking at the machine," it said.

Ako-chan waited.

"The four voices are here. They are safe. They have been sent forward — to whoever is waiting for them in the future. That part is done."

"Then why—" she started.

"The machine itself," said Obia. "It is still open."

She did not understand right away.

"When this machine was made," Obia said slowly, "a connection was built into it. Between two times. The time it was made — far ahead, in the future. And this time. Now."

"The disturbance," said Ako-chan.

"Yes. The disturbance came through that connection. The future is very badly hurt. The hurt was leaking through. That is what the animals felt."

"But the ground is quiet now," she said. "The hedgehog said so. Back in England. Before all of this."

"Quieter," said Obia. "But the connection is still there. Both ends. The future end and this end. Things still pass through it — not loudly, but they do. That is what you felt this morning."

She looked at the machine around her. The smooth walls. The quiet lights.

"To close it," said Obia, "someone has to go to the future end. And close it from the inside."

A long silence.

Dango lifted his head from the floor. His ears moved.

"You mean—" Ako-chan began.

"Yes," said Obia.

She stood very still.

She had known something was not finished. She had felt it since this morning — the thin thread under the quiet. Now she knew what it was.

She looked at Obia.

Obia looked back.

Neither of them spoke.

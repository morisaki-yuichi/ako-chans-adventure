The floor moved one last time.

Then it stopped.

The seam opened. Cold came in — clean and sharp. Not the red earth. Not the dry heat of the old ground. This cold she knew. Antarctica.

The ice cave glowed pale blue around them. The same walls. The same ice. The machine sat in the same place it had always been, half-buried and still. Nothing had moved while they were gone.

She stepped out first. Her boot touched the cave floor. She breathed in slowly. Cold air, ice, and stillness. The right kind.

"We are back," said Obia.

Dango pressed against her leg. He made a small sound — not a bark. Something between worry and relief.

The others came out one by one. Pen-san looked at the blue walls, then at General Adelie. Suke-san went to the edge of the cave. He stood with his nose up and read the air. Riro climbed out of his capsule and stood still, eyes wide. Daru looked at his feet on the cave floor, then at Gabu. Gabu stood near the machine door and did not move for a while.

In the yellow pod, Ruka pressed one paw against the window.

Four voices.

Held somewhere in Obia. Somewhere in the machine. Four animals who had been gone from the world for a very long time. Gone so long that most people had forgotten the shape of their voices.

Now those voices were somewhere safe.

Ako-chan put her hand on Dango's head and kept it there.

Pen-san sat down slowly beside her. He did not say anything. That was not like him. She did not push him.

"Four," he said at last. Very quiet.

She looked at the blue walls. She looked at all of them — Dango, Suke-san, Riro, Daru, Gabu, Ruka, Pen-san, General Adelie, Obia.

"Yes," she said. "Four."

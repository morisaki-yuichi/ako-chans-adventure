That evening, Ako-chan opened the red notebook.

She found the page she had written a few days before. *Everything is quiet. Ruka is on my desk. Dango is asleep. The oak tree is outside. We are all here. For now.*

She turned to a blank page.

She held the pen. She sat for a while with the empty page in front of her.

Then she began to write.

*A signal. From the far south. Obia hears it. It does not stop. And there is a memory inside Obia — locked. Obia cannot open it. I think the key is in the south. Under the ice.*

She stopped. She read what she had written.

Obia was in the garden again, facing south. Dango was beside her on the sofa, his chin on her knee.

She went to the window.

"Obia," she said.

Obia turned.

She opened the door. The night air was cool and still.

"Do you want to go?" she asked.

Obia looked at her. It was quiet for a moment.

"I think we need to," it said. "The signal will not stop. And the memory — I cannot find the key here. I think it is there. And I think I have been waiting for it. Without knowing."

Dango stood up beside Ako-chan. He pressed his shoulder to her leg.

She looked at the dark sky. The stars above the oak tree. South.

She went back inside. She sat down at her desk. She added one more line to the notebook:

*We are going south again.*

She closed it.

Ruka was still on the desk. Two small eyes, looking at her.

The garden was quiet around them.

In the morning, Gabu stood at the edge of the sand.

The flying boat was behind him, ready. The others were inside. He was looking at the red walls.

Ako-chan came and stood beside him. Not too close. She did not speak.

The sun was low. Long light on the stone. The walls were a deeper red in the morning than in the afternoon. She had not known that before.

After a long time, Gabu said: "The lizards came back. When the south was quiet. They came back."

"Yes," she said. "You were right."

"I counted the days."

"I know."

He looked at the nearest rock wall. At the shadow at its base. At the pale sand below.

Then he turned, and he walked to the flying boat.

Inside, Daru was already at the window. He looked at Gabu as he came up the ramp. Gabu looked back.

Ruka was in her warm corner. She looked at Gabu too.

Gabu walked to his side of the cabin. He did not say anything. He just settled there — still, and certain.

Ako-chan sat down. Obia started the engines.

The flying boat rose. The red walls fell away on both sides, lower and lower. Then just the wide desert below — red and pale and still.

She opened the red notebook.

*Day thirty-one. Daru is with us. Gabu is with us. Ruka is between them. We go east now.*

She closed it.

Dango was at her feet. Daru was at the window. Gabu was still. Ruka was in her corner.

The desert became small below them.

Then it was gone.

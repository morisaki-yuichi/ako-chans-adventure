The flying boat went east. Below: the edge of the desert, then pale rock, then sea.

Daru was at the window. He had been there since Jordan. The land changed below him — from red sandstone to brown to dark water. He did not look away.

Gabu sat on the other side of the cabin. Still and settled. Ruka was beside him. She had chosen that spot herself in the morning — she climbed down from her warm corner and went to sit by Gabu's side. She had not moved since.

Dango lay at Ako-chan's feet, chin on his paws. The engine made a low, steady sound. Outside, the sky was wide and pale.

Ako-chan was thinking of Suke-san.

She thought of the park near the forest. The way he had always known things before she did. Compact, certain, tail high. The way he had stood at the tree line the first time — still, watching — not coming closer until he was ready. She thought of what he had said when they parted. "You will keep listening," he had said. "Yes," she had said. "I will."

She had kept her word.

Obia was at the front of the cabin, watching the sensors.

"How long?" Daru asked, not turning from the window.

"One more day. Maybe less," Obia said.

Daru said it softly, to himself: "One more day."

Gabu did not speak. Ruka turned her head once toward the window, then settled again.

The flying boat was warm inside. Outside, clouds moved low over the sea.

Dango lifted his chin. He looked at Ako-chan. She put her hand on his back.

The sea below was dark and still. The sun moved slowly toward the west.

Somewhere ahead: pine trees. Wet earth. Green hills.

Japan was waiting.

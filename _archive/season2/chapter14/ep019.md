Three days later, the flying boat was almost done.

Dango walked around it slowly. All the way around. Nose close to the hull. He stopped at each join, sniffed, moved on. He did this twice. Then he sat down in front of the nose and looked up at it.

Ruka was in her usual corner near the hedge. She had been there every day now. She seemed to understand that this place was important.

Ako-chan stood beside the body of the flying boat and put her hand on it. It was smooth and cool. Much larger than Obia's ship from before. The wings stretched out wider than she had expected.

"When?" she asked.

"Three more days," said Obia. "The inner connections. Then it is done."

Ako-chan looked south. She could not feel anything from there. But she knew the signal was still calling. Patient. Small. Far away.

She thought about who they needed to collect.

Daru was on his coast in Croatia. Gabu was in his desert in Jordan. Suke-san was in the forest near the sea in Japan. Riro was in the Pacific. Pen-san and the Colonel were on the ice.

A long way. In all directions.

"We will need to go east first," she said. "Then further east. Then south."

"Yes," said Obia.

Dango came back from the nose of the flying boat and sat beside her.

She looked at the hull. The wings. The thing Obia had built — with her hands helping.

Almost.

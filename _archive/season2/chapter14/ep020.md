On the ninth day, Obia said: "It is ready."

The morning was still and grey. Dew on the grass. Ruka was in her corner near the hedge. Dango sat at the edge of the field.

Obia walked to the front of the flying boat and did something inside it. A low sound started. Very quiet at first. Then steady.

The hull moved.

Not much. Just a small rise from the ground. Two hand-widths. Then it stopped there — steady, the hull hanging in the air above the flattened grass.

Nobody spoke.

Dango's ears were up. He did not move.

The sound was low and even. The flying boat hung there and waited. Ako-chan put her hand under the hull. Cool air. A faint movement, like the air was pressing gently from below.

"Can it carry us all?" she asked.

"Yes," said Obia.

The flying boat came down slowly. It settled back onto the grass.

Obia walked back to Ako-chan.

"Tomorrow," she said. "We start east."

"Yes," said Obia.

She looked at Dango. He looked back at her. His tail moved once.

That evening, she opened the red notebook. She held the pen for a moment. Then she wrote:

*The flying boat is ready. Tomorrow we go to collect everyone. Daru first. Then Gabu. Then Suke-san. Then Riro. Then south.*

She stopped. Then she added one more line.

*We are going back.*

She closed the notebook. The oak tree was outside the window. The field beyond the hedge was dark. Somewhere in it, the flying boat was waiting.

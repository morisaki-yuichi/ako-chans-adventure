That night, Ako-chan sat at her desk.

The room was dark except for one lamp. Outside, the garden was quiet. The oak tree was only a shape in the dark. A few stars were visible through the window.

She reached into her coat — the same coat she had worn from England all the way south and all the way back — and took out the red notebook.

She set it on the desk.

She opened it slowly to the last written page.

The entry was still there, in her own writing: *Day thirty-seven. Suke-san is with us. Riro is with us. We are all here. We go south.*

Below it: blank pages.

She picked up her pen.

She thought of the wolf in the fog, and the Auks standing still on the cold rock. She thought of the sea cows moving wide and slow through the oldest water. She thought of the warm sound from the oldest ground — carried up through feet and shell and into the air. All four voices, held somewhere in the future now, doing what they were always meant to do.

She thought of Obia.

She thought of its eyes in the cave — looking at her through the closing seam. That last look, clear and still.

She thought of what Obia had asked.

*And write it down. In the red notebook. All of it. Every voice.*

She put the pen to the page.

She wrote: *Obia——*

She stopped.

The room was very quiet. Dango slept at her feet. Ruka was awake — on the warm stone near the base of the lamp, very still, watching nothing. The lamp made its small steady light.

She had so much to say. All of it. Everything. But she did not know how to begin.

She looked at the word she had written.

Then she breathed in.

And began.

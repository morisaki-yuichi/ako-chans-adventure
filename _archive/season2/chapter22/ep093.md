Dango had been still all morning.

That was not like him.

He had not barked. He had not pushed in front of anyone. He had stayed pressed against Ako-chan's left side, watching Obia. His ears were down. His tail was low. He was holding himself in.

Then something broke.

He crossed the floor in three quick steps. He pressed himself hard against Obia's front, both paws up on Obia's chest. His head pushed hard into Obia's shoulder.

He held on.

Obia did not push him away.

Dango made a sound — low and pressed and constant, from somewhere deep in him. Ako-chan knew that sound. She had heard it only a few times. It was the sound he made when something was too big for him to carry.

Obia lowered its head beside his.

"I was there from the very beginning," Obia said, quietly. "And so were you. You were watching before anyone else. Before she knew what was wrong. You saw it all."

Dango pressed harder.

Ako-chan went to him. She knelt beside them and put her arms around Dango. He was tight all the way through — every part of him pulled in and hard. He was shaking a little.

"Dango," she said.

He made the sound again.

She held him gently. She did not pull fast. She waited, and then brought him slowly back, little by little.

He came. He did not want to, but he came.

He pressed against her leg and put his head down. He would not look at her.

She put her hand on him and kept it there. He was still trembling. She stayed and did not move.

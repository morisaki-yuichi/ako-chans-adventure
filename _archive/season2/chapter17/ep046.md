The flat ice did not last.

After a while, the ground changed. The surface tilted — a slope, gentle at first, then steeper. They were going down.

Suke-san slowed. He looked at the ground and the angle of the slope. Then he kept walking.

"Signal," said Obia. "Stronger. Below us."

Below — not ahead. Not to the side. Below.

Ako-chan looked at the ice under her feet. Somewhere deep under it, something was waiting.

They kept going down.

The slope curved. Then the ice opened — a crack, long and dark, going down into the ice. Wide enough to walk through. Cold air came up from inside — not the blizzard cold. Something older. Still.

Dango pressed close to her leg.

She looked into the crack. Dark at first. Then, from the walls, a faint light — the ice itself glowing pale blue-white from inside.

"The signal," said Obia. "Strongest so far. It comes from below. From inside."

Suke-san walked to the edge and looked down. His ears went up. He looked at Ako-chan.

She looked at Dango.

Dango looked at the crack. His ears went flat once. Then they came back up.

She looked at the pale blue light from inside.

She had not been in this exact place before. But something in her chest said: go down.

"Down," she said.

Suke-san went first.

Then Dango. Then Ako-chan. Obia and the others came after, careful on the slope.

One by one, they went into the ice.

Suke-san went first.

He stepped out onto the ice and stopped. Not from fear — he stopped to feel. Nose up. Ears forward, then one turned back, then forward again.

Then he moved.

He did not wait for directions. He read the wind and started walking.

Ako-chan followed with Dango at her side. Behind them, Obia guided the green pod, the orange, Riro's capsule, and the yellow pod across the ice.

The blizzard had grown smaller — thin snow, sideways wind. Enough to see ahead. Not enough to see far.

Suke-san stopped once.

He turned slightly, then walked on in a new direction.

"Do you know the way?" Ako-chan asked.

He did not turn around. "The wind. I know the wind here."

He said it quietly: "I know this cold."

She understood. He had been here before. Not this exact place — but this cold, this ice, the way the wind moved on flat white ground. His body remembered it.

She thought of him on the ship deck in the blizzard. That long night, all night, not speaking. Just there beside her.

She kept walking.

The ice was flat at first. Then it changed — small bumps, then bigger ones. The wind came in short hard gusts. The yellow pod moved carefully behind them, and the green and orange pods with it.

"The signal is below us now," said Obia. "South and down. We are close."

South. And down.

Ako-chan looked at the ice ahead. White and still, stretching far.

Suke-san went on without stopping.

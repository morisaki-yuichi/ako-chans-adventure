The white did not end.

It went on all the way down — blizzard, hull, blizzard. And then: impact.

Not hard. A jolt. A sudden stop. Then stillness.

The engines changed sound. Lower. Then quiet.

Nobody moved.

The blizzard hit the outside of the hull — that was the only sound now. Wind, and the small sounds of the flying boat settling.

They were on the ground.

Antarctica.

Dango stood up. He looked at Ako-chan. Then at the door. Then at Ako-chan again.

"We are down," said Obia. It came from the front of the cabin. "All readings are good. We are safe."

Suke-san had not moved from his spot near the door. He sat facing forward, ears still flat. His nose was going — quick, small movements, reading the air through the hull.

"Are we there?" Daru looked out from the green pod.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

Gabu pressed one foot against the inside of the orange pod. Just once. Then was still.

Riro looked at the door. He checked his fur in two places. Then stopped.

In her corner, Ruka was in her shell, fully in. The yellow pod sat beside her, warm and glowing.

The blizzard went on outside. Wind and snow and cold.

Nobody opened the door.

They waited. For the blizzard to pass. Or to get smaller. Or to give them a way through.

Dango sat against Ako-chan's leg and stayed there.

Outside: only the blizzard. Inside: all of them, quiet, waiting.

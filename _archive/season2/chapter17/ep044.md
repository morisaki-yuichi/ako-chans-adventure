By morning, the blizzard had not stopped. But it was smaller now.

Obia came from the front.

"The signal is still there," it said. "We should go now. While we can see."

It moved through the cabin. As it passed each one, something changed — a deeper hum, a brighter warmth inside. The green. The orange. Riro's in the corner.

Antarctic mode.

Ako-chan put on her cold-weather suit. It weighed almost nothing but blocked the cold completely. Obia fitted the small boots to Dango's paws, then the vest. Suke-san's were already on. He stood by the door.

Then Obia opened the door.

Cold came in at once — hard and sharp, nothing like the cold at home or in the mountains. This was a different kind of cold. The wind pushed.

Everyone looked at the yellow pod.

Ruka was still in her corner. The pod glowed beside her. Nobody said anything. Nobody moved.

Then Ruka moved.

She came out of her shell — just her head, just her eyes. She looked at the open door. She looked at the yellow pod.

She walked to the pod.

She walked in.

The door of the pod closed behind her. The yellow glow settled around her, warm and steady.

Nobody had said a word. Nobody had asked her to go in.

Ako-chan looked at Dango. He looked back.

Then she stepped out.

Cold hit her all at once. Below her feet: ice. Real ice, flat and wide. The blizzard was smaller now, the snow coming sideways in thin fast lines.

She took one step. Then another.

They were on Antarctica.

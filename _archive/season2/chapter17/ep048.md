After a long moment, they went deeper.

The cave curved — not sharp, just a long slow curve to the right. The blue-white glow stayed steady on the walls.

Then Suke-san stopped.

He did not growl. His ears went up and forward — not a warning. Something else.

Then Ako-chan heard it too. Small sounds. Quick. Getting closer.

Something was running.

From deeper in the cave — running fast, toward them.

Tall. Black at the back, white at the front. A bright line of yellow-orange at each side of the neck. Wings out for balance.

Pen-san.

He ran straight to her — no slowing, no looking — and pressed against her with his whole body.

"You came back!" he said. "You came back, you came back!"

She went down on her knees.

"You came back," he said again. Quieter now. Just her.

"I promised," she said.

He went still.

She held him. He was tall now. Not the small round shape she remembered. He pressed the side of his head against hers and was quiet.

Dango came beside her. He made one small sound.

Pen-san looked up.

"Hello, dog," said Pen-san.

Dango's tail moved once.

Around them: the cave was still. The blue-white light was steady. The others had all stopped — Suke-san, the pods with Gabu and Daru inside, Ruka in the yellow pod, Riro in his. All of them watching.

Ako-chan stayed on her knees, holding Pen-san.

In the ice. In the blue light.

She did not want to move.

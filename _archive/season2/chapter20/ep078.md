The machine arrived without sound.

The seam opened. Pale sunlight came through — warm and dry, nothing like the ice or the fog or the ocean. A wide open land. Red earth. Dry grass moving in the wind.

Ako-chan stepped out first.

Dango was already past her.

He ran — no direction from Obia, no signal to follow. His nose led him and his legs followed, and he ran across the red earth without stopping.

She ran too.

Behind her: Obia, then Suke-san, then Riro. Pen-san ran beside her. General Adelie moved fast and steady at the back.

Dango stopped.

He had found them.

Three small shapes in the open ground — Daru, Gabu, and Ruka. All there. All whole.

Ruka was out of her shell.

Ako-chan reached them and went to her knees. She opened her hands.

Ruka walked forward on the red earth and stepped into her palms.

She was warm. She was shaking a little. But she was there.

Daru pushed against Ako-chan's knee with the top of his shell — the same way he had always done, since the first day on the Dalmatian coast. Gabu stood at Ako-chan's side, very still, watching her face.

She held Ruka against her chest.

Dango pressed close on one side. Suke-san came to the other.

After a moment, Ako-chan looked up.

The land around them was wide and open and very old. In the distance, near the line of dry grass and red rock, something large was moving slowly away.

She watched until it was gone.

She did not call out. She did not say anything.

She just watched.

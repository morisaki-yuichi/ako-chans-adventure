The Meiolania lowered its head.

Slowly. All the way down until the two horns were near the red earth. It held that position — completely still.

Daru did not know what to do. He looked at Gabu. Gabu's eyes were steady.

The sound came from deep inside the creature. Not from its mouth — from somewhere further in. Low and warm and wide. It moved through the air and then through the ground. Daru felt it in his feet. In his shell. Behind his eyes.

The sound was not words.

But he understood it all the same. It said: I know what you are. I am glad you are still here.

The oldest welcome he had ever felt.

Behind him, something moved.

A small motion. Then Ruka's head appeared — just the top of it at first, then the eyes. Then all of it. Slow. Very careful.

She looked at the Meiolania.

The creature did not lift its head. It stayed low.

The warm sound came again — quieter this time, softer.

Ruka took one step forward.

One step. On the red earth, in a time long before everything she had ever known.

She took another.

Daru held completely still. Gabu held completely still. The grass held still. The pale sky held still.

Ruka stopped two steps from the great lowered head.

The dark, calm eyes looked at her.

Her eyes looked back.

Neither moved for a long moment.

Then, very slowly, Ruka lowered her own head. All the way down, the way the Meiolania had.

The warm sound filled the air all around them.

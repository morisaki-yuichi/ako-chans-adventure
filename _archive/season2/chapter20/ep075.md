It came slowly.

Each step was heavy. The red ground carried all its weight, and the animal moved as if it knew this and did not mind.

As it came closer, Daru and Gabu could see it clearly.

It was a tortoise. That was the shape — clearly. But larger than either of them had ever seen. The shell was wide and dark, the color of old rock, with long raised ridges running from front to back. The neck was thick. The legs were short and very wide.

And its head had horns. Two of them — one above each eye, curved slightly forward. Not sharp, but solid. Part of the skull itself. The face was ancient. The eyes were dark and calm.

Behind: a thick tail with hard, round spikes at the end. They were not sharp, but they were heavy. Everything about the animal was heavy.

Daru did not step back. Gabu did not step back.

Ruka's shell was behind them.

The creature came to twenty steps. Then ten. Then it stopped.

It looked at Daru.

Then at Gabu.

Its head moved very slightly — reading them, taking in the sight and the smell of them. It was not in a hurry. It had no reason to be in a hurry.

Daru could feel his heart moving fast. He kept his feet on the ground. He did not know exactly why, only that he was not going to move.

Gabu was thinking. Daru could have told that just by looking at him. Gabu was thinking and staying.

The ancient animal looked at them both. It did not come closer.

It waited.

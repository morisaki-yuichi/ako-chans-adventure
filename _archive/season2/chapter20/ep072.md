Nobody spoke for a long time.

Then Suke-san stood. He looked at Obia, then at Ako-chan. "Three voices," he said. "We have three. That is what we came to do." He turned. "We go back."

Obia said nothing.

General Adelie stepped forward. "He is right. Three jumps. Three times it grew harder. The machine needs the ground beneath it. We do not push further."

Daru's face was still pressed against the pod wall. Gabu had not moved from the orange light.

Ako-chan looked at the floor. She thought about a fourth voice — something older than the wolf, older than the Auks, older than the sea cows. Something that had been in the world long before names for anything. A voice that had never been heard, not yet, not by anyone outside this moment.

She wanted it.

But she looked at Suke-san. His ears were still up. His eyes had not closed. She looked at Riro, holding his front paws together, very still. At Pen-san, watching her with his quiet dark eyes.

"Home," she said.

Something in the room shifted — small, but real. Like a breath let out.

Obia turned to the panel. The lights along the walls began to move in a different rhythm — the faster pulse of return.

Then something moved in the corner.

Ruka came out of her yellow pod. She walked to the edge of it and stood there, looking at Ako-chan — steady, careful, clear.

Ako-chan held out her hand.

Ruka did not step forward. But she stayed. Head up. Eyes open.

She did not go back inside.

The shake was still in her hands.

Ako-chan pressed her palms flat on her legs and waited. The machine was steady now. The lights had come back to their slow movement along the walls. Everything was quiet.

But Suke-san was not quiet.

He moved in short steps — not his usual slow loops. Back and forth, one end to the other. He had not stopped since the shaking ended.

Obia moved to the side panel. Its eyes went very still.

"Three jumps," Obia said. "I am reading the data now. The first was small. The second was larger. The third was larger still." Obia paused. "Each one harder than the last. The difference is growing between them."

Suke-san made a short sound in his throat.

"Any jump from here," said Obia. "Even back. The pattern may continue." Obia stopped.

"What happens?" said Ako-chan.

"I do not know," said Obia. "I do not have data for this."

She looked around the room. Dango was at her side. Daru had his face pressed against the pod wall, watching Obia from behind the green glow. Gabu watched from the orange light. Riro sat with his front paws together, looking at nothing.

Ruka's yellow pod sat still and warm in its corner. Ruka was inside, very quiet.

Pen-san looked only at her.

General Adelie stood near the center of the space. Straight. Still. Waiting.

Suke-san sat at last — near her feet, head up. His ears did not go down. His eyes did not close. He was watching, and she could not tell what he was watching for.

There was no sound outside the machine.

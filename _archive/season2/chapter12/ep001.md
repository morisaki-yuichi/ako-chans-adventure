The garden was the same. The oak tree was taller now. The rosebush had grown along the fence. The morning light came through the branches the same way it always had.

Ako-chan stood at the back door. She was taller too. Her red glasses were the same. Dango sat beside her. He was bigger now. Not a puppy. A dog. His fluffy tail moved slowly in the cool air.

The garden was very still. Not the wrong kind of still. The good kind. Everything was in the right place. The hedgehog was somewhere under the rosebush. The robin was in the oak tree. The roses on the fence were open. It was summer.

Then something moved near the south wall.

Something small. Something slow.

Ruka.

A tiny tortoise came out from under a leaf. Not much bigger than Dango's paw. Ruka moved one step. Stopped. One more step. Stopped again.

Dango looked down at Ruka. He sat up very straight. He was a senior. He knew it.

Ako-chan sat down on the step. The stone was cold. She watched Ruka cross the last of the grass. It took a long time. That was fine.

"Good morning, Ruka," she said.

Ruka did not answer with words. But two small eyes looked up at her. Then one small foot moved forward.

Dango put his chin on Ako-chan's knee. She put her hand on his head. His tail moved once. Slow and sure.

The robin sang in the oak tree. The morning went on around them.

She had been home for a few years now. So much had changed. And yet the garden was the same garden.

She was glad it was.

Obia had a corner in the garden.

Not the south wall corner. That was Ruka's. Not the oak tree corner. That was the robin's. Obia's corner was near the old shed. The same place where Ako-chan first saw it, a long time ago.

In the evenings, Obia went there.

It stood very still. Its eyes were quiet. Sometimes blue-white. Sometimes just dark circles in a silver face.

Dango walked past Obia every evening. He did not stop. He did not bark. He just walked past. That was how it was between them now.

Ako-chan came out with a cup of tea. She sat on the step. The evening was cool and growing dark.

"Are you listening?" she asked.

Obia's eyes did not change. "Yes," it said.

"What do you hear?"

"Animals," said Obia. "The robin. The hedgehog. The fox, further away. The usual ones."

"Nothing else?"

Obia was quiet for a moment. "I hear something. But I cannot name it," it said.

Ako-chan held her cup. The tea was warm.

She looked at the shed. The old wood, the dark gap at the bottom. She thought about that first morning. Two bright eyes in the shadow. She did not know what they were then. She was not afraid. Just curious.

She was still curious.

Ruka came around the corner of the step. Slowly. Heading for the south wall. Already heading for bed.

"Goodnight, Ruka," said Ako-chan.

Ruka did not answer. Just walked on.

Obia watched Ruka go. Its eyes were soft.

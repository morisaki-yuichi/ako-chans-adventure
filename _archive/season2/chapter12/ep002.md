Ruka was very small. Smaller than any of the animals Ako-chan had known before.

She found Ruka in Italy. In Puglia, on a small road above the sea. The stones there were white. The grass was dry and warm. The sea was very blue and very wide.

She sat on a low wall. She looked at the sea. She was alone that day, just walking and thinking.

Then something moved in the dry grass below the wall.

She looked down.

A tiny tortoise. Looking up at her.

She picked Ruka up very carefully. Ruka pulled inside at once. Head in. Feet in. Everything in. The shell was very small and very smooth.

Ako-chan waited.

Then — slowly, very slowly — two eyes came back out. Small and dark. Looking right at her.

She did not hear a voice. Ruka did not speak. But she felt something. A small, still feeling. Certain.

She brought Ruka back to England.

That was just a few weeks ago. Now Ruka had a corner by the south wall of the garden, where the sun stayed the longest. Every morning, Ruka walked there. Slow and sure.

Dango watched but did not follow. He was the senior. He had other things to do.

Now Ako-chan sat on the back step and watched Ruka reach the sunny corner and stop. Ruka stood very still in the morning light. Not asleep. Just still.

"You like it here," said Ako-chan.

Dango came and sat beside her. He looked at Ruka too.

The three of them stayed that way for a little while. The garden was warm and quiet. Nobody was in a hurry.

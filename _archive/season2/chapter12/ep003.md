Dango was very clear about one thing.

He was the senior.

He was with Ako-chan longer than anyone. He knew the garden. He knew the house. He knew where the hedgehog slept and which branch of the oak tree the robin used most. He crossed the sea. He walked on ice. He saw the aurora and the blizzard and the inside of a very special ship.

Ruka did not do any of these things.

Ruka was a baby.

But Dango was not unkind. He was a senior, not a bully. He watched Ruka with a careful eye. When Ruka went too close to the fence, Dango walked over. He sat nearby. Just watching. Just in case.

Ruka did not seem to notice. Ruka just moved slowly along the fence, looking at every stone and leaf.

Ako-chan watched from the step.

"You are very good with her," she said.

Dango turned and looked at her. His tail moved once. Of course. What did she expect?

He went back to watching Ruka.

In the afternoon, Ruka stopped near the south wall and sat in a patch of sun. Dango lay down a little way off. He stretched out his long body. He put his chin on his paws.

The garden was quiet.

Ako-chan brought out a cup of tea. She sat on the step. The three of them stayed that way until the sun moved behind the oak tree and the light changed.

Dango's eyes were half closed. He was nearly asleep.

Ruka did not move at all.

Nobody needed to say anything.

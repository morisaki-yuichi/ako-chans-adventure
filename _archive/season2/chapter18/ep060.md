The seam opened.

Not with sound. Not with force. Just — opened. A section of the metal moved back, smooth and slow, and a space appeared in the side of the shape.

Light came from inside. Not blue-white. Not the glow of the cave. Something else — pale and very clear, like the light before sunrise, except still and even. Nothing like anything outside.

Ako-chan stepped through first.

Inside, it was wider than she expected. Much wider. The ceiling curved up high above her. The floor was smooth and not cold, even though the ice was just outside. Small points of light moved slowly along the walls in long quiet patterns.

She heard the others come in behind her.

Dango's boots on the floor. The soft sound of the pods and the capsule moving. Suke-san's quiet steps. Then Pen-san. Then General Adelie, last.

She turned around.

Everyone was inside.

They all stood and looked. No one spoke.

She thought of the ship, long ago — how the world outside had felt very large, and the space inside had felt small but safe.

This was different.

This was large and still and quiet. And it felt like something that had been waiting — not just for them to arrive, but for this moment. This decision. These people, these animals, this voice.

Obia stood near her.

"The door will close," it said. "Are you ready?"

Ako-chan looked at Dango. He looked back at her.

She looked at the others. One by one.

Then she looked forward.

"Yes," she said.

The seam closed behind them.

The cave outside was gone.

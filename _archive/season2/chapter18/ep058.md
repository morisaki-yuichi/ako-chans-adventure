"Vanishing," said Ako-chan.

She was not asking. She was thinking aloud.

"Animals that are vanishing. Animals that are gone."

"Yes," said Obia. "Animals whose voices can no longer be heard. Because they are no longer here."

Ako-chan looked at the machine. The light in the seam was still steady. The glow in the walls was still bright.

"But I can hear voices," she said.

"Yes."

"The animals that are here now — I can hear them. But the animals in the message. They are already gone. Their voices are already quiet."

"Not completely," said Obia. "Not in every time. There were animals that disappeared a long time ago. But some of them — not so long ago. Not so far back. Their voices are still in the world, if you know where and when to go."

Ako-chan was quiet.

"The machine can go to those times," said Obia. "It can go back. To when those animals were still alive. To when their voices were still there."

"And I can hear them."

"Yes. And I can take what you hear and send it forward. Into the future. To the person who made me. To the world that needs those voices."

Ako-chan thought about that for a moment.

She thought about the hedgehog in the garden. She thought about the whales — how their voices had felt in her chest like something very old and very large. She thought about Daru, and Gabu, and Suke-san, and Riro, and Pen-san.

All the voices she had heard.

Now there were voices she could not hear. Voices that had gone quiet. Voices no one could reach.

No one except her.

She looked at Obia.

"So we go back," she said.

"Yes."

"To find them."

"Yes."

She turned and looked at the others. All of them. Waiting.

Nobody moved.

General Adelie's words were still in the air. *Something is here. Under the ice. We have felt it. A long time.*

Ako-chan looked at the ice at her feet. The glow came from below — pale blue-white, pressing up through the floor. She could feel the signal in her chest, steady and very strong. Stronger than anywhere above.

Then General Adelie turned.

He walked forward into the wide space. Not fast. Each step placed carefully, the way he always walked.

Ako-chan followed. The others came behind her.

The space was large. The ceiling curved high above. The ice walls curved away on both sides. And somewhere ahead, in the dark past the glow, the signal was pulling — stronger with every step.

"Close," said Obia. "Very close."

Dango was at her side. She did not need to look. She could feel him there, keeping pace.

Pen-san moved up beside her. He was very quiet. He had walked this way before, she realized. He knew where they were going.

He took a few steps ahead and stopped.

The glow was brighter here — pooling up from somewhere lower, somewhere the ice went down.

The signal was at its strongest now. Not just in her chest. Everywhere. In the floor. In the walls. In the air around her.

"Obia," she said.

"Here," said Obia. "Right here."

Pen-san looked back at her. He did not say anything. He just looked, and then looked ahead again, into the dark where the glow was brightest.

Something was just ahead.

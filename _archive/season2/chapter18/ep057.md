The light was very still now.

Obia stood with its paw on the metal and did not speak for a long time. The seam glowed, steady and pale. The blue-white light in the cave walls was bright — much brighter than when they had come in.

No one spoke.

No one moved.

Then Obia said, very quietly:

"I remember."

Ako-chan waited.

"I remember now. Some of it. Not all of it. But some."

Obia's eyes were still on something far away. Its voice was slower than usual. Careful.

"Someone made this machine," it said. "A long time from now. In the future. I cannot see their face. But I know their hands. I know their work. Because they also——"

Obia stopped. Then: "They made me."

The cave was very quiet.

"I was made by the same person who made this machine. And when they made me, they left something inside me. A message. Locked, so I could not read it until I came here."

Pen-san made a very small sound.

"The message," said Obia. "It says: *Save the voices of the vanishing animals.*"

The words stayed in the cave.

Ako-chan heard them. She heard them a second time inside her head.

*Save the voices of the vanishing animals.*

She looked at the metal shape. She looked at Obia. She looked at her own hands.

The animals that no longer had voices. The animals whose voices had gone quiet, one by one, because they were gone from the world.

But she could still hear voices.

She could always hear voices.

And Obia was here. And the machine was here.

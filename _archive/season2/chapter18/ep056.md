The glow grew.

Not fast. Not loud. Steady — growing brighter in the walls, then in the floor, then in the metal itself. A faint light from somewhere deep inside the shape.

Obia had not moved.

Its paw was still flat on the surface. Its eyes were still. It stood without moving, without speaking.

Then Obia spoke. The voice was very quiet.

"Something is opening."

Nobody moved.

"The door," said Obia. Still not moving. Still not looking at anything Ako-chan could see. "The door — it is opening."

A thin line appeared in the surface of the metal. Barely visible at first — a seam, running from somewhere above to somewhere below. Light came from behind it. Pale and very clear.

Dango's breathing was the only sound.

Ako-chan wanted to speak. She did not.

Pen-san had pressed close to her again. She could feel him there, very warm, very still.

The seam grew more visible — like something coming into focus. The light behind it was steady. It did not flicker.

General Adelie stood at the front of the group. He had not moved from the moment Obia touched the surface. His eyes were sharp and quiet.

Obia's paw did not move from the metal.

"Something was locked," it said slowly. "Inside me. I knew it was there. I could feel the edge of it. I could not open it. But now——"

It stopped.

The light in the seam was steady now.

"Now it is opening," said Obia. "Someone put a door inside me. And this — this is the key."

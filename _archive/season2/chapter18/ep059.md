Ako-chan looked at each of them.

Dango. His ears were up, his eyes on her. She knew that look. He would go wherever she went.

Suke-san. Still, quiet, face turned toward her. Waiting.

Riro was at his capsule, but he had been listening. He always listened more than people thought. He looked back at her and did not move away.

Daru's green pod glowed near the wall. She could see his face through the glass, pressed close, eyes bright. Ready. He was always ready.

Gabu's orange pod. Steady. Watching.

And Ruka in the yellow pod — small, still. Her eyes calm and clear.

Pen-san was at her side. He had not moved since Obia spoke. Even Pen-san was quiet now.

General Adelie stood in front of the machine. Straight. Facing her.

He looked at her for a moment. Then he spoke.

"We have been waiting for you to come back," he said. "For this."

She met his eyes.

"I did not know what it was," said General Adelie. "When we found it. I could feel that it was waiting. I did not know for what." He was quiet for a moment. "Now I know."

Nobody spoke after that.

Ako-chan turned to the machine.

The seam was still there. Steady and pale. The light behind it did not flicker.

She thought of the words she had written long ago, in the red notebook, the last night of the first journey: *We are not finished.*

They were not.

She took a breath.

"We go in," she said.

Nobody moved against her.

Nobody said no.

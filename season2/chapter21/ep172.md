The light fades, and Ako-chan opens her eyes. She stands on cold, hard ground. The space between times is gone. They made the jump at last. They are somewhere real now. Solid ground holds her feet at last. The land is wide and gray and bare. A cold wind blows across the open ground.

Obia looks all around the empty land. "We are in the same time," it says. "The trail ends here." But the three friends are not in sight. There is no red Pod. There is no blue Pod. There is no small yellow shape. Ako-chan's heart jumps in her chest. She runs a few steps and stops. "Ruka! Daru! Gabu!" she calls. Her voice flies out across the wide land. But no voice calls back to her. The cold wind takes her words away.

The land runs flat on every side. Strange tall plants stand far apart. The sky hangs low, gray and heavy. Ako-chan and Obia walk on, side by side. Their feet press into the cold ground. They look left. They look right. They call the friends' names once more. Still no voice answers them. Then Ako-chan sees something far away. A huge dark shape stands on the land. It is taller than any plant or hill.

The shape does not move at all. It is dark and very still. Ako-chan stares hard across the land. Something is over there by that shape. She feels it deep inside her. "This way," she says to Obia. Her tired feet find new strength. The two of them turn toward the far shape. Whatever waits there, they will go to it.

She woke before the others.

The machine was quiet around her. The small lights moved. Dango was asleep against her side.

She opened herself to listen — the way she always did, slowly, wide, letting whatever was there come through.

The voices came. Animals far away, moving through their days. Birds somewhere above the ice. Small things deep in the water. She could reach the edges of them even through the machine's walls.

She listened more carefully.

Something was not right.

It was not the old disturbance. She knew that feeling well — the loud push from the south that had driven the animals wild, that she had followed all the way from England. That was gone. She was sure of that.

But something was still here.

A thread. Thin and wrong under the quiet, like a note that did not belong in the song. The animals near the top of the ice sounded just a little strained. Not badly. But not fully free either.

She could not name it.

Dango opened one eye and watched her.

"Not yet," she said. Not to him. Just out loud.

He sat up.

She opened the red notebook. She looked at the last entry she had written — *Day thirty-seven. Suke-san is with us. Riro is with us. We are all here. We go south.* She held the pen. She did not write.

She had thought that when the four voices were collected, something would settle. Something would go quiet the way the hedgehog's ground had gone quiet — the way it did after they came home the first time. She had felt that without knowing she felt it.

But this quiet was not right.

Something was still there.

She did not know yet what it meant.

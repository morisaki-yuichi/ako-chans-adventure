The light flares white around the five. Then comes the jump, hard and wild. It is the strongest jump of them all. It pulls and drags at Ako-chan's body. She holds her friends with all her might. The cold deepens. The light tears past. Then, all at once, it stops.

Ako-chan opens her eyes. The ice age is gone. The gray land is gone. They are back in the dark, cold cave. The old machine hums close beside them. They made it. They are truly home now. Then four shapes rush toward them fast. Suke-san. Dango. Pen-san. The General. "You came back!" Suke-san barks loudly. Dango spins around and around with joy. He cannot hold his happy feet still. Suke-san pushes his nose into Ako-chan's hand. The cave fills with warm, glad noise.

Pen-san leans down to little Ruka. He presses his cheek to her small shell. "I was so afraid," he says, soft and low. Daru and Gabu roll out to the others. Ruka leans into Pen-san's soft warmth. Pen-san is so much bigger than her now. The whole group draws in close and tight. Everyone is here. Everyone is safe. The General gives a long, proud salute. The long, dark wait is over now. Warmth returns to the cold cave at last.

Ako-chan holds her friends in the warm dark. Now all three voices are safe. The wolf. The seabird. The ancient one. The long task is done at last. And yet something still feels strange to her. She cannot name it. Not yet. The three voices came home. But the journey is not over yet. Not quite yet.

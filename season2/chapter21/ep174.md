Ako-chan kneels below the giant tortoise. The great shell rises high above her. All around them, the land is still. The cold wind drops to nothing. Then something reaches into Ako-chan. It is not a sound. It is not a word.

Ako-chan closes her eyes and listens. The voice is not words at all. It comes slowly, like a deep, warm wave. It moves through her, soft and wide. It feels very old. It feels very big. It feels very still. There is no fear in it. There is no anger in it. There is only deep, quiet peace. The ancient one is older than the hills. It saw the world grow and change. All of that flows into Ako-chan now. She holds the feeling close inside her. A tear runs down her quiet face.

Obia stands close beside her. Its eyes glow, bright and clear. It takes the old feeling from her heart. The blue light leaps up into the sky. "I sent it," Obia says. "The voice is safe now." It will live on, far away in time. Daru and Gabu watch in silence. Ruka looks up at the great shell. Daru does not say a word now. Even he has no words for this. No one moves. No one speaks at all.

And then it is done. The third voice is theirs at last. They came here by a broken jump. They did not choose this place or time. But they saved the last voice all the same. By chance, the long work is complete. Ako-chan opens her eyes, slow and full. She will carry it always.

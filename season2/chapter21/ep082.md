They stayed inside the machine for the night.

It was warm and wide and quiet. The small lights moved slowly along the walls. Outside, the cave was dark and cold.

Obia worked at the side panel. Everyone else settled. After a while, Obia turned.

"All four," it said. "The wolf. The Great Auks. The sea cows. The Meiolania. All of it is here. All of it recorded."

Ako-chan let out a slow breath.

Then Daru said: "I want to talk about it."

So they did.

Daru went first. "What did it feel like?" he asked Suke-san. "When it looked at you. I was in the pod. I could see it. But I could not feel what you felt."

Suke-san thought for a moment. "Old," he said. "Something older than names."

Pen-san talked about the Great Auks. "It made a sound from very deep inside," he said. "I did not know a bird could sound like that." He stopped. "I think I knew. But I did not know I knew."

General Adelie said nothing. But he was listening very carefully. Ako-chan could see that.

Riro talked about the sea cows. "The way it moved," he said. "Like nothing was in a hurry. Like the water had been there forever." He pressed his front paws together. "And the voice — wide. So wide."

Nobody talked about the Meiolania. They did not need to. Daru and Gabu were quiet about it. Ruka sat between them.

The lights moved on the walls. The machine held still around them.

"All four," Obia said again. Not to anyone. Just into the air.

Ako-chan closed her eyes and held all four voices in her mind, one after the other.

They were there. All of them still there.

The silence lasted a long time.

The lights on the walls kept moving. Slow and even, the same as always, as if nothing had changed.

Dango made the sound again — that low, private sound from somewhere deep in his chest. It was not a bark. He put it into the air and left it there.

Suke-san looked at the wall. Not at Obia, not at Ako-chan. At the smooth wall of the machine. His ears were back. He stayed very still.

Riro groomed. Not calmly — starting and stopping, starting again, moving too fast through his fur. His paws did not slow down.

Daru sat down. He did not say anything. That was the most unusual thing — Daru, with nothing to say.

Gabu was watching Obia. He had been watching since Obia began speaking. He did not look away. His eyes were steady and very still.

Pen-san moved one step closer to Ako-chan and stopped there. He stood very still beside her.

General Adelie looked at the floor in front of him. Just the floor.

Ruka had stepped away from Gabu's side. She was standing on her own, facing Obia, not in her shell but not moving either.

Nobody moved toward Obia. Nobody moved away. They just stayed where they were, each one alone inside the same quiet.

Ako-chan did not speak.

There was nothing to say yet. The words had landed. They were still landing. She could feel them settling into all the different quiet places inside her.

She looked at Obia.

Obia's eyes were steady and bright.

Dango pressed harder against her leg.

She put her hand on him and held it there.

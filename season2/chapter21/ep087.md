They spent the next day inside the machine and in the cave.

Nobody said: we are saying goodbye. But that was what everyone was doing.

Daru followed Obia.

Not closely — a few steps behind, stopping when Obia stopped. When Obia moved to the side panel, Daru found a spot nearby and stayed there. When Obia moved to check the capsules, Daru went with him.

After the fourth time, Obia turned and looked at Daru.

Daru looked back.

"I am just here," said Daru.

Obia did not say anything. It turned back to its work.

Daru stayed.

Gabu watched from the other side of the machine. He did not follow. But he did not look away from Obia for very long either. When Obia was at the panel, Gabu's eyes were on the panel. When Obia moved, Gabu's eyes moved.

Suke-san sat near the machine's door. Not blocking it. Just near it. He was very still.

Riro groomed all morning. He went through his fur slowly and completely — nothing missed, nothing rushed. By midday he looked exactly right. He began again.

Pen-san sat close to Obia at one point during the afternoon. Not asking to be held. Not talking. He just sat there and looked at the wall, very near Obia's feet.

Ako-chan stayed out of the way. She watched.

Ruka walked slowly across the machine floor. She pressed the front of her shell against Obia's foot and stayed there for a long time.

Obia did not move its foot.

When the light on Obia's panel had grown dim and the day was over, Daru walked back to Gabu and sat down beside him.

They did not speak.

The machine held them, small and warm, while outside the cave was dark and very old.

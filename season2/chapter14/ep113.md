It is late in the night. The work is over for now. The garden is dark and still. The half-built boat rests in the shadows. Its wide wings spread like two long arms. Ako-chan sits on a wooden box. She is tired, but she is not sleepy. Obia stands close beside the frame.

Then Obia goes very still. Its hands stop in the air. Its blue eyes flicker, on and off. Ako-chan knows this look by now. Obia is reaching inside itself. It looks for the locked memory again. The same one from those nights at home. Ako-chan holds her breath and waits. "Still locked," Obia says at last. "I cannot open it." Its voice is low and flat.

The garden is very quiet around them. No tools move now. No wind stirs the trees. A single light shines from the house. Ako-chan sits in Obia's pale blue glow. She watches the side of its face. The silver one seems far away now. It is here, but not all here. Part of it is locked somewhere deep.

Ako-chan thinks of the signal from the south. She thinks of the memory Obia cannot read. The two things sit in her mind together. She does not understand either one. The night feels large and strange around her. The boat waits there, half done.

"What is in there, Obia?" she asks softly. But Obia does not answer her. It does not know the answer. It cannot reach the thing inside. The memory stays shut, dark and deep.

Ako-chan does not push it. She rests her hand on Obia's cold side. Its eyes go dim, then steady. The night holds them both, quiet and close. Somewhere inside Obia, something waits. No one knows what it is. Not even Obia. Not yet.

Two nights later, Ako-chan looked out from the window before sleeping.

Obia's light was in the field.

It was a small blue-white point, moving slowly along the dark ground. Obia had been working since evening. Ako-chan did not know when it stopped. She had looked out twice before and it was always there.

She watched for a while.

Things had changed. She could see that much from the window. Two nights ago, the field was empty. Now there was a shape in the middle of it. Long. Low to the ground. Not finished — but already a shape. She could see the beginning of the wings.

Dango came to the window and stood beside her. He pressed his nose to the glass. His breath left a small cloud.

Outside, Obia's light moved and stopped. Moved and stopped.

Ruka was on the desk behind them. Ako-chan turned and looked. The small tortoise was still, facing toward the window.

Ako-chan went back to bed. She lay and listened. The night was quiet.

In the morning, she put on her coat before breakfast and walked to the field.

The shape was bigger than she had expected from the window. The body ran almost the full length of the field. The wide sections on each side were not finished, but she could see what they would be.

It looked like a boat. But it also looked like it could rise.

Obia was at the far end, working.

It did not look up.

She stood and watched for a long time. She did not speak.

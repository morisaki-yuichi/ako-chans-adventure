The night is deep now. Ako-chan should be asleep. But she stays out in the dark garden. The house behind her is quiet. Obia stands beside the half-built boat. Then a low hum starts inside it. The sound is back again.

The hum is stronger than before. Obia turns slowly toward the south. Its blue eyes flicker bright and fast. It listens to the signal for a long time. The pull feels heavy tonight, and clear. "I do not know why," Obia says. "But we must go." Its voice is calm and sure.

This is not like that first faint call. Back then, the signal was thin and far. Now it is deep and clear and strong. It does not fade in and out now. It stays. It pulls at them without a rest. Whatever waits is closer now.

Ako-chan feels it in the air too. Something reaches up from far away. It tugs at her, soft but strong. The whole garden seems to lean toward it. Even the cold night feels strange and awake. The hum rises and falls, slow and deep.

Inside the house, Dango wakes up. He comes to the door and stands there. His ears are up. His tail hangs low. He looks out at Obia in the dark. He knows the night is changing now. He does not lie back down.

The hum grows soft at last. But the pull does not go away. It is stronger now than at the start. Ako-chan looks out past the low fence. Something is out there, far to the south. Something wants them to come. And soon, they will go.

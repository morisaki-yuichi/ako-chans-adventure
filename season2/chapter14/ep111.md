The small Pod is ready now. But the big work starts today. Ako-chan and Obia stand in the garden. A wide drawing lies open on the grass. It shows a boat. A boat with wings.

They will build a flying boat. It must carry all of them, far to the south. Obia points at the drawing. "We start with the frame," it says. Ako-chan nods. Together they lift the first long bars. Obia's paws are quick and exact. Ako-chan holds the heavy parts in place. The frame rises slowly on the grass. It is big. Much bigger than the ship was.

Sweat runs down Ako-chan's face. The work is hard and slow. The bars are long and heavy. Obia does not get tired. It fits each piece in just the right place. The garden fills with the sound of metal. Ruka watches from her little Pod. She rolls a slow step, then stops to look. Dango sniffs at the new metal smell.

Ako-chan and Obia work well together. Obia plans each step with care. Ako-chan works fast and hard. They pass parts back and forth. They do not need many words. The frame takes shape between them. Ruka stays close in her Pod. She watches the two of them work.

Piece by piece, the frame grows. Ako-chan steps back to look at it. It is only the start. There is much more to build. Wings. An engine. A floor and walls. But the shape is there now, tall on the grass.

Evening comes to the quiet garden. The big frame stands in the low light. Ako-chan wipes her face and smiles. Their old ship sailed the cold seas. This time, the journey goes by sky.

The morning is bright and busy. The flying boat is almost done. Ako-chan and Obia work on the deck. Tools and parts lie all around. Dango watches them from the grass.

Then Dango wants to help too. He trots over to the pile of tools. He picks one up in his mouth. It is a big metal wrench. He carries it proudly across the garden. His tail wags fast and hard. But he bumps a box of small parts. They spill out across the ground. Bolts and screws roll everywhere.

Dango drops the wrench with a clang. He sniffs at the rolling bolts. Then he chases one across the lawn. His thick legs kick up the grass. His ears bounce as he runs. Parts go this way and that. The neat pile is a mess now.

Ako-chan turns around and sees the mess. She cannot stay cross with him. She laughs out loud. "Oh, Dango," she says, smiling. He looks up at her, proud and happy. He thinks he helped a lot. Ruka sits still by the wall. She is calm in all the noise. She does not move at all.

Ako-chan kneels and rubs his ears. She picks up the bolts, one by one. Dango lies down close beside her. He drops his head onto her knee. He is happy just to be near. The wrench rests where he left it.

The garden is loud and warm today. Soon they will fly far to the south. But not yet. Today there is sun and grass. Today there is a happy dog. It is a calm, good day before the road.

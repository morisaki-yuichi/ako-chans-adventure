The frame stands tall in the garden now. It rose part way yesterday. Today they build the wings. Ako-chan and Obia start early. The morning air is cool and damp.

They lift a long, flat piece. It is wide and light. It is one wing. Obia holds it to the frame. Ako-chan turns the bolts tight. The wing locks into place. They lift the other wing next. Now the boat has two wide wings. Then they fit the engine in the back. It is heavy and dark and round. Piece by piece, the shape comes alive.

The garden is full of sound. Metal taps on metal. Tools turn and click. The smell of oil hangs in the air. Ako-chan's hands are black with it. Obia works without a rest. Their hands move close, then apart. They pass each tool between them. They hardly speak at all.

Ako-chan steps back to rest her arms. They feel heavy and sore. She looks up at the wide wings. Soon they will lift the boat. Soon they will carry everyone south. She can almost feel the sky already. Then she picks up her tools again.

The sun goes down. The garden grows dark. But the work does not stop. Obia's eyes light the metal with a soft glow. Ako-chan works on by that pale blue light. Luca watches from her Pod. Dango sleeps near the warm engine. The wings stretch far above them.

At last, both wings are on. The frame is not just a frame now. It has wings. It has an engine. In the dim garden, it stands almost ready. Ako-chan looks up at the great shape. Something big is taking form here. And it is theirs.

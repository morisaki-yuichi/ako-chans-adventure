Ako-chan woke early. The garden was still and cool.

Obia was at the table. Something was spread out in front of it. Not paper. A flat pale surface, and on it, lines of light moved slowly. Measurements. Shapes. Numbers.

"What is that?" asked Ako-chan.

"A plan," said Obia. "For what we need to build."

She came closer and looked. The shape was long. Two wide sections, one on each side. Like a bird's wings.

"A flying boat," said Obia. "To go south. And to collect everyone before the south."

Ako-chan looked at the numbers. "It is very big."

"Yes."

"Have you made one before?"

"No," said Obia. "But I know what it needs."

Dango came out from the hall. He sniffed the edge of the table, then sat down beside Ako-chan.

"Where will you build it?" she asked.

"Not here," said Obia. "There is a field past the hedge, to the north. I looked at it last night. It is wide enough."

She thought for a moment. The plan on the table showed something larger than the ship from before. Much larger.

"Will you need help?" she asked.

Obia looked at her. "I will need someone to hold parts," it said. "Some things cannot be done alone."

Ako-chan put on her coat. Dango stood up.

The three of them walked out through the gate. The sky was grey and getting lighter. Past the hedge, the field opened up. Flat. Wide. Brown and still.

Obia walked to the center and stopped. It looked at the sky, then down at the ground.

"Yes," it said. "This will do."

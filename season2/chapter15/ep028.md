Gabu came forward.

Three steps. Stop. Three steps. Stop.

The same as before. The same careful pace. The same dark eyes studying each thing in turn. He reached the edge of the shade and stopped.

He looked at Ako-chan. Then at Dango. Then at Obia.

Then at Daru.

Daru was watching Gabu with very wide, very bright eyes. He did not speak. That was unusual for Daru.

Gabu looked at Daru for a long time.

"Daru," he said. One word.

"Gabu," said Daru. One word back.

Then Gabu turned. He looked at the flying boat. At the open hatch.

Ruka was there. At the edge of the hatch. Her small face was in the light.

Gabu looked at her.

He was still for a very long time. Nobody moved.

Ruka did not pull back. She looked at Gabu with her steady small eyes.

Finally, Gabu looked at Ako-chan.

"Small," he said.

"Yes," said Ako-chan. "Very small."

"Italy?"

"Yes. From Puglia."

Gabu turned back to Ruka. He looked at her for another long moment. Then he walked forward — three steps, stop — until he was at the bottom of the ramp. He looked up at her.

"Hello," he said.

Ruka looked at him. Then she walked forward. Very slow. One step at a time. Down the ramp. She stopped in front of Gabu.

They looked at each other. Two tortoises. One very small. One not as small.

The sand was hot. The red walls stood on both sides.

Daru, beside Ako-chan, was completely quiet.

She did not think she had ever seen Daru completely quiet before.

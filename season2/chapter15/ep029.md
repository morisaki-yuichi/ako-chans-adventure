That afternoon, Daru talked to Gabu.

"The lizards came back," said Daru. "On my coast. Did they come back here too?"

"Yes," said Gabu.

"When? I counted. On my coast they came back in the spring, about forty days after—"

"Spring," said Gabu. "Same."

"Did you count the days?"

"Yes."

Daru stopped. He looked at Gabu. "Of course you counted the days," he said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

They were sitting in the shade of the flying boat's wing, side by side. Bigger than before — both of them. Still young, but not the babies they had been in Season 1. Their shells were fuller. Their colors a little deeper.

Ruka was between them.

She had walked there herself and stopped. Neither Daru nor Gabu had asked her to come. Neither had moved away.

Daru looked at her. Then at Gabu. Then back at her.

"She is very small," he said.

"Yes," said Gabu.

"Smaller than we were."

"Yes," said Gabu. "Smaller."

A long pause.

"Is she all right?" said Daru.

"Yes," said Gabu. "She is all right."

Ako-chan was sitting a little way back, watching. Dango was beside her. He was watching too.

Ruka sat very still between the two of them. The red walls of Wadi Rum stood all around. The evening light was coming now — orange on the stone, long shadows on the sand.

Gabu looked at Ruka for a while. Then he moved, just slightly. Closer.

Ruka did not move away.

Ako-chan looked at Dango. Dango looked at her. His tail moved once.

She did not say anything.

The flying boat rose from the Dalmatian coast in the late morning and turned south, then east.

Daru was at the window. He had been there since they lifted off. He watched the coast of Croatia get smaller. He watched the sea come. Then the sea went too, and land came back — brown and wide and flat.

"What is that?" he asked.

"Desert," said Ako-chan.

"I have never seen a desert," said Daru.

"Gabu has," she said.

Daru looked at the brown land below and said nothing for a while.

The land changed. Paler. Drier. The roads were white lines in the dust. No trees. Nothing green.

"It is very empty," said Daru.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

Dango was on the cabin floor, watching Daru watch the window. He looked patient.

Then the color changed.

Daru pressed his face to the glass.

The land below turned red. Not brown-red. A deep, real red — the color of something very old and very solid. And above the red land, tall walls of rock rising straight up against the blue sky.

"What is that color?" said Daru. He said it quietly.

"Red sandstone," said Obia. "Wadi Rum. We are close."

The flying boat dropped lower. The red walls were enormous now — taller than anything in Croatia. The pale sand between them was still and flat.

Daru was not talking.

That was unusual.

He just looked.

The flying boat came down on a wide flat area between two rock walls. When the door opened, hot dry air came in. It smelled like stone and sun.

Daru's nose moved.

"Gabu lives here?" he said.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

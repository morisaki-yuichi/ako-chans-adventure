The flying boat comes down through grey mist. Green mountains rise on every side. The air is cool and damp. Ako-chan knows this land at once. It is Japan. The slopes are deep and green.

The boat rests in a high quiet valley. Ako-chan steps out into the open air. The ground is wet and dark with rain. She breathes the air in slow and deep. It smells of earth and pine and stone. Somewhere up there, a friend waits. She looks up at the green slopes. She listens hard for any small sound. But the mountain holds its quiet still.

Dango stands very close beside her leg. His nose lifts high into the cool air. His ears turn slow towards the dark hills. He knows this place. He knows this smell. He came here once, three years ago. A low sound rises in his throat. It is not fear. It is more like hope.

Daru and Gabu wait inside their Pods. The red Pod and the blue Pod are still. Even Daru is quiet now. The valley is wide and grey. Mist drifts low across the wet grass. A single drop falls from a high branch. No bird sings in the grey, still air. The only sound is the soft, falling mist. The mountain keeps its secret for now.

Ako-chan waits at the edge of the trees. She does not call out a name. She only watches the slopes above. Somewhere, a friend feels them here too. She feels him near, just out of reach. But the mountain shows no one yet. Not yet. The last friend stays out of sight.

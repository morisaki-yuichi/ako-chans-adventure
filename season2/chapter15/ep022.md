The flying boat came down in a flat open space near the coast. The limestone was pale and hot in the afternoon sun. Ako-chan stepped out first.

The air hit her. Salt. Rock. Heat coming up from the ground. She knew this smell.

Dango jumped down behind her. He put his nose to the limestone and breathed in slowly. He looked up.

"I remember this," he said.

"Yes," said Ako-chan. "Croatia."

The coast stretched out below them — white rock, dark blue water, long flat light. She had walked here once before. She had been going south then. She had not known what was waiting for her.

Now she knew who was here.

"Is the signal strong?" she asked.

"It is there," said Obia. "I am still looking for the exact place."

Ako-chan went back to the hatch and checked on Ruka. The small tortoise was in her corner, eyes open, watching the new light come in through the door.

"We are in Croatia," Ako-chan told her.

Ruka did not move. But her eyes went to the door and stayed there.

Ako-chan sat down on the warm limestone near the flying boat and looked out at the water. The sun was low now. The Adriatic turned gold at the edges. A few birds crossed high above and went south.

Dango came and sat beside her. Not close. Just near.

Obia stood very still at the edge of the flat rock, facing south.

"Tomorrow morning," it said, after a long time. "I will know the place by then."

The sun went down. The rock held its warmth.

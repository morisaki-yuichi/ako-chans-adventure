The morning was still and clear. The field past the hedge was quiet. The flying boat sat at the center of it, waiting.

Obia moved to the front. A low sound started — quiet at first, then steady. Dango was at Ako-chan's feet, ears up.

The flying boat rose. Smooth and slow. The grass below bent flat. The hedge fell away. Then the garden, small and green from above. Ako-chan looked down. She could see the oak tree from above. Just a dark circle of leaves.

Then England was below them.

She turned. Inside the cabin, in a warm corner near the back wall, Ruka was still. A small padded space with soft orange light. Ako-chan had set it up the night before. Ruka's eyes were open. She was watching.

"Is she all right?" Ako-chan asked.

"Yes," said Obia.

Dango settled on the floor beside her. He looked out the window.

The sea came below them. Wide and bright. Then the coast of another land — green hills, pale roads, brown and gold fields. They were moving fast. Faster than any train.

"How long?" she asked.

"A few hours," said Obia.

She looked out. She thought about Daru. The first time she saw him, he came running straight to her feet. He said: *I saw you coming. From up on the ridge. You took a long time.*

Something moved in her chest.

Below, the sea turned a brighter blue. The land at the edges was pale and rocky. White limestone, broken in the sun.

"The Adriatic," said Obia.

Ako-chan pressed her hand flat on the window. Croatia was below.

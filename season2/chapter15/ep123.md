The mist hangs low among the trees. Ako-chan waits in the cool valley. The others stand close behind her. Then the grass moves high on the slope. Something steps out from the dark pines. A small, quick shape on careful feet.

It is Suke-san. He comes down the hill. He is bigger now, but still small and quick. His red-brown fur is thick and warm. His sharp fox face turns side to side. He runs to Ako-chan first of all. But then he stops and checks them all. His careful eyes move over each one. Ako-chan. Dango. Obia. The Pods. He makes sure every friend is safe.

Then Suke-san turns to Dango. The two dogs stand and face each other. Both are grown now. Both are changed. For a long moment, neither one moves. The cool mist drifts between their feet. The mist beads soft on both their coats. Neither dog looks away for long. "You are big now," Suke-san says at last.

Dango does not bark. He makes no sound at all. He steps in close to Suke-san's side. He presses his head against the smaller dog. The touch is warm and slow and sure. Ako-chan hears the meaning clear. You too, the quiet press says. Suke-san's curled tail lifts a little higher.

Suke-san takes his place by Ako-chan. He stands at her right, a step behind. It is the same spot as always. His sharp eyes rest, calm at last. Daru and Gabu watch from their Pods. The valley feels warm and full now. The last friend is back with them now. The whole family stands here once more.

Daru did not stop talking.

He talked about the seasons. He talked about the lizards coming back. He talked about a new crack in the rock he had found and a very good flat stone above the water and the color of the sea in winter when it was different from summer.

"Did you know the sea changes color in winter?" he said. "I never knew that before. Now I know."

He was walking beside Ako-chan on the path back to the flying boat. Bigger than before. Same speed. Same energy. Same Daru.

Dango walked behind them. He did not say anything. But his tail was moving.

When they reached the flying boat, Daru ran up the ramp first. He was inside before Ako-chan had taken three steps. She heard him stop.

She went in.

Daru was very still in the middle of the cabin. He was looking at the warm corner near the back wall.

Ruka was there. She was looking at Daru.

"That is a tortoise," said Daru. He said it quietly. Very quietly, for Daru.

"Yes," said Ako-chan. "That is Ruka."

"A baby tortoise," he said.

"Yes."

Daru took one step forward. Then he stopped. He looked at Ruka. Ruka looked at Daru.

Ruka pulled back, just a little. Her head went in.

Daru stayed very still. He waited. He was not good at waiting, usually. But he waited now.

After a long moment, Ruka's head came back out.

She looked at him.

Daru looked back.

"Hello," he said. Very soft.

Ako-chan stood in the doorway. She did not say anything. Outside, the sea was bright and still.

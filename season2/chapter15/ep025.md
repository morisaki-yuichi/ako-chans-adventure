In the morning, Daru had a plan.

"There is a rock," he said. "My rock. I want to show it. Before we go."

He looked at Ruka.

Ako-chan carried Ruka down the ramp. Daru led them along the coast path. Not far — maybe ten minutes. Then he stopped at a flat, pale limestone ledge above the water.

"This rock," said Daru. "This is the best rock. The best on this whole coast."

Ako-chan set Ruka down on it.

Ruka walked very slowly to the edge of the rock. She looked down at the water. Then she turned and looked at the rock itself. Then she turned again and looked at Daru.

Daru looked back.

Ako-chan was standing a little back, watching them. There was something in the way Daru watched Ruka look at his things. Something quiet. A pride she had not seen in him before. Or maybe it was something new — something that had grown over the last few years, the same way he had grown.

He was a senior now. He had not known that before. He seemed to know it now.

Ruka sat down on the rock. Very still. Small in the middle of all that pale limestone and bright sea.

Daru walked to the edge and stood beside her. He looked at the water.

Ako-chan watched the two of them.

"We need to go soon," she said.

Daru looked at the coast for a long time. The white rock. The dark blue water. The long light. He had been here every day for years.

He turned.

"I am ready," he said.

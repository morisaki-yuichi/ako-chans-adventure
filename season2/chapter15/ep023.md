In the morning, Obia had found the place.

"South," it said. "Along the coast path. About two kilometers."

They left Ruka in her warm corner inside the flying boat. Ako-chan left the small inner light on for her.

The coast path was narrow and rocky. The sun was already strong. The sea below was very blue. Dango walked ahead, then came back, then walked ahead again. He was looking for something.

Ako-chan knew what.

After about an hour, Obia stopped.

"Close now," it said. "The signal is very close."

Ako-chan looked ahead. The path turned around a low rock face. There was scrub on the right side. On the left, the sea.

Dango stopped. His ears went up. His head was very still.

"There," he said.

Ako-chan looked.

Far down the path, at the curve where the rock turned — something was moving. Small. Fast. Very fast.

It was coming toward them.

She knew that run. She knew exactly that run. Straight. No pause. No slowing.

Dango sat down. That was all he did. He just sat.

Ako-chan stood still. The shape got bigger. Bigger.

Then it was at her feet.

Daru pressed against her shoes. He was breathing fast. He was warm. And he was bigger — not by a lot, but she could feel it. More weight. More shell. He pressed harder than before.

"You came back," he said. Between breaths.

Ako-chan knelt down on the limestone.

"You came back."

She put both hands around him.

"Yes," she said. "We came back."

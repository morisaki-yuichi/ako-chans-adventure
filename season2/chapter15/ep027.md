Morning in Wadi Rum.

The air was very dry. The sand between the rock walls was pale and still. The sun was already strong, and it came straight down from a sky with no clouds.

Daru stepped off the ramp. He looked down at the sand. He touched it with one foot. Then he looked up at the red walls.

"Very hot," he said.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

"Much hotter than Croatia."

"Much hotter."

Daru walked a short distance, stopped, turned, and came back. He did this several times. The sand was too hot to stay on long. He went back to the shade of the flying boat's wing.

Obia was scanning.

"The signal is close," it said. "Within one hundred meters."

Ako-chan looked at the rocks. She knew how this would go. Gabu would not come out when called. Gabu would come when he was ready.

She sat down in the shade and waited.

Dango sat beside her. He looked at the rocks.

Daru sat too, for once.

They waited. The heat pressed down.

Then Dango's ears moved. Very slowly, he turned his head to the left.

There was a large orange rock, two meters high, five meters away. Nothing had been there a moment ago.

There was something there now.

Small. Dark shell. Very still. On a low ledge beside the rock.

Two eyes. Dark and steady and open.

Not moving. Not looking away.

Just watching.

Ako-chan felt something go still inside her. Something warm.

"Hello, Gabu," she said.

The eyes did not move. But they did not look away either.

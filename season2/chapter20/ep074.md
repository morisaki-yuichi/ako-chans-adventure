The air smelled of dry earth and something older.

Daru looked around. Red ground. Pale sky. Dry yellow grass that moved in a soft wind. A tree with thin grey-green leaves. Far away, dark hills.

Not ice. Not fog. Not ocean. Nothing they had seen before.

He looked down.

Ruka was there — on the red earth, very still. She had pulled her head all the way inside her shell. Her legs were in. Everything was in. Just the shell on the red ground.

Gabu stood beside her.

Daru pressed himself against the shell. "Ruka," he said. Very softly. No answer.

He looked at Gabu.

Gabu looked back. Then he looked at the land around them — slowly, one direction, then another. Checking. Reading. His eyes moved the way they always did when he was deciding something.

The wind came again. The dry grass bent. The tree's leaves made a soft sound.

They were alone — just the two of them and a silent shell on the red earth. No machine, no Ako-chan, no Obia. Nothing else they knew.

Daru had never felt small in quite this way before. Not afraid, exactly. But very, very small.

He pressed closer to Ruka.

Gabu looked south. His eyes stopped moving.

Far away, near the red rocks and the dry grass — something was there. Large. Low to the ground. Very slow.

Gabu took one step forward, putting himself between the shape and Ruka's shell.

Daru saw it too. He moved to stand at Gabu's side.

Neither spoke. Both watched.

The shape came slowly closer.

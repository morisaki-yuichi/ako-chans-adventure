The seam closed.

The floor moved — a long, slow push upward, like something very large waking up underneath them.

Then it started.

This one was different. Heavier. The light inside the machine went wrong — not off, but pressed and strange, as if they were moving through something much thicker than before. The walls made a sound she had not heard before.

Suke-san was on his feet before she could think.

"Something is wrong," he said. "Now."

Then the crack.

Not in the metal. In the air itself — a white line, too bright, that split the space where nothing should split. It moved fast. It widened. The sound it made had no name she knew.

Suke-san made a sound. Not words. Something older than words.

Dango lunged. She had never seen him move that fast. His front leg reached toward Ruka —

He missed.

Ruka was gone.

Half a second of silence.

Then Daru ran. No sound. No pause.

Gabu was already moving. Gabu, who never moved before thinking, who had always watched and waited and counted — was already at the edge of the crack.

Together they went through. Without stopping. Without looking back.

The crack closed.

One hard shake. Then stillness. The lights came back. The walls went quiet.

Ako-chan stood in the space where three of them had been.

Her hands were very cold.

She looked at the empty floor. Then at Obia.

"Find them," she said. Her voice did not shake.

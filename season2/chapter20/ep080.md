They did not leave right away.

The land was wide and quiet. The dry grass moved in a soft wind. The red rock was warm. Above them, the pale sky held still.

Nobody pushed to go.

Suke-san walked out a little and stood at the edge of the open ground. His nose was up. He turned very slowly in the wind, reading things that none of the others could read. After a while he came back and sat near Ako-chan. He did not say what he had found.

Dango stayed at her side the whole time.

Ruka was still in her hands. She had not gone back into her shell.

Daru and Gabu sat close together. They were not talking. That alone was unusual for Daru. He just sat with his eyes on the red earth.

"This place," said Pen-san, very quietly. "It is very old."

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

General Adelie stood near the machine's door, looking at the land. He looked the way he always did when something mattered — still and careful, taking it all in.

Ako-chan thought about the voice she had received. All four voices now, held somewhere in Obia and in the machine and in whatever was waiting for them in the future. Four voices that had been gone from the world.

Now they were somewhere.

She looked at the red earth one more time.

"Ready," she said.

One by one, they walked back to the machine. Daru went in first, then Gabu. Pen-san. General Adelie. The others.

Ako-chan went last.

She looked at the old ground. Then she stepped inside.

The seam closed.

They sat together on the red earth — all of them.

Daru talked first. Fast at first, the way he always did. The crack, the fall, the land they landed in. The thing that had come through the grass. How big it was. How old it looked.

Gabu let him talk. When Daru ran out of words, Gabu took over. Slowly. One thing at a time.

Ako-chan listened.

She opened herself the way she did when she listened to animals — wide and still, letting their voices in. She did not try to catch it. She just listened.

Daru's voice was bright and full of the red earth and the fear and the wonder of standing in front of something so ancient and not running. She felt his heart move through his words.

Gabu's voice was different. Deep and slow. And inside it — carried carefully, the way Gabu carried everything — was the sound the Meiolania had made. The warm sound from somewhere deep inside the great creature. Wide and low. The same ancient ground. The same shell. The same patience.

She held it. She let it fill her the way the sea cow's voice had filled her. Different, but the same kind of old.

Then she nodded to Obia.

Obia went still. Then: "Done."

Nobody spoke for a moment.

"Four," said Riro, very quietly.

"Four," said Pen-san.

Ako-chan held Ruka a little closer.

Four voices now. All of them given. The wolf. The Auks. The sea cows. And this — the oldest of all, from the oldest ground she had ever stood on.

She did not know what to say.

So she said nothing.

The gray quiet presses all around them. It has no edge and no end. Ako-chan cannot bear it. She turns to Obia at her side. Her whole body shakes hard. The empty light gives back no sound. There is only Obia, and her fear.

"Find them!" she cries out loud. Her voice breaks in the empty air. "Find them! Please, Obia!" She grips its smooth, cold side. Her hands will not stay still. Her eyes are wide and wet. Tears spill down her face. They fall into the empty light.

Then the worst thought comes. This is her fault. Suke-san wanted to stop. The General wanted to stop. They were afraid for her. But she said, "I will go." She chose this herself. She pushed them all on. And now the three are lost.

Her shoulders shake harder now. The tears keep falling fast. "I chose this," she says. Her voice is small and broken. "They were right. But I did not listen." She cannot take it back. Now Ruka is somewhere far away. Daru and Gabu are gone too. And it is all because of her.

The gray holds her sobs close. No one can answer her here. There is no ground to fall on. There is no friend to run to. There is only the quiet, and Obia. Its glow is all she has. She feels more alone than ever.

Ako-chan covers her face with her hands. The dark guilt rises up inside her. It is cold and deep and strong. It tries to pull her down. It wants to swallow her whole. She sinks down into the dark.

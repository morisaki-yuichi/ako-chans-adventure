Nobody moved for a while.

Obia stood at the panel, checking one thing and then another. Its body was very still.

"Tell us," said Ako-chan.

"The fourth target is Ice Age Australia," said Obia. "Long before the wolf. Long before the Auks and the sea cows. The jump is the longest of all." Obia turned to look at her. "After each jump, the turbulence has grown. After this one — I cannot say how strong it will be."

Suke-san stood. "Then we do not go."

Ako-chan looked at him.

"Three times," Suke-san said. "Three times, and each one harder. My instinct says stop."

Dango pressed close to her other side.

She thought about the fourth voice. Older than the wolf's cold forests, older than the Auks' grey rock, older than the sea cows' slow water. Something that had been in the world long, long before any of them. And it had never been heard — not yet.

"We go," she said.

Suke-san was quiet for a moment. "You are sure."

"Yes."

He made a sound — low, in his throat. Not a yes. Not a no. Something that said: I hear you. I still do not like it.

General Adelie stepped forward. "Then we prepare."

Obia turned back to the panel. The lights along the walls shifted slightly — the machine making itself ready.

Ruka came out of her yellow pod. She stood at the edge of it, very still, and looked at Ako-chan.

Suke-san pressed against her right side. He said nothing more.

But he pressed a little harder than usual.

No one moves for a while. The cave is calm and warm. But Obia is still by the machine. It does not come to the others. Its blue eyes flicker, low and slow. Ako-chan stands up slowly. "Obia?" she says. "What is it?"

Obia is very still. It listens to something far away. The animals are still not calm. Ako-chan feels it too, faint and strange. A low hum runs under the ice. It runs deep, below the old, cold floor. It does not go away with the voices. Obia turns its head, slow and careful. "Something is still wrong," it says. The words fall into the quiet cave. Ako-chan's heart sinks low. She wanted the task to be over.

The friends feel the change too. Suke-san lifts his nose and reads the cold air. His ears strain forward, then flatten back. A low growl starts in his chest and dies. He does not like what the air carries. No one understands. They saved the voices. They did the whole task. So why does the air still feel wrong? The machine hums on, low and deep. Dango leans hard against Ako-chan's leg. His whole body trembles against her. He lets out a low, soft whine. He does not understand, but he feels it too.

Ako-chan looks at the old machine. The blue light moves across its metal. The sound does not feel finished. They came so far for this. They saved every voice. And yet the trouble is still here. It was here all along. The warm calm slips out of the cave. Ako-chan wraps her arms around herself and waits.

The flying boat lifted into the grey Antarctic sky.

Nobody spoke.

The engine was steady. The wings cut the cold air. Below, the white ice fell away — flat and wide and very large, getting smaller and smaller until it was only white below the clouds.

Ako-chan sat at the window. Dango pressed against her side. He had been pressed against her since the cave. She had not moved him.

Suke-san sat a little to the right and back — his place, always his place. He looked out his own window. His ears were forward. His eyes were on the clouds below.

Riro sat near his water capsule. Very still. His fur was smooth and full. He was not grooming. He just sat with his paws pressed together and looked at nothing in particular.

Daru was at the front window, looking down. He had been almost quiet since the cave — for Daru, the longest silence she had ever heard from him.

Gabu sat beside him. He watched the ice become sea below. He said nothing. He stayed close.

Pen-san was between Ako-chan and General Adelie. He was looking at his feet.

General Adelie sat straight. Eyes forward.

The ice gave way to dark water. Later, the water gave way to clouds.

Ako-chan put her hand into her coat pocket and touched the red notebook. She had done this every hour or so since the cave — just to feel it there.

She looked out at the sky.

It was grey and wide and very large.

The sound of the engine was the only sound.

They all looked out their windows and let the sky carry them north.

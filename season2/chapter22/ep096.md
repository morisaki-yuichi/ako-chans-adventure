Nobody moved.

They stood in the cave and looked at the machine.

A long time passed. The blue ice walls glowed. The cold air held. Nothing happened.

Then the machine began to change.

Not fast. Not loud. It was slow — the way light changes in a room as the sun moves. The great hull grew thin. The outline got soft. The shadow under it grew lighter.

And then it was gone.

The cave was empty where it had been. Same ice. Same floor. Same blue glow on the walls. But nothing else.

She could not say exactly when it happened. One moment the machine was there. Then it was not.

Dango made a very small sound. Then nothing.

Nobody else made any sound at all.

Ako-chan looked at the empty floor. She had thought she might feel something — a sound, a sign, a shift in the air. But there was nothing. Just the cave, and the cold, and the absence of something very large.

She breathed in slowly.

The thread under the ice was gone. She was sure. That thin wrong feeling she had carried for so long — the pull from the south, the note beneath every other note — was not there.

For the first time in a very long time, she heard only what was near her.

She turned around.

Everyone was there. Suke-san on her right. Dango on her left. Pen-san and General Adelie beside each other. Riro, still. Daru and Gabu with Ruka's yellow pod between them.

All of them looking at her.

She looked back at each of them.

She was glad they were there.

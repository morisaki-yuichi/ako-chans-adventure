No one speaks in the cave. The truth sits heavy in the cold air. Obia will go. Obia will not come back. The machine hums low and deep. The friends stay close together. The blue light does not move.

Then, slowly, the friends come to Obia. Dango walks to Obia's feet. He lies down there, quiet and low. He does not bark. He does not whine. He just stays near. Then Suke-san steps forward too. For the first time, he leans his body into Obia. Once he did not trust Obia at all. In Japan, he watched it with sharp, wary eyes. He kept his distance for so long. Now he presses against it, warm and sure. All his old wariness is gone now. He stays pressed close to Obia's side.

Ako-chan cannot hold it in. Her eyes fill with tears. They fall, slow and warm, down her face. She does not wipe them away. Her shoulders shake without a sound. Daru and Gabu watch from their Pods. Ruka stays small and still beside them. Pen-san bows his big head low. His dark eyes are wet and large. The General stands stiff and silent. No one says a word. There is nothing left to say. The weight of it fills the cold cave.

Obia stands among them, calm and still. It lets every friend stay close. Its blue eyes move over each face. The machine hums, low and steady. The goodbye is very close now. It waits, just there, in the cold dark. Soon Obia will step into the light. But for now, they hold it close. No one wants this moment to come.

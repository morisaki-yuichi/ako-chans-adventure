The cave was very still.

Suke-san had not moved from his place. Right of Ako-chan, a little back, a little to the side. He stood there, ears straight, looking at Obia.

Then he moved.

He walked forward — steadily, one paw at a time. He crossed the cold floor and stopped in front of Obia.

He looked up at Obia. Obia looked down at him.

Ako-chan watched.

She had known Suke-san since Japan. She remembered the first day — his ears going flat, the clear flat words: *Not that thing.* He had said it more than once. He had meant it.

She remembered the long hard time when nobody trusted Obia. Suke-san had been the one who saw first — who understood, before any of them, that there was no bad thing inside Obia. Only something alone and steady, doing what it was made to do.

He had been the first to stand beside Obia when no one else would.

Now he was standing here again.

A long time passed. The cave lights moved slowly. The cold held still around them.

Then Suke-san lowered his head.

All the way down. Slowly. Without hurrying. The same way the Meiolania had lowered its head on the oldest ground — completely, with nothing held back.

He stayed there.

Obia placed one hand on top of Suke-san's head. Very lightly. Just resting there.

Suke-san raised his head again.

He looked at Obia for one more moment. Then he turned and walked back to his place.

He did not look back.

Ako-chan could not speak. There were no words for what she had just seen.

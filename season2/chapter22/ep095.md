Obia turned toward the machine.

The seam opened — smooth and silent, the way it always did. Pale light came through from inside. Even and clear, like the sky just before sunrise.

Obia took one step in.

Then it stopped.

It turned — not all the way, just enough. Its eyes found Ako-chan once more across the cave.

She looked at it.

It looked at her.

A moment passed. Long and quiet and full.

Then Obia turned back, and went inside.

Ako-chan did not move.

She could still see through the seam — the curved ceiling, the small lights moving along the walls, all of it still and ready. She could see Obia's back. She could see its eyes going dim as it went deeper in.

Then the seam began to close.

Slowly. Without sound.

The pale light grew thinner. The opening grew smaller.

Dango pressed hard against her leg. She put her hand on him.

The seam closed.

The machine was dark.

She looked at the place where the opening had been. The hull was smooth and whole. No mark. No line. No sign that anything had moved.

She breathed in.

She breathed out.

The cave was very quiet.

She stood there. Dango pressed harder. She kept her hand on him and did not move.

Somewhere very far below the ice, something was turning. She did not know if she truly felt it or if she only wanted to feel it.

But she stood there and let herself believe it.

Something was beginning to close.

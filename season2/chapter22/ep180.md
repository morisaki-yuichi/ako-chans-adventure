The cold air keeps leaking from the machine. The cave is very quiet now. Everyone looks at Obia. Obia stands by the open door of light. Its blue eyes are still. The pale glow spills across the ice. Ako-chan waits, tense and afraid. "Can we close it?" she asks. "Can we shut the door?"

Obia is quiet for a moment. Then it speaks, slow and clear. "Someone must go," it says. "And close the door from the other side." The words are heavy. They fall into the cold cave. To close the door, someone must cross to the future. Someone must shut it from over there. And that someone cannot come back. There is no other way to close it. Ako-chan's face goes stiff. Her hands go cold and tight. She understands now. One of them will not return.

No one moves. No one speaks. Dango looks up at Ako-chan. He searches her face for an answer. He finds none, and lays his ears back flat. Suke-san stands very still beside her, his eyes on Obia. The machine glows, cold and blue. Its light fills the silent cave. The friends look at each other, slow and afraid. The leaking cold touches every face. The hum of the machine grows louder. No one wants to say the next words.

Ako-chan stares at the open door of light. Someone must go. Someone must close it forever. And that someone cannot come home again. She holds her breath in the cold, blue glow. The truth sits heavy on the whole cave. Ako-chan cannot make her voice work. And no one knows yet who will go.

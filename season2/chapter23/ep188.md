General Adelie steps forward on the ice. He stands straight and tall. The white rings around his eyes are bright in the light. The cold wind pulls at his feathers. But he does not move at all. He holds his place like a true soldier. He looks at Ako-chan, calm and firm. It is his turn now to say goodbye.

"The work is done," he says. His voice is short and clear. "Go safely." Then the General bows his head. He lowers his chin in a long, deep salute. He holds it for a moment, very still. Ako-chan bows back to him. She knows what this salute means. It comes from a hard, proud heart.

The wind blows cold across the ice. It tugs at the General's black and white feathers. Pen-san stands close beside him. The two of them will stay in the south together. Dango watches them, quiet for once. Suke-san dips his head to the old soldier. Even Daru is still inside his Pod. The General was always strict with them. He gave them hard orders. He watched the ice through every storm. But he kept them safe, every time.

Ako-chan looks at the General one last time. Under all his strict words, there was always something warm. A true and steady friendship. He will not say it out loud. He does not need to. The deep salute already says everything. Slowly, the General lifts his head again. His eyes are calm and proud. The wind carries thin snow across the ice. Ako-chan gives him one last nod. Then she turns at last towards the flying boat.

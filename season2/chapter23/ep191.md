The third day, the sky is calm. The grey clouds are gone now. It is wide and blue above them. The flying boat rides on, smooth and steady. The engines hum, low and soft. Below, the sea is smooth too. Ako-chan sits at the controls. Warm light fills the cabin now. The long flight feels easy.

No one talks much. Each one looks out at the open sky. They think about the long journey. They remember the cold snow and the wolves. They remember the storm and the gentle birds. They remember the giant shell in the ice. And they remember Obia most of all. Ako-chan looks up at the blue. She thinks of Obia's pale blue eyes. The memory is sad. But it is calm now too.

Ruka sits close beside Ako-chan. She is the smallest of them all. She is the newest of the family too. She does not say a word. She just stays near, warm and still. Ako-chan looks down at her. The small shell has soft black lines. Ako-chan touches it gently. Ruka does not move away. Dango sleeps at her feet, slow and deep. Suke-san rests with his eyes half closed. Daru and Gabu are quiet in their Pods. The cabin is warm and full of peace. The quiet sky holds them all.

The sadness does not go away. But it changes, slowly, in the quiet. It grows softer, like the wide, pale sky. Ako-chan breathes out, long and slow. The whole journey lives inside her now. So does Obia. She is not alone up here. Her friends are all around her. And the boat flies on, towards home.

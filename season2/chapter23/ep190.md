The flying boat flies on through the gray sky. It is the second day in the air. The sea rolls far below, dark and wide. Inside, it is warm and quiet. Ako-chan sits at the controls. Beside her is the other seat. Obia always sat there. Now it is empty.

No one takes the empty seat. Dango stays at Ako-chan's feet. Suke-san keeps to the back. The tortoises rest in their Pods. The seat beside her stays bare all day. Sometimes Ako-chan forgets. She turns her head to speak. "Obia, look—" she starts. Then she stops. The seat is empty. No one can hear her now. She closes her mouth and looks ahead. Her chest feels tight for a moment. She used to tell Obia everything. Now she keeps it all inside.

Dango lifts his head now and then. He looks at the empty seat too. He waits, as if someone will come. But no one comes. Then he puts his head back down. He lets out a low, soft sigh. Suke-san watches him, but stays quiet. Ruka does not move at all. Outside the window, the gray sea slides past. The clouds drift by, slow and low. Far below, the cold water has no end. The cabin stays very quiet. No one fills the empty space.

Day after day, they get used to it. The empty seat becomes a normal thing. Soon no one turns to look for Obia. No one waits for Obia's quiet voice. And that is the hardest part of all. To stop looking for Obia hurts the most. Outside, the long gray sea rolls on.

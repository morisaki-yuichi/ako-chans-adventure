Morning comes, soft and bright. Ako-chan sits on her bedroom floor. An old bag lies open beside her. A map of the world is on the floor. Her finger rests on the far south.

She remembers the first time she left. She was ten then, small and afraid. She packed warm clothes and the red notebook. Now she is thirteen years old. She is not so afraid this time. She knows the road. She knows the cold. And she is not alone now.

Obia stands quiet in the doorway. Its pale blue eyes watch her. Dango sits close at her feet. Luca rests on the warm windowsill. Ako-chan looks at each of them. This is her family now. Then she looks down at the map. "We go again," she says softly.

Obia does not say a word. But its eyes glow a little brighter. Dango stands up and wags his tail. He knows that something is starting. He pushes his nose into the open bag. Ako-chan rubs his ears and smiles.

The words feel strange and warm at once. The map shows the long way down. Sea and land and ice, far to the south. Luca does not move on the sill. Her small shell glows in the morning light. Ako-chan reaches out and touches her shell. Luca pulls her head in, then out.

Three years ago, she wrote four small words. I have to go. She wrote them alone, in the dark. This time, she does not write at all. She says it out loud, to all of them. We go again. The morning light fills the quiet room. The long road is waiting in the south. And this time, they go together.

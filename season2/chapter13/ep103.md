Ako-chan watches Ruka in the warm sun. She still cannot name the day Ruka became family. But she remembers the day they first met. It was on a trip to Italy, to a place called Puglia. The summer sun was bright and hot.

White stone walls stood along the narrow streets. The light off them was white and clear. A dry wind came in from the sea. Then Ako-chan saw something small move. A tiny tortoise walked in the sun. It belonged to no one at all. It went slowly along the wall.

Ako-chan knelt down on the warm stone. She did not grab the little tortoise. She held out her open hand and waited. The tortoise stopped. It looked at her still hand. Then, slowly, it climbed up onto her palm. Its small shell was light and smooth.

For a moment, neither of them moved. The little tortoise sat still in her hand. Its dark eyes looked up at her. The white stone walls rose all around them. The Mediterranean light was soft and gold. Ako-chan felt the small heart beat there.

The wind was dry and smelled of salt. The stone walls shone in the southern light. The little tortoise rested calm in her open hand. It did not try to pull away. Ako-chan held the small warm weight close. She knew she could not leave it behind.

She carried the little tortoise all the way home. After some days, she gave her a name. Ruka. She became one of the family little by little, with no clear day. Now, in the English garden, Ruka sits in the sun. Ako-chan smiles at the bright little shell. That first day feels close, even now.

The morning light is soft and gold. Ako-chan sits on the back step. A warm cup of tea is in her hands. The garden grass is wet with dew. The air is cool and still.

Three years passed since the long journey. Ako-chan is taller now. She is thirteen. Her hands look bigger on the warm cup. She thinks more before she speaks these days. But she still wears her red glasses.

Dango is not a puppy anymore. He is sturdy and calm and strong. His bark is deep now, not high. But he still lies at her feet. He yawns wide and long, then rests his head on her foot. He is happy beside her, like always.

Obia still stands by the old shed. Its eyes glow pale and blue. The silver one looks no different than before. It waits there, quiet, as it always does. The garden looks just the same. The same fence. The same old shed. The same soft English light.

The tea sends up a thin line of steam. The grass smells of earth and morning. A bird sings somewhere in the trees. Everything is slow and gentle today. The garden is quiet, the way it is now. No animal calls out in fear. The world feels safe and whole again.

Nothing happens this morning. And that is good. But sometimes Ako-chan still thinks of the road. The blue sea. The white ice. The friends far away in other lands. She wonders if they think of her too. She holds her cup close and smiles. The journey is over now. But it stays warm inside her chest, quiet and deep.

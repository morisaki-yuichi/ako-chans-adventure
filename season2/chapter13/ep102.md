Ruka is a small tortoise. She lives with Ako-chan, Dango, and Obia now. Her shell is mostly black. The black spreads over it like a net. Yellow and orange show through the gaps. A round yellow spot marks each cheek. She can sit in the palm of one hand.

Ruka is everywhere now. In the morning, she sits on the warm doorstep in the sun. By noon, she moves to the cool floor by Dango's water bowl. Later, Ako-chan finds her up on the shelf. It stands by the front door, high and safe. She likes that quiet spot the best of all.

Ruka walks slowly, on her small feet. She takes one step. Then she stops. She looks. Then she walks again. There is no rush in her at all. Time seems to slow down when she moves.

Out in the bright sun, Ruka stretches her legs wide. She reaches her head and neck up high. The warmth sinks into her skin, not just her shell. She holds still like that for a long time. She could stay this way for hours. Ako-chan kneels and watches her closely.

Dango sniffs at Ruka as she goes past. Then he lies back down with a soft sigh. Ruka does not hurry for anyone. She finds the warm spots, one by one. The whole house holds her favourite places. Ako-chan watches her for a long, still moment.

Now it is hard to imagine the house without Ruka. When did Ruka become part of the family? She cannot name the exact day. But Ruka is here, in every sunny corner. She fits here, in her own slow way. And that feels right and good. She belongs here now, in every quiet day.

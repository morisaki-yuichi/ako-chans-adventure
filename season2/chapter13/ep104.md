It is late at night. The house is dark and still. Ako-chan cannot sleep. She comes down the stairs. The garden is black behind the glass. Everything is quiet and calm.

Obia stands by the window. It does not move. Its eyes glow pale and blue. They are brighter than before. A low hum comes from inside it. The sound is faint, but it does not stop. Ako-chan stops on the last step. She watches the silver one in the dark.

Then Obia turns to her. Ako-chan turns toward it too. "There is a signal," Obia says. "From the south." Its voice is flat and low. Ako-chan holds her breath. The word "south" sits cold in the silent room. She knows that word too well.

Ako-chan stands very still on the step. Her heart beats faster in her chest. A signal, calling in the night. She knows what this means. It means the long road again. The ice. The cold sea. Leaving home once more.

Outside, the garden is dark and empty. No wind moves in the trees. Dango is asleep upstairs. Ruka is still on her shelf. The faint hum fills the small room. It comes and goes, soft and strange. Ako-chan listens hard for it. But she cannot hear the signal. Only Obia can.

For three years, the nights were calm. No voice called from far away. The ground was quiet. The world was whole. But this night is different. Ako-chan looks at Obia's pale blue eyes. The hum goes on, low and steady. Something is starting again. She feels it deep in her chest. The long quiet is over now.

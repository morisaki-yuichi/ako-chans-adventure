Night comes again. The air is cold and sharp. Ako-chan sits with Obia in the dark room. Obia still works on the signal. Its eyes flicker, slow and pale. Outside, the garden is black and silent.

Then Obia goes very still. Its eyes stop flickering. For a moment, it does not move at all. "There is something in me," it says. "I cannot read it." Ako-chan leans closer to Obia. "What do you mean?" she asks. But Obia is quiet. It does not know the answer.

The room feels colder now. Ako-chan pulls her coat close. She watches Obia in the shadows. Its eyes glow faint and low. Something is wrong, deep inside it. And Obia cannot say what it is. Ako-chan's chest feels tight with worry.

Ako-chan thinks of all their travels. Obia was always sure and calm. It always knew the next step. But now it does not know itself. The silver one seems almost afraid. That frightens Ako-chan most of all.

There is a memory, locked away inside Obia. It cannot reach the memory. It cannot read its own mind. The signal called from the south. And now this. Two strange things in two nights. Ako-chan does not understand either one. She wishes she could help it.

Obia stands quiet by the window. Its blue eyes look out at the dark. It does not know what it forgot. It does not know why it came here. Ako-chan sits close beside Obia. The cold night holds them both. The hum is gone now. Only the cold remains. Neither of them speaks at all. They wait together in the dark.

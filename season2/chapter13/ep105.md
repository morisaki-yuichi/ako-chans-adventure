The night is long. Obia stands very still. Its eyes flicker, on and off, pale and blue. It is reading the signal. Ako-chan sits on the floor beside it. She waits in the dark and does not speak.

Hours pass. The dark window turns slowly gray. Then it grows soft and pale with morning. Dango breathes deep and slow upstairs. Ruka does not move on her shelf. Still Obia reads, through the long, quiet night. The hum rises and falls inside it.

Ako-chan watches the signal work in Obia. She cannot hear it. She cannot read it. But she sees its light in Obia's eyes. The pale blue glow turns and shifts. It is like a thought with no words. She waits, and the slow hours pass.

At last, near morning, Obia speaks. "We must go south," it says. "I do not know why." Ako-chan looks up at the silver one. Its eyes are steady now, not flickering. The message is clear at last. South. That is the way.

The first light fills the quiet room. Dango stirs, then sleeps once more. Ruka sits still in the gray dawn. Ako-chan's heart beats fast and hard. She knows this feeling well. She felt it three years ago. The road is calling her again. And Obia does not know why.

Outside, the morning birds begin to sing. A new day starts, fresh and bright. But it is not an ordinary day. Ako-chan looks south, through the window. The pull is there, far away and faint. She cannot see it. But she can feel it. It tugs at her, soft and sure. Another journey is about to begin.

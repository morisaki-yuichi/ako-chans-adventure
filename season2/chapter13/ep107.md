The signal can wait for one quiet day. The morning is gray and soft. Ako-chan sits on the floor with Dango. He is warm and heavy against her side. Three years made him sturdy and strong.

The little puppy is long gone now. His chest is broad and his legs are thick. His bark rolls deep and low, like far thunder. When he was small, it was high and sharp. Ako-chan remembers that little puppy voice. She smiles at the grown dog beside her.

His paws are wide now, like soft pads. His fur is thick and red and white. When he shakes, his long tail swings hard. He takes up half the floor when he rests. But he still thinks he is a small puppy. He climbs onto Ako-chan's lap and does not fit.

But some things did not change at all. Dango still naps right by her chair. He still leans his weight on her leg. He still wants her close, always. He follows her from room to room. He is all grown now. But his heart is the same.

Then Ruka walks slowly across the floor. She comes near Ako-chan's open hand. Dango lifts his head at once. He watches the small tortoise come close. He makes a low sound, deep in his chest. Then he leans in closer to Ako-chan. He does not want to share her. Ako-chan laughs and pats his furry head.

Some things changed in three years. Dango grew calm and sure. His body is heavy, his voice is low. But other things stayed just the same. He wants Ako-chan all to himself. He rests his head softly on her knee. He closes his eyes, calm and warm. Changed and unchanged, both at once.

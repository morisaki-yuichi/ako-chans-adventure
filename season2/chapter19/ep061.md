The floor moved.

It was barely anything — a soft shift, like the machine finding its feet. The light inside the walls changed. Not brighter. Stiller. A low sound came from somewhere deep inside, held for a moment, then gone.

A section of the wall opened.

Cold air came in. Wet cold. Heavy — full of trees and rain and something very old. Not the sharp clean cold of Antarctica. This was different. A cold that had lived in forests for a very long time.

Ako-chan stepped out first.

Fog. Deep fog. The kind that came from the mountain itself, from the ground between old trees. She could not see more than ten steps ahead.

The trees were enormous. Their bark was rough and dark. Roots came up out of the ground and went back down. Moss grew over everything — the roots, the rocks, the fallen pieces of wood. The air smelled of wet earth and old trees and rain.

She heard the others come out behind her. Dango first. Then Suke-san. Then Obia, steady and quiet. Pen-san and General Adelie after that. The pods moved out slowly — green, orange, yellow. Riro's capsule last.

Japan, Obia had said, before they came through. The end of the nineteenth century. The high mountains.

Ako-chan looked up.

The treetops were gone, lost inside the grey. A crow called once, far above, and went quiet.

Suke-san came to her side. His nose was up. His ears were straight. He was reading the air — all of it, very carefully.

He did not speak.

She did not speak either.

The fog moved slowly between the trees. And somewhere inside all that grey — they were not alone.

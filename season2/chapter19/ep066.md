Pen-san walked forward.

Slowly, which was not like him at all. He walked one step, then another, his eyes on the birds. General Adelie moved beside him. Both slow. Both quiet.

Ako-chan did not stop them.

The birds watched them come. They did not run. They turned their heads — first one way, then the other — and watched.

Pen-san stopped three steps from the nearest one.

The bird was bigger than Pen-san. Much bigger. Its face was different — a long heavy bill, white patches over the eyes. But the shape of it: the black back, the white front, the small folded wings, the way it stood on the rock — Pen-san knew that shape. She could see it in the way he held still.

He said one word, very softly.

"Hello."

The bird looked at him for a long moment. Then it made a sound — low, not loud, coming from deep inside its chest. A sound that stayed in the cold air after it was done.

Pen-san stood still.

Ako-chan felt it too — not as clearly as Pen-san heard it. But she caught the edge of it. Something warm inside something very old. Something that had been here first, before the cold got colder.

General Adelie stood very straight. He watched the birds and did not speak. But the way he held himself — very careful, very still — told her that this mattered to him. That he would carry it.

Even Dango was quiet behind her.

Even Daru was quiet.

The fog off the water moved around them all.

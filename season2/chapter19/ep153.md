Ako-chan stands on cold, wet stone. A hard wind tears at her coat. Below her, the gray sea roars. Tall waves rise and crash. They break against the rocky shore. This is a lonely island in the North Atlantic.

The storm fills the whole sky. Dark clouds race fast overhead. Cold rain stings Ako-chan's face. She holds tight to a black rock. The wind is strong and wild. It is hard to stand at all. The four of them stay close.

There is no house on the island. No path runs across the stone. No tree stands against the wind. The island is bare and gray. There are only the rocks, the sea, the sky. Nothing moves but the wind and the sea.

Far off, on the low rocks, there are birds. Many birds stand near the sea. They are large and black and white. They do not mind the storm at all. Pen-san stares hard at them. The General watches the dark waves. Obia stands close, its blue eyes bright.

The sea is loud all around them. Waves boom against the rocks below. White spray flies up in the wind. The black rocks shine, wet and dark. Ako-chan pulls her coat in tight. Even here, the cold cuts deep. This is a hard, wild place. The storm shows no sign of end.

Ako-chan looks across the wet shore. The birds stand still in the rain. Something pulls at her heart. She cannot say what it is. Somewhere on this island, the last of them wait. She does not know them yet. But she feels them, close and near. Her heart feels heavy in her chest.

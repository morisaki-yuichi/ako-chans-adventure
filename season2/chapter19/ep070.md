The sea cows did not go right away.

They stayed near the edge of the ice, turning slowly in the water. One raised its head again and made a sound — low and long. Then the other.

Riro sat on the ice and listened.

He did not groom. He did not speak. He just sat with his front paws together and watched them.

Ako-chan stood beside him. She thought of the voice the sea cow had given her — wide and slow and full of the world as it had been. It did not know. Whatever was coming, it did not know. And it had given its voice freely all the same.

After a while, the sea cows turned away from the ice. They moved slowly into the deeper water. They grew smaller. Then they were part of the waves. Then they were gone.

Riro looked down.

"Ready," he said. Very quietly.

They walked back to the machine.

The seam closed. The floor moved.

This time the shake came immediately — before she was ready for it. It moved through the floor, through her feet, up through her chest. The walls made a sound she had heard before, but lower now. Lower and harder.

The small lights along the walls flickered once.

Suke-san stiffened. His ears went flat. His nose was working. He looked at the walls, then the floor, then Ako-chan.

The shake lasted four seconds. Five. Then it stopped.

Suke-san looked at her.

"Three times now," he said.

She said nothing.

She had felt it too.

The storm blows on around them. Ako-chan stands before the birds. One bird steps a little closer. Its dark eyes hold her gaze. Rain runs down its wet feathers. The birds wait all around her. Ako-chan grows very still.

Then the voice comes to her. It is soft, softer than she thought. Only Ako-chan can hear it. She closes her eyes to listen. The gentle words fill her mind. "We are not afraid. We never learned to fear. We stay. We do not fly away." The voice is calm and low. There is no anger in it.

The voice does not ask for help. It does not cry out in pain. It holds nothing against the world. It is tired, and it is at peace. It lived a long, long time. Now it is ready to rest.

The storm still howls all around. But the voice stays soft and calm. It does not rise or break. Around her, the wet birds wait. Rain drips from their dark feathers. Cold spray flies from the sea. The wind pulls at Ako-chan's coat. Yet inside, all is quiet and still.

The wolf's voice was proud and strong. This voice is not like that. It does not fight. It does not blame. It is soft and calm, even now. And the calm is the saddest part.

Ako-chan holds the voice inside. Her eyes stay shut a long while. The gentle words stay with her. They do not fade away. She will carry them with her. She opens her eyes at last. The bird still stands close by. Its quiet gaze meets her own. The gentle voice hurts her heart.

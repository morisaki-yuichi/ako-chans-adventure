Ako-chan opens her eyes and turns. The storm still beats on the island. She looks at Obia beside her. "I have it," she says, soft and low. "I have their voice now." Obia steps close to her side.

Obia's blue eyes start to glow. They shine brightly through the gray rain. Obia takes the voice from her now. A soft hum rises from inside it. The voice flows into Obia. Then it sends the voice away. It sends it far across time. It sends it to the future. Where it goes, no one knows. But it will be safe there.

Ako-chan watches the blue light grow. It shines against the dark storm. For a moment, it is very bright. The wet birds stand still and calm. They watch the strange blue light. They do not move or call. The rain falls on their dark wings. Around them, the storm still rages. Waves crash hard on the black stone. The wind howls cold across the rocks.

The General stands tall and straight. He watches the light cross time. Then he lifts one wing up high. He holds it there, stiff and still. It is a silent salute. He honors the voice as it goes. Pen-san stands close, quiet and small. He watches the General's salute. He stands as still as he can.

The blue light pulls back inside Obia. The hum fades into the storm. "I sent it," Obia says. "The voice is safe now." Ako-chan lets out a long breath. Now the second voice is safe. The storm cannot reach it now. It will live on, far ahead in time.

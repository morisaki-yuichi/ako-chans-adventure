Ako-chan stands close to the birds now. They do not move away from her. Their calm eyes meet her own. The rain falls, soft and grey. The flock stands all around her. They have no fear of her at all. She could reach out and touch them.

And that is the saddest thing of all. These birds never learned to fear. So they do not run from harm. People come to this lonely island. The birds do not run away. They only stand and wait. And so, each year, there are fewer.

Ako-chan looks from bird to bird. Each one is calm and trusting. None of them sees the danger. They do not know they should run. It hurts her to watch them stand. So calm. So sure. So safe in their own minds.

Pen-san stands close at her side. He does not chirp or play. His big eyes are dark and sad. He sees himself in them. The General stands stiff and silent. Even he says nothing now. The wind tugs hard at her hair. The waves break on, grey and cold.

Ako-chan looks across the rocks. The flock is smaller than it seems. There are gaps along the shore. Some stone is bare and empty. The low sky presses down, cold and grey. She can walk right up to them. None of them steps away.

Ako-chan's heart grows heavy and sore. She wants to warn them all. She wants to say, "Learn to run. Learn to fear." But they would not understand her. They do not know fear at all. They will fade away, calm and still. No fear, right to the very end.

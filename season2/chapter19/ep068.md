The shake had barely faded when the seam opened again.

Air came in — cold and salt. But nothing else. No trees. No rock. No land at all. Just open water, going in every direction, and the flat grey sky sitting above it.

Ako-chan stepped out.

They were on a wide low shelf of ice at the water's edge. And the water —

She had been on open seas before, far from land. She had crossed the Pacific. She knew what it was to see no shore. But this was different. Not a single ship on the horizon. No smoke. No sound but the water and the wind. Not one mark of any hand anywhere.

The Bering Sea. The eighteenth century.

Riro came out of his capsule.

He stood at the edge of the ice and looked out at the water. His fur was full. His eyes were very wide.

For a long time he said nothing.

Then, very quietly — the way he said things when he was not saying them to anyone in particular — he said: "It is different. The water here. It is not like my water. It is more."

Ako-chan did not answer. She understood.

Something about the water had all of itself still inside it. Nothing taken out. Nothing changed.

Riro looked at her. Then he looked back at the water.

She could feel it too — something out there, moving below the surface. Not close yet. Not hurrying. Moving the way it always moved, the way it had always moved.

It was coming.

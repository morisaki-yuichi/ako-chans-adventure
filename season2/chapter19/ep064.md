Ako-chan moved toward the wolf.

Slowly. No sudden movements. Nothing in her hands. She walked slowly. She had nothing to hide. The wolf watched her come.

It did not move away.

She stopped three steps away. She looked at it — the grey coat, the narrow face, the dark eyes. A small wolf. An old one.

Then she opened herself to its voice.

She had done this before, with hedgehogs and robins and whales. But this felt different. Something deep in the mountain, and old. Not asking to be heard. Just still, the way the mountain was still. The way things are before they have names.

She held herself very quiet and waited.

The wolf looked at her, and gave its voice — not in words, but in everything before words. Cold winters. Long open runs. Fog between the trees. The mountain in every season. The sound that rose and fell with the world.

When it was done, she looked at Obia and gave one small nod.

Obia went still. Its eyes changed quality — not brighter, but somewhere else, doing something far away and quiet. A moment passed.

"Done," said Obia.

The wolf stood. It walked back three steps toward the fog. Then it stopped.

It raised its head and gave one long howl. Clean. Clear. Going up through the trees, through the grey sky, up past everything.

The sound fell away. The forest went still.

They walked back to the machine in silence. The seam closed.

The floor moved.

And then — deeper in the walls than before — a shake. Low and brief. Like something pushing through.

The first one.

The gentle voice still rings in Ako-chan. But now it is time to go home. Obia lifts its head and speaks. "Hold on," it says. "We jump back now." The light begins to swell again. It grows bright around the four. Pen-san huddles close to Ako-chan.

The light flares white and harsh. Then something pulls them hard once more. This time the pull is the strongest yet. It grips her body and squeezes tight. Her bones seem to creak and ache. Her ears ring. Her head spins fast. The island and the birds fall away. For one long moment, all is light. The pull does not want to let go. This jump back is the hardest of all.

Then, all at once, it stops. The bright glare drains away again. The cold cave air comes back. Hard ice lies beneath her feet. But her legs shake and nearly fold. Ako-chan sways and almost falls. The world tips and slowly steadies. The old machine hums close by. They are back in the deep cave.

The friends run to them at once. Dango reaches Ako-chan first. Suke-san presses close at her side. But Pen-san sinks down on the ice. His big body droops, tired and weak. His head hangs low and heavy. He cannot stand up straight at all. The General bends close to help him.

Ako-chan kneels beside Pen-san. She rests a gentle hand on him. She holds him a little closer. His breath comes, slow and tired. This jump took more than the others. Each jump wears their bodies down. Each time, it cuts a little deeper. Ako-chan worries for them all.

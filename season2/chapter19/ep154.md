The four move toward the low rocks. Many birds stand along the shore. They are big seabirds, black and white. They stand tall in the cold rain. The gray sea booms behind them. Ako-chan comes slowly closer. Her boots slip on the wet stone.

Ako-chan looks at them with care. The birds have small, short wings. The wings are far too small to fly. These birds cannot lift off the ground. And they do not run from the four. They just stand and watch, calm and still. The General studies them for a long time. "They cannot fly. And they do not run," he says. These are the great auks of the north.

The birds move slowly on the rocks. They waddle, slow and heavy. The sea is their true home. On land, they are slow and clumsy. But in the water, they are fast. Here on the stone, they only wait. The cold waves crash close by.

Pen-san stares at them without a sound. He does not move at all. The birds look so much like him. Black and white. Round and tall. They cannot fly. He cannot fly. His small wings are just like theirs. Pen-san watches them, quiet and still. He cannot look away from them.

The birds watch the four come near. They show no fear at all. Their dark eyes are calm and soft. They blink against the falling rain. They do not know to run away. Ako-chan feels a cold worry rise. Fear keeps wild things safe. These birds have no fear at all. Their calm cannot keep them from harm. And so they are not safe.

White. Everywhere, only white. Deep snow lies across the whole land. It covers the ground, soft and thick. Tall trees stand black against the snow. Their branches bend low under the weight. The sky above is pale and gray. This is a mountain, high in old Japan.

Ako-chan takes one step forward. The snow gives way beneath her boot. It crunches softly in the still air. Her warm breath turns white before her face. She looks around at the silent trees. No house stands here. No path cuts the snow. No smoke rises in the empty sky. There is no one here at all. Only the four of them, alone in the white.

Obia stands quiet at her side. Its blue eyes glow softly in the gray light. They throw a small ring on the snow. Ako-chan pulls her warm coat close. The cold here is deep and clean. It smells of cold pine and old snow. Nothing here moves but the four of them.

Dango lifts each paw high through the snow. He shakes the cold flakes from his fur. He stays pressed close to Ako-chan's feet. Suke-san stands just behind her. He lifts his nose into the wind. His ears turn slowly, sharp and still. He tests the air, careful and quiet.

The silence is deep and wide. No bird calls in the trees. No wind moves the heavy branches. The whole mountain holds its breath. Ako-chan stands very still and listens. Far off, the slope seems to wait.

Then Suke-san goes stiff. His low growl breaks the quiet. Something is here in the silence. Ako-chan feels it too. They are not alone on this mountain.

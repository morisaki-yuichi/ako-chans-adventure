The light swells until it fills the world. It is white and far too bright. Ako-chan cannot see her own hands. The machine roars loudly in her ears. She feels Dango pressed tight to her leg. Suke-san stands close on her other side.

Then something pulls them hard. It grabs her whole body at once. It drags her forward through the light. Her feet leave the cold cave floor. She cannot tell up from down. The world spins white around her. Bright streaks of light rush past her face. They blur and bend in every way. Dango's warm side stays at her leg. That small touch is all she feels. She holds onto it through the rush. For one long moment, all is light and noise. Then, all at once, it stops.

The roar fades into a deep hush. The bright glare drains away to gray. Ako-chan feels the ground come back. It is hard and cold beneath her feet. The air is different now. It bites at her cheeks. It smells clean and sharp and strange.

Dango shakes his head and blinks. He looks up at Ako-chan and whines low. Suke-san lifts his nose at once. He tests the strange new air. Obia stands close, its blue eyes calm. The four of them are still together. But the cave is gone now. The machine is gone too.

Ako-chan takes one slow, careful breath. The cold air fills her chest. Her head still spins from the jump. Slowly, she opens her eyes wide. A pale gray sky hangs far above. She does not know this place at all. She does not know this time.

Suke-san's growl fades into the snow. Ako-chan follows his sharp gaze. Between the black trees, shapes appear. They stand still against the white. At first, she cannot tell what they are. Then they step out from the shadows.

They are wolves. A small pack, here in the snow. Their gray coats blend with the trees. They are thin. Their ribs show beneath the fur. But they stand tall and proud. Their heads are high. Their sharp eyes hold no fear. They watch Ako-chan and her friends. They do not run. They do not hide.

Ako-chan counts them, one by one. There are only a few. So few. Their breath comes, slow and quiet. It rises white in the cold air. The oldest one stands at the front. Its eyes are deep and still. It looks straight at Ako-chan.

One young wolf stands close to the old one. It is smaller than the rest. Its legs are thin in the deep snow. The pack keeps it in the middle, safe. They guard the little one with care. Ako-chan watches them, and her heart aches. So few, and one so small.

Something heavy settles in her chest. She feels it without words. This is the last pack on the mountain. Soon there will be no more. Almost none are left now. Their tracks lead back into the trees. The wide mountain is empty all around. The snow will hold their marks, then forget.

The wolves do not move. They simply stand and watch. Thin, proud, and quiet in the snow. This is the shape of the last ones. Ako-chan's eyes fill, but she does not look away.

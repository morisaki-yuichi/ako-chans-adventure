Obia moved forward.

It raised one arm. A beam of white light came from it, bright and steady. The beam fell across the shape in the ice.

And they all saw it.

Curved at the top. Wide at the base. Long smooth lines from one end to the other. Metal — dark grey, almost silver where the light touched it. No sharp edges anywhere. Every corner was a curve. Every line flowed into the next.

Not a ship. Not a building. Nothing with a name she knew.

Beautiful. And very, very wrong.

Something this shape did not belong here. Not in this cave. Not under this ice. Not in this time. She could feel that just by looking at it — the way you feel when you find something where it should not be.

Nobody spoke.

Pen-san was very still beside her. Dango pressed to her left side. Suke-san was behind her, close. The orange glow of Gabu's pod and the green of Daru's were at the edge of the group. And the yellow pod — Ruka inside, her face near the wall, watching.

Obia moved the light slowly across the surface. Ako-chan's eyes followed. Each part her mind tried to understand and could not, and moved on.

The surface was smooth. Very smooth. Ice had grown up around the base, covering the lower part. But where the metal showed, it was untouched. Not broken. Not old in any way she could name.

The beam of light was the only movement.

Nothing else moved.

Nobody spoke.

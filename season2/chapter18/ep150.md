The cave is quiet now. The friends sit close around Ako-chan. The old machine hums low and deep. Its light glows softly on the ice. No one wants to speak first. They wait for Ako-chan.

Ako-chan holds her knees and breathes. Her eyes are far away and still. "The wolves were the last ones," she says. "Soon there will be no more." Her voice is low and quiet. The friends listen close. They do not say a word. They wait, quiet and still.

Pen-san leans his big head close. His dark eyes are soft and kind. "Was it... sad?" he asks. Ako-chan thinks for a long moment. She shakes her head slowly. "No," she says. "It was proud."

The friends grow quiet again. Dango rests his head on her knee, warm and heavy. Suke-san sits close at her right, a step behind as always. His eyes stay on her face, watchful and still. The machine hums on in the cold.

The friends think about her words. A proud voice, from the very last ones. Daru looks down, quiet for once. Even his quick voice has nothing to say now. The cave feels heavy and full. They all feel the weight of one voice. No one knows quite what to say.

Ako-chan looks at the old machine. One voice is safe now. But the work is not done. Two more voices wait somewhere in time. She does not know where. She does not know who. But she will listen for them too. She leans against Dango's warm side. Its low sound fills the quiet dark. Somewhere far off, the voices wait for her.

Obia stood two steps from the surface.

Then one step.

It raised one front leg slowly. Its paw was silver — the same color as the metal of the shape. The leg moved forward, steady and slow, until the paw was almost touching the surface.

Nobody spoke.

Obia's paw touched the metal.

Everything stopped.

Not just the group. Not just the cave. Something larger stopped — she could not name it, but she felt it. The signal in her chest, the one that had been pressing since they entered the cave, went very quiet. And then, in the quiet, it became something else. Not smaller. Not larger. Just different. Like a door opening instead of knocking.

Obia did not move.

Its eyes were still. Not looking at the surface. Not looking at anything. Looking at something far inside itself.

Dango pressed to Ako-chan's side. He did not make a sound. Suke-san was beside her too — she could feel them both, one on each side, solid and quiet.

Even Pen-san did not speak.

Ako-chan counted her own breaths. One. Two. Three.

Obia did not move.

Its paw was flat against the metal. The pale silver of Obia's paw and the dark silver of the shape.

Four breaths. Five.

The cave was completely still.

Then the glow in the ice walls grew a little brighter. Not much. Just enough to notice.

And something was changing — not outside, not in the cave, but somewhere inside Obia. She could not see it. But she could feel it.

Obia still had not moved. It stood with its paw on the metal and waited.

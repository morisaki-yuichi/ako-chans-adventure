The cave went on.

General Adelie led. His steps were careful and exact, each one placed the same distance as the last. The path grew narrow. The ice walls pressed close on both sides.

The blue-white glow did not go away, but it was different here. Deeper. Not like light from outside, but like something the ice held inside itself.

Obia walked close to the left wall. Its eyes moved slowly across the surface.

"Close," it said. "Very close."

Dango pressed to Ako-chan's side. She felt him against her leg — steady and warm. Suke-san was behind her. She did not look back. She knew he was there.

The path turned once. Then again. Then it went down.

The floor sloped slowly at first, then more steeply. General Adelie did not pause. He walked the slope as if he had walked it many times. He had, Ako-chan thought. He said they found it long before she came.

The signal was very strong now. Not in her ears or her hands — in her chest. A deep pressure that was almost like sound, but not quite.

"Obia," she said.

"Still there," said Obia. "Growing stronger."

The path curved and opened out.

General Adelie stopped.

Below him, the cave widened into something much larger. The glow came from below — pale, steady, pressing up from the floor.

Pen-san moved to Ako-chan's side. He was very quiet. Even for Pen-san.

"It is just ahead," he said.

Ako-chan stood at the edge of the slope and looked down into the dark space below.

Something was there. Very large. Very still. And it had been waiting a long time.

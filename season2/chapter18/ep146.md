For a moment, no one moves. The wolves watch with careful eyes. The friends stand still in the snow. The air between them is tight. Neither side moves an inch. Snow drifts down between the trees. One wolf lifts its lip. A low sound rolls in its chest.

Then Suke-san moves. He steps forward, slow and low. His body stays close to the snow. He keeps his head down, his tail still. He is a dog, just like them. He shows them he means no harm. "We are not hunters," he says. "We listen."

The wolves' hackles rise along their backs. Their eyes lock onto Suke-san. The old one watches, calm and still. Dango steps up beside Ako-chan. A low growl builds in his throat. But Ako-chan rests a hand on him. He goes quiet, though his body stays tense.

The cold wind moves between them. It carries Suke-san's scent to the pack. The wolves lift their noses to read it. They learn he is not a threat. Still, they do not trust him yet. Their bodies stay tense and ready.

No one breathes. The snow holds the silence tight. Suke-san does not move again. He waits, low and patient, in the cold. The wolves study him, long and hard. Ako-chan's heart beats hard and fast. Behind her, Obia stands silent and still.

Then the old wolf shifts its weight. It lifts one paw from the snow. Slowly, it takes a single step. One step closer. Toward them. The pack holds its breath behind it. Suke-san stays low and does not move. Ako-chan watches him, still as stone. No one knows what comes next.

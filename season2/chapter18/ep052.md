They went down.

The slope was steep enough that Ako-chan stepped carefully, one foot at a time. Obia moved the pods and the capsule slowly behind her, holding them steady on the slope.

The cave below was much larger than anything above.

The ceiling went up and up. The walls curved away. And there, in the middle of the open space, was a shape.

Half-buried in the ice floor. Very large.

Ako-chan stopped.

She could not understand it at first. It was too large. Her eyes found one part — curved metal, smooth and dark — then moved to the next part, and the next. Each part was bigger than she expected.

It was like trying to see a mountain from too close.

Dango made a sound.

Low. Very low. Not a bark. Not a warning. Something she had not heard from him before. He pressed to her side and made the sound once more, then was quiet.

Nobody moved.

The shape sat in the ice and did not move either.

The glow here was brighter than in the tunnels above. It came from the ice all around the shape — pale blue-white, steady, even. It made the dark metal look like it had light inside it.

Ruka was in her yellow pod, near the back of the group. Ako-chan could see it at the edge of her sight. Ruka was still. Just watching.

All of them were just watching.

General Adelie stood at the front. He did not look at the shape. He looked at Ako-chan.

"Now you see it," he said.

Ako-chan opens her eyes and turns. She looks at Obia beside her. "I have it," she says, soft and low. "I have the wolf's voice." Obia steps close to her. Its blue eyes start to glow.

Obia takes the voice from her now. Its eyes shine, bright and clear. A soft hum rises from inside it. The voice flows into Obia. Then it sends the voice away. It sends it far across time. It sends it to the future. Where it goes, no one knows. But it will be safe there.

Ako-chan watches the blue light grow. It fills the space between the trees. For a moment, it is very bright. The wolves' eyes shine in the glow. Then the light pulls back inside Obia. The voice is gone from the snow. It is somewhere far ahead in time.

The wolves do not move at all. They watch the strange blue light. Snow drifts down between them all. It lands softly on the old wolf's back. The pack stands quiet in the cold. They watch, calm and unafraid. They see the voice go away. The snow keeps falling, soft and slow.

Obia's eyes grow soft again. The hum fades into the snow. "I sent it," Obia says. "The voice is safe now." Ako-chan lets out a long breath. Now the first voice is safe.

She looks back at the old wolf. Its deep eyes meet hers once more. Something passes between them, quiet and warm. The old wolf dips its head, slow and proud. Then it turns back to its pack. One voice will not be lost. It will live on, far away in time.

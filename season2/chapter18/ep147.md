The old wolf stops a few steps away. It stands close now, but calm. Its deep eyes hold Ako-chan's gaze. The pack waits behind it in the snow. The whole mountain is still now. Ako-chan does not move at all.

Then the wolf speaks. Its voice comes, low and slow. Only Ako-chan can hear it. She closes her eyes to listen. The words rise quiet in her mind. "We are the last. We do not run. This snow is ours. We are not afraid." The voice does not shake. It is calm and sure.

The air seems to tremble around her. The snow falls, soft and light. Even the wind seems to hold still. Suke-san stands frozen at her side. Dango stiffens against her leg, frozen still. Obia waits close, its blue eyes still. No one breaks the silence. The wolf's words hang in the cold.

The voice moves through her like wind. It is old, older than the snow. She feels the long years in it. She feels the snow, the hunt, the cold. But under it all, there is pride. The pack walked this land for ages. They will walk it proud until the end.

Ako-chan keeps her eyes shut tight. She holds the voice deep inside. It is not loud. It is not angry. There is no sorrow in it at all. Only something old and proud and strong.

She opens her eyes at last. The old wolf still watches her. Its gaze is steady, full of pride. The voice was not sad. It was proud. Ako-chan understands now, without words. She breathes it in, slow and deep. The pack stands tall in the snow.

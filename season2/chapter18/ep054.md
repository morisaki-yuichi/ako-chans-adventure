Nobody moved for a long time.

Then, slowly, Ako-chan walked. Not toward it. Around it.

She moved to the right, keeping the same distance from the surface. Obia's light followed. The shape was longer than she had thought. Much longer. The far end of it disappeared into the cave wall and the ice — she could not see where it ended.

The others moved with her, slowly, in a wide circle.

Daru's green pod glowed on one side. Gabu's orange beside it. Riro's capsule, with its quiet sound of water. And Ruka in the yellow pod — still, watching through the clear wall.

Pen-san walked beside Ako-chan. When they reached the far side of the shape, he stopped.

"We came here many times," he said. "General Adelie and I. We walked around it. Just like this." He looked at the surface. "But we never touched it."

General Adelie came to stand beside them.

"We did not know what it was," he said. "Only that the signal came from it. And that it was not ours."

Obia was standing close to the base of the shape now. Its eyes moved across the surface — not just looking. Reading.

"The signal is here," said Obia. "Strongest right at the surface." It paused. "There is something inside it. I can feel the shape of it — the edge of something. But I cannot reach it from here."

Ako-chan looked at Obia.

Obia looked at the shape.

The signal pressed from the metal — steady and very strong. It had always been there. Waiting for them to come south.

Waiting for this.

Obia took one step forward.

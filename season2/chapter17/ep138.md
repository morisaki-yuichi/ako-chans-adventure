The light grows steady inside Obia now. Its blue eyes burn calm and clear. The shaking stops. Obia stands tall. The friends wait in the frozen hall. No one breathes in the deep cold.

Then the broken pieces join at last. Obia lifts its head and speaks. The voice comes clear across the ice. "Save the voices of the dying animals." A long pause. "Send them to the future." The words ring in the silent hall. That is why Obia came so far. That is the task, deep and true.

The friends stand frozen where they are. Daru forgets to move his feet. Gabu draws far back into his shell. Pen-san's wide eyes go wider still. Dango looks up at Ako-chan's face. Not one of them makes a sound. The cave holds its breath around them all.

For a moment no one moves at all. The words hang in the frozen air. The machine glows soft in its ice. Its light falls on every still face. Outside the glow, the deep cave waits. Inside Ako-chan, something turns and wakes.

Ako-chan feels it settle in her heart. The dying voices — she can hear them. She always could, her whole life long. Now she knows why that gift is hers. She will be the one to listen. Then Obia will send the voices on. She feels ready for it now.

Now the whole long journey makes sense. The signal. The south. The ice. All of it led to this one task. To save the voices before they fade. And carry them on, into the future. That is the meaning of it all.

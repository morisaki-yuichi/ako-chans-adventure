General Adelie led them deeper into the cave.

His steps were slow and exact. Pen-san walked beside Ako-chan, pressed to her leg.

The cave widened. The ceiling went up. The blue-white glow in the walls grew stronger — and with it, the signal. Pressing from all sides now.

"Very close," said Obia. "Very strong."

They came to a place where the cave opened into a wide space. Not a room — something larger. The ice curved all around, up and over, like the inside of something immense.

General Adelie stopped.

He faced the group. He looked at Ako-chan.

"We found it some time ago," he said. "Something is here. Under the ice. Something that does not belong."

Ako-chan waited.

"It is not like the old disturbance," said General Adelie. "That is gone now. This is different. Older. Something that was put here."

He looked at Obia.

"Something is here. Under the ice. We have felt it. A long time."

Obia's eyes were still. It did not speak.

Nobody spoke.

Even Pen-san was very still.

Dango pressed to Ako-chan's side. Suke-san was behind her — she could feel him there, solid and quiet.

The signal pressed from below. From the ice floor. From whatever was down there, under everything.

Ako-chan looked at the ice at her feet.

She thought of the words she would write later, when she found the notebook:

*We are all here. We came back. And it is here too. Waiting for us, under the ice.*

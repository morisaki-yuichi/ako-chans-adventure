The path opens into a wide space. The cave ends in a high hall. Ako-chan stops still at the edge. The others gather close behind her back. Obia's light spreads across the room. And there, in the far wall, something shines.

Half-buried in the old ice — a machine. Its metal is smooth and grey and strange. Pale light glints along its curved side. Part of it lies deep in the ice. Part of it stands free and bare. Round marks run across its frozen shell. No one ever saw a thing like this. "What is this?" Ako-chan says softly. She steps closer, slow and careful.

A faint hum comes from the machine. It is the same sound as the signal. The signal came from this place all along. Dango stops and will not move. Suke-san's ears lie flat and low. Pen-san stares with his big dark eyes. Even Daru is quiet in his Pod.

The hall is still and very old. Ako-chan's footsteps ring on the floor. Her breath drifts white in the blue light. The machine sits deep in its ice. The cold of long years hangs around it. Ruka watches without a sound. Gabu's quiet eyes do not leave it.

Obia walks slowly towards the metal. Its blue eyes shine brighter now. It stops before the half-buried machine. For a long moment, it says nothing. Then, soft and low, it speaks. "...I think I know it." Ako-chan turns to look at Obia.

No one moves in the cold hall. The blue light rests on the metal. It rests in its bed of ice. It seems to wait for Obia. Only for Obia.

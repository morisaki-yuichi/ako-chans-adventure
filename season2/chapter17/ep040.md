The water below was different.

Darker. Heavier. The colour of deep winter. Thin plates of ice moved on the surface — not joined yet, just drifting, turning slowly.

Riro saw them first.

He pressed his nose to the glass. He was very still.

"Ice," he said.

Obia came from the front of the flying boat, carrying two things, stacked carefully.

"Yes," it said. "We are close now. There are things we need."

It set them down in the middle of the cabin.

Two pods. The same shape as before, but bigger — a full size larger. One glowed green. One glowed orange.

Daru opened one eye. Then both.

"That is mine," he said.

"It is," said Obia. "Bigger now. For the cold."

Gabu was already watching. He did not move. But his eyes went to the orange one and stayed there.

Riro had not moved from his corner. His capsule hummed quietly beside him. He looked at the two new ones, then turned back to the window.

Then Obia lifted the last thing.

Small — much smaller than the others. A little pod, the same round shape but made for something much smaller. It glowed yellow. A warm, steady yellow.

"Ruka," said Obia.

Ruka was in her corner. She had been watching the whole time from inside her shell. Now she pulled back further. Just the top was visible: bright yellow, strong dark marks.

Daru looked at the pod. He looked at Ruka's shell.

"Same colour," he said.

"Yes," said Obia.

It set the pod down near Ruka's corner. Close, but not touching. Obia went back to the front.

The flying boat flew on. Below: more ice now, the plates thicker, closer together.

Ruka did not come out.

But she did not move away from the yellow pod either.

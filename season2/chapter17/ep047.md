The blizzard sound stopped.

Not slowly — it just stopped. One step into the crack and the wind was gone. The snow was gone. The sound was gone.

Only their footsteps. Only the quiet hum behind them.

The walls of the crack rose on both sides, then widened below into a cave. The ceiling was ice. The floor was ice. Everything was ice.

And the ice glowed.

Blue-white. From inside the walls — a deep, steady light, like the light inside a winter lake. It lit the whole space from below and from the sides.

Nobody spoke.

Dango's paws made small sounds on the ice floor. The yellow pod moved slowly behind. The green and orange pods followed. The hum of Riro's water capsule. All these small sounds, and the blue-white glow around everything.

They went deeper.

The cave opened wider. The ceiling rose.

And then Obia stopped.

"The signal," it said.

They all felt it — not heard, felt. A pull. A pressure from deeper in the cave. Not large and frightening. Just very close.

The signal was strong here. Stronger than anything Obia had measured before.

Ako-chan went still.

The cave was quiet. The blue-white light was steady. The air was cold and old — the kind of cold that had been there a very long time.

Nobody moved.

She did not know how long they stood like that. Just standing in the blue light, feeling the pull from the dark ahead.

Something was in there.

Close.

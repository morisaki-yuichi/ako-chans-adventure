The flying boat came out of the clouds.

And there it was.

White. Grey. A wall that stretched all the way across the horizon — left and right, as far as she could see. Not sky. Not water. Something else.

Antarctica.

Nobody spoke.

Dango pressed his nose against the glass. His breath left a small pale cloud. He stayed there, looking.

Ako-chan looked at the white wall and felt something move in her chest. Not only fear. Something deeper. Something quiet and very large.

She knew this place.

Last time, they came by ship. Three whales went ahead of them. The ice shelf had looked just like this: flat, white, and still, growing as they got closer. She had written in the red notebook: *Day sixty-five. Antarctica. The whales went back. We go on.*

She thought of the aurora — green at the edges, white at the centre. All of them standing below it in the cold, looking up. Nine.

She thought of the morning they left. Pen-san with both small wings up. The Colonel beside him, straight and still. The two shapes growing smaller as the ship moved north. Then gone.

She had made a promise that morning. She was going to keep it.

Gabu watched from inside the orange pod, very still. Riro sat at his window with front paws together. Suke-san faced south, ears up, and did not turn.

Even Daru was quiet. He pressed close to his window and said nothing.

The white wall filled the whole view now. The light was cold and pale. The sky pressed down.

She kept her hand on Dango's back. He was warm against her palm.

Almost there.

As they flew closer, the sky ahead changed.

The ice below spread wider. The clouds came lower and heavier. Then the two met: ice and cloud and wind, pressing together.

The window went white.

Not clouds. Blizzard.

Obia stood at the front of the cabin. Its eyes were hard blue-white.

"Signal," it said. "Still there. Still below us."

Wind and snow hit the hull from all sides. The sound was very loud. Dango pressed to Ako-chan's side and she put her hand on him.

"Can you still hear it?" she asked.

"I can still hear it," said Obia. "We go down."

The flying boat began to descend. Slowly. Carefully.

Riro pressed back against his capsule and said nothing about his fur. Daru pulled back slightly inside the green pod — not all the way, just bracing. Gabu was completely still in the orange pod. His eyes did not move.

Suke-san had moved to the front of the cabin when the blizzard started. He faced forward, body low, ears flat. He was using the same stillness he used in the forests of Japan — that listening kind of quiet that used his whole body.

Outside: white. All of it white.

The flying boat went down, and down.

Ako-chan could not see the ice. There was nothing to see — just the white and the sound of the wind. But Obia heard the signal. And she trusted that.

The signal did not stop.

Obia kept its eyes forward. It kept going down, through the white, toward the sound below.

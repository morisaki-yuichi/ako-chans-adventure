The flying boat flies on above the sea. Hour after hour, it moves south. Below, there is only water. The land is far behind them now. No coast. No island. Just the wide grey sea. The horizon runs on without an end.

Ako-chan looks out the long window. White clouds stream past the wings. They break apart, then join again. The boat hums under her feet. She feels it deep in her chest. Far below, a lone bird crosses the waves. Then it is gone, and the sea is empty. There is no place to land out here. South is the only way now.

Inside, the cabin is calm and quiet. Each friend keeps to a still place. Dango sleeps close beside her feet. Suke-san watches the grey sky, sharp and silent. Daru and Gabu rest in their Pods. Ruka does not move in her yellow Pod. Obia stands steady by the controls. No one speaks for a long time. Only the engine fills the air.

The hours pass, and the world changes. The light outside turns thin and pale. The water below grows darker than before. Its grey turns deep, almost black. The air in the cabin grows cold. Ako-chan pulls her coat in close. She breathes on the glass and watches it cloud. Frost starts to touch the window edge. The warm seas are far behind them now.

South, and ever south, they fly. The water turns hard and cold below. Ako-chan watches it from her window. Something waits at the end of the sea. She cannot see it yet. But it is there. And it draws near with every hour.

Behind Pen-san, another bird comes near. This one is smaller, but very straight. It walks with slow, sure steps. Its back is black, its belly white. White rings circle its two eyes. Ako-chan knows that face at once. Her heart jumps with sudden joy.

"Colonel Adelie!" Ako-chan calls out. The bird stops still in front of her. He stands tall and proud as ever. But Pen-san shakes his head, glad. "He is a General now!" he says. "He went up in rank! A real General!" Pen-san puffs his chest out with pride.

The General lifts his chin up high. "Correct. General." His voice is short and firm. "Now—report." His eyes are sharp and still. He did not change at all in three years. Small, strict, and straight as a rod. Ako-chan stands up tall and smiles.

Pen-san beams beside his old teacher. He is so proud of the General now. The white eye-rings stand out bright. His black coat shines in the pale light. His feet are firm and even on the snow. He holds his head high and still. Years of duty show in every move.

"We came from the north," she says. "We followed a signal to the ice." The General gives a single short nod. Dango watches the strict bird, quiet. Daru calls out a hello from his Pod. The friends gather close around them all.

The General turns to lead them on. But first he looks at Ako-chan once more. For just a breath, his eyes go soft. He is glad to see her back. He says nothing of it, of course. Behind the strict General, a friend smiles.

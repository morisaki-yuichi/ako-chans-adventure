A small dark shape in the water below.

Not drifting. Moving — a smooth easy loop, then a long glide on the surface. On its back. Front paws pressed to chest. Eyes up at the sky.

Not struggling. Not cold and frightened, the way he had been the first time. This was something else. This was at home.

"He looks well," Obia said.

Riro had seen the flying boat. He turned in the water and swam toward it. Quick, easy strokes.

Obia brought the flying boat down low over the waves. A wide flat surface opened at the side, close to the water.

Riro swam up. He put his front paws on the edge and pulled himself out. He shook once. He stood up and looked around.

His fur was full and well kept. Every part of it.

He looked at Ako-chan.

"You came back," he said.

"Yes."

"I found good water."

"I can see that."

He looked at her for a moment. Then he reached up and touched his own fur along his back — pressing it down, smoothing it. It was already flat and clean. He smoothed it again anyway.

"I still cannot reach the back part myself," he said.

Ako-chan laughed.

Riro looked past her into the flying boat. He saw Dango. Obia. Suke-san. Then he saw Daru. Then Gabu.

"Everyone is here," he said.

"Almost," Ako-chan said.

He looked at her.

"One more stop," she said. "South."

Riro nodded. He looked back at the sea — one long look at the dark cold water.

Then he walked up into the flying boat.

Inside, Obia had set something in the corner near the left wall. A capsule — bigger than the one from the ship. Already running. A low hum, and the sound of water moving inside.

Riro looked at it. He pressed one paw flat against the surface.

"For the long way south," Obia said.

He turned around once and settled against it.

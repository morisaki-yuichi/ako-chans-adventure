Riro was in his corner.

He had been there since morning. He was grooming — chest to stomach to his sides. Very thorough. Very serious. Behind him, the capsule hummed quietly.

Daru watched. "He does this for a very long time," he said to Gabu.

"He always has," Gabu said.

Neither of them moved. Daru's head was still. Gabu's eyes were still. Outside the window the sea went by, grey and cold.

Then Riro stopped.

He had seen Ruka.

Ruka was in her warm corner. She was looking at him.

Riro looked at her. He looked at her shell. He moved his head a little to the right. He looked at the shell again.

"No fur," he said.

"No," said Ako-chan.

Riro got up and walked to Ruka. Slowly. Ruka watched him come. She did not pull back.

He stopped close to her. He studied the shell — the bright yellow, the bold dark marks. He reached out one paw and very gently touched the edge of the shell. Ruka turned her head to look at his paw.

Riro pulled his paw back.

He sat back and studied her.

"The shell is very smooth," he said.

"Yes," Ako-chan said.

"It does not need grooming," Riro said. He sounded almost satisfied about this — as if he approved of the design.

He walked back to his corner and settled there again. After a moment, he began to groom once more.

Ruka watched him for a long time. She did not look away. Her head was still. Her eyes were not.

Dango had watched the whole thing from his place near Ako-chan. His tail moved once.

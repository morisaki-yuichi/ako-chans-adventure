Riro found his spot in the flying boat.

He walked slowly along the cabin, looking at everything. He pressed one paw to the wall, then pressed it again a little further along. He looked at the floor. He looked at the ceiling. Then he found a corner near the left window, turned around twice, and settled there.

He began to groom.

Daru watched. "He does this for a very long time," he said to Gabu.

"He always has," Gabu said.

Riro worked from his chest to his stomach to his sides. Very thorough. Very serious.

Then he stopped.

He had seen Ruka.

Ruka was in her warm corner. She was looking at him.

Riro looked at her. He looked at her shell. He moved his head a little to the right. He looked at the shell again.

"No fur," he said.

"No," said Ako-chan.

Riro got up and walked to Ruka. Slowly. Ruka watched him come. She did not pull back.

He stopped close to her. He studied the shell — the pale yellow color, the dark marks. He reached out one paw and very gently touched the edge of the shell. Ruka turned her head to look at his paw.

Riro pulled his paw back.

He sat back and studied her.

"The shell is very smooth," he said.

"Yes," Ako-chan said.

"It does not need grooming," Riro said. He sounded almost satisfied about this — as if he approved of the design.

He walked back to his corner and settled there again. After a moment, he began to groom once more.

Ruka watched him for a long time. She did not look away.

Dango had watched the whole thing from his place near Ako-chan. His tail moved once.

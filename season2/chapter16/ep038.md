Riro was in his corner.

He had been grooming since morning — chest, then stomach, then sides. Each part done slowly, done well. Outside the window the North Pacific moved below, dark and wide. Riro did not look at it. He was busy.

Daru was watching him from across the cabin.

"He does this for a very long time," Daru said to Gabu.

"He always has," said Gabu.

Neither of them moved. The flying boat flew on. Below: grey water. Above: grey sky. The same view it had been for hours.

Then Riro stopped.

Ruka was looking at him. She was in her corner on the other side of the cabin. She had not moved. But her eyes were on him, calm and steady.

Riro looked back. He looked at her shell — the bright yellow, the bold dark marks. He moved his head a little to the right and looked at it again.

"No fur," he said.

"No," said Ako-chan.

Riro got up. He walked across the cabin to Ruka, slowly, one step at a time. Ruka watched him come. She did not pull back.

He stopped close to her. He studied the shell carefully. Then he reached out one paw and touched the edge of it, very gently. Ruka turned her head to look at his paw. She did not move away.

Riro pulled his paw back. He looked at his paw. He looked at the shell.

"The shell is very smooth," he said.

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

"It does not need grooming," Riro said. He sounded almost pleased — as if he approved of the design.

He walked back to his corner and settled there again. He began to groom once more.

Ruka watched him. She did not look away.

Dango had watched the whole thing from his place near Ako-chan. His tail moved once.

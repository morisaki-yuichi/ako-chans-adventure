Land appeared in the morning.

Dark green hills, long and low across the horizon. The sea below the flying boat turned grey-green instead of black.

Dango sat up before anyone spoke. He pressed his nose to the window and left a small cloud on the glass.

"Japan," Obia said.

Ako-chan looked out. The coast came closer — pine trees at the tops of the hills, pale rock at their feet, and water between. She could not hear any animals yet. But she felt them. Quiet. Watchful. Already aware.

The flying boat came down slowly between the hills. A wide green field below. They landed without a sound.

The door opened. Cool air came in — and with it the smell of pine, wet earth, old stone.

Dango went out first. He put his nose to the ground and turned left, then right. His tail moved — not fast, but certain.

Ruka stood at the top of the ramp. She looked at the field. A bird called from the trees at the edge — clear and high. She turned her head toward it.

Daru came down the ramp quickly. He touched the grass with one foot. "It is soft," he said. "Not like the coast. But it is good."

Gabu stood at the top of the ramp for a moment. He looked at the field, then at the trees. He came down slowly.

Ako-chan breathed in. Wet earth. Pine. Cold stone. She had come to Japan by ship the first time. She had stepped off in a harbor and smelled exactly this.

"Obia. Where?"

"Two kilometers east. I know the place."

Dango was already facing east. He had not moved his eyes from the trees.

Ako-chan looked at the green hills and thought: he already knows we are here.

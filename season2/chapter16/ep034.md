"You came back," Suke-san said.

"Yes." Ako-chan kept her hand low. "We came back."

He looked at her carefully. Missing nothing.

"You kept listening," he said.

"Yes."

He studied her face for a moment more. Then he looked at Dango.

Dango came forward one step. He stopped just in front of Suke-san. The two dogs stood close. Neither barked. Neither growled.

"Hello," said Dango. Quiet.

"Hello," said Suke-san. Just as quiet.

They stepped back, one each. That was enough.

Suke-san looked at Obia. His ears stayed up. His body was still. He did not tighten or pull back — he just looked, one clear, long look.

"You watched over her," he said.

"Yes," Obia said.

Suke-san looked away. That was all.

Then he saw the small shape at Daru's feet. A bright yellow shell, very small. Strong dark marks on the top. It was looking at him.

He went still.

He moved one slow step to the left to see better. The small tortoise turned its head to follow him.

Suke-san lowered his nose — slowly, a little at a time. Not a quick sniff. He took his time. Ruka watched him. She did not pull back. She did not go into her shell.

A long moment.

Suke-san straightened up. He looked at Ako-chan.

"Its name?" he asked.

"Ruka."

He looked at Ruka once more. Then at Daru. Daru's eyes were very bright. Gabu was watching in his usual way — patient, taking everything in.

The park was quiet around them. The trees moved a little in the wind.

Suke-san looked toward the flying boat, then back at Ako-chan.

"When do we go?" he asked.

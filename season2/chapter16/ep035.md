They walked back to the flying boat together.

Suke-san walked on Ako-chan's right — a little back, a little to the side. The same place as always. As if he had never stopped.

Dango walked on her left.

Daru had run ahead. His face was already at the window of the flying boat — nose pressed to the glass, watching them come down the road.

Gabu stood at the top of the ramp. He watched Suke-san come up. Suke-san looked back at him.

Neither spoke. That was enough.

Suke-san walked into the cabin and found his spot — right side, a little back, a little to the side from Ako-chan's place. He sat down. He looked out at the green field still outside the window — the trees, the pale stone road they had come down.

He was settled.

Ruka was in her warm corner. She looked at Suke-san. He looked at her once, then looked away.

Dango lay down on Ako-chan's left. Obia started the engines.

The flying boat lifted off the green field. The trees went below. The dark hills of Japan spread wide under the morning sky.

They went north.

Ako-chan looked around the cabin — Daru at the window, Gabu still, Ruka between them, Suke-san settled and quiet, Dango at her side, Obia at the front.

Seven, now. And one more to find.

She thought of Riro floating on his back in the cold grey water. Paws pressed to his chest. Eyes on the sky. She thought of what he had said when they left him there: "Good water. Good food. Good fur. Good friends."

The water ahead was the right kind for him. Cold and moving and deep.

She looked out at the grey sea and did not look away.

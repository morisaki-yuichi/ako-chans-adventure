The North Pacific was cold and wide.

The flying boat went low over the water. Below: dark waves, white foam, nothing else. No land. No ships. Just the sea and the grey sky.

Daru was at the window. He had been there a long time. "There is nothing there," he said.

"Look at the water," Ako-chan said.

Daru looked. He was quiet for a moment. "It is always moving."

"Yes."

"It never stops."

"No."

He pressed his nose close to the glass. His breath made a small cloud.

Gabu was watching from farther back. Ruka was between him and Daru, looking at the water with the same still attention as Gabu.

Suke-san was on Ako-chan's right. He was not looking at the water. He was looking ahead.

"How close?" he asked.

Obia: "Getting close. The signal is moving in the water. I am following it."

Suke-san's nose went up. He turned his head a little. "I can smell it," he said. "The water here. It is different."

"Different from Japan?" Ako-chan asked.

"Yes. Colder. Older."

Dango moved to the window. He looked down at the waves below.

"He is here somewhere," Dango said.

"Yes," Ako-chan said.

She looked at the dark water. She thought of Riro — the small wet shape she had lifted from the sea the first time. So cold, so heavy. She thought of his fur and the way he cared for it. She thought of his face when he had said goodbye: "Good water. Good food. Good fur. Good friends."

The water below was good here. The right kind of cold. The right kind of moving.

"There," Obia said.

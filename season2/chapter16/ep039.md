The flying boat turned south.

The North Pacific stretched behind them. Below: grey water, then lighter grey as they moved. The cold color of the north was fading.

Inside, everyone was settled.

Daru was at the window. He was always at the window. This time he was quiet. He watched the water change color below.

Gabu was still. Ruka had moved: she was between Gabu and Riro now. Not touching either. Just there. She had chosen it herself.

Suke-san was on Ako-chan's right, a little back, a little to the side. The same place as always.

Dango was on her left.

Riro was in his corner, fur in good order. He had his front paws together and was looking at the water below with a calm, satisfied expression.

Obia was at the front.

Ako-chan looked around the cabin at each of them. Daru at the window. Gabu still. Ruka between Gabu and Riro. Riro at peace with his fur. Suke-san quiet and certain. Dango close.

Eight of them. Moving south.

She thought of the ones they were going toward. Pen-san. The Colonel. And whatever was waiting under the ice. The signal Obia was following. The locked memory.

Dango pressed against her side.

She put her hand on his back.

"Not long now," she said — quietly, to herself as much as to him.

Dango made a small sound.

Outside, the clouds were moving fast. The sky ahead was a deeper colour than the sky behind.

She opened the red notebook and looked at the last entry: *Day thirty-one. Daru is with us. Gabu is with us. Ruka is between them. We go east now.*

She closed it again without writing. Not yet.

South was getting closer.

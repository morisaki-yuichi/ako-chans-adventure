The sky ahead was clear.

The flying boat went south through the afternoon and into the evening. Below: the sea. Above: pale sky growing darker at the edges.

Obia: "We will reach the cold waters by tomorrow."

Suke-san's ears moved once. Then were still.

Daru was asleep at the window, his face tipped against the glass. His shell rose and fell slowly.

Gabu was watching the cabin. Not the window — the cabin. His eyes moved from Ruka to Riro to Suke-san. Checking. Settled.

Ruka was in her warm corner. She had gone into her shell. Just the top was visible — bright yellow, strong dark marks.

Riro had finished grooming. He sat with his front paws together, very still, looking at the water below.

Dango was at Ako-chan's side. Not lying down — sitting up, looking out the window beside her.

Ako-chan opened the red notebook.

She looked at the last entry: *Day thirty-one. Daru is with us. Gabu is with us. Ruka is between them. We go east now.*

She wrote:

*Day thirty-seven. Suke-san is with us. Riro is with us. We are all here. We go south.*

She closed the notebook.

The flying boat flew on. The sea below was darker now. The sky ahead was the color of deep water.

South.

Somewhere there: ice. Pen-san. The Colonel. And something under the ice that had been waiting a long time.

Dango pressed close to her side.

She held the red notebook in both hands. She was not afraid — or not only afraid. Something else too. Something that felt like being ready.

The south was waiting.

They were going.

The park was at the end of a quiet road.

Not large. Trees at the edges, close and dark green. An open path in the middle, pale stone showing under the roots. It smelled of bark and earth.

Obia: "The signal is here."

The birds in the branches were quiet. No wind.

Dango stopped.

He did not bark. He did not growl. He just stopped and looked ahead.

In the middle of the path, very still, stood a small compact dog. Reddish-brown. Round face. Pointed nose. Ears straight up. Thick tail curled high over his back.

He was not hiding. He was not circling the trees.

He was standing in the open, watching them come. He had known they were coming. He had chosen his place and waited.

The two dogs looked at each other across the path. A long time. Neither moved.

She thought of the first time — how Suke-san had circled their camp for two nights, how he had watched from the trees, how he had only come when he was ready. This time he had come out before they even arrived.

Ako-chan walked forward. She walked past Dango, down the path, until she was close. Close enough to see the dark bright eyes. The still, certain body.

She knelt down.

Suke-san looked up at her. Direct. Not one thing extra.

Behind her: Daru, Gabu, Ruka in a row. Dango at her side. Obia a little back.

The trees were quiet around them.

She put her hand out slowly, palm up.

Suke-san stepped forward. He pressed his nose against her palm — briefly. Then he stepped back.

"You came back," he said.

A few days pass in the quiet garden. The autumn light grows pale and low. Slowly, the friends learn their new home. But they still ask Ruka for help. Where is the warm wall? Where is the safe corner? Ruka always has the answer. She came from this garden, after all.

One bright morning, Daru watches Ruka. She rolls straight to the best sunny spot. She never gets lost, not once. Daru turns to Gabu, full of wonder. "Ruka knows everything here!" he says. Gabu watches the little turtle too. He gives a slow, quiet nod. "...She does," he says. It is true, and they both feel it.

Ruka is the smallest of the three. Her shell is the littlest of all. Ako-chan met Daru and Gabu long ago. She found Ruka last of everyone. That was in Puglia, by a warm white wall. The little turtle climbed onto her hand back then. Now Ruka leads them through the garden. The newest friend knows the most.

Around them, the garden settles, soft and still. The autumn air is cool and calm. Yellow leaves drift down from the trees. Each friend has a place of their own now. Ruka's place is right at the front. She is small, but she stands first.

Daru and Gabu follow her without question. They are bigger, but she is wiser here. Ruka came last of all, yet she leads first. Time and place changed the order between them. Ako-chan watches the three turtles and smiles. Her family is finding its new shape. The little one shows the big ones the way. Somehow, that feels exactly right.

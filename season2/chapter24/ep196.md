A few days pass in the quiet garden. The autumn light grows pale and low. For Daru, Gabu, and Suke-san, this place is still half-strange. But one among them is wholly at home. Ruka rests at her old sunny spot, calm and unhurried. She settles there the way she always does.

One bright morning, Daru watches her. She never seems lost, not once. He turns to Gabu, full of wonder. "Ruka knows everything here!" he says. Gabu watches the little turtle too. He gives a slow, quiet nod. "...She does," he says. It is true, and they both feel it.

Ruka is the smallest of the three. Her shell is the littlest of all. The others are bigger, older friends. But here, they follow the small one's lead. The big newcomers watch where she goes. She does not seem to notice at all. She only rests in her warm light, sure of every corner. She knows each warm stone, each patch of shade. The garden kept them for her, long before the others came.

Around them, the garden settles, soft and still. Yellow leaves drift down through the cold air. The big ones came a long, hard way to this place. The small one was simply here, from the start. No one decided the order between them. It turned on its own, quiet and slow.

Daru and Gabu defer to her without a word. They are bigger, but she knows this place best. Time and place changed the shape of the family. Ako-chan watches the three small shells and smiles. The last to come is the still centre now. Somehow, that feels exactly right.

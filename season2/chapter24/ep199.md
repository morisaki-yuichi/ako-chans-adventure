It is night now. Ako-chan sits at her small desk. The lamp is on, soft and warm. It is the only light in the room. Outside, the winter garden is dark. Dango lies quiet at her feet. The house is still all around her. The room is warm and very quiet. On the desk is the red notebook.

She did not open it for a long time. The red cover is worn and soft. She opens it to the very first page. There are the words from that first night. "I have to go." Four small words. They are the only words in the book. She reads them slowly, one more time. So much happened after that night.

Ako-chan turns to a new clean page. It is white and empty and new. She picks up her pen and holds it. For a long moment she does not move. Then she writes. Just one word. A name. "Obia—" The pen stops there. No more words come.

The lamp hums low in the quiet room. The red cover glows in the soft light. Dango lifts his head and watches her. He does not understand her stillness. Then he rests his head down again. Only that one short line sits there.

The name sits alone on the white page. After it, there is only a long dash. She wants to write more. But the words will not come. How can one line hold so much? She holds the pen above the page. But she does not write again. Ako-chan just looks at the name. She looks at it for a long, long time.

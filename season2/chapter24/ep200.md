It is night again. The lamp glows, soft and warm. Ako-chan sits at her small desk. But tonight she is not alone. Dango lies close against her feet. Suke-san rests quiet at her side. Daru, Gabu, and Ruka are near too. The animals gather close in the warm light. The red notebook lies open before her.

She reads it from the very first page. "I have to go." The journey begins right there. Then she turns the pages, one by one. On Croatia's rocks, Daru ran to her. Daru lifts his head at his own page. In the hot desert, Gabu waited, calm and still. Far away in Japan, Suke-san fixed the old boat.

The pages turn to the cold white south. A great storm came, and Obia spoke at last. Then the flying boat rose into the sky. The story holds the three lost voices now. The wolves in the snow. The gentle birds. The giant shell, deep in the ice. And then the day they said goodbye to Obia.

As she reads, the journey comes alive again. Old voices and faces rise up once more. The friends lean close and listen with her. Each one is here, warm in the soft light.

At last she reaches the final page. There sits one name. "Obia——" After it, the page is still white. She did not write more, not yet. The whole long journey lives in this book. Every voice, every face, every step. Her family is here, warm beside her. Dango, Suke-san, Daru, Gabu, Ruka. They are all here now. All but one. The story is full tonight. Only the last line waits for more.

The house was quiet. Dango was asleep at the foot of Ako-chan's bed. Ruka was in the corner, very still, inside the shell.

It was late.

Ako-chan was nearly asleep. The room was dark. The oak tree made a soft sound outside the window.

Then a light came on.

Blue-white. From the garden.

She sat up.

Obia was outside. Standing in its corner. But its eyes were different. Not the soft blue-white she knew. Brighter. Harder.

She went down the stairs. She opened the back door. The garden was cold and very dark. The light from Obia's eyes was the only light.

"Obia?" she said.

"I hear something," said Obia.

Ako-chan waited.

"Very far away," said Obia. "Very small. I cannot tell what it is yet."

"Where is it from?"

Obia turned its head slowly. South. Its eyes moved south.

"That way," said Obia.

The night air was cold on Ako-chan's face. She looked south too. Past the garden. Past the fence. Past the road and the town and the sea far beyond.

"The ground is quiet," she said. Almost to herself.

"Yes," said Obia. "The ground is quiet. But this is not from the ground."

A cloud moved over the stars. The garden went very dark.

Dango came to the door. He pressed against her leg. He looked south too.

The three of them stood there. Ako-chan. Dango. Obia.

None of them spoke.

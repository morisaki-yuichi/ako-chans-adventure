The red notebook was almost full.

Ako-chan opened it on her desk. The first page still said the same three words: *I have to go.* She remembered writing them. The morning before they left. Before the train. Before everything.

She turned the pages slowly. Italy. Croatia. The desert. Japan. The sea. The ice.

Ruka was on the desk beside her. Ruka could not read. But Ruka watched anyway. Very still. Very close.

Dango was asleep near the door.

Outside, the oak tree was dark against the evening sky.

Ako-chan stopped near the end of the notebook. She read what she wrote the day she came home: *Day one hundred and seven. England. We are home.* And then, lower on the page: *We are not finished.*

She sat with that for a while.

Were they finished? She did not know. The ground was quiet. The voices of the animals were calm. Daru was in Croatia. Gabu was in Jordan. Suke-san was in Japan. Riro was in the north Pacific. Pen-san and General Adelie were on the ice.

All of them were where they should be.

And yet.

She picked up her pen. She wrote the date. She looked at the blank page.

*Everything is quiet,* she wrote. *Ruka is on my desk. Dango is asleep. The oak tree is outside. We are all here.*

She looked at what she wrote. Then she added one more line.

*For now.*

She closed the notebook. Ruka moved one step closer. One small foot on the edge of her hand.

Ako-chan did not move.

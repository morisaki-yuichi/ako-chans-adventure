The hedgehog came in the evening.

It always came this way. Slowly, from under the rosebush. Moving through the grass without hurry.

Ako-chan saw it first. She was sitting on the step. She did not move.

The hedgehog came up to the path and stopped. It looked at her.

"Hello," said Ako-chan.

"Hello," said the hedgehog. It sat still for a moment. Then it said, "The ground is quiet."

"Yes," said Ako-chan.

"Not just tonight. For a long time now. The ground has been quiet for a long time."

Ako-chan nodded. She remembered when it was not quiet. When the hedgehog could not sleep. When the robin did not sing the right way. When the fox said the smell of the night air was wrong.

"I am glad," she said.

"Yes," said the hedgehog. Then it turned its head a little. "What is that?"

Ruka had come around the corner of the step. Moving in the way Ruka always moved. Toward the hedgehog. Small and careful.

The hedgehog did not move.

Ruka stopped. Very close. The two of them looked at each other.

Dango watched from near the fence. He sat up straight.

"That is Ruka," said Ako-chan. "Ruka is new."

"New," said the hedgehog.

"Yes. From Italy."

The hedgehog looked at Ruka for a long moment. Then it turned back to Ako-chan. "Good," it said. And walked on through the garden.

Ruka watched it go. Then looked back at Ako-chan.

Dango made a small sound. Not a bark. Just a small sound from his throat.

The evening light was low and gold. Very still.

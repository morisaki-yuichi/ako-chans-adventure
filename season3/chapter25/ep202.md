It is a slow afternoon in early winter. The garden is quiet under a pale sky. The light comes low and golden through the trees. A soft wind moves the last brown leaves. The air smells of cold earth and wet grass. Ako-chan sits on the old wooden bench. She has come out to sit with them awhile.

Each friend has a place of their own. Dalu rests on the warm sunny stone. His shell soaks up the low winter sun. Gabu sits in the shade of the tree. He likes the cool, dim ground there. Suke-san stays just behind Ako-chan, to her right. Luca waits up high on her shelf. From there she watches the whole garden. Nothing moves them along. They are simply where they belong. They have kept these same spots for years now.

Dango lies in the middle of them all. His warm weight leans against her foot. The wind turns, and the bare branches sway. A few late leaves drift down to the grass. Somewhere a wood pigeon coos, slow and round. The cold air carries the smell of damp earth. Ako-chan listens to the small, calm voices. Each one is settled. Each one is near. The whole garden breathes in the quiet light.

Nothing happens here. No sound calls from the south. No strange thing pulls at the wind. For years, there was a pull that never stopped. It hummed under everything, day and night. Now there is only this. The garden. The friends. The light. Ako-chan closes her eyes and feels it. The bench is cool and solid beneath her. The afternoon holds still around them all. The quiet is not empty. It is full.

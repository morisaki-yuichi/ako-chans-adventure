It is a slow afternoon in early winter. The garden lies hushed under a pale sky. The light comes low and golden through the trees. Ako-chan sits on the old wooden bench. She has come out to sit with them awhile.

Each friend has a place of their own. Dalu rests on the warm sunny stone. Gabu sits in the shade of the tree. Suke-san stays just behind Ako-chan, to her right. Luca waits up high on her shelf. From there she watches the whole garden. Their Pods sit quiet and empty nearby. Here at home, they rest outside them. They have kept these same spots for years now. "I was first to the stone today," says Dalu. From the shade, Gabu answers, slow and dry. "...You are always first." Dalu stretches out in the sun, happy. Luca says nothing at all, and that is her way.

The three tortoises have grown alike in size. To a stranger's eye, they look almost the same. But Ako-chan can tell them apart with ease. Dalu's shell is bold, deep black on yellow, and he still moves first. Gabu's yellow shell has a dark spot on each plate, and his quiet has grown deeper. Luca's shell shows a wide net of black. She moves slowly, stopping, then moving again. Suke-san watches them all from his place. Once his eyes were sharp and wary. Now his watching has softened into care.

Dango lies in the middle of them all. His warm weight leans against her foot. A wood pigeon coos somewhere, slow and round. The cold air carries a thread of woodsmoke. Seven years have shaped each one of them. The years rest gently in each small body. Nothing happens here. And the quiet is not empty. It is full.

It is late, and the house is asleep. Ako-chan sits at her small desk. One lamp glows softly in the dark room. Outside, the garden is black and still. Dango lies quiet and warm at her feet. On the desk rests the old red notebook. She has not opened it for a long time. Tonight, something made her reach for it.

She lifts the cover, worn soft with years. She turns the pages slowly, one by one. Most are white — she wrote so little down. But the whole journey lives in her still. The first words. The far places. The friends. The long road there, and the road home. Then she reaches the very last line. One name, and a long dash after it. "Obia—" The ink is old and dry now. Seven years, and the line has not grown. She has never found the words to follow it.

Ako-chan picks up her pen and holds it. The lamp hums low in the quiet room. The red cover glows in the soft light. The old ink is faded, but still clear. The house is silent all around her. Outside, the dark garden does not stir. Dango lifts his head and watches her. He does not understand her stillness. Then he settles his head back down.

She holds the pen above the empty page. For a long moment, she does not move. But the words still will not come tonight. They have not come in all that time. Slowly, she closes the red notebook. The name stays inside, alone in the dark. Something is missing from the sleeping house. She feels it near her, soft and close. Then she turns off the lamp.

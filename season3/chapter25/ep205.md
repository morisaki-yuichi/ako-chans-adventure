Rain falls softly on the window. It is a dull afternoon, cold and wet. Ako-chan sits inside, warm and dry. Dango sleeps by her feet, slow and calm. The rain taps the glass, again and again. She listens to it, half awake.

The sound carries her back, far back. Once, long ago, there was a great storm. They were sailing south across a cold sea. The waves rose high and dark and wild. The wind screamed. The rain came down like stones. The ship rocked and groaned in the swell. Then, through the roar, a deep voice came. The cold spray stung against Ako-chan's face. A great whale rose beside them in the waves. "This way," it said. "Follow us. Go where we go." The whales made a great ring around the ship. Their broad backs held back the breaking waves. They led them safely through the night.

Ako-chan opens her eyes again. The storm is gone. The sea is far away. No waves rise outside. No wind cries. Only the gentle rain remains, light and kind. The room is warm. The fire burns low. The whole house is hushed and dim. Dango breathes slow and deep beside her. The roar of that night lives only in her memory.

She listens to the rain a little longer. The memory does not hurt her now. It is simply there, quiet and whole. It rests in her, deep and calm. So many storms. So many voices in the dark. They carried her all the way home again. Through wind and water, through the long night. Now the rain falls light on a calm house. And Ako-chan is warm and safe.

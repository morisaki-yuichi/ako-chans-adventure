The garden is the same each day. But each friend is their own small world. Ako-chan watches them in the soft light. Seven years have shaped each one of them. She knows each shell, each step, each shape. She can tell them all apart with ease. Each one carries the journey in their own way.

The three tortoises have grown alike in size. To a stranger's eye, they look almost the same now, hard to tell apart. But their shells and their ways are still different. Daru's shell is bold, deep black on yellow. He still moves first. He races ahead in his Pod. But he is calmer now than long ago. Gabu's yellow shell has a dark spot on each plate. He walks in a steady, even rhythm. He watches everything before he takes a step. His quiet has only grown deeper with the years.

Ruka's shell shows a wide net of black. A small yellow spot marks each cheek. She moves slowly, stopping, then moving again. She takes her time, and the others let her. Suke-san stands a little apart from them. Once he watched the world with sharp, wary eyes. His ears would turn at the smallest sound. Now his watching has softened into care. He keeps his calm place at Ako-chan's side.

Ako-chan watches the three Pods glow — red, blue, yellow. Each color moves in its own slow way. The years sit quietly in each small body. In the way Daru rushes. In the way Gabu waits. In the way Ruka stops to look. She sees it in every small, sure movement. Time has settled into all of them. And into her too, she thinks.

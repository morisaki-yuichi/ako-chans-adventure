It is a bright, cool afternoon in winter. Ako-chan takes the friends to the park, as she often does. It is just a short walk down the road. The park is wide and green and open. Tall bare trees stand along the paths. Frost still lies in the shaded corners. Children play and call far across the grass.

They go together in their usual way. Dango walks proudly at the front. His nose lifts at every new smell. Suke-san keeps to the back, calm and watchful. In the middle roll the three Pods. Daru's glows red, quick and eager. Gabu's glows blue, steady and even. Ruka's glows yellow, slow and calm. The three lights move across the green grass. The Pods hum softly as they turn. A few people turn to look as they pass.

The air smells of cold grass and earth. Dry leaves crackle under their feet. The winter sun comes down through the branches. It scatters light across the path in patches. Somewhere a dog barks, then falls quiet. Children laugh beyond the line of trees. The cold air is fresh against her face. Ako-chan breathes in the open, easy air. It feels good to be out here together.

Ako-chan sits on the wooden bench by the trees. She lets out a slow, deep breath. Dango lies at her feet right away. Suke-san settles just behind her, on guard. The three Pods glow softly in the grass. Everyone finds their place, easy and sure. The friends are at home in this place. The afternoon feels still and open. This is their park, known and loved. They have walked here for a long time.

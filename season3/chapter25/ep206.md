Evening comes slowly to the garden. The light grows soft behind the bare trees. Suke-san sits by the open door. The stone step is cold beneath his paws. He looks out at the still, calm garden. The air smells of damp leaves and coming frost. The garden settles into the hush of dusk.

Suke-san watches the world the way he always has. Long ago, he watched for danger. His ears would lift at every small sound. His nose would catch the smallest wrong thing. But the world holds no danger now. "Quiet," he says, low and calm. "The world is quiet now." Ako-chan hears him from inside the house. His sharp old watching has turned gentle. Now he keeps watch the way a friend does.

A small wind stirs the bare branches. Somewhere far off, a dog barks once. Suke-san's ears turn toward the sound. Then they settle back again. He is calm. The garden grows dim and cool around him. The first stars show in the darkening sky. Lights glow in the windows far away. A late bird calls its last note of the day. The smell of frost grows stronger in the dark. The night air is sharp and clean. Ako-chan listens to it all from her chair.

But Suke-san does not go inside yet. Something in him is still waiting. He does not know what it is. He has waited like this for a long time. He turns his head toward the dark corner. He looks at the place where no one stands. Then he looks back out at the quiet night. He stays by the door a little longer.

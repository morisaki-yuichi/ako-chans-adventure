The garden lies silent in the winter light. In one corner stands the old wooden shed. Beside it, the ground has stayed bare for years. Nothing has grown to fill that space. The grass around it leans low and thin, as if it still remembers to keep the spot clear. Each morning, frost settles there and traces the same shape. The little space waits, the way it has waited a long time.

That corner was once a place of watching. On dark nights, a pale blue glow shone there. The light rose softly in the cold and quiet. That glow kept close beside Ako-chan, always near. Its quiet light was part of the garden then. But no glow rises in that corner now. The nights are dark there, and very still. No one keeps the long watch anymore. The bare ground holds only frost and shadow.

The other animals feel the empty space too. By now, they move around it without a thought. Their feet simply know the shape to avoid. Dango never lies on that bare grass. Suke-san passes by without a sound, as he always does. Even Ruka slows at its edge and turns away. The corner stays bare and still. The wind moves through it and finds nothing. Even the morning sun seems to pass it by. The light falls on the ground and rests on no one.

Ako-chan stands at the window and looks out. Her eyes rest on that one empty corner. The garden is full of warm life again. The friends are home. The long journey is over. They all came home at last. But that one place stays empty in the cold. The absence has a shape of its own now. It waits there, quiet, where the glow used to be.

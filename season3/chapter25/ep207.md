It is a bright, cool afternoon in early winter. Ako-chan takes the friends to the park, as she often does. It is just a short walk down the road. The park is wide and green and open. Tall bare trees stand along the paths. Children play and call far across the grass.

They go together in their usual way. Dango walks proudly at the front. Suke-san keeps to the back, calm and watchful. In the middle roll the three Pods — red, blue, yellow. The Pods hum softly as they turn. A few people turn to look as they pass, and then walk on. Ako-chan sits on the wooden bench by the trees. Dango lies at her feet right away. Everyone finds their place, easy and sure. They have walked here for a long time.

A heavy flap comes down from the high branches. Kara-san lands on the back of the bench. He is the oldest crow in the park. Ako-chan gave him his name years ago, and he has never minded it. "You are late today," he says, rough and plain. "The bench was cold without you." "Good afternoon, Kara-san," says Ako-chan, smiling. "Hm. Afternoon. Quiet sky. Nothing wrong." He picks at a wing feather, then settles. Day after day, the visit is the same. The same bench, the same hour, the same voices. The sky changes colour, from pale to gold. More leaves drift down and gather on the grass. The afternoons pass, gentle and alike.

Then, one afternoon, something is different. Far across the park, someone stands alone. A still figure, watching from the trees. Ako-chan does not know him yet. But there he is, quiet in the distance.

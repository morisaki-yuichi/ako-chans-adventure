The garden lies silent in the winter light. In one corner stands the old wooden shed. Beside it is a bare patch of ground. Nothing sits there. No one stands there. The cold grass grows flat and low. Frost lingers there long into the morning. The little space waits, but it stays empty.

That corner was once a place of watching. On dark nights, a pale blue glow shone there. The light rose softly in the cold and quiet. It stayed close beside Ako-chan, always near. Its quiet light was part of the garden then. But no glow rises in that corner now. The nights are dark there, and very still. No one keeps the long watch anymore. The bare ground holds only frost and shadow.

The other animals feel the empty space too. Dango walks wide around that patch of ground. He does not lie on the bare grass. Suke-san passes by it without a sound. Even Daru's quick Pod slows down there. Ruka stops at its edge and turns away. The corner stays bare and still. The wind moves through the corner and finds nothing. Even the morning sun seems to pass it by. The light falls on the ground and rests on no one.

Ako-chan stands at the window and looks out. Her eyes rest on that one empty corner. The garden is full of warm life again. The friends are home. The long journey is over. They all came home at last. But that one place stays empty in the cold. The absence has a shape of its own now. It waits there, quiet, where the glow used to be.

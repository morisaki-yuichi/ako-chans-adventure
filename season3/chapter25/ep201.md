Morning light fills the kitchen. Ako-chan stands at the window with her tea. Steam rises from the cup, warm against her hands. Outside, the garden is wet with dew. Seven years have passed since the long journey ended. Ako-chan has grown since then. The garden looks almost the same. But small things have changed.

Dango lies in his usual spot by the door. His muzzle has turned white around the edges. He is older now, and a little slower. But he is still here, close beside her, as always. His tail thumps against the floor when she comes near. When she opens the back door, the cold morning air comes in to meet her. She steps out onto the wet grass with her cup. Dango comes out after her and settles on the step.

"The stone is warm already!" calls Dalu across the garden. "Come and feel it!" His yellow-and-black shell catches the early sun. "Good morning, Dalu," says Ako-chan. From the shade by the tree, Gabu lifts his head. "...Morning," he says, slow and calm. Luca watches from her shelf and says nothing. Her small eyes follow Ako-chan across the grass. Suke-san comes to stand at her right, a step behind. A blackbird sings in the bare trees, the same song it has sung all week. The voices in the garden are calm. Nothing is wrong. Nothing pulls at the air.

Ako-chan drinks her tea among her friends. The dew turns bright as the sun climbs higher. It is a gentle morning, like so many others. She has watched a thousand mornings like this one. And yet, after all this time, the stillness feels strange. She is not quite used to the quiet, even now. Some part of her still waits, soft and small. She does not know what for.

Morning light fills the kitchen. Ako-chan stands at the window with her tea. Steam rises from the cup, warm against her hands. Outside, the garden is wet with dew. The grass shines green and quiet. Far away, a bird calls, soft and low.

Seven years have passed since the long journey ended. Ako-chan has grown since then. The garden looks almost the same. But small things have changed. Dango lies in his usual spot by the door. His muzzle has turned white around the edges. He is older now, and a little slower. He sleeps more in the daytime than before. But he is still here, close beside her, as always. His tail thumps when she comes near.

The morning is full of small, gentle sounds. A blackbird sings somewhere in the bare trees. The kettle ticks as it cools on the stove. A car passes on the far road, then fades. The window glass is cool against her arm. The light moves slowly across the kitchen floor. Beyond the glass, frost lingers on the cold lawn. Ako-chan listens, the way she always has. The voices in the garden are calm now. Nothing is wrong. Nothing pulls at the air. The world feels settled and slow and safe.

Ako-chan drinks her tea and watches the garden. The dew turns bright as the sun climbs higher. It is a calm morning, like so many others. She has watched a thousand mornings like this one. And yet, after all this time, the stillness feels strange. She is not quite used to the quiet, even now. Some part of her still waits, soft and small. She does not know what for.

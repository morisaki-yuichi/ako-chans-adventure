The winter sun is low but warm today. It falls in a bright patch on the grass. Ruka moves slowly across the sunny ground. Her shell shines in the pale gold light. Ako-chan sits nearby and watches her. She has nowhere else she needs to be. The garden is calm and still around them.

Ruka takes her time, the way she always does. She walks a few steps, then she stops. She rests in the bright light for a while. Her dark eyes look out at the garden. Then she moves again, slow and sure. She is as large as Daru and Gabu now. But her quiet is all her own. She wears her years lightly now. The minutes pass, and Ruka does not hurry. Time seems to slow down around her.

The sun warms the smooth curve of her shell. Her small feet press gently into the grass. She lifts one foot, then sets it down with care. The light catches the yellow spots on her cheeks. Ako-chan watches the net of black on her back. For a moment, she remembers white stone walls. A dry, warm wind. A small tortoise in the sun. The memory is far away and soft. Then it fades, and the garden returns.

Ruka stops again in the middle of the light. She stretches her legs out, loose and easy. She does not move for a long while. Ako-chan does not rush her at all. She has learned to wait, the way Ruka waits. So much time has passed since that first day. All of it rests now in Ruka's stillness. The sun holds them both, calm and warm.

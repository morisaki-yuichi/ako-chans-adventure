The garden is dark. It is before the sun. The air is cold and still. Dew sits heavy on the grass. A thin mist rests by the fence. Ako-chan stands at the back door in her red glasses. She is small, only ten years old. Dango sits close beside her, a little corgi puppy. The morning is quiet, the way it always is.

Then something feels different. The wind stops. The small sounds fade away. Ako-chan does not know why. Her eyes move slowly to the far corner. The corner is dark with mist. Something small is there, and something very still.

She looks harder. Then she sees them. Two eyes. Pale and blue. They shine softly in the shadow. They look right at her red glasses. Below the two lights, she can just make out a small silver shape. And at its throat there is a little red collar. Ako-chan holds her breath and leans a little closer.

But in one blink, the eyes are gone. The corner is just grass now, just grass and mist. A drop falls from a branch and taps the grass. Soft and gray and still. The old oak stands quiet. The hedge does not move. The birds do not sing yet. Her breath is white in the cold. The girl does not move. She waits for the eyes to come back, but they do not. Something small turns in her chest. Dango leans on her leg, quiet too. She does not know what she has seen. She does not know that her whole long journey starts right here. The sun is still not up. This is where it all began, and where it has come home.

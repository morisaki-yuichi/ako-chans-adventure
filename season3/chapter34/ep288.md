More years pass than the man can count. The ice cave does not change. The cold is the same cold. The deep blue light is the same light. But the boy who came here long ago is gone. In his place there stands a man. His back is taller. His hands are harder, marked and sure. The work has made him into someone else.

He bends over the same long table, the way he has bent for years. His tools are worn smooth from his grip. Lines cross his face that the cold has cut there. The park is far from his mind now. The red light on the green grass is almost gone from him. That life belongs to a boy, and the boy is gone.

The silver one waits beside him, as it has always waited. Its blue-white eyes have not changed at all. They are the one thing in the cave that time has not touched. The two penguins stand in the shadows, older now, still keeping their long watch. They have grown a little worn at the edges. They do not leave. Season after season, they have kept near him in the cold, as steady as the ice itself.

The man looks down at his own hands. Then he looks at the great shape he has built. It is almost finished now, after all these years. He knows exactly what it is for, and he has known it for a very long time. There is no more doubt in him, and no more questions. There is only the work, and the cold, and the patient blue-white eyes. The boy once wanted to understand the beginning. The man is going to make it.

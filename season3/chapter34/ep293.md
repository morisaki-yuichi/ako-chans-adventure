Ako-chan wakes in the dark, long before morning. The house is quiet around her. Nothing woke her — no sound, no dream that she can hold. She lies still for a while, and then she rises. On the way past her desk, her hand takes the red notebook. She does not stop. She simply takes it, and goes down the stairs.

The garden is black and cool, and the dew is heavy. She steps out onto the wet grass in the dark. By the old shed, the bare corner waits, the way it has waited for years. She stands before it with the notebook held against her. The air is very still. Far off, the first bird has not yet sung. It is the hour before the world begins.

Behind her, the door clicks softly. Dango comes out, slow and stiff, and stands against her leg. He does not ask anything of her. He only leans there, warm in the cold, the way he has leaned all his life. The two of them look at the dark corner together. The mist lies low along the fence, thin as breath. Somewhere above, the last stars are thin and pale.

Nothing happens. The corner stays dark and empty and still. After a long while, Ako-chan lets out a slow breath and goes back inside. But at her desk, she does something new. She opens the red notebook to the last line, and leaves it open. She lays the pen beside it, ready. Then she turns off the lamp and goes up to sleep. The name waits in the dark, uncovered, with the pen beside it.

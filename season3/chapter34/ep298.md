The cave is empty now. The light is gone, and the machine is quiet. Only a faint glow comes from its dark side. The man stands where he stood, and he does not move. The cold settles back into the deep blue space. Everything that was loud is silent now.

The silver one is gone. It went where it had to go, and it will not come back here. The work is finished. There is nothing left to build, and nothing left to send. For so many years his days were full of one single task. Now the task is done, and the cave feels very large around him. The silence sits where the hum of the work used to be.

The two penguins come out of the shadows at last. They move to where the silver one used to stand. They are old now, and slow, and quiet. They look at the empty floor for a long, still moment. Far off in the dark, the deep ice cracks and groans. The sound runs through the cave and fades into nothing. It is the only sound left in all the deep blue dark.

The man sits down on the cold floor. Tomorrow, and the days after it, do not enter his mind. He thinks only of the small silver shape that is gone. In the ice, at the end of the world, he learned what it was to care for something. He learned it late, and he learned it well. And now that something has gone, out of his hands and into the long past. He stays where he is. The cave holds him, and the cold, and the silence.

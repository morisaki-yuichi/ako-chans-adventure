Evening settles on the house in England. The lamp is warm, and the curtains are drawn. Dango sleeps on his blanket by her chair, old and deep. Ako-chan sits with her tea and watches him breathe. His white muzzle rests on his paws. He is a very old dog now, and the evenings are his.

Then, in his sleep, his paws begin to move. They twitch, and paddle, and push at the air. His ears stir. A soft sound comes from him, half a bark, worn small by sleep. Somewhere behind his closed eyes, he is running. Young, and quick, and far away. Over grass, perhaps. Over sand, over snow, over the deck of a boat on a wide sea. Ako-chan watches, and does not wake him. She rests her hand lightly on his side, and lets him run.

The house is quiet and full around them. Suke-san lies against the door, where he has always lain. The three tortoises are settled for the night, three small hills in the lamplight. On the desk, the red notebook lies open to its last line, the pen beside it, waiting. The night outside is mild and still. A soft rain begins, and stops again.

Dango runs a while longer, then slows, then sleeps deep again. His breathing settles, slow and even under her hand. Ako-chan finishes her tea in the warm light. The journey lives in all of them still. It is in an old dog's dreaming feet. It is in a tortoise by a far bench, and in a line that waits for its ending. The house sleeps. The ending is close now, and it is not sad.

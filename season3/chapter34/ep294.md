The moment has come. The man sets the silver one before the open machine. He kneels down to its level one last time. The blue-white eyes hold his. The red collar shines faintly in the deep blue light. Then he stands, and steps back, and the silence breaks.

He sends it to a garden, long ago, in England. Who first set all of this in motion, he does not know. He stopped asking that question many years back. The silver one must go now, to that place and that time. His hands move over the machine. The hum climbs higher and higher. The two penguins press back into the shadows.

Light begins to fill the cave. It pours from the heart of the machine, white and rising and bright. It washes over the ice, over the man, over the small silver shape. The blue-white eyes glow one last time inside the flood of light. And then, in a single soundless breath, the silver one is gone. For one breath the whole cave shines brighter than day, and then it dims. The light folds in on itself and slowly fades. The cave goes dark and still.

This was the beginning of everything. The man knows it, standing alone in the quiet. The thing he made has gone back to where it all started. The loop has closed, here in the ice, and no one will ever see how. He looks at the empty space where the silver one stood. The hum dies away beneath him. The echo runs along the walls and then is gone. Nothing moves in the great cave but the man himself. He is alone now, with the dark and the cold.

It is night now. Ako-chan sits at her small desk. The lamp is on, soft and warm. It is the only light in the room. Outside, the garden is dark and calm. Dango lies asleep at her feet, old and slow now. The house is still all around her. On the desk lies the red notebook.

She opens it to the very first page. There are the words from that first night. "I have to go." Four small words. They began the whole long road. She turns the pages slowly, one by one. The whole journey lives inside this book. At last she reaches the final page. There sits one name. "Obia——" After it, the page is white. It has waited there, unfinished, for a very long time.

For so many years she could not write past that line. She would pick up her pen, and then set it down. The words never came to her. Tonight she picks up the pen again. Her hand is steady. She does not know what has changed.

But something has. The room feels full tonight, in a way she cannot explain. The night feels quiet and whole, as if something has come home. She does not understand it, and she does not need to. The pen moves. It moves on its own, past the long dash, onto the white. She writes. No one told her to. No one asked her to. What she writes there is hers alone. Outside, the window is turning pale. The long night is ending, and a new day is near. The whole house sleeps in the soft dark around her. Ako-chan writes, and her heart is calm and full. At last, the red notebook is no longer waiting.

The machine hums low behind them. But here, between the man and the silver one, there is only silence. He has shut everything else out of his mind. The cave is cold and deep and quiet. This is the last time. He knows it well, standing here in the blue light.

He looks down at the silver one. The blue-white eyes look back up at him. They have looked at him like this for years, through all the long work. The red collar rests at its small throat. He reaches out and touches it once, lightly, with one finger. The silver one does not move. It only watches, calm and steady, the way it always has.

There is so much he wants to say to it now. The words rise in his chest and stop there. He opens his mouth, and then he closes it again. What could any words hold, after all this time together? The silence says more than he ever could. So he lets the silence stay, and he does not break it. The quiet holds them both, and neither one moves inside it.

The two penguins stand in the shadows and watch the two of them. They are very still, as if they understand. They have watched this silver one for as long as the man himself has. The cold presses in from the dark ice walls. The man holds the small blue gaze and does not look away. He knows that this is goodbye. It is something he has known for a long time now. But he also knows something stranger, something harder to hold. This is not only an ending. Somehow, this is also where it all begins.

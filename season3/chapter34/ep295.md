It is finished. After all the long years, the machine is complete at last. It fills the deep end of the cave, tall and dark and still. The man stands before it and does not move. His breath rises white in the cold blue air. For a moment he cannot believe that the work is truly done.

Then he reaches out and wakes it. A low hum begins, deep in the heart of the machine. The sound runs through the ice floor and into his feet. Small lights come on, one after another, along its dark side. The hum grows steady, and then it holds. The machine is alive now. It is waiting, the way he has waited, through all these years.

The silver one stands close beside him. Its blue-white eyes turn up toward the great dark shape. The red collar sits at its throat, the same as ever. The man looks down at it for a long, quiet moment. The hum of the machine echoes off the far ice walls. The two penguins watch from the shadows and do not stir. They have kept this long watch from the start, and they keep it now, at the very end.

So much time has gone into this one thing. So many days in the cold and the deep blue light. And now it stands ready, humming, full of a power he can feel in his chest. There is only one thing left to do. He has to send the silver one away. Everything has come down to this single act. After it, nothing will ever be the same again. The man breathes out slowly and lets the long moment hold.

The morning comes early, pale and clear. Spring light fills the old greenhouse. The boy stands by the table with a small pack in his hand. The small silver one sits beside him, ready to go. Its pale blue eyes are calm in the soft morning light. Outside, the grass is wet, and the cool air smells of new leaves.

Ako-chan comes in through the open door, and the animals come with her. Dango walks first, then Suke-san, then the three Pods with their gentle glow. They feel it already, somehow — that today is not like other days. The boy looks up at the red light he has watched for so long. "I am going away," he says. His voice is low and quiet. "I am taking it with me." He does not say where he is going. He does not say why. Ako-chan looks at him for a long moment. Then she nods, and she does not ask.

The animals gather close around the table. Then, very low, Dalu speaks from his Pod. "Do we tell her?" he says. "What we think it is?" Gabu answers, slow and sure. "She knows more than we do," he says. "Her hands knew." Suke-san says nothing at all. He only moves closer to Ako-chan's side. The small voices pass low among the Pods. Ako-chan's eyes are on the boy, and the morning holds her there. No bird is singing in the bare trees outside. A long and gentle hush settles over the glass room.

The boy folds the top of his pack and breathes out slowly. Something is ending here, in this small bright space. And something new is starting, far away and out of sight. Everyone in the room can feel the change now. No one wants to be the first to break the morning.

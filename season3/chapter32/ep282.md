The morning is full and bright at last. The boy lifts his pack and turns towards the gate. The small silver one rises and moves to his side. The red collar shines at its silver neck. Ako-chan stands on the grass and does not follow. The animals gather close around her feet.

The boy and the small silver one walk away together. They cross the bright grass towards the edge of the park. The boy does not look back, not even once. He keeps his eyes on the road that runs ahead. It is a long way to the place he has to reach. A far and cold and distant place, white at the bottom of the world. He carries his pack, and his silence, and the small silver one walks on beside him.

Ako-chan watches the two shapes grow small. Dango stands at her side, his white muzzle still. Suke-san holds his steady gaze upon them. Daru and Gabu wait in their Pods, still and quiet. And Ruka, lowest of all, looks after the boy the longest. Her small dark eyes follow him to the very last. The boy grows smaller against the bright green grass. The small silver one stays close at his side.

The two figures reach the gate and pass beyond it. The sky opens wide and bright above the trees. Somewhere far to the south lies the cold white land. The small silver one goes there now, the red collar at its neck, the boy walking close beside. Ako-chan stays behind, her red light shining on the grass. And the long road carries them towards the beginning of it all.

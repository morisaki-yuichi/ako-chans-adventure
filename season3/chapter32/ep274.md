The morning grows brighter over the park. The boy steps outside, his pack upon his back. The small silver one moves at his side. The animals follow them onto the cool grass. The light is thin, and the air is very still. It is time, and everyone seems to know it now.

Then one of the tortoises begins to move. The yellow Pod opens, and Ruka climbs slowly out of it. On her small legs, she crosses the wet grass toward him. She comes to the boy's feet and stops there. She does not climb back. She does not turn away. She presses close to his shoe and stays. This is the tortoise who once belonged to no one at all. Ako-chan found her long ago, among the white stones of Puglia. Now she will not leave the boy's side.

The boy looks down at the small still shell. Daru and Gabu wait behind, quiet in their Pods. Dango watches with his head held low. Suke-san stands near, his ears up and sharp. No one moves Ruka. No one calls her back. Time seems to stop around her small dark shape on the grass.

The boy bends down slowly and kneels by her feet. He looks at Ruka, and Ruka looks up at him. He has no word for this, and he does not try to find one. He only stays there, low to the ground, near the small warm shell. Something passes between them, quiet and whole and true. Then, gently, he lays one finger on the edge of her shell. She does not pull away. She only holds on.

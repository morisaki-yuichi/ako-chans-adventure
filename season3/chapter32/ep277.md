The morning is bright now, and the air is warm. The boy stands ready, his pack upon his back. The small silver one waits beside his feet. The animals are gathered on the grass around them. Then Ako-chan turns and walks back towards the bench. She reaches into her coat and takes out something small.

It is a collar — small, and simple, and red. There is nothing of the machine in it at all. No light, no metal, no moving part. Just soft red, worn smooth by long years and gentle hands. No one says where it came from. No one says who made it, or when. The boy has never seen this collar before today. Ako-chan holds it in her open palm and looks down at it. Then she closes her fingers around it, gently.

The animals go very still upon the grass. Suke-san watches the red in her hand. Dango lifts his head from where he lies. The three tortoises wait without a sound. Ruka stays nearest of all, close by the silver feet. The morning light falls on the small red shape, and the whole park has grown quiet around it. Somewhere far off, a single bird begins to call.

Ako-chan does not explain herself at all. She does not look for any words. She turns from the bench, the red collar held in her hand. Then she walks straight towards the small silver one. Her steps are slow and sure across the grass. The boy watches her come, his eyes on the small red thing. But he waits, and he says nothing. Whatever this is, he can feel that it matters.

Ako-chan kneels by the small silver one in the grass. Her hand still rests near the red collar at its neck. The morning is warm, but something old stirs deep in her. A faint ache rises, soft and slow, from far away. She only feels the pull of it, and she lets it come.

Her mind slips back to a white and frozen day. She is kneeling in the snow, long ago and far from here. A small red collar lies cold in her open hand. The one who is gone lies still before her in the snow. She sets the collar back around its neck, gently. Her fingers move then just as they move now. The same red. The same hands. The same deep quiet, with no words at all.

And there is another day she half remembers too. A small red collar pressed once into her own two hands. A voice she loved spoke words she did not understand. "I do not know why," the voice said. "But it is important." She kept it, all these years, close and safe. It waited in a drawer, through every season, never far. And now, in the warm spring grass, she gives it away again.

The two reds rise together in her mind, snow and bright grass. She blinks, and she is back inside the warm morning. The small silver one watches her with its pale blue eyes. She presses her palm once, lightly, against the collar. Then she stands, and brushes the grass from her knees. The red collar rests at the silver neck, where her fingers have left it. It is done, and the morning goes on.

The boy stands very still upon the grass. At his feet, the small silver one sits and waits. Its pale blue eyes look up, calm and clear. Ako-chan comes close with the red collar in her hand. The animals watch from every side. The whole park seems to hold its breath this morning.

Ako-chan kneels down beside the small silver one. She does not speak a single word. Gently, she opens the red collar in her hands. Then she sets it around the silver neck and closes it. Her fingers move slowly, sure of every step, as if they have done this somewhere long before. She has no clear memory of such a moment, and yet her hands are certain. The red rests there, bright against the silver body. The pale blue eyes do not look away from her. And the collar is there now, where it belongs.

No one moves on the grass at all. Dango holds still, his white muzzle low. Suke-san watches with steady eyes. Luca draws close to the silver feet and waits there. Dalu and Gabu watch quietly from inside their Pods. Even the morning wind seems to pause and hold. High in the bare tree, Kara-san watches, and for once says nothing. The small red collar glows softly in the light.

The boy looks at the red against the silver. The collar is simply there, and that is all. There is no question in it, and no answer. It rests at the silver neck, quiet and sure, as though it has always belonged there. And maybe, somehow, it always has. The boy breathes in the still morning and lets it be.

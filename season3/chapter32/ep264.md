The cold morning air sits low over the park. Suke-san stands a little behind Ako-chan, as he always does. His ears point up, two sharp triangles in the light. He has watched this boy for many days now. He has smelled no harm in him, not once. This morning the boy is leaving, and Suke-san knows it well.

Suke-san looks at the boy, straight and long. Long ago, he trusted no one who was new. He did not trust people, nor the one who came from far away. He watched, and he waited, and he kept his guard up high. But that was another time, and much has changed since then. Now he holds the boy in a steady, open gaze. It is the look he gives only to those he has come to trust. "Go," says Suke-san, clear and calm. "We will remember."

The boy cannot hear those words. He cannot hear the animals, and he never will. But Ako-chan hears them, soft and clear, and her eyes grow still. The cold air moves between them all. Suke-san does not look away from the boy. His gaze is steady as a stone beneath a stream. The whole park is silent around the two of them.

The boy feels something in that long, quiet look. He cannot name it, but he feels its weight. It is not a warning. It is not fear. It is like a gentle hand laid on his back. It sends him onward, out into the wide morning. He stands a little straighter in the cold air. The small triangles of Suke-san's ears do not move at all. The boy carries that look with him as he turns to go.

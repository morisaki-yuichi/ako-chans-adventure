The sun climbs higher and warms the wet grass. Ako-chan stands near the boy now, close and quiet. The small silver one waits between them on the ground. For so long, Dango has guarded the space at her side. He has pushed in close whenever the boy came near. He has watched the boy with careful, jealous eyes.

But this morning, Dango does something new. He looks at the boy, and at Ako-chan, and at the small silver one. Then, slowly, he takes one step back. He does not push between them. He does not growl, low and deep, as he once did. Instead he moves aside and lets the space open up. For a dog who guards what he loves, it is a great and quiet gift. Then he lies down on the cool grass and watches, and that is all.

Ako-chan looks down at Dango on the grass. His muzzle is white now, white with all his years. His eyes follow the boy without any anger in them. Suke-san stands near, calm and still. The three tortoises wait inside their Pods. Ruka rests near the silver feet, small and quiet. The whole group has grown still around that one small step.

Ako-chan understands what the step means. She has known Dango his whole long life. She knows how hard it is for him to move aside. She bends and lays her hand upon his warm head. Dango leans into her touch and lets out a slow breath. The space beside her stays open, and no one fills it now. That open space is his last and greatest gift.

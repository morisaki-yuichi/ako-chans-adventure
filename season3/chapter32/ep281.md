The boy looks up at the red light at last. As always, he cannot see a face there at all. Where others see eyes and a mouth, he sees only red. A bright red light in a world of dull grey. He has known that red for many long months now. This morning, it is the last he will see of her.

But this morning the red is somehow different. It is brighter, or deeper, or more full. He cannot say which, only that it has changed. The red light says nothing to him, and he says nothing back. Yet the red itself seems to speak, clear and whole. It tells him things he has no words for. It tells him that he is known, and that he matters. He holds his breath and lets the red wash over him.

The small silver one waits at his feet with its new red collar. Two reds now — one at his feet, one before his eyes. Around them the grey faces of the world have fallen away. There is only the grass, and the morning, and the light. The cool air does not move at all. The boy stands inside a silence that feels almost full.

He will leave in a moment, and he knows it well. But he will carry this red inside him wherever he goes. In the long grey years that lie ahead of him, he will remember it. The red that spoke to him without a single word. He looks at the red light, and the red light stays. And the boy holds it in his eyes for as long as he can.

Night fills the small room where the boy sits. A lamp burns low above his quiet desk. Outside the window, the park is dark and far. The streetlights glow small and orange below. His mind will not leave the distances. Of all that time he can feel but cannot see.

A clean white sheet lies flat before him. Slow and unsure, he picks up a pencil. Then his hand begins to move on its own. A line, and then another, crosses the page. He does not fully know what he is making. He only knows that he must make something. The need is quiet, but it is strong. The wish has grown in him for many days. He wants to find the beginning he cannot see. How is a bond like hers born, and built? If he cannot find it, perhaps he can make it. His hand moves faster now across the white.

The lamp throws his shadow on the wall. The pencil scratches soft in the still room. Outside, the dark park waits beyond the glass. A cold wind taps the window now and then. The lines spread slowly over the empty page. Something is taking shape there, faint and small. The room is warm against the cold outside. The night presses black upon the glass.

But there is more, beneath the first wish. Something deeper stirs, low in his chest. He cannot name it, and does not try. It pulls at him, low and steady, like a tide. The boy only bends closer to his work. For the first time in his life, he begins. The lines grow on the page, into the night. He works on, alone, and does not stop.

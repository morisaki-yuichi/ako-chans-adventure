The cold has softened a little today. The boy sits and looks down at the grass. Near the red light's feet, the little tortoise rests. Her small cart glows a soft, pale yellow. She does not move, and she does not hurry. She is simply there, as if she always was.

The boy watches the little tortoise for a while. Her head reaches out, slow, from the warm Pod. Her short legs stretch easy in its glow. She is not afraid, and she does not hide. Sometimes she shifts a little, then rests again. Most of the time she only rests and looks. The red light's feet are close beside her. The tortoise does not need to check on them. She belongs at those feet, plain and sure. The boy cannot say how he knows this. But he knows it, watching the small still shape.

The pale cart throws a soft glow on the grass. The thin winter light lies over them both. The wind bends the dry stalks low, then lets them rise. A bird calls once, far off, then is quiet. The cold air is sharp and clean today. Time seems to slow around the little tortoise. The boy's breath goes slow as well, watching.

There is no effort in the way she stays. No worry, no waiting, nothing to prove. She is there the way the ground is there. She asks for nothing, and waits for nothing. The boy thinks about that easy belonging. He looks at the little tortoise, and wonders how long. How many winters have the two of them shared? How many slow, quiet days like this one?

The afternoon is a little longer now. The light stays in the park past its old hour. Winter still holds the trees and the cold ground. But the dark comes slower than it did before. The boy sits in the pale light and thinks. He has watched the red light and her animals for days.

He knows their distances now, each careful one. The small dog at her side, the reddish dog behind. The little tortoise resting at her feet. Every gap speaks of a long, slow time. And yet one thing escapes him, every time. He cannot see the beginning. When did the dog first walk so close? When did the reddish dog first take his guard? There must have been a first day for each. A first meeting, a first careful step. But that door is shut, and he cannot find it. He looks and looks, and sees only the now.

The wind moves cold across the open grass. Far off, the red light wavers and glows. The bare branches scratch against the dull sky. A few birds cross, then drop from sight. The boy pulls his coat closer to his neck. The grass is stiff and white with old frost. A dog barks somewhere beyond the gate. The thin light fades slowly towards the dusk.

The beginning will not leave his mind. It sits there, just out of his reach. He can see what time has built, so clearly. But not the first stone, not the start. Where does a bond like this begin? He turns it over, slow, in his mind. There is no first day that he can find. The boy does not know, and cannot guess. He walks home with the question still in him.

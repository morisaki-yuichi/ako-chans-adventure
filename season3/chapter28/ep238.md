The park fills with people in the cold light. They cross the paths, grey and quick and far. For years, they meant nothing to the boy. They were all the same to him — a dull mass. He looked past them, the way you pass a wall. Faces without faces, a crowd without names. That was the whole of people, to him.

But today, something small will not stay still. Near his bench, the little tortoise rests. She came across the grass to his cold feet. A creature chose him — him, out of everyone. He looks from the tortoise to the passing people. For so long they were only grey to him. Now a strange thought brushes him, light and quick. What if he has missed something, all this time? What if the grey hides more than he saw? He cannot say it clearly, even to himself. But the old, sure feeling has begun to move.

The wind carries voices across the grass. A child laughs somewhere past the trees. The strangers walk on, not knowing him. The little tortoise stays close at his feet. Her small head turns, slow, towards the crowd. The cold light lies flat on everything. The day is sliding towards its early end. The bare branches rattle in the wind.

The boy watches the people with new eyes. He does not know them, and never will. They are still grey, still far away. But maybe grey was never the whole truth. One small creature crossed the grass to him. Something that was hard in him bends, a little. It is only a small shift, and slow. But it is real, and it is new. The boy does not understand it, but he feels it.

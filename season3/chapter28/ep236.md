The afternoon is calm, and the park is quiet. Ako-chan sits on the bench with her friends. The boy is on his bench, far across the grass. He watches them, as he does every day. But today Ako-chan feels something turn around her. Her animals are watching the boy in return.

Suke-san sits to her right, ears up and soft. "He comes each day," he says, low and sure. "No bad feeling. He only wants to see." Daru leans forward in his bright red Pod. "I like him," Daru says. "He stays so still!" From his blue Pod, Gabu watches, slow and calm. "...He is alone," Gabu says. "But not unkind." Ruka says nothing at all, in her yellow Pod. She only turns her small head toward the boy. Ako-chan hears them, each quiet voice in turn. She listens, still, and does not speak.

The pale Pods glow soft against the grass. Dango lies close, his warm breath rising white. The wind moves gently through the trees. The cold light holds them all in place. The bare branches stand dark against the sky. A crow settles on a far, high branch. Four small watchers face the lonely bench. They take the boy in, and do not turn away. Their eyes are soft, not sharp or cold. There is no fear in them, and no doubt.

Ako-chan looks from her friends to the distant boy. They have chosen him, in their quiet way. They watch him as a friend, not a stranger. But the boy only watches back, and waits. He does not see how they hold him there. He does not know that he is welcome now. One day, perhaps, he will understand. For now, they keep their quiet watch, and wait.

The boy's eyes have found Suke-san now. The reddish dog stands a step behind Ako-chan. He keeps to her right, and a little back. He is not at her side, like the small one. He holds that guarding place, calm and still. The boy wonders why the dog stands there.

Ako-chan sees where the boy is looking. She knows that place better than anyone. She remembers when Suke-san came to them in Japan. He was only a small puppy then, and wild. Back then he did not trust the newcomer. He growled low and kept his careful distance. "I do not know yet," was all he said. For many days he watched, and would not rest. But the friend who left helped them, more than once. One cold day, the one who is gone walked alone into a storm, to keep them safe. Slowly, Suke-san's hard doubt began to soften. In the end, he was the first to speak. "I trust it," he told them all.

From that day, he took his place at her right. A step behind, where he could watch and guard. The winter wind moves cold across the park. Suke-san stands now just as he stood then. His ears are soft, his old sharpness gone. But the guarding place is still his own.

The boy looks at that careful step of space. He cannot know the long road behind it. Ako-chan keeps the memory, quiet and warm. Distances have reasons, she thinks to herself. Some are written over many years. The boy reads the gap, but not its cause. That story is hers, and not yet his.

Evening comes early, and the park grows dim. The boy stays on his bench as the cold deepens. Across the grass, the red light gathers her friends. Soon she will rise and take them home. The boy watches the small circle she makes. And a new thought turns, slow, inside him.

They have a beginning, all of them, together. He cannot see it, but he knows it is there. Somewhere behind them lies a first day. He has been reading their bond for so long. Now, for the first time, he reads himself. He has no such beginning, with anyone. Nothing began for him, ever, with a face. In his world of grey faces, no one started. No first meeting, no slow-built closeness, nothing. He has always been alone, and apart. The faces passed, and none became a friend. No one came close, and no one stayed.

The long shadows stretch across the cold grass. The red light begins to move towards the gate. The animals fall in around her, each in place. The boy's feet do not move from the ground. The streetlamps wake, one by one, in the dusk. The empty bench beside him holds only cold. The wind grows sharp in the falling dark. His hands are stiff inside his thin coat.

A strange question rises, and will not sink. What is he, among all the grey? Why is he here at all, and alone? He has wondered about her, and her friends. He has never once wondered about himself. Why him, and not some other grey shape? The question is heavy, and wholly new. The boy sits in the dark and feels its weight. It will not let him go tonight.

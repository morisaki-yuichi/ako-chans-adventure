The greenhouse glows in the last of the daylight. On the table lies the small silver body. It is almost whole now. The limbs are joined. The head is set in place. Two pale blue eyes wait in the still face, not yet lit. Only one panel stays open on its side.

The boy and Ako-chan sit and look at it. There is little left to do now. The wires are joined. The body is closed but for that one small panel. Soon they will fit the last of it together. Soon it will be ready to wake. They both know this, though neither says it aloud. The knowing brings a quiet gladness to the room. But under the gladness lies a small, strange weight. When the work is done, these afternoons will end. Ako-chan feels it first, and her hands grow still.

The blue dusk comes slowly beyond the glass. A glowing cart waits in the corner, calm and still. The dog sleeps near the door, warm and quiet. The lamp throws soft light on the silver body. The smell of metal and solder hangs in the air. Beyond the glass, the spring sky fades to deep blue. A late bird calls, far off across the park.

The boy looks at the almost-finished shape. He feels the gladness, bright and clear and near. The weight beneath it has not reached him yet. He has not thought about what comes after this. The waking is enough to fill his mind. Tomorrow, or the next day, it will be done. For now, he only watches it, and is glad. He looks at the small body, and waits for that.

The morning light is clear inside the greenhouse. The small silver body lies ready on the table. Only one panel remains open on its side. On the table sits the last small part. It is tiny, no bigger than a fingernail. It has waited there since the night before.

The boy lifts the last part with great care, his fingers slow and sure above the work. Ako-chan leans in close to help him set it. Together they guide it down into its place. Her hand comes to rest beside his on the body. Then his fingers brush against hers, and neither pulls away. They hold there, two hands upon the silver shell. For one long moment, the boy forgets to breathe. The part slides home with a small, soft click. The last panel closes flush against the body. It is whole now. The work is done.

Outside, the spring birds call across the park. The dog raises his head at the birds, then lowers it again. A glowing cart sits still beside the table leg. Warm light spreads across the finished shell. Dust floats gently in the bright morning air. The greenhouse is warm now, and full of light.

The boy and Ako-chan lift their eyes at last. They look at the small silver body between them. They look at each other, and say nothing at all. There is nothing now that needs to be said. The thing they made is finished and whole. It lies still, its eyes closed and dark. Now there is only one thing left to do. Whatever comes next, they will meet it together. They must wait for it to wake.

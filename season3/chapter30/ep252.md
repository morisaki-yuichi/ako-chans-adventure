The afternoon light fills the old greenhouse. Ako-chan stands inside the open door. The boy sits at his table, working. His hands move slowly over the small silver shape. Outside, the animals rest on the spring grass. Dango lies near the door, watching her closely.

She did not plan to stay so long today. At first she only fixed one small part. A wire set wrong. A joint turned the wrong way round. But her hands did not want to stop there. So she pulled a stool up to the table, and sat down close beside the boy to work. She does not ask herself why she helps him. She only knows she cannot leave the shape alone.

The work passes between them now, slow and easy. He holds a part; she turns a small screw. She lifts a tool; he takes it from her hand. Their two hands move above the table together. Neither of them speaks a single word. The pale sun warms the glass walls around them. A Pod glows, soft and yellow, by the wall. Ruka waits inside it, still and calm. The smell of warm metal hangs in the quiet air. Dust drifts through the bars of afternoon light. Beyond the glass, the first green buds show on the trees.

Ako-chan looks at the silver shape between them. Her hands know the work, though she cannot say how. Somewhere along these quiet afternoons, the work changed from his to theirs. Once it was his alone. Now, without a word, it belongs to them both. She does not think too hard about it. She only stays, and works, and lets the light fall warm.

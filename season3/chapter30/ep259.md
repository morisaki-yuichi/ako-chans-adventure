The afternoon is mild, the greenhouse full of light. The boy and Ako-chan work close at the table. Their shoulders nearly touch above the silver shape. Dango lies on the floor, a little apart. He watches them with his chin upon his paws.

Once he could not bear to see them this close. In the park, he pushed between them and growled. Later, in the greenhouse, he growled harder still. He shoved his body between the two of them more than once. But that was many afternoons ago now. Today Dango only lies and watches from his spot. He does not rise now. Nor does he push between them. He only lets the boy sit close beside her. For a dog who shares nothing, this is much to give. He lets out one long, slow breath, and rests his chin back down.

Soft white hairs show around his mouth. Ruka has never minded the boy at all. She sits calm in her yellow Pod, as always. The two tortoises watch from their glowing Pods. Suke-san waits in the doorway, quiet and sure. The spring light falls warm across the floor. Outside, the park is bright and still. Birdsong drifts in faint through the open door.

Ako-chan looks down and sees Dango there. She sees the space he has left between them now. She knows what that small space costs him. Without a word, she reaches down to him. She rests her hand softly upon his head. Dango leans into it, and closes his eyes. His tail gives one slow, soft sweep across the floor. The old jealousy has loosened, just a little, at last.

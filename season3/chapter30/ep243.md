The low sun slants through the greenhouse glass. The smell of warm solder fills the room. At the table, the boy and Ako-chan work on. Around them, the animals have taken their places. Each one is quiet. Each one is watching the work.

Suke-san sits in the open doorway, facing in. His ears stand up, turned towards the table. Luca waits at the table leg, close and still. Dango lies a little apart from the rest. In their Pods by the wall, the two tortoises watch the silver shape grow. Dalu's red Pod glows softly in the corner. "It is taking shape," he says, slow and low. Beside him, Gabu's blue light barely stirs. "...Yes," says Gabu, after a long pause. Ako-chan hears their quiet voices, and looks up.

She smiles a little, and bends back to the work. Then Gabu speaks again, lower still. "...I have seen this shape before," he says. The words hang in the warm air. From the doorway, Suke-san answers, very low. "Say nothing," he says. "Not yet." The words stay small, under the sounds of the work. This time, Ako-chan does not look up. The animals hold their quiet, and keep watching. Suke-san's gaze does not leave the table. The light moves slowly across the dusty floor. Outside, the early spring wind breathes through the bare trees.

The animals do not move from their places. They watch the shape the way one waits for someone known. No bark. No voice above the small sounds. Ako-chan feels the strange warmth of the room. The stillness sits close around her, soft and old, like something half-remembered. She lets the feeling stay, and does not chase it.

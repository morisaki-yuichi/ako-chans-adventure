The greenhouse glows in the last of the daylight. On the table lies the small silver body. It is almost whole now. The limbs are joined. The head is set in place. Two pale blue eyes wait in the still face, not yet lit. Only one panel stays open on its side.

There is little left to do, and they both know it. The knowing brings a quiet gladness to the room. But under the gladness lies a small, strange weight. When the work is done, these afternoons will end. Ako-chan feels it first, and her hands grow still. She looks at the open panel for a long moment. Then evening comes, and they leave the last part for morning.

The morning light is clear inside the greenhouse. On the table sits the last small part, tiny as a fingernail. The boy lifts it with great care. Ako-chan leans in close to help him set it. Together they guide it down into its place. Her hand comes to rest beside his on the body. Then his fingers brush against hers, and neither pulls away. The part slides home with a small, soft click. The last panel closes flush against the body. It is whole now. The work is done.

Outside, the spring birds call across the park. Dango raises his head, then lowers it again. Warm light spreads across the finished shell. The boy and Ako-chan lift their eyes at last. They look at each other, and say nothing at all. There is nothing now that needs to be said. The thing they made is finished and whole. It lies still, its eyes closed and dark. Now they must wait for it to wake.

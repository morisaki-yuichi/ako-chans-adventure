The greenhouse is quiet in the afternoon. Ako-chan sits alone at the table now. The boy has gone to fetch more wire. Her hands keep working while she waits. They move on their own, sure and calm. She watches them, a little surprised.

Her fingers know this kind of work too well. They have bent metal before. They have joined wires, and turned small screws, and smoothed a plan flat upon a table. Once she built a flying boat this way. Once she made the small rolling Pods. She did not build them alone back then. Someone worked beside her — someone who is gone now. Far away now, and long ago. Her hands still hold the shape of those days.

The old table and this one blur together. The same hands. The same slow, careful work. But a different one sits where that friend once sat. The light is not the same. The room is not the same. Only her fingers carry the past inside them. A Pod glows yellow in the corner, soft and low. Luca shifts once, then settles back to stillness. Dust floats through the pale afternoon light. Outside, a soft wind crosses the young grass. The greenhouse holds its quiet all around her.

Ako-chan lowers her hands into her lap. It is strange, what the body keeps and holds. She did not tell her hands to remember. They remembered all on their own. She looks at the work for a long moment. Then she looks at her own two hands. She picks up the next part, and the afternoon goes on. The light moves on across the quiet floor.

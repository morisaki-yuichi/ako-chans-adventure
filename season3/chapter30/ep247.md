The boy walks to the greenhouse in the morning. The spring air is cool against his face. Her name comes with him now, all the way. Ako-chan. He holds it as he walks. Something has changed in him, and he knows it. On the days she comes, the world feels different. The hours move in a way they did not before.

He cannot say what it is. When she is near, his hands grow light and quick. The work that fought him now comes easy. A part that would not fit slides into place. Some warmth rises in his chest when Ako-chan appears at the door. It beats there, soft and strange, all afternoon long. The nights feel short now. He used to count the long, slow, empty hours. Now the days fill up, and pass too fast. He lies awake and wonders at the change in himself.

The animals have noticed him too, by now. The red-brown dog no longer watches from far off. A small glowing cart rolls closer to his feet each day. The bare trees outside have small green buds. The light stays longer in the evening sky now. A crow calls somewhere out across the park. The cold of winter has begun to lift at last.

He has no name for the thing he feels. It has no shape that he can hold or see. But it is real. It lives in the rush of his chest, in his quick hands, in the short bright days. On the Ako-chan days, the bright hours run fast and warm. When she is gone again, the ache stays on, long past dark. The feeling goes with him, all the way home.

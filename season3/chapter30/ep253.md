The light comes pale and low through the glass. The boy and Ako-chan sit close at the table. Their shoulders almost touch above the work. The half-built shape lies between their hands. Outside, the spring air is cool and still. The animals have followed them in from the path.

Ako-chan leans nearer, her shoulder light against his. Then a warm weight pushes hard between them. It is Dango. He shoves his body between the two of them, and growls — low and long, in his chest. He has never seen them sit so close before. In the park, his warning was a small thing. Here, this near, it comes out stronger. "Dango," says Ako-chan, soft but firm. He gives one slow huff, and sinks down at her feet. Still, he does not leave. He watches the boy with one open eye.

Soon the work draws their hands back in. She passes a part; he fits it into place. He holds a wire; she binds it tight and sure. A rhythm grows between them, quiet and even. The tools click softly against the metal. Their breathing falls into one slow pace. Luca rests by the table leg, calm and still. The afternoon light moves slowly across their hands. Outside, a bird calls once, then falls quiet again.

The boy has never made anything with another person. He has always worked alone, in the dark and quiet. Now another pair of hands moves beside his own. The shape grows faster between the two of them. He does not have a word for this thing. But he feels it settle, warm, inside the cold room.

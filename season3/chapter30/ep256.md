The evening light turns gold inside the greenhouse. The boy works close beside the red light at the table. The silver shape lies open between their hands. For a long while, neither of them speaks. Only the small sounds of the work fill the room.

"Hold this," she says, and lifts a part towards him. He takes it, and holds it firm in place. She fits a wire behind it, sure and quick. They work on. Then something turns over inside the boy. He has set a piece, but is not sure of it. A need rises in him — to ask, to check. He has never asked anyone anything before. In the grey world, there was never anyone to ask. The wish to speak feels strange and new. "Like this?" he says, low and unsure. "Yes," she answers. "Just like that."

The words feel strange in his mouth, but not wrong. They pass between them, small and easy. "This one?" "No, the other one." "Here?" "Yes." Once, at some clumsy move, they almost laugh. The lamp glows warm against the falling dark. Outside, the spring dusk settles, soft and cool. The small flame of the lamp leans and steadies. Their two voices fill the quiet, low and warm.

The boy holds the new thing quietly inside, for he has spoken, and someone has answered him. The words were not heavy. They did not hurt. He turns one thought over, slow and surprised. These words, passing between them, are not grey. Nothing about this hour is grey at all. He does not understand it. But he wants more of it. Something in him has opened, just a crack.

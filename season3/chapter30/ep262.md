Night has come down over the park. The greenhouse is dark but for one small lamp. On the table lies the finished silver body. It is whole now, and still, and quiet. Its two eyes are closed, pale blue and dark. They do not shine. Not yet. The room is colder now the work has stopped.

The boy and Ako-chan stand close beside the table. They do not work. The work is done. They only stand and look at the small silver shape. It waits there in the lamplight, silver and calm. Tomorrow it will wake. Tomorrow the eyes will open and shine. Tomorrow it will look at them at last. The thought is large, and neither of them speaks. Their two breaths are the only sound in the room. The night holds still around them, deep and soft.

Outside, the early spring wind moves through the trees. The dog sleeps near the door, slow and warm. A glowing cart rests against the far wall. The lamp burns low and steady on the table. Thin frost gathers along the cold glass walls. The park beyond is dark and bare. No light shows in any window now. The town has gone to sleep beyond the trees.

The boy looks at the closed pale eyes and waits. A strange feeling rises, two things at once. He is afraid of tomorrow, for he has never made anything that could wake. And in the same breath, he longs for it to come. He wants to see those eyes open more than anything. Fear and longing hold him there, side by side. He looks at the silver body, and waits for morning.

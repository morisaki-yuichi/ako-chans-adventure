The greenhouse holds the warm light of late day. The boy and the red light sit side by side. Their stools are close, almost touching, at the table. The half-made shape lies quiet between them. Outside, the spring evening is soft and clear.

Once the red light was far away. In the park, it crossed the grass at a distance. He watched it from his cold bench, day after day. Now it sits a hand's breadth from his arm. He is still not used to it, this nearness. The red glows warm so close beside him. Sometimes their hands reach for the same part. They almost touch, then draw back, slow and shy. He feels the warmth of her there, beside his own. It is strange to him, to be this near to anyone.

The silver shape grows under their four hands. A panel here. A fine wire there. A small glowing cart rests against the wall. The red-brown dog lies near the door, quiet now, and still. The last light fades softly against the glass. The warm air smells of metal and dust. A bird settles on the roof, then lifts away.

The boy does not move away from the nearness. Once the red light was only the one light that was not grey. That was all it meant to him, at first. Now it is much more than that. He cannot say what more it is. But he knows this one thing: he does not want the red light to leave. He wants it to stay close, here, beside him. He has watched that red light for so long now. To have it near, this near, still amazes him.

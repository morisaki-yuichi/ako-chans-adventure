The low sun slants through the greenhouse glass. The smell of warm solder fills the room. At the table, the boy and Ako-chan work on. Around them, the animals have taken their places. Each one is quiet. Each one is watching the work.

Suke-san sits in the open doorway, facing in. His ears stand up, turned toward the table. Ruka waits at the table leg, close and still. Dango lies a little apart from the rest. In their Pods by the wall, the two tortoises watch the silver shape grow. Daru's red Pod glows softly in the corner. "It is taking shape," he says, slow and low. Beside him, Gabu's blue light barely stirs. "...Yes," says Gabu, after a long pause. Ako-chan hears their quiet voices, and looks up.

She cannot say what they mean, not yet. But the animals watch as if they know the shape. Suke-san's gaze does not leave the table. The small silver form pulls at every quiet eye. The light moves slowly across the dusty floor. Outside, the early spring wind breathes through the bare trees. The whole room seems to lean toward the thing they build. The solder smell drifts thick in the warm air. Dust turns slowly in the last of the light.

The animals do not move from their places. They watch the shape the way one waits for someone known. No bark. No voice but those two small lines. Ako-chan feels the strange warmth of the room. She does not understand the stillness gathered there. But it sits close around her, soft and old, like something half-remembered. She lets the feeling stay, and does not chase it.

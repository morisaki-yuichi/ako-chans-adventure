The next day, the boy is at his table. The red light is there too, near the door. She is back today, quiet, and watches him. The greenhouse holds the cold afternoon light. The wrong spot waits where she pointed it out. He looks at it a moment, breathes, then bends to it and begins, without a word.

He takes the small joint apart again. His hands are slow and careful now. He sets it the other way, as it should be. A wire slides back into its place. The parts meet, and this time they hold. He works in silence; she watches, silent too. He thinks of her voice from the day before. The way it dropped, low and certain. "Here is wrong," she said, so sure.

There was something in those few words. Not just the truth of where to look. Something else lived under them, soft and deep. He cannot name it; he has no word. The red light glows warm beside the table. Her quiet fills the cold room, somehow. The small dog lies in the doorway, still. Small lights glow on the grass beyond. Outside, the afternoon light begins to fade. The boy fits the last small part in place.

He sits back and looks at the shape. It is a little more right than before. The wrong has gone; he can feel it. The work has moved one step ahead. It is small, but he feels it clearly. A new warmth stays with him from the hour. It sits in his chest, steady and quiet. But already he wants to come back tomorrow, to sit here and build beside her.

The afternoon walk takes its usual path. Ako-chan crosses the park with her friends. Dango walks close, Suke-san a step behind. The three Pods glow softly along the way. The day is cold, the low sun pale. Children call far off across the field. At the park's far corner stands an old greenhouse.

She has passed it many times before. Today its door stands a little open. A light burns inside, warm and small. Through the doorway, she sees the boy. He sits bent over the long work table. His hands move slowly above something small. On the table is a shape, half-made. Metal and wires catch the lamp light. Ako-chan slows on the cold path. Then, without meaning to, she stops.

The light from the doorway falls on the grass. Dango stops too, and looks up at her. The Pods glow low — red and blue and yellow. The boy hears her step, and his hands go still. He does not turn. No one has seen him here before. His shoulders tighten in the lamp light. The cold air sits between them, thin and clear. Luca turns her small head towards the door. Suke-san waits, calm, at Ako-chan's side. Dango's warm breath rises white in the cold.

Ako-chan has not moved from the path. Her feet stopped before any thought came. The half-made shape holds her where she is. The feeling is quiet, but it is strong. Her breath slows. Her eyes do not move. The cold deepens on the path, but she stays. The boy waits, tense, in the lamp light. And Ako-chan says nothing at all. She only watches, quiet, through the doorway.

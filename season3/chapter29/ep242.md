Night again, and the greenhouse is cold and still. The work lamp glows over the cluttered table. Outside, a single streetlight burns far off. Its weak orange light falls through the glass. The boy sits with a part in his hand. He turns it once, and does not move.

Why is he making this? He asks again. He wants to know how a bond is born. That is one reason, and it is true. But there is another, deeper in his chest. It has no clear shape, and no words. He cannot bring it up into the light. When he reaches for it, it slips away. He lets his hand fall still on the table. The part lies cold and quiet in his palm. Then, slowly, he picks the work back up.

The cold metal bites against his fingers. A pen rolls and stops on the paper. The lines of his plan are dark and sure. His own breath is the only sound inside. Beyond the glass, the streetlight does not move. The park holds its long winter silence. A car passes far off, then is gone. He pulls his coat tighter at the neck. The night presses cold against the walls. The lamp light trembles, then holds steady again.

He does not know the whole of why. Perhaps he never will, fully. But the not-knowing does not stop his hands. He fits one small part to another, and they almost meet, then slip apart. He tries once more, slow and patient. The two reasons stay tangled in him. The clear one, and the one without a shape. The reason can stay hidden, for now. He keeps on, though he cannot say why.

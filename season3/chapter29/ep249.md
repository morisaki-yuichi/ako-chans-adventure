Days have passed, and Ako-chan keeps coming. She helps a little, here and there. Today the greenhouse is quiet and pale. The boy works close beside her at the table. The form on the table has grown. It has limbs now, and the start of a head. It is no longer only parts and wires.

The body has turned a soft silver now. It is small — no bigger than a dog. Two eyes sit in the front, not yet lit. They are a pale blue, dark and still. Ako-chan looks at it, and her hand stops. Something pulls, deep and quiet, in her chest. She cannot say what holds her here. The feeling comes before any thought. She does not chase it. She only feels it.

For a moment, an old ache rises in her. Someone who is gone moves through her mind. A silver shape. A pair of pale blue eyes. A friend who left, long ago and far. She breathes once, slow, and lets it pass. The ache is soft, and far, and old. She does not say the memory out loud. She does not join it to the work before her. The two stay apart, and she lets them. The cold light rests on the small still body.

Ako-chan's hand stays still above the table. She cannot say why this shape holds her. She only knows she cannot leave it alone. The boy works on, and does not notice. He does not know what she half-remembers. His eyes stay down, fixed on the work. His hands are sure now, where hers paused. The small body waits, its eyes unlit. And Ako-chan, quiet, cannot look away.

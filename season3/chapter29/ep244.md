Several nights now, the same small room of glass. The boy has come to know its every corner. The cold table, the lamp, the dark beyond. He works through the evening into the night. The pile on the table grows each time. Failed parts, bent wires, plans drawn twice over.

He has gathered more parts from many places. Some he found, and some he made by hand. He has redrawn his plan three times now. Each new line fixes an old mistake. His notes crowd the margins, small and tight. The failed tries stack up at the table's edge. A small thing that broke. A joint that would not hold. He does not throw them away. They are the steps that brought him here. Piece by piece, a form begins to show.

The smell of metal and solder fills the air. A tool taps, then scrapes, then taps once more. The lamp light shines on the cluttered table. His shadow falls long across the glass wall. Outside, the bare trees stand black and still. The cold seeps in through the old panes. His hands are dark with work and dust. A drop of solder cools and turns dull. The failed parts gleam beside the newer ones. The wind hums low along the roof. The night is full of small, patient sounds.

Then, late, something happens at his hands. A small part turns, the way he meant. A tiny movement, the first of its kind. It is nothing much — a twitch, a stir. But it moves because he made it move. He leans in, and stares, and holds his breath. Above the pile of failures, a thing has stirred.

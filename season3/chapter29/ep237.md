Ako-chan stands now inside the doorway. The greenhouse is cold and full of light. The boy has not moved from his table. His work lies between them, half-made. Neither of them has spoken a word. The animals wait quiet on the grass outside. The pale afternoon sun falls through the glass.

She steps closer, slow, to the table. Her eyes move over the shape he built. She does not know how she knows. But her hands have made things before. A flying boat. The small rolling Pods. Some memory wakes in her fingers now. She leans down, close above the work. Her eyes find one part among the many. She lifts one hand and points at a spot. "Here is wrong," she says, quiet and sure.

The boy looks where her finger rests. A small joint, set the wrong way round. He feels it too, now that she points. The light holds the two of them still. Dango waits in the doorway, watching close. The Pods glow softly on the grass outside. A breeze moves the bare trees beyond the glass. Dust drifts slowly through the lamp's pale light. A long silence sits between the boy and her. She lowers her hand, and says no more.

The boy does not ask how she knows. He does not doubt the words at all. Somehow, deep down, he feels they are true. He trusts the words without knowing why. The wrong spot is wrong; he sees it now. He wonders how her hands could know it. Her certainty has passed into his own. He reaches for a tool in the quiet. The boy will make it right, without a word.

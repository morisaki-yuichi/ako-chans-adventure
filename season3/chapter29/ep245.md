Dusk comes early still, and cold. The park empties as the light grows dim. The boy walks to the greenhouse in the gloom. The grass is stiff with frost beneath his feet. He lights the lamp and sits alone. The glass walls hold the falling dark. Around him, the table waits with his work.

He has always been alone, all his life. In a world of faceless gray, alone was normal. He never minded the empty rooms, the quiet. It was simply how his days were made. No one looked for him; he looked for no one. But tonight feels different from the rest. The work gives his hands a purpose now. And the purpose makes the quiet larger, somehow. He looks up from the table, into the dark.

One small lamp lights the long table. Beyond it, the park lies silent and black. No voices come through the old glass. The wind moves softly against the panes. His tools shine faint in the narrow light. The cold settles deep around his feet. A bare branch taps the roof, slow and dry. The dark leans close against the glass. He works the small parts with careful hands. The night holds the greenhouse close and still.

For the first time, the quiet feels heavy. He thinks of the red light, far away. She is not here tonight, in the dark. And the room is more empty for it. The lamp cannot fill the silent space. He has been alone for years, and never known it. Now, in one night, he feels it whole. The boy sits with the new ache, and works.

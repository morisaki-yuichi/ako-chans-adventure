Night, and the rain falls softly outside. The greenhouse glass runs wet and dark. The boy sits alone at his table again. The red light is not here tonight. The lamp burns warm above his quiet work. The room is still, and his hands are idle. But his mind is somewhere else just now.

He keeps hearing her voice in his head. "Here is wrong," said low and sure. There was something underneath the words. He has turned it over, night after night. Was it sadness? He is not sure. Was it longing? He has no word for it. Few names for feelings live in him. He has never needed them, until now. But the something was real; he felt it.

The rain taps softly against the panes. The unfinished shape rests in the lamp light. Its pale eyes wait, dark and unlit. The night is long and wet beyond the glass. His tools lie quiet where he left them. He does not pick them up tonight. A slow drip falls from the old roof. The cold has crept in under the door. The shape throws a faint shadow on the wall. He only sits, and thinks, and listens. The water runs in lines down the glass.

The something has begun to grow in him. From it, another feeling slowly comes. He cannot name that one either, yet. It is warm, and quiet, and will not leave. Never has he felt a thing like this. It pulls him toward her, soft and sure. He wants to know her — who she is — and to know the red light more. The boy sits with the rain, and wonders.

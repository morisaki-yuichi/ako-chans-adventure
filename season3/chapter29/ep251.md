The night has worn on, late and still. The greenhouse holds the last of the dark. The boy has worked long hours again. The shape on the table has grown once more. Through the old glass, the sky grows pale. A thin light shows far off in the east. The long night is almost over now.

He should stop now, and go, and rest. But he does not clear the table away. He leaves the tools out where they lie, and the parts in their half-built rows. Tomorrow he will take them up again. And tomorrow, perhaps, she will come too. The thought sits warm inside the cold room. He sets one part ready for the morning. He smooths the plan flat upon the table.

The first light creeps along the glass walls. The frost on the panes begins to shine. Outside, the park is bare and quiet and still. A single bird calls somewhere far off. The lamp still burns, small against the dawn. His breath shows faint in the cold air. The world wakes slow beyond the old glass. A pale gold touches the frost on the panes. The half-made shape waits in the growing light.

He looks at the table, set for tomorrow. The work was his alone, not long ago. Now another presence moves through it. Her quiet has settled into the work. He did not plan for that, but it is there. Now he waits for the red light to come, and longs for the days she is here. The lonely work is not so lonely now. Without knowing it, the boy has changed. He is no longer building all alone.

The afternoon walk takes its usual path. Ako-chan crosses the park with her friends. Dango walks close, Suke-san a step behind. The three Pods glow softly along the way. The day is cold, the low sun pale. Children call far off across the field. At the park's far corner stands an old greenhouse.

She has passed it many times before. Today its door stands a little open. A light burns inside, warm and small. Through the doorway, she sees the boy. He sits bent over the long work table. His hands move slowly above something small. On the table is a shape, half-made. Metal and wires catch the lamp light. Ako-chan slows on the cold path. Then, without meaning to, she stops.

The light from the doorway falls on the grass. Dango stops too, and looks up at her. The Pods glow low — red and blue and yellow. The boy hears her step, and his hands go still. He does not turn. No one has seen him here before. His shoulders tighten in the lamp light. The cold air sits between them, thin and clear. Ruka turns her small head towards the door. Suke-san waits, calm, at Ako-chan's side. Dango's warm breath rises white in the cold.

Ako-chan does not know why she stopped. She only knows she cannot walk on. The half-made shape pulls at her, somehow. She cannot say what it is, or why. The feeling is quiet, but it is strong. She does not understand it, and does not move. She stands in the pale light and looks. The boy waits, tense, and does not move. And Ako-chan says nothing at all. She only watches, quiet, through the doorway.

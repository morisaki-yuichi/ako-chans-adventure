Night again, and the greenhouse is cold and still. The work lamp glows over the cluttered table. Outside, a single streetlight burns far off. The boy sits with a part in his hand. He turns it once, and does not move. Two nights now, and the work fights him.

Why is he making this? He asks again. He wants to know how a bond is born. That is one reason, and it is true. But there is another, deeper in his chest. It has no clear shape, and no words. When he reaches for it, it slips away. He lets his hand fall still on the table. The part lies cold and quiet in his palm. Then, slowly, he picks the work back up.

Nothing fits the way he hoped it would. A wire slips loose and will not hold. Two parts that should join stay apart. For a moment, he wants to push it all away. Then he thinks of the red light in the park. He thinks of the bond he cannot explain — the easy closeness, the years he cannot see. The wish to understand it moves his hands again. A small part falls and rolls away. He finds it in the dark and brings it back. The frost creeps white along the panes. An owl calls once, somewhere in the park.

The work is hard, harder than he thought. And yet a strange calm settles in him. Each failure shows him one more wrong way. The two reasons stay tangled inside him. The clear one, and the one without a shape. The reason can stay hidden, for now. There is no hurry here, in the dark. The boy bends close, and tries again.

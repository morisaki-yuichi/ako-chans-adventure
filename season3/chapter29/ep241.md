The old greenhouse stands in the park's far corner. No one uses it now. No plants grow there. Glass walls hold the cold night around him. A work lamp burns on the long wooden table. Outside, the dark park is silent and still. The boy sits alone, and he begins.

Small parts lie spread across the table top. Bits of metal, thin wires, screws, a few tools. He has gathered them slowly, over many nights. On a sheet of paper, his plan waits. Faint pencil lines cross the white page. He picks up a wire and turns it slowly. He cannot fully say why he is doing this. The wish to know the beginning drives his hands. If he cannot find it, he will make it. So he bends to the table, and starts to build. His fingers are cold, but they stay steady. He lays each part where the plan shows it.

The lamp throws a small warm circle of light. Beyond it, the glass walls hold the dark. A tool clicks softly against the metal. His breath shows faint and white in the cold. The pencil plan curls at one worn corner. Through the old glass, the park is black and far. The cold creeps in beneath the wide door. The lamp is the only warmth he has. He has told no one of this place. Not the red light. Not anyone at all.

He sets the first parts side by side. They do not join yet. They only wait. The shape is not a shape, not yet. He cannot see what it will become. But his hands keep moving in the quiet. Something is starting here, small and unsure. The boy works on, alone, into the night.

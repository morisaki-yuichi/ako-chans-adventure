Days have passed, and Ako-chan keeps coming. She helps a little, here and there. Today the greenhouse is quiet and pale. The boy works close beside her at the table. The form on the table has grown. It has limbs now, and the start of a head. It is no longer only parts and wires.

The body has turned a soft silver now. It is small — no bigger than a dog. Two eyes sit in the front, not yet lit. They are a pale blue, dark and still. Ako-chan looks at it, and her hand stops. Something pulls, deep and quiet, in her chest. The feeling comes before any thought. For a moment, an old ache rises in her. A silver shape moves through her mind. A pair of pale blue eyes, long gone. She breathes once, slow, and lets it pass. She looks at the shape, then at her own hands. Then she passes the boy the next part.

At the door, Suke-san has gone very still. His ears stand high towards the table. Dalu's red Pod has stopped its slow rolling. On the glass roof, a heavy foot lands twice. Kara-san peers down through the old panes. "A silver egg," he says, dry as ever. "Strange nest, you two." Ako-chan smiles, but her eyes stay on the shape. The crow watches a while longer, then is gone. The cold light rests on the small still body.

The boy works on, his eyes on the work. His hands are sure now, where hers paused. The shape holds her, and she lets it. The small body waits, its eyes unlit. And Ako-chan, quiet, cannot look away.

The greenhouse is warm in the late day light. The boy sits at the table with the small silver one. Its pale blue eyes rest on him, calm and clear. The red light moves quietly near the door. The animals lie around the room, soft and still.

The boy looks at them all, and a thought comes to him. He has never felt like this before. For all his life, the world was gray and far. Faces meant nothing. People meant nothing. Nothing reached him; nothing pulled at his heart. Through the empty days he moved alone, and did not mind. He asked for nothing, and nothing asked for him. That was simply how the world was made. He thought it would always stay that way.

But now something has changed in him. His heart turns toward the silver one on the table. It turns toward the red light by the door. It turns toward the quiet animals on the floor. Outside, the spring wind moves softly through the trees. The light fades slowly toward evening in the glass. The room is warm around him, full and alive. He feels each of them like a small fire in the cold.

The boy sits very still and lets the feeling stay. He does not fully understand what has happened to him here. But he knows that it is real, and that it is large. The gray world is still out there, far beyond the cold glass. He can see it from here, dull and small and quiet. Yet he is no longer all the way inside it. Something has pulled him out, and he cannot go back. He does not want to go back. Not now. Not ever again.

The night is deep and still over the park. The boy sits alone beside the small silver one. Its pale blue eyes glow softly in the dark. But the boy already feels there is more to do.

On the table before him lies the grey notebook. He opens it flat, in the middle, where the lines change. On the left, the old pages: short cold records of the shapes. On the right, the newer pages: drawings, joints, wires. Two kinds of lines, from one hand. He looks from one side to the other for a long time. Two reasons live in him, and he looks at both. The first is gentle. He wants to see how a bond is born. He wants to follow it back to the very first day.

The second reason is darker. It is old, and quiet. For most of his life, people have meant nothing to him. He has looked past them, grey and far and all the same. That cold thought has never fully left him, even now. He wants to gather the lost voices — the ones that are gone, the ones people drove away. He wants to keep them as proof of what was taken. So that none of it is forgotten. So that the loss has a witness, and a name. It is not a kind wish. But he does not look away from it.

The boy holds both reasons in the dark, side by side. One is soft. One is heavy. The red light has shaken the cold thought, but not undone it. The pale blue eyes watch him without a word. He feels the warm pull and the old coldness both at once. How they live in one heart, he does not know. But they do. And he will carry them both.

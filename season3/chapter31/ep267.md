The animals have drawn back a little now. The pale blue eyes shine on the table. The boy stands beside the red light and watches. For a long while, no one in the room moves. The morning is quiet around them.

The red light turns towards the small silver one. The boy feels her looking, there at his side. She says nothing. She does not reach out. She only stands and looks. She looks at the pale blue eyes, at the silver body, at the thing they made. And then the red light wavers. For one moment, the steady red shakes, the way a flame bends in a sudden draft. It has never done that before. In all the days he has watched it, the red has been sure and whole. Now something moves through it, and is held back.

The boy turns to look at her fully. He cannot see her face — no face has ever come clear to him. But the light is enough. The light tells him things a face cannot. And the light is holding something in, pressing it down, keeping it still. The lamp burns low on the table. The animals wait in the soft morning hush. The silver shape looks on with its calm blue eyes.

Then the red light steadies again. Whatever moved through it has been pushed back down. She does not speak of it. She does not let it show. The boy stands very still beside her and says nothing. What just passed through the red, he cannot name. He does not know what she holds, or why. But he saw it move. He will not forget that it moved.

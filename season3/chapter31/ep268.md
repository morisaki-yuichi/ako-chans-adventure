Night has come again over the park. The boy sits alone in his quiet room. The greenhouse is dark and locked across the grass. But his mind is still inside it, at the table. He cannot stop thinking of the red light.

He has seen that red many times now. In the park, far off, it crossed the grey. At the table, close, it glowed beside his hands. Always it was sure. Always it was whole and steady and bright. But yesterday, when the pale blue eyes opened, the red light shook. Only for a moment. Then it held itself still again. The memory turns over and over in his mind. Why did the sure red waver? What moved through it? He has no answer. The question stays with him through the long night.

The room is cold and dim around him. One small lamp burns on the desk. Outside, the spring wind passes through the bare trees. A late car hums far down the empty road. He should sleep, but he cannot. The waver is too large in his mind. It will not let him go.

Slowly, a new thought comes to him. The red light was not looking at him then. It was looking at the thing he made. She saw something in those pale blue eyes — something he cannot see, something he does not know. The small silver one means something to her. He built it with his own hands, and still he does not know what it is. For the first time, the boy feels that his work holds a secret. And the secret belongs to her.

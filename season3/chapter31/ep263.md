Morning comes pale and cold to the greenhouse. Thin light falls through the dusty glass walls. On the table lies the finished silver body. The boy and Ako-chan stand close beside it. They have waited all night for this one hour, and now it has come. Neither of them speaks. The room is very still.

The boy reaches out and touches one small switch. For a moment, nothing happens at all. Then a low sound comes from somewhere inside the body. It is faint, deep, and even. A small shake runs through the smooth silver shell. The two closed eyes do not shine yet. But something is moving in there now. Something is waking. Ako-chan holds her breath beside him. The boy does not breathe either. They only stand and watch. They do not move.

The low hum grows a little stronger in the quiet. Outside, the early spring wind stirs the bare trees. A bird calls once, then falls silent again. The animals near the wall have all gone still. Daru's red Pod glows softly in the corner. Dango lifts his head and watches the table. The thin morning light slides slowly across the floor. The whole room seems to lean towards the sound.

The silver body shakes once more, then settles. The hum runs steady now, low and warm. Slowly, so slowly, the closed eyes begin to stir. A faint glow moves behind them, deep and blue. It is not bright yet. It only waits there, like a small flame under ash. The boy bends closer over the table and holds himself still. The thing he made is trying to open its eyes. He does not know what he will see.

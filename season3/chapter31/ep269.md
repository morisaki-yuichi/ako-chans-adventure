The afternoon is soft and dim outside. In the greenhouse, the small silver one sits on the table. Its pale blue eyes follow the boy as he works. He does not want to leave it now. He does not want to lose it. The feeling is new, and he holds it close.

Ako-chan sits nearby and watches them both. The pale blue glow fills her with a strange ache. She has seen that light somewhere before. Her mind slips back to a white and frozen place from long ago. She is holding a cold silver body in her arms. The snow falls all around, and it does not stop. The one who is gone lies still against her chest. She holds it tight and cries into the cold. "Please come back," she says, soft and low. And then, deep in the dark eyes, a small light stirs. Pale blue, faint, then slowly warm and bright. The eyes open and find her face at last.

The memory is so close she can almost touch it. She remembers the red collar in her hand. She remembers setting it gently back in place. She remembers how the friends came close around her there. The ache rises in her chest, soft and deep. But she says nothing. She lets the memory rest where it lies. The small silver eyes shine on, here and now.

The boy watches her grow quiet and far away. He feels his own heart pull toward the thing he made. It is a warm pull, and a heavy one. He has no word for it. In the gray world, he never learned the name of this. But he knows he would not give it up for anything now.

The animals stand close around the table. Above them shines the pale blue light. They look up at it, and they go still. Not one of them makes a sound. Ako-chan stands behind them and listens.

Something moves through the animals in the quiet. They know this light. They know this silver shape. Suke-san looks up at it for a long time, and does not move. Luca holds still at the foot of the table. Dango's ears are soft, his tail low and slow. Deep in each of them, an old knowing stirs — this is someone we know. Then, very low, Gabu speaks. "It is him," he says. "It cannot be," says Suke-san, just as low. "It is too young." The words stop there. No name comes. The rest is too large for any sound. They only look, and wait, and hold the silence close. Ako-chan stands behind them, her eyes on the pale blue light. If the small words reach her, she gives no sign.

The animals have fallen still again. And the silence after those few words has a weight she has not felt before. It presses on the warm air of the room. It fills the space between the still animals. She feels how deep it runs, and her own breath goes quiet. The pale blue eyes move over the gathered animals, calm and slow.

There is something here that none of them can name. There is something coming that none of them can see. Far away, in a cold white place, a day is waiting that no one knows yet. The animals cannot say it. Ako-chan cannot hear it. It sits beyond all of them, past the edge of this quiet morning. For now there is only the silver shape, the pale light, and the heavy, knowing hush.

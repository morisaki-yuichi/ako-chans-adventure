The animals stand close around the table. Above them shines the pale blue light. They look up at it, and they go still. Not one of them makes a sound. Ako-chan stands behind them and listens.

Something moves through the animals in the quiet. They know this light. They know this silver shape. Suke-san looks up at it for a long time, and does not move. Ruka holds still at the foot of the table. Dango's ears are soft, his tail low and slow. Deep in each of them, an old knowing stirs — this is someone we know. But not one of them says it. No name comes. The knowing sits in them, heavy and quiet, too large for any sound. They only look, and wait, and hold the silence close.

Ako-chan can hear the animals' voices when they speak. Now they do not speak at all. And the silence has a weight she has not felt before. It presses on the warm air of the room. It fills the space between the still animals. She does not know what it means. But she feels how deep it runs, and her own breath goes quiet. The pale blue eyes move over the gathered animals, calm and slow.

There is something here that none of them can name. There is something coming that none of them can see. Far away, in a cold white place, a day is waiting that no one knows yet. The animals cannot say it. Ako-chan cannot hear it. It sits beyond all of them, past the edge of this quiet morning. For now there is only the silver shape, the pale light, and the heavy, knowing hush.

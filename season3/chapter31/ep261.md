Morning light fills the greenhouse, pale and thin. The boy bends over a wide sheet of paper. New lines run across it, fine and sure. The small silver one sits beside him on the table. Its pale blue eyes follow the moving pen.

For two days he has drawn, and thought, and drawn again. He knows now what his two reasons need. To see a bond from its first day. To keep the voices that are gone. For both, he needs one thing. He needs a way to cross time itself. It is a vast thing to build, far larger than this small body. But he can see its shape now, rising in the lines on the page. He cannot build it here. To make it, he must go to another place.

The place is far away, and cold, and to the south. He does not yet know it well, and he does not ask why. Something draws him there, the way the red once drew him. He folds the plan and looks out through the glass. The spring sky is wide and bright above the trees. The road south runs somewhere far beyond it. Then he turns back to the silver one and watches its calm eyes. He will not go alone. He will take it with him.

But he will not tell the red light. Quietly, in his heart, he begins to make ready. There will come a morning when he must leave, and say nothing. He looks at the bright glass, and at the road he cannot yet see. The small silver one waits beside him, patient and still. Outside, the animals move across the warming grass. The boy holds his plan, and his silence, and lets neither one show.

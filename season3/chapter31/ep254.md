The pale blue eyes shine in the dim greenhouse. The small silver one sits up slowly on the table. The boy stands close, still caught in its gaze. Then, near the wall, something stirs. The animals are moving. One by one, they come.

Dango comes first, low to the floor. He walks to the table and looks up at the light. He does not bark or growl. Suke-san leaves the doorway and steps near, ears high. Then the two tortoises come down from their Pods. Dalu walks out fast, then stops below the table. Gabu follows in his steady way and waits. Luca moves last of all, slow and calm. She stops beside the others and grows still. They gather close around the silver shape, quiet and sure.

The boy watches them, and he does not understand. The animals are not afraid. They show no fear at all. They come the way one comes to a known thing. It is something long missed, and found again at last. Their eyes stay on the pale blue light. The room is warm with their soft breathing. The lamp glows low against the morning chill. Dust turns gently in the thin spring light.

The small silver one looks down at them from the table. Its blue eyes move from face to face, calm and clear. The animals look back and do not turn away. Something passes between them in the quiet — something the boy can see but cannot read. He has watched these animals for many days now. He knows their ways, their distances, their care. But this is something else, and it is older than anything he knows.

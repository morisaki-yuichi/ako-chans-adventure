Summer is coming to the park. The grass is deep and green, and the afternoons are long. Ako-chan sits on the old bench with her friends. Dango sleeps against her foot, heavier these days. The walk here is slower now, and that is fine. No one is in a hurry anymore.

Across the grass stands the other bench, empty. The boy's bench, worn smooth at one corner. No one sits there now. The people of the park pass it by. Then, quietly, a yellow glow leaves the group. Luca's Pod rolls out across the warm grass, slow and steady, the way it did one winter day. It crosses the whole open field. It stops in front of the empty bench, and stays.

Luca looks up at the worn seat for a long time. She does not climb out. She does not turn back. She only waits there, small and still, where he used to sit. "She misses him," says Ako-chan, softly. Suke-san's ears turn towards the far bench. Dalu and Gabu keep their glow low. Dango lifts his head, then rests it again. The summer light lies warm over the empty seat.

After a while, the yellow Pod turns and comes back. Luca settles at Ako-chan's feet, and says nothing at all. That is her way. The afternoon goes on, gentle and slow. But now the park has an empty place of its own. A bench at the far side, worn smooth at one corner. The family knows its shape, and keeps it, like the quiet corner of a garden far away. Some places stay full of someone. The bench is one of them now.

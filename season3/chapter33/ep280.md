Late spring settles warm over the park. Ako-chan walks the old path with her friends. Dango pads slowly at her side. The three Pods roll and glow on the bright grass. At the far corner stands the old greenhouse, closed. Its glass is clean where the lamp used to burn.

A heavy flap comes down onto the bench back. Kara-san lands, and tips his head at her. "Where is the quiet one, then?" he asks. "Gone south," says Ako-chan. "Far south." "Hm. South is cold." The crow scratches at his neck feathers. "He took the silver egg?" "He took it." "Hm." Kara-san looks at the closed greenhouse, then back at her. "The bench will be colder," he says, rough and low. Then he lifts away over the trees.

Ako-chan stops at the greenhouse door and looks in. The long table is clean and bare. The tools are gone, and the little rows of parts. Only a short pencil lies forgotten by the wall. Luca's yellow Pod slows at the doorway, waits there a moment, and moves on. The spring light falls through the glass onto nothing at all. It is very quiet, the way a finished room is quiet.

That night, at her desk, Ako-chan opens the red notebook. She turns to the last line and looks at it. One name, and a long dash, faded now. She holds the pen above the page for a long while. Nothing comes tonight either. But before she closes the cover, she reads the name once, slowly. Outside, the late spring dark is soft and full. Somewhere far south, under other stars, the quiet one is walking.

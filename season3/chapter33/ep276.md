The cave holds its breath. Pen-san and General Adelie stand without moving, and the truth settles over them like falling snow. They understand what the boy cannot. The small silver friend left them long ago. It crossed into the years ahead and was lost. And now it has come back. Young. New. Walking beside the one who will one day send it away.

So this is where it began. The two birds feel the shape of it, though they have no words for such a thing. The friend they knew in the future was born here, in this cave, beside this boy. The end they remember and the start they are watching are the same moment, folded together. It is too strange to hold. And yet they hold it, here on the silent ice.

But there is no one to tell. The boy cannot hear the voices of animals. He never could, and he never will. The red light who listened to every creature is far away. She is on green grass, at the top of the world. She is not here. No one is here to receive what the two birds know. Their understanding has nowhere to go.

So they say nothing. They stand in the blue-white glow and let the knowing fill them, quiet and deep and aching. The boy bends to his pack and does not notice. The silver one blinks its pale eyes. The ice does not speak. The blue glow holds steady on the walls, cold and even. The cave seems to listen, and to hear nothing at all. Only the two old birds carry the weight of this moment. Here, in the cold, no one else will ever know.

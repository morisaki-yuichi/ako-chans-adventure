Pen-san feels it before he sees it. A hum in the air, low and familiar, a sound from a life long past. He turns his great head towards the cave mouth. Beside him, General Adelie goes stiff, the white rings around his eyes wide and fixed. Out of the dark comes a boy. And at the boy's side comes something small and silver, with eyes of blue-white light.

The two birds freeze. Neither one moves a feather. They know that shape. They know those pale eyes, and the low hum. Most of all, they know the small red collar at its neck. They travelled with it once, across ice and sea and time. They would know it anywhere, in any year, in any light.

Pen-san's heart beats hard against his chest. The friend who crossed the white land with them. The friend who vanished into the future and never came back. It is here. It is young and small, and it walks beside a stranger. The red collar is the same. The blue-white eyes are the same. There is no mistaking what stands before them in the cold.

General Adelie does not blink. His old soldier's body holds itself straight and still, the way it always has. But something moves behind his eyes — something he cannot speak, something too large for sound. In the two birds, the years wind backward all at once. A whole journey returns in a single breath. Neither bird makes a sound. The only movement in all the cave is the slow flicker of the silver one's pale eyes. And the silver one only looks at them, calm and quiet. It knows nothing of who they are.

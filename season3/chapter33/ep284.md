The boat can go no farther. Ahead lies a wall of ice, tall and white and silent. The boy steps onto the frozen shore, and his breath turns to white mist in the air. The small silver one moves beside him, its blue-white eyes lighting the ground. They walk along the wall until they find an opening — a dark mouth in the white.

Inside, the cave is old. The ice here is thick and blue, pressed down by ages no one can count. The boy's footsteps echo and fade. Far above, the roof curves up into shadow. The cold here runs deeper than the wind outside. It is a still cold, the kind that waits. Water has frozen in this place for a very long time, and nothing has moved it.

This is the spot. The boy does not know how he knows, but he knows. Here in this old cave, he will build what he came to make. A machine to cross time. It will take years. He has nothing yet, only his tools and his will and the silver one at his side. He sets down his pack and looks into the dark. The work has not begun. But it begins here.

The silver one's eyes throw pale light across the walls. The light moves, and the shadows move with it. Then the boy stops. Deep in the cave, where the blue fades to black, something is there. He cannot see it well. But he feels it — a presence, quiet and watchful. Not one. Two. Two shapes that wait in the dark. They seem to have waited a long time for someone to come.

The sea is grey and wide, and the boat moves south. For many days now, the water has carried them towards the cold. The boy stands at the rail and watches the waves roll past. Beside him, the small silver one stands still. Its blue-white eyes face the same far line. No one else is with them, and it grows colder every day.

The boy has never gone so far with only the silver one for company. In the park, there were always others near. The red light. The dog. The small ones in their glowing Pods. Now there is only the sea, the wind, and the low hum at his side. The silver one does not speak. It does not need to. It stays close, the way it has stayed close since the morning they left.

As the days stretch on, the first ice appears. Thin white sheets float past the boat, pale against the dark water. The air bites at the boy's face, and he pulls his coat tighter. The red collar at the silver one's neck is the only warm colour in all that grey. The boy looks at it and thinks of the one who tied it there. He does not say her name. He does not know it.

The world feels different now. Quieter than the park, quieter than the long road down. The boy did not expect that. He thought distance would feel empty. Instead it feels still, like a held breath. Somewhere far behind him, a red light is shining on green grass. He cannot see it anymore. But he carries the memory of it south, into the white silence.

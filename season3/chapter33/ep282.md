Pen-san stands near the working boy and does not leave. He is a great Emperor now, tall and broad, with a smooth gold throat. Long ago he was small — a round ball of soft down who cried to be held. That was another life. In that life, a small silver friend walked beside him across the white land.

Now that friend is here again, and it does not know him. Pen-san watches the blue-white eyes follow the boy's hands. The same eyes. The same soft hum. The same red collar, bright against the silver. Everything is the same, and nothing is. The friend who once knew his name now looks at him the way it looks at the ice. Calm. Clear. Empty of all their days together.

"You came back," says Pen-san, soft and low. "But you do not know us yet." The words drift across the cave and land on no one. The boy cannot hear them. The silver one does not turn. Pen-san lets the silence take his words away. He has said them anyway. Some things must be said, even to the wind.

The old ice glows faint and blue around them. The boy fits stone and steel together at the cleared floor. The General stands his watch at the wall. Pen-san settles himself, slow and heavy, a little nearer to the silver one. Time is strange, he thinks. It takes friends away, and it brings them back wrong. But it has brought this one back. That is more than he ever hoped for. He tucks his head down and keeps his vigil. He will not leave the silver one again.

Time begins to move. At first the boy counts the days, and then he stops counting. Light comes and goes at the cave mouth. Long bright weeks come, when the sun never sets. Then long dark months, when it never rises. The ice outside breaks and freezes and breaks again. Season follows season, and the boy stays, and the work goes on.

Each day he rises and bends to his task. Slowly, with great patience, something starts to take shape on the cleared blue floor. It is far from finished. It will not be finished for a long time yet. But it is no longer only an empty place. The boy's hands have grown surer over the turning months, steadier and older than before.

The silver one never leaves him. Through all the changing light, its blue-white eyes stay the same, calm and tireless. They watch every move of his hands. The two penguins keep their watch as well. They stand in the shadows, season after season, two patient shapes that do not go. The cave holds them all, the worker and the watchers, while the years move quietly past. Nothing in this place hurries. Everything waits for the work to be done.

Somewhere far to the north, a red light still shines on green grass. The boy thinks of it less often now. He is changing, here in the cold and the deep blue light. The boy who sat on a park bench is fading, a little more each day. In his place, something new is growing. Someone who works in the ice, at the bottom of the world. Someone who is becoming what he will need to be.

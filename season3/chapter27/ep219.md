The afternoon light falls soft across the bench. Ako-chan sits a little way from the boy. Neither of them says very much at all. She does not ask him where he comes from. He does not ask her name, or anything. The quiet between them is easy and calm. It does not need to be filled with words.

Luca rests in her Pod by their feet. The small yellow glow is steady and warm. For a while, the silence holds them both. Then Dango pushes his way between them. He wedges his body into the small space. He looks up at the boy and growls, low. It is one short, deep sound, and then quiet. He has never liked to share her with anyone. "Dango," says Ako-chan, soft but firm. The corgi grumbles and settles by her feet. Luca does not stir at all through this. She stays exactly where she was, calm and still.

The boy draws his hand back, slow and careful. He is not sure what the low sound meant. But one thing has reached him, clear and whole. Dango. The growling dog has a name. He turns the word over, once, then again. He cannot see her face, and he never will. But a name is a thing he can hold. It is the first name he has ever kept. The afternoon light moves on the cold grass. Dango rests his chin upon his paws. His white-flecked muzzle sinks against the ground. The pale Pod glows on, steady and yellow. A few dry leaves turn over in the wind.

Nothing is decided here, on this cold day. No plans are made. No promises are spoken. But Ako-chan feels it, quiet and sure. She will come back to this bench tomorrow. And somehow she knows the boy will too. They will both be here, by the same trees. The quiet has become a kind of meeting.

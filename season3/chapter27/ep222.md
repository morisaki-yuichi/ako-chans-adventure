Suke-san leaves Ako-chan's side and crosses the grass. He walks toward the boy on the far bench. Daru watches from his Pod, and Gabu too. Ruka's small cart has already gone ahead. The cold wind carries the boy's quiet scent. Suke-san reads it as he comes, step by step.

He stops in front of the boy and stands. He plants his feet and looks up at him. His reddish coat is bright against the frost. His three-cornered ears stand sharp and high. His eyes meet the boy's at the height of his knee. Suke-san looks at him for a long moment. He smells no fear, and no cruelty either. There is no bad feeling in this one. Long ago, Suke-san read another stranger this way. He learned to trust slowly, and he remembers. "No bad feeling," he says softly. "But I will watch." Ako-chan hears his low voice on the wind.

The boy does not pull back or look away. He sits very still and meets the gaze. The grass smells of cold earth and old leaves. Dango lifts his head, far across the field. The pale Pods glow red and blue and yellow. Suke-san's tail stays low, calm and quiet. Then he turns and walks back, without a sound. His soft steps press into the frozen grass.

The boy follows the reddish dog with his eyes. That straight look stays with him in the cold. It was not like the looks of people. Their eyes slide past him and hold nothing. But this one weighed him, and truly saw him. Something has changed, small and quiet, today. The boy does not yet know its name.

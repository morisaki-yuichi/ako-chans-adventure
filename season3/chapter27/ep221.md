The winter afternoon is pale and still. The boy sits on his usual bench. Across the cold grass, something is moving. One of the little tortoises has left the others. Its small glowing cart rolls slowly towards him. The pale yellow light moves over the frost. The boy watches it come, and does not move.

The cart crosses the grass, slow and steady. It comes nearer, then nearer still. At last it stops, right at his feet. The small tortoise sits inside its glowing cart. It lifts its head and looks up at him. Its dark eyes are calm and round. They blink, slowly, once and then again. The boy holds very still on the bench. No animal has ever come this close to him. People are only grey shapes, far away. But this small thing is here, and real. It has come to him, across the whole cold field.

The pale light glows softly on the grass. A thin shadow falls behind the little cart. The wind moves once through the bare branches. Far off, the red light has stopped too. The dry leaves lie quiet around his feet. The boy's hand rests, frozen, on his knee. His breath is shallow in the cold air. He does not dare to move and break this.

The small tortoise keeps looking up at him. It is not afraid. It does not turn away. Something warm stirs slowly in his chest. Slowly, carefully, the boy leans down. He bends towards the small, waiting thing. He is closer now than he has ever been. The little eyes follow him all the way down. And the boy, for once, is not alone.

The boy comes back to the bench each day. The red light is there, most days, waiting. Today she sits nearer than before, and speaks. Her warm voice drifts to him on the cold air. He still cannot see her face at all. There is only the red, steady and bright.

She does not tell him very much at all. She made a journey once, she says. She went very far away, across the world. She crossed the sea, and the ice, and more. Then her voice slows, and almost stops. There was someone who was always with her. Someone who stayed beside her the whole way. But that one is gone now, she says. She does not say where, or how, or why. The red light turns and looks far off. It gazes past the trees, toward the cold sky. The boy does not ask her anything back. He has no words, and would not use them.

The winter wind moves low across the grass. The bare branches creak softly overhead. The little tortoise sits still in its cart. Its small head stays out, calm and low. The pale light glows on the frozen ground. A crow lifts from a branch and is gone. The cold grows deeper as the day fades. The red light is quiet for a long while. The boy waits beside her, saying nothing.

Many of her words drift away from him. The journey, the sea, the far-off ice — they fade. But a few words stay, and will not leave him. Someone who is gone. He turns them over, slow, inside his mind. He does not know who, or why it hurts. But he feels the weight beneath her quiet voice.

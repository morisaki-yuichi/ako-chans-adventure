The boy has gone home now, in the dusk. Ako-chan sits alone with the animals. The words she spoke still echo in her. Someone who is gone. The memory will not wait. It rises now, cold and bright and clear. She lets herself remember the friend who left.

It happened in a cave of old ice. Far in the south, where the cold runs deep. They all travelled there together, the whole way down. The journey's work was done at last. But the one who is gone had to travel on. There was a door of white light, opening. A small, calm shape stepped towards it. The pale blue eyes shone, soft and steady. Then the white light grew, and filled the cave. The pale blue glow began to fade away. The friend who left was going to the future. The light shrank, smaller and smaller, then was gone.

The cave was so silent afterward. The cold ice pressed in from every side. Pen-san and the others stood frozen, watching. No one moved. No one made a sound. The pale blue light did not come back. The place beside her stayed bare in the dark. She reached out her hand to nothing. Her fingers closed on empty air. Nothing was there. Nothing at all.

Years have passed since that distant day. The friend who left has never come back. Ako-chan still keeps a small red notebook. One line waits there, unfinished, on the page. She has never been able to write the rest. She does not know, even now, how it ends. The park is dark. That place is still empty.

The boy stays on his bench in the cold. The little tortoise rests near his feet. Far across the grass, the small dog watches. The reddish dog has gone back to its place. And further still, the red light moves softly. The winter wind is thin and sharp today.

The boy thinks about the way they look at him. People look at him too, all the time. But their gray faces slide past and away. Their eyes never hold him, never stop. A person's gaze means nothing he can read. It is empty, like fog with no shape. The animals are not like that at all. The little tortoise looks straight up at him. The dog looked straight at him too, before. Their eyes are clear, and dark, and real. They rest on him and do not slide away. They seem to ask something, or to tell him something. For the first time, the boy feels seen.

The pale cart glows yellow on the frost. The small head turns, slow and careful. The dog sits far off, calm and still. Its sharp ears point toward the bench. The red light waits at the edge of the trees. A crow calls once from a bare branch. The cold air bites at his bare hands. But the boy hardly feels it now.

Something is entering his gray world, quietly. It comes through the eyes of small, living things. It is not dull. It does not slide away. It looks at him, and it stays. The boy sits very still and lets it come. He does not understand it yet. But he does not want it to stop.

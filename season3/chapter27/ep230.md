The afternoon is bright and very cold. The boy sits and watches from his bench. Today he is not only watching the red. He is watching the spaces around her too. The small dog, the reddish dog, the tortoises — each one keeps its own careful place.

The small dog walks close beside the red light. It stays near her feet, and matches her steps. When she stops, the small dog stops as well. The reddish dog keeps back, a little behind. It stands always to her right, and watches. The little tortoise rests just by her feet. Its pale cart glows there, calm and still. None of them is too far, or too near. Each holds a place that seems exactly right. The boy looks at the spaces in between. The careful gaps, the steady distances, the quiet order. They are not by chance, he is sure. Each space seems to carry a hidden meaning.

The cold light falls flat across the grass. The thin shadows lie long beneath them all. A crow moves on a bare black branch. The wind comes and goes through the trees. The boy's eyes travel from one to the next. He measures each distance, slow and careful. He reads them like marks he cannot name.

He cannot say what the spaces mean. But he is certain now that they mean something. Each gap was made by something he cannot see. These distances did not happen in a day. Behind them lies a long, long stretch of time. A time he does not know, and was not part of. The boy sits very still and wonders at it.

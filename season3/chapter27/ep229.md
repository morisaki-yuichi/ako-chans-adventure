Night has come, and the boy is home. He sits alone by his cold window. Outside, the dark park is far away. The streetlights burn small and orange below. He cannot sleep. His mind keeps turning. His mind returns to the red light.

He has never thought so much about a person. People were always grey, and far, and empty. They passed by, and meant nothing. But the red light is not like them. He thinks of how she comes most days. He wonders why she comes there at all. And he wonders, too, why he keeps waiting. Each afternoon, he walks the same cold road. He sits, and his eyes search for the red. When it is not there yet, he aches. When it comes, something in him grows quiet. He does not understand this, not at all. He has never wanted to be near anyone.

The window glass is cold against his hand. The dark presses soft against the house. A car goes by, then all is still. The orange light falls on the empty street. The house is silent all around him. The night is long and very deep. Then he closes his eyes and sees the red. It glows there, warm, behind his eyelids. It will not leave him, even in the dark.

The boy has always been alone, and content. Being apart was simply how things were. But tonight, the quiet feels strange and thin. There is a red light, somewhere in the dark. And he wants the morning to come quickly. For the first time in all his life, the boy waits for tomorrow.

Ako-chan sits on the bench in the pale light. The boy is quiet beside her now. She has told him she once went far away. And saying it has brought the journey back. The cold park fades a little around her. The old roads rise again behind her eyes.

She remembers the white rocks by the sea. The sun was bright on them, long ago. That was where Daru first came to them. He was so small then, and so quick. He ran across the stones, fast and glad. "Let me see! Let me see!" he cried. She remembers the hot red sand of the desert. Gabu came slow and careful over the dunes. She remembers a green mountain, far in the east. It rose steep and high above the trees. That was the place that carried Suke-san home. So many places, so many friends, so far.

The winter light lies thin upon the grass. The memory light is warmer, and more gold. The two glows seem to meet and overlap. The bare trees stand quiet around the bench. Ruka rests still inside her yellow Pod. A bird calls once, far off and thin. The cold afternoon holds its breath around them. The boy watches the far sky, saying nothing. Ako-chan lets the old roads shine a while.

At the edge of all of it, there is one who is gone. She does not let that memory come too close. The journey ended years ago, she knows. They all came home, at last, from the cold. And yet, somehow, it has never really ended. It still goes on, quietly, deep inside her. The far places live on, bright, behind her eyes.

The little tortoise still rests by his feet. The boy is leaning down towards it, close. Then the red light moves across the grass. It comes towards him, slow and bright and steady. He has only ever watched it from afar. Now it is here, near enough to touch.

The red light stops a little way from him. He cannot see her face, even this close. Where a face should be, there is only red. It glows warm and clear in the cold air. All the other people are dull and faded. But this one light is red, and always has been. Then, for the first time, the light speaks. "This little one likes you," she says, softly. Her voice is warm and quiet in the air. The boy does not know what to say. No words come up his tight, dry throat. He has never answered anyone in his life.

The small tortoise blinks up between them, calm. The pale cart glows yellow on the frozen ground. The glow waits, gentle and unhurried, before him. The wind moves the bare branches overhead. Somewhere the small dog shifts in the grass. The boy's heart beats hard against his ribs. The cold air stings, but he does not notice. Everything in him is fixed on the red. He swallows, and his throat works once.

He cannot speak. The words will not come. But the red is still waiting there for him. So the boy lifts his chin, just a little. He gives one small, slow nod, and no more. It is the most that he can give. The warm glow seems to understand him. And the boy, who is always silent, has answered.

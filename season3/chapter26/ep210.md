It is another quiet afternoon in the park. The boy sits on his bench as always. The grey shapes of people move far off. Nothing here is ever very different. The bare trees stand dark against the sky. Frost lies white in the shaded grass. The cold air bites at his bare face. The boy watches the slow, faceless crowd pass.

Then something crosses the open park. A red light moves across the grass. It is not grey like all the others. It is red — clear and bright and warm. The boy sits up. His breath stops short. He has never seen such a thing before. Every person has always been grey to him. But this one light burns red. It moves with a small dog at its side. The boy follows it with his eyes. He cannot look away from the red.

The red light crosses the wide green space. The small dog trots close beside it. The low sun lays gold across the grass. A thin wind stirs the empty branches. The red glows warm against the pale winter grass. The boy's hands stay still on his knees. He hardly feels the bench beneath him. The red moves on towards the far trees. Then it slips behind them and is gone.

But the red stays before his eyes. Its bright mark hangs in the winter air. The boy looks at the empty place. He looks for a long while after. The cold deepens, but he does not move. He does not know what he has seen. He has no name for it at all. He only knows it was not grey.

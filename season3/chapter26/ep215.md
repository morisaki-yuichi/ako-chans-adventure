Now the boy comes to the park every day. He sits on the same hard bench. He waits through the long winter afternoon. The bare trees creak in the bitter wind. More dry leaves gather at his feet. A pale fog hangs along the far paths. The people pass, faceless and slow.

Most days, the red light comes too. It crosses the park with its small dog. The three little carts roll close behind. The boy knows the hour it will come. He finds himself watching for it now. His eyes turn to the far path early. He waits for the first sign of red. When it does not come, the day feels long. When it comes, something in him settles. A small warmth wakes inside his chest. He does not understand why this is so.

Each day the sky wears a new colour. Pale white, then thin gold, then dull cloud. The leaves on the ground grow deep and brown. The same bench holds the same cold. The same wind moves the same bare trees. Crows call from the high, empty branches. Their black shapes cross the white sky. The cold deepens as the days go on. But the boy waits for one thing only. One red light, in all the grey.

The boy has lived in grey for years. Nothing has ever pulled at him before. But now there is this one bright thing. One red light to wait for each day. In the whole grey world, there is this. One small warmth he cannot explain. And it is enough to bring him back. It is enough to make him want tomorrow.

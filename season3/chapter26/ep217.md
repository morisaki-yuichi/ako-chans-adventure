Winter holds the park quiet and bright. Ako-chan sits with her friends as always. The bare trees stand black against the sky. The low sun gives a thin, pale light. Frost still hides in the shaded corners. The cold air is sharp and clean. A pale fog still hangs near the pond.

The park is full of small lives. A crow sits high on a bare branch. It calls down, rough and plain and clear. A squirrel runs up a frosted trunk. Two ducks paddle on the cold pond. A small wren hops in the dry hedge. Ako-chan hears all their small voices. The crow speaks of seeds and quiet days. The squirrel chatters fast about its store. The ducks talk low about the icy water. Every voice is calm. Nothing here is wrong. The world is settled, and it is at peace.

A robin lands near Ako-chan's feet. It cocks its head and looks at her. Dango watches it but does not move. The crow lifts off in a slow, dark flight. The squirrel freezes, then darts away again. Cold leaves rattle along the winter paths. Ako-chan breathes the bright, still air. She hears the small park speak all around. Each voice is soft and kind.

Far across the park, the boy sits too. He looks at the same trees and birds. But he hears no voices on the wind. He does not know the park can speak. Two worlds rest in one quiet place. One is full of voices. The other is silent. Side by side they pass the winter afternoon. And neither one knows of the other yet.

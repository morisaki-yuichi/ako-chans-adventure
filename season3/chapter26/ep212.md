The afternoon is cold and clear today. Ako-chan sits on the bench by the trees. Dango lies close beside her feet. Suke-san keeps to his place behind her. The three Pods rest in the grass nearby. Dalu's glows red, Gabu's blue, Luca's yellow. The wind carries the smell of frost.

Then Suke-san lifts his nose to the air. He turns towards the far end of the park. A boy sits alone on a bench there. Suke-san watches him, calm and still. The boy is not like the other people. He does not look at the people at all. He is looking straight at them — at the animals. Suke-san reads him by his smell and air. He is sure of what his nose tells him. "That one is alone," says Suke-san softly. "He watches us. No bad feeling."

Dalu turns his red Pod towards the far bench. "Then I want to see him closer!" he says. "Stay," says Suke-san, calm and low. Gabu speaks last, slow, from his blue Pod. "...Too still, for a young one," he says. "I will wait." Only Luca turns her small head towards the boy, and keeps it there. She says nothing at all. The animals fall quiet again. The frost glitters on the grass between the two benches. The boy watches on, far across the grass.

Ako-chan hears them, each small voice in turn. Three friends, three answers, and no one agrees yet. But she does not turn to look. She keeps her eyes on her own friends. The boy is only a far-off shape to her. She does not yet know that he is there. Not really. Not the way he already knows them.

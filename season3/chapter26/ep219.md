The afternoon sun turns gold and low. Long shadows stretch across the frosted grass. The boy sits and watches as always. The red light moves at the park's edge. Soon it will turn and go home. The cold creeps in as the sun drops. The park empties slowly in the fading day. The boy stays on his bench, alone.

A question has grown in the boy's mind. Why is only one light red? Every other person is dull and grey. He has counted them his whole life. All the same. All faceless. All grey. But this one light is not like them. This one burns clear and red. He does not know the reason why. He cannot explain it to himself. There is no answer he can find. And still the red is there, each day. It does not fade. It does not turn grey.

The sun sinks lower behind the trees. The red light starts to move away. It crosses towards the far park gate. The boy watches the way it goes. He turns his cold palm up, open and bare. The last glow is thin and pale. But there is nothing there to hold. The question stays, with no reply. A crow calls once in the dim dusk.

The boy has never wished for much. The grey world asked nothing of him. He took it as it was, and lived. Nothing reached him, and nothing was lost. But now he wants one simple thing. He wants to know more about the red. Now, at last, something in him reaches out — towards a thing that is not grey.

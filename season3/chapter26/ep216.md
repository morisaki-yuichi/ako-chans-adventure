The afternoon is cold and clear today. Ako-chan sits on the bench by the trees. Dango lies close beside her feet. Suke-san keeps to his place behind her. The three Pods rest in the grass nearby. Daru's glows red, Gabu's blue, Ruka's yellow. The cold wind carries the smell of frost.

Then Suke-san lifts his nose to the air. He turns towards the far end of the park. A boy sits alone on a bench there. Suke-san watches him, calm and still. Daru stops walking in his Pod. He glances at the boy too, just once. The boy is not like the other people. He does not look at the people at all. He is looking straight at them — at the animals. Suke-san reads him by his smell and air. He is sure of what his nose tells him.

"That one is alone," says Suke-san softly. "He watches us. No bad feeling." Ako-chan hears his small, low voice. She catches the quiet words on the wind. Dango lifts his head, then sets it down. The grass smells of cold earth and frost. Suke-san's ears turn towards the far bench. Daru takes a slow step in his Pod. The three little tortoises stir in their Pods. The boy stays far off, watching still. His eyes do not leave the small group.

Ako-chan does not turn to look. She keeps her eyes on her own friends. She has heard Suke-san, and that is all. The boy is only a far-off shape to her. She does not yet know that he is there. Not really. Not the way he already knows them.

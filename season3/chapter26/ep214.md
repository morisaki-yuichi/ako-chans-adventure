The next day, the boy comes back early. He sits on his bench and waits. He does not say why, even to himself. The winter park is quiet and hushed. The pale people drift along the paths. Frost still whitens the shaded ground. His breath shows pale in the still air. He keeps his eyes on the far grass.

Then the red light comes again. This time it passes a little closer. The boy can see it more clearly now. A small dog walks proudly at the front. Behind it roll three glowing little carts. Each one carries a small tortoise inside. They give off a soft, low light. One glows red, one blue, one yellow. The boy can see the dog and carts well. He can see each tortoise clearly too. But the person among them is only red.

The three small glows move close together. They roll slowly over the cold grass. The dog lifts its nose at every smell. The carts hum softly as they turn. The red light walks among the little lights. The bench is hard beneath him. The boy leans forward, watching it all. He has never cared to watch before. Now he cannot make himself stop.

The red light turns and moves away. The small dog and the carts follow it. The boy stays on his bench a while. He watches until they are far off. Their glow fades into the winter dusk. The cold dusk gathers under the bare trees. The park grows grey and quiet again. Then one clear thought rises in his mind. He will come here once more tomorrow.

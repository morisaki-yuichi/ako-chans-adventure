The boy keeps to his far bench each day. The red light comes with its animals. From across the park, he watches them. A wide stretch of grass lies between. He does not cross it. He stays where he is. Frost lies in the shade between them. The afternoon is cold, clear, and still.

He does not know how to go closer. He has never walked up to anyone. People are only grey, and grey means nothing. He has had no one to walk towards. So he watches from far away instead. He sees how the red light moves. He sees it bend down to the small dog. He sees its hand rest on the dog's back. He sees the little tortoises roll near its feet. He watches each small thing they do together. He learns them slowly, day by day.

The winter wind crosses the open grass. It carries the far sound of the dog. A reddish dog sits guard behind the red light. The little carts glow soft against the pale ground. The reddish dog keeps its steady watch. Its ears turn at each far sound. The boy's hands are cold inside his sleeves. He hardly feels the cold at all, though. His eyes stay on the small group.

It is enough, for now, to watch. The wide grass keeps him safely apart. The distance feels safe and right to him. He does not need to be closer yet. The red light and its animals fill his quiet winter afternoon. And yet something stirs in him slowly. Something he has never felt is moving. He cannot give the feeling a name.

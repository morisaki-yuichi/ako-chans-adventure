## Overall Issues

### Story Development

The four chapters form a well-structured three-act setup:
- Ch12: the peaceful interlude (introducing Ruka, establishing the new normal)
- Ch13: the inciting mystery (the signal, the locked memory)
- Ch14: the preparation (building the flying boat)
- Ch15: the reunion arc begins (Croatia, Jordan)

Pacing is generally good. Ch12 is the most leisurely (appropriate as a "life has resumed" opening), Ch13 escalates steadily, Ch14 is active but episodic (the day gaps work), and Ch15 is emotionally satisfying. No plot points are left dangling within this range.

**One structural concern:** Ch14's Ep15 through Ep20 (six episodes) covers the flying boat build across 14 days (story_days 12–26), with most episodes being quiet observation scenes. The build is handled with restraint, but the six-episode span with minimal plot movement may test reader patience. Specifically Ep16 ("Night Work") and Ep17 ("Hands") cover consecutive days and could potentially be condensed into one episode without losing anything essential. This is a structural note rather than an error.

### Setting Consistency

**Generally consistent.** The major elements carry over correctly from Season 1:
- Obia's inner light (S1 Ep16, Ep77) is correctly referenced in S2 Ep12 without over-explaining.
- The Colonel is referred to as "the Colonel" throughout (timeline confirms Ako-chan does not know about the promotion yet). ✓
- The hedgehog's "the ground is quiet" (Ep4) is consistent with S1 resolution.
- Gabu's three-steps-stop movement pattern is maintained.
- Daru's running straight with no pause is maintained.
- Suke-san's right-side-slightly-back position is mentioned (Ep19, Ep22 implied).

**One setting inconsistency — Ruka's space in the flying boat (Ep21):**
Ruka is placed in a "warm corner near the back wall" with "soft orange light." The orange light choice is ambiguous — it unintentionally echoes Gabu's S1 pod colour. This could be changed to "warm yellow" (matching the description of Ruka's mini-capsule in design-decisions.md: "発光色：黄色 / yellow") or simply "a warm soft light" without colour specification.

**Ruka's arrival date (Ep2) — already noted above** — is the most significant setting inconsistency in the batch.

### Text Naturalness

The prose is consistently clean and well-paced throughout. The short-sentence rhythmic style (a strength from S1) is maintained. The following minor awkwardness points are worth noting:

**Ep2:** "She brought Ruka back to England. That was just a few weeks ago." — the timeline contradiction already noted also reads awkwardly because it conflicts with the "few years" time-skip established in Ep1. A reader of Ep1 ("She had been home for a few years now") who then reads Ep2 ("just a few weeks ago") will find Ruka's arrival date confusing.

**Ep8:** "She thought about the animals before the journey. The hedgehog. The fox. The swallow. The barn owl." — this is a fine callback list, but note the original S1 Chapter 1 animals also include the robin. The robin is mentioned separately in this episode ("The robin landed in the oak tree and sang once."), so its omission from the callback list is slightly incomplete. This is a very minor point.

**Ep15 (ending):** As noted above, the episode ends with Obia saying "Yes. This will do." — this is abrupt. An episode should close with at least a brief beat showing how Ako-chan receives the moment. The bare dialogue close works as a stylistic choice, but the episode is also the shortest in the batch (272 words), suggesting the beat was trimmed rather than intentionally spare.

**Ep19:** "She thought about who they needed to collect. Daru was on his coast in Croatia. Gabu was in his desert in Jordan. Suke-san was in the forest near the sea in Japan. Riro was in the Pacific. Pen-san and the Colonel were on the ice." — this is a useful reminder but reads slightly list-like, which is a sign of word-count filling. The prose could be tightened, though it serves the episode's planning purpose.

**Ep29:** "Of course you counted the days," said Daru. — In context, this line is delivered as though surprised but immediately self-correcting. It works, but it slightly undermines the effect because the next line is also "Of course you counted the days" level-flat ("Yes," said Gabu.). The beat is sound; the phrasing is slightly mechanical as noted above.

### Difficult English

The overall vocabulary level is well-controlled for A1-A2. The following specific items are worth flagging:

**Ep1:** "She had been home for a few years now. So much had changed. And yet the garden was the same garden." — "And yet" is a subordinating phrase more typical of A2+ writers' English. For pure A1 it could be "But the garden was the same garden." Not a hard violation for A1-A2.

**Ep5:** "Were they finished? She did not know." — The implied question is subtle. A1 learners may find the rhetorical question form (no question mark, not a stated dialogue) slightly unusual. Minor.

**Ep8:** "She thought about the animals before the journey. The hedgehog. The fox. The swallow. The barn owl. All of them had felt the same thing." — "All of them had felt" uses the past perfect ("had felt"). The writing guide explicitly **prohibits past perfect** for Season 1 (A1), though Season 2 allows more flexibility (A1-A2). However, the Season 2 tense table in writing-guide.md lists allowed tenses as: present, past, present progressive, plus future (will) and can/could — it does not explicitly permit past perfect. Past perfect constructions should be checked carefully. "All of them had felt the same thing" is a mild case; "they had felt" could be rewritten as "they all felt."

**Ep9:** "A natural sound is not like this." / "A signal is made. Something or someone makes it." — These are accessible.

**Ep11:** "Obia had always carried more than she knew." — This is an idiomatic construction. The phrase "more than she knew" (meaning "more than Ako-chan was aware of") is not immediately decodable by A1 learners without context. Borderline A2. Acceptable for Season 2 but worth noting.

**Ep12:** "Something is locking it. And I do not have the key." — Clean A1.

**Ep13:** "I think someone put it there. I think it was closed on purpose." — "on purpose" is an idiom (= deliberately/intentionally). For A1-A2 learners of Japanese background, this is a common enough idiom that it's likely fine, but it is an idiom the guide says to watch. Could be replaced with "I think someone closed it. I think it was not by accident."

**Ep15:** "A flat pale surface, and on it, lines of light moved slowly. Measurements. Shapes. Numbers." — "Measurements" (5 syllables) may be at the edge of the target vocabulary range. It can be replaced with "numbers" or "lines and numbers."

**Ep18:** "Not checking. Just — looking. As if it was adding something to what it knew about her." — "As if" is a subjunctive-adjacent construction. It is comprehensible in context, but idiomatically the phrase "adding something to what it knew" is slightly opaque. For A2 it is acceptable; for strict A1 it would need rephrasing.

**Ep20:** "Two hand-widths" — this is a concrete, visual measurement and works well at A1.

**Ep21:** "Something moved in her chest." — this idiomatic body-sensation is used throughout the series for emotional experience and is well-established within the text's own register. Fine.

**Ep25:** "A pride she had not seen in him before. Or maybe it was something new — something that had grown over the last few years, the same way he had grown." — This is the most complex sentence in the 30-episode range. It contains a comparative clause with "the same way," a time reference ("over the last few years"), and a subtle implication ("had grown" for both Daru's size and his maturity). For A2 this is acceptable but sits at the upper edge. The sentence is not a hard violation, but it is the kind of passage that could trip an A1 reader.
